# The Nine-Lens Framework

The fixed format every module follows. Nine lessons, 6 minutes each, in the same order every time.

The nine lenses are not arbitrary. Each engages a different cognitive register, so a student who comes alive in one register and skims the others still gets the concept. By the end of the 54-minute module, the same idea has been encountered nine ways — which is approximately the depth at which a non-trivial idea actually sticks.

Within a module, the lenses run sequentially. Across the 52 weeks, each lens repeats every week — which means by week 5, the student has done the "see-it" lesson five times in a row, the "count-it" lesson five times in a row, and so on. That repetition trains the *meta-skill* of how to learn this material. The cognitive load drops; comprehension rises.

---

## The nine lenses, in order

### Lens 1 — **See it** (visual encounter)
*Purpose:* Plant the concept in the visual cortex before the verbal one.

What it is: 6 minutes of looking at a diagram, animation, or hand-drawn figure with minimal narration. The host points, traces, slows down. Students draw along on paper.

Output: by minute 6, the student has the canonical image in their head.

### Lens 2 — **Count it** (numerical / arithmetic)
*Purpose:* Make the concept arithmetically concrete with hand calculation.

What it is: a pencil-and-paper worked example. Whatever this week's concept is, do it numerically. If it's the Doubling Circuit, double 1, 2, 4, ... reduce, repeat. If it's the Number Map, compute cells of the grid by hand.

Output: by minute 6, the student has *done* the math, not just watched it.

### Lens 3 — **Build it** (geometric / physical / hands-on)
*Purpose:* Move the concept from page to space.

What it is: a paper construction, a 3D model, a dot-to-dot, a folding exercise, a physical movement. For every theme there's a way to make it tangible — even if it's tracing a path with the body or arranging objects in a room.

Output: by minute 6, the concept lives in the student's hands and body, not just their eyes.

### Lens 4 — **Hear from Marko / Randy** (primary-source voice)
*Purpose:* Center the originators. This is *their* work.

What it is: a primary-source clip, quote, or transcribed passage. Either Rodin or Powell narrating the concept in their own words, with the host providing minimal framing. Where neither has spoken on this exact concept on the public record, a Karen Elkins / Art Tawanghar / sanctioned lecturer stands in.

Output: by minute 6, the student has heard the originator's voice on this concept — preserving the lineage and the cadence of the work.

### Lens 5 — **Feel it** (spiritual / philosophical / contemplative)
*Purpose:* Engage the meaning-making register. Numbers are not symbols; they're alive.

What it is: 6 minutes oriented around the contemplative dimension. The Bahá'í origin of "Abha". The Symbol of Enlightenment as enlightenment. The 9 as Spirit. Sometimes a guided contemplation; sometimes a reading; sometimes a host reflection. Avoids being preachy — the goal is resonance, not conversion.

Output: by minute 6, the student has held the concept against questions of meaning. Even students uninterested in the spiritual framing have engaged with it once and made up their own mind.

### Lens 6 — **Apply it** (practical / engineering / use case)
*Purpose:* Show the concept doing work in the world.

What it is: a concrete application. For mathematical themes, this might be the Rodin Coil winding, a phyllotaxis prediction, a DNA mapping. For geometric themes, this is the torus surface as a coil substrate, the Decoqubit as a quantum-computing motif. For everything: where does this *land* outside the diagram?

Output: by minute 6, the student knows one thing this concept does that the world cares about.

### Lens 7 — **Compare it** (mainstream math / science adjacent)
*Purpose:* Place the concept on the map of conventional knowledge. Students will be asked about this; we equip them.

What it is: 6 minutes of intellectual respect for the conventional view. "Mainstream mathematics calls this modular arithmetic. Here's how it works there. Here's where Rodin extends it." "Topology calls this surface a torus, written `S¹ × S¹`. Here's the standard treatment. Here's how the ABHA Torus is the same object plus a number-decoration plus a physical claim." This lens is the bridge — students should leave it able to talk to a professor.

Output: by minute 6, the student knows the conventional vocabulary and where Rodin's work sits in relation to it.

### Lens 8 — **Defend it** (objections + responses)
*Purpose:* Pre-arm the student for pushback they will absolutely encounter.

What it is: the strongest objection to *this specific concept* (not the field as a whole) and the org's preferred response. Tone: respectful, specific, doesn't dismiss the questioner. The student practices articulating the response.

Output: by minute 6, the student can answer the objection in their own words without becoming defensive.

### Lens 9 — **Connect it** (synthesis: looking back, looking forward)
*Purpose:* Weave the concept into the year. This is the lens that turns 52 weeks into one course.

What it is: explicit connection. "Last week you saw X; this week's concept is Y; here's the relationship. In Q3 we'll meet this again from a third angle. Notice these three things now so you'll recognise them later."

Output: by minute 6, the student has the concept slotted into the year-long mental map. They know what came before, what's coming next, and what to keep an eye on.

## The lens-to-track mapping

In v1 the course had three audience tracks. v2 dissolves them into the lens structure:

| v1 audience | Where they live in v2 |
|-------------|----------------------|
| Foundations / beginner | Strongest engagement: Lenses 1, 3, 4, 5, 9 (visual, hands-on, source-voice, contemplative, narrative) |
| Intermediate / STEM-literate | Strongest engagement: Lenses 2, 7, 8, 9 (arithmetic, conventional comparison, defense, synthesis) |
| Builder / practitioner | Strongest engagement: Lenses 3, 6, 8, 9 (hands-on, application, defense, synthesis) |

Every student watches all 9 lenses; they self-select where their attention sharpens.

## What a single lesson looks like (the 6-minute spec)

Every 6-minute lesson has exactly the same internal shape:

| Minute | Section | What's in it |
|--------|---------|--------------|
| 0:00–0:30 | **Hook** | One sentence: "By the end of these 6 minutes, you'll be able to ___." Optional pinned visual. |
| 0:30–1:00 | **Setup** | Where this fits this week (we're on lens N of 9 for theme T). Quick prior-lens callback. |
| 1:00–4:30 | **Body** | The lens-specific teaching. 3.5 minutes. Tight. |
| 4:30–5:30 | **Try it** | The student does something — solve a problem, repeat a phrase, draw a line, take a measurement. |
| 5:30–6:00 | **Bridge** | One sentence: "Next lesson we'll come at the same idea through ___." |

The hook + bridge structure means a student who watches lessons out of order can still navigate; a student who watches sequentially gets a tight braid.

### Per-lesson asset budget (production planning)

| Lens | Diagram count | Animation count | Build/demo? | Quote citation? |
|------|---------------|-----------------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1 See | 1–2 | 0–1 | — | — |
| 2 Count | 0–1 | 0 | — | — |
| 3 Build | 1 (template) | 0–1 | yes | — |
| 4 Hear | 1 (still) | 0 | — | yes (verbatim) |
| 5 Feel | 0–1 | 0 | — | sometimes |
| 6 Apply | 1 | 0–1 | — | — |
| 7 Compare | 1–2 | 0 | — | — |
| 8 Defend | 0 | 0 | — | sometimes |
| 9 Connect | 1 (concept-map fragment) | 0 | — | — |

Per module: ~7 diagrams, ~2 animations, 1 build/demo, 1+ verbatim quotes. Per year: ~360 diagrams, ~100 animations, 156 build/demos, 52 verbatim quote clips. These numbers feed the production plan.

### Style consistency

All 1,404 lessons share:
- Same opening titlecard format ("Week N — Theme T — Lens L of 9")
- Same lower-third typography
- Same colour palette (per `05_assets/DIAGRAM_INVENTORY.md`, awaiting org sign-off)
- Same closing card with theme number and Q1/Q2/Q3/Q4 progress bar

A student watching their 87th lesson should feel they're 87 lessons into one course, not eight courses cobbled together.

## What this framework demands of the org

1. **Editorial line on Lens 5 (spiritual / philosophical).** How explicit should the Bahá'í framing be? How much contemplation vs. exposition?
2. **Editorial line on Lens 7 (compare).** How much mainstream math should the course actually teach as a contrast? (Some students will love this; some will resent the comparison.)
3. **Editorial line on Lens 8 (defend).** Tone — respectful engagement, brief acknowledgement, or full debate? Affects 156 lessons.
4. **Approved verbatim-quote list for Lens 4.** ~52 quotes, one per theme, ratified by Rodin / Powell / Elkins / org.
5. **Style standards.** Colour palette, typography, music motifs. Once locked, these don't change for 1,404 lessons.
