## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Marko's *prime number sieve* claim. The Symbol of Enlightenment, when decorated with the right structure, displays the *prime numbers* in a non-arbitrary pattern. Marko has presented diagrams (Phyllotaxis Prime Number Sieve) showing primes mapped onto positions in a way that suggests structural significance.
>
> The claim has two parts:
>
> *Part one.* Primes display patterns under digital-root reduction. *True.* Primes other than 2 and 3 always have digital roots in {1, 2, 4, 5, 7, 8} — the units of `(ℤ/9ℤ)*`. This is a number-theoretic fact: any prime > 3 is coprime to 3 (so not in family 3) and coprime to 9 (so a unit mod 9). The fact is well-established.
>
> *Part two.* The specific patterns Marko's sieve displays reveal *deep structure* about prime distribution. *Contested.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What's contested. Marko's sieve diagrams show primes displayed in specific positions on a phyllotactic spiral. The diagrams are visually striking. But:
>
> *Mainstream number theory* has well-developed prime-distribution results — the prime number theorem, Riemann hypothesis, etc. — none of which involve the Symbol's specific geometry.
>
> *Marko's sieve* hasn't been independently verified to predict prime distribution beyond what mainstream techniques already predict.
>
> *Visual patterns* in primes are common: there are *many* ways to display primes that look meaningful. Whether Marko's specific display reveals structure mainstream methods miss is unclear.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> So the calibrated answer to *is the prime-number structure really there?*:
>
> *Marko's sieve correctly notes that primes other than 2 and 3 are units mod 9 — this is a standard number-theoretic fact. Marko's stronger claim that the sieve reveals deep structure beyond mainstream prime theory is not established. The Phyllotaxis Prime Number Sieve is suggestive visualisation; whether it predicts prime distribution beyond standard techniques requires independent verification, which has not been published in mainstream number theory journals.*
>
> Sixty-three words.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. When you describe the prime-sieve aspect of VBM, lead with the *mainstream-true* part: *primes other than 2 and 3 are units mod 9*. Mention Marko's sieve as a visualisation Marko has developed; don't claim it as established mainstream number theory."
