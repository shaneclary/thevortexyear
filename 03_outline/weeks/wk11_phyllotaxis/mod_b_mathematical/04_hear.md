> *"Some years after I drew the Symbol, I plotted the prime numbers on a phyllotactic spiral. The primes — except for two and three — all fell on the same six positions. The family-three positions were empty of primes. The pattern was striking. I named this *the Phyllotaxis Prime Number Sieve*. Whether the sieve reveals deeper structure than mainstream number theory already shows — that's a question I leave to specialists. What I can tell you is that visualising primes this way changes how you see them. Instead of an unpredictable scatter, you see a *coherent pattern* organised around the family structure of mod-9. Primes have a *home* on the Symbol — six homes, distributed across the units. The Phyllotaxis sieve is a *display tool*. Whether it predicts new prime distributions is contested. Whether it makes prime distribution legible — that's straightforwardly true."*
