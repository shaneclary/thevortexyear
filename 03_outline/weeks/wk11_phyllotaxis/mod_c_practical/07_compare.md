## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "*Plant geometry (phyllotaxis).* Many interlocking spirals on a 2D disc or 3D conical surface. Spirals at Fibonacci ratios (5:8, 8:13, 13:21, 21:34, 34:55, ...). Each leaf or seed is one node; nodes total in tens to thousands.
>
> *Engineered Rodin Coil geometry.* One helical spiral on a 3D toroidal surface. Helix follows the doubling cycle (24 positions per traversal × 4 traversals = 96 turns). Wire passes through 96 nodes.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three differences.
>
> *Difference one — surface.* Plants: 2D disc or 3D cone. Engineered Rodin Coil: 3D torus. Different topologies.
>
> *Difference two — number of spirals.* Plants: many spirals (5+8+13 = 26 visible in pineapple, or 21+34 = 55 in sunflower). Coil: one helix.
>
> *Difference three — node count.* Plants: tens to thousands of nodes. Coil: 96 turns.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Where they're similar.
>
> *Both use Fibonacci-related counts.* Plants follow Fibonacci spiral counts. The coil's 24-position cycle is the Pisano period of Fibonacci mod 9.
>
> *Both result from optimal-packing principles.* Plants pack growth optimally; the coil's specific winding is mathematically optimal for the doubling cycle.
>
> *Both are scale-invariant under the Pisano period.* The 24-period mod 9 governs both phyllotactic numbers' digital roots and the Rodin Number Map's columns.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Useful framing. *Plants and engineered Rodin Coils share Fibonacci's period-24 structure but differ in topology, spiral count, and node count. Plants pack growth on 2D discs with many spirals; engineered coils put a single helix on 3D toroidal surfaces.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Engineering implication. If you want to match plant geometry's *optical efficiency*, you'd build something more like a phyllotactic disc than a toroidal coil. If you want to match the *electromagnetic structure* the doubling cycle produces, you'd build a toroidal Rodin Coil. The two engineering goals point to different geometries.
>
> Some advanced builders combine elements: a toroidal Rodin Coil with phyllotactic stators or shielding. Hybrid configurations are research-frontier."
