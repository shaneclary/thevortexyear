## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "A *biomimetic Rodin Coil* uses phyllotactic placement rather than the canonical doubling-cycle helix.
>
> *Standard Rodin Coil.* 24 winding positions, single helix following the doubling cycle 1-2-4-8-7-5.
>
> *Phyllotactic Rodin Coil (proposed).* Many winding positions (often 55 or 89, Fibonacci numbers), placed at 137.5° rotations and increasing distances from a centre point. The pattern fills a 2D disc rather than a torus.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Implementation requires:
>
> *Custom former.* 3D-printed disc with 55 (or 89) drilled positions matching the phyllotactic spiral pattern. STL files don't exist standardly — builders generate them from phyllotaxis algorithms.
>
> *Custom winding.* Wire passes through positions in a *non-cyclic* sequence (since the phyllotaxis isn't cyclic). The wire visits each position once, in spiral order from inner to outer.
>
> *Custom termination.* Wire ends at the outermost position; doesn't return to start.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What this might give you. Marko's hypothesis is that the doubling-cycle structure produces specific electromagnetic effects. The phyllotactic structure is *related but distinct*. A phyllotactic Rodin Coil might:
>
> *Produce different field structures* due to its 2D-disc rather than 3D-toroidal geometry.
>
> *Couple to incident light* better than toroidal coils (mimicking plants' optical efficiency).
>
> *Reveal connections between the doubling-cycle and Fibonacci spirals* — both have period 24 mod 9.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Honest qualifier. *No standard published research* on phyllotactic Rodin Coils exists. The design is speculative. A few practitioners have built variants and reported anecdotal results; rigorous comparison studies haven't been published.
>
> *Building a phyllotactic Rodin Coil* is appropriate for advanced builders interested in research-frontier work. Most students should stick with the canonical doubling-cycle build.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. The phyllotactic-coil idea is *open*. If you want to do experimental research, this is a frontier. If you want to engineer reliable coils with established theory, stick with the canonical build."
