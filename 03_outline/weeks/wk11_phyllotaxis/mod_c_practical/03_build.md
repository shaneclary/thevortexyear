## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "A *phyllotactic generator* is a device that places points (seeds, holes, markers) at successive 137.5° rotations and increasing radii. Useful for:
>
> Build templates for phyllotactic art.
>
> Test patterns for Rodin Coil winding (placement guides).
>
> Educational demonstrations of golden-angle packing.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Two implementation options.
>
> *Option A — 3D-printed disc.* A flat disc with holes drilled at successive 137.5° angles and increasing radii. Each hole accepts a seed-shaped marker (or a pin). Place markers in sequence; the spiral pattern emerges.
>
> Course materials include an STL file for a 60-position phyllotactic disc. Download, print on any 3D printer, populate with markers. Total cost: $5-10 in PETG filament.
>
> *Option B — manual construction with paper and protractor.* Same construction by hand. Take a sheet of paper, draw a centre dot, and place 60 dots at successive 137.5° rotations with increasing radii. Slow but free.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Why build one?
>
> *For Rodin Coil work.* The phyllotactic generator gives you a template for *non-doubling-cycle winding patterns*. Some experimental Rodin Coils use phyllotactic placements rather than the canonical Doubling Circuit positions. The generator helps mark these.
>
> *For visual study.* Watching the spirals emerge in real time as you place dots is a visceral demonstration of the math.
>
> *For demonstration.* Keeping the generator in your build space is a daily reminder that the math is producing real geometry.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Build log. *Phyllotactic generator (sketch or STL) added.* Date the entry."
