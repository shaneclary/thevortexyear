> *[pause]*
>
> Observations.
>
> *Family three (roots 3, 6, 9): 1 prime.* That's just the prime 3. Other family-three positions are empty (because primes > 3 can't be in family three).
>
> *Roots 6 and 9: zero primes.* No prime > 3 can sit at these positions.
>
> *Distribution across units (1, 2, 4, 5, 7, 8): 3, 5, 3, 4, 5, 4 = 24 primes.* Not perfectly uniform. Some units (2, 7) have 5 primes; others (1, 4) have 3.
>
> The non-uniformity is small for primes ≤ 100; it averages out over larger ranges. Marko's claim that the *non-uniformity reveals deep structure* is contested. The non-uniformity is real but consistent with random distribution within the units."
