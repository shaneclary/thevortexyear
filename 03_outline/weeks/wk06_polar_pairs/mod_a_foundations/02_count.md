> Three pairs sum to 9: *(1,8), (2,7), (4,5)*. Confirmed.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What about pairs from the full {1, 2, ..., 9}? Adding 9 itself doesn't produce new sum-to-9 pairs, because 9 + anything-non-zero exceeds 9. Adding 3 or 6 creates the pairs (3,6) which sums to 9 — but neither 3 nor 6 is in `(ℤ/9ℤ)*`, so this pair lives in the side cycle, not the main Polar Pair structure.
>
> The pair (3, 6) is real and important — it's the *3-6 oscillation* we'll cover in Week 8 — but it's not called a Polar Pair in standard VBM terminology. The three Polar Pairs are specifically (1,8), (2,7), (4,5) — the pairs from the multiplicative group's units.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One observation. The Polar Pairs are the *six elements coprime to 9*, partitioned into three antipodal pairs. The arithmetic property (sum to 9) and the group property (units of mod 9) and the geometric property (diametrically opposite on the Symbol) all coincide. The pairs are picked out three different ways and the same six elements emerge. That coincidence is part of what Marko reads as evidence of structure."
