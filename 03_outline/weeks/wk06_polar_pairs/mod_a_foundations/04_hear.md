> *"The polar pairs hold the geometry of the Symbol together. If you take away the doubling cycle, you still have the pairs. If you take away the family groups, you still have the pairs. The pairs are the most basic structural fact about the Symbol — three diameters, three sums to nine, three pairs of mutually-opposite elements. When I am teaching someone for the first time and they ask me what the Symbol *is*, I do not start with doubling. I start with the pairs. Look at the diagram. Three lines through the centre. The lines connect numbers that sum to nine. That is the Symbol's bones. Everything else — the cycles, the orbits, the family groups, the wraps to torus — sits on top of the bones. If you carry one fact about the Symbol, carry the pairs. They will let you reconstruct everything else."*
> — Marko Rodin, paraphrased. **Source:** P1-1, P1-3.
