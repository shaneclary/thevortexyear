## 0. Authoring notes
Pairs as the fastest reduction shortcut. *Sum to 9 and you're done.* Apply to mental arithmetic.

## 1. Outcome
Student can use Polar Pairs to reduce numbers mentally faster than digit-summing.

> "The pairs make reductions fast. Today: how to use them mentally."

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)
> "Lens 6 of 9. The pairs as memory shortcut. Six minutes."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "When you reduce a number by digit-summing, you sum the digits and reduce. The polar pairs let you do this *faster mentally* by recognising sum-to-9 pairs in the digits.
>
> Here's how. Look at the number 1789. Digits: 1, 7, 8, 9.
>
> Standard reduction: *1 + 7 + 8 + 9 = 25*. Then *2 + 5 = 7*.
>
> Polar-pair shortcut: notice the polar pair (1, 8) in the digits. *1 + 8 = 9*. Drop them — they cancel to nine, which absorbs into the digital root as +9 ≡ 0. Notice the pair (7, ?) — no 2 in the digits, so the 7 doesn't have a partner here. Wait, but there is a 9 — and 9 is its own self in the digital root. So we have 7 + 9 left. *7 + 9 = 16 → 7*. Match.
>
> *[pause — let me restate this more carefully]*
>
> The shortcut: when you see digits that form a polar pair, *drop them*. They reduce to 9, which doesn't change the running total mod 9.
>
> Example: 198. Digits 1, 9, 8. Drop the (1, 8) pair. Left with 9. Digital root is 9. Verify: *1 + 9 + 8 = 18 → 9*. Match.
>
> Example: 247. Digits 2, 4, 7. Drop the (2, 7) pair. Left with 4. Digital root is 4. Verify: *2 + 4 + 7 = 13 → 4*. Match.
>
> Example: 4527. Digits 4, 5, 2, 7. Drop (4, 5). Drop (2, 7). Nothing left. Digital root is 9. Verify: *4 + 5 + 2 + 7 = 18 → 9*. Match.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> The shortcut speeds up reductions of numbers with multiple digits. Once you've internalised the three pairs — (1,8), (2,7), (4,5) — your eye picks them out of digit strings automatically. You drop the pairs, sum what's left, and arrive at the digital root faster than digit-by-digit summation would allow.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical note. The shortcut is most useful for mental arithmetic — checking your work, verifying a quick calculation, casting out nines on a long total. For computer-assisted reductions (the spreadsheet macro from Wk 3), digit-summing is fast enough that the shortcut isn't needed.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One observation worth carrying. The shortcut is exactly what *casting out nines* did historically — clerks dropped sum-to-nine pairs from columns of digits to speed verification. The polar pairs are the modern naming of an old technique."
