## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

**[ORG-CLIP]**

> *"Antiphase is a property of paired strands. When two wires sit on opposite sides of a torus and carry currents in opposite directions, their magnetic field components add along some axes and cancel along others. The cancellation is direction-specific. For a polar pair on a Rodin Coil, the cancellation falls along the line connecting the two strands — the line through the centre of the torus. Drive both strands of a pair in antiphase and you get a clean field-cancellation along that axis. Drive them in phase and you get reinforcement along that axis. The choice — phase or antiphase — is a design parameter you choose at the terminals. The Wk-35 build's standard configuration is *antiphase per pair* — each pair's two strands drive opposite directions. That gives you three perpendicular cancellation axes, which produces the most balanced field structure. Other configurations are possible and have specific uses, but antiphase is the default."*
> — Randy Powell, paraphrased. **Source:** P1-7, P1-13.
