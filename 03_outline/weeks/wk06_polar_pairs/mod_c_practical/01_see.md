## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The Polar Pairs aren't just diagrammatic — they correspond to *physically opposing wire strands* on a Rodin Coil.
>
> Recall: the Doubling Circuit becomes a helical winding on the torus. Each turn lands at a vertex. The Polar Pairs — (1, 8), (2, 7), (4, 5) — sit *opposite each other* on the torus's surface.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What does this mean physically? Two ways.
>
> *Single-strand coil — Polar Pairs are crossings.* In a single-strand Wk-33 build, the wire visits all six vertices in cycle order. The pair-relationship is *temporal* — the wire passes vertex 1 at turn N, then visits vertex 8 at turn N+3 (after three doublings). The pair members are visited *three turns apart* in the same wire.
>
> *Multi-strand coil — Polar Pairs are simultaneous strands.* In a Wk-35 multi-strand build, you wind multiple wires simultaneously. The pair-relationship can become *spatial* — strand A starts at vertex 1, strand B starts at vertex 8, both proceed through their cycles in parallel. Now (1, 8) is *physical opposition* of two strands at every moment in the build.
>
> The Polar Pairs structure works *both ways* — temporally in single-strand, spatially in multi-strand. Both are valid expressions of the underlying pair relationship.
>
> *[pause — show single-strand timing diagram and multi-strand spatial diagram side by side]*
>
> The multi-strand version is what enables *direct field-cancellation*. When two strands carry currents in opposite directions through physically-opposing positions, their magnetic fields can cancel along the antipodal axis. Three pairs of antipodal strands give you three independent cancellation directions — one per pair.
>
> Wk 35's multi-strand build uses exactly this. Six strands wound simultaneously, paired into three Polar Pairs. The build's electromagnetic behaviour reflects the pair-cancellation structure.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Today is preview. The actual multi-strand build is Wk 35. You should leave this lesson with the visual understanding: *Polar Pairs are antipodal strands*. The math you've spent this week on becomes hardware in Q3."
