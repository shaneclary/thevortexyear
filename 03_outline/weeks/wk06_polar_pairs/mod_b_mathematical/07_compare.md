## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The Polar Pair structure — pairs of additive inverses summing to the modulus — exists in every modular arithmetic system. The specific pairs depend on the modulus.
>
> *Mod 5* — units {1, 2, 3, 4}. Pairs summing to 5: (1, 4), (2, 3). Two pairs. The cyclic group `(ℤ/5ℤ)*` has 4 elements, so there are 4/2 = 2 pairs.
>
> *Mod 7* — units {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}. Pairs summing to 7: (1, 6), (2, 5), (3, 4). Three pairs. Same count as mod 9.
>
> *Mod 9* — units {1, 2, 4, 5, 7, 8}. Pairs summing to 9: (1, 8), (2, 7), (4, 5). Three pairs.
>
> *Mod 11* — units {1, 2, 3, ..., 10}. Pairs summing to 11: (1,10), (2,9), (3,8), (4,7), (5,6). Five pairs. The group has 10 elements.
>
> *Mod 13* — units {1, ..., 12}. Pairs: (1,12), (2,11), (3,10), (4,9), (5,8), (6,7). Six pairs.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Pattern. *In mod n where n is prime or has particular structure, there are (n-1)/2 Polar Pairs in `(ℤ/nℤ)*`*. For mod 9 (where 9 is composite), there are 6 units and 6/2 = 3 pairs.
>
> The number of pairs equals half the size of the multiplicative group of units.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What's specific to mod 9.
>
> Mod 9 has the *digit-summing trick* — every power of ten reduces to 1 mod 9 — that makes the Polar Pairs visible *in the digits of any number written in base 10*. Mod 7 and mod 11 don't have such a clean digit-trick. Mod 9's Polar Pairs are the easiest to *recognise* without a calculator.
>
> Mod 9 also has *exactly three* pairs — small enough to memorise, large enough to be structurally rich. Mod 5 has two pairs (less rich); mod 11 has five (harder to memorise at sight).
>
> So mod 9's Polar Pairs sit in a sweet spot: visible in digits, three in count, structurally rich, base-10-friendly. That sweet spot is part of what Marko points at when he claims base 10 is privileged.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One consequence for the year. The Symbol of Enlightenment is the *visualisation* of mod-9's Polar Pair structure. The same structure exists in mod 7 or mod 11, but their Symbols would have 6 or 10 vertices and different visual properties. The choice of base 9 (= base-10 modular arithmetic) gives the Symbol its specific aesthetic and structural balance."
