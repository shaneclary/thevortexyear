> "Mod A heard Marko on 'pairs hold the topology'; Mod B heard Marko on antipodes. Today: *Powell on the engineering of counter-winding cancellation*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What 'cancellation' means in counter-winding.*
>
> *In a counter-wound coil*, *currents flowing in opposite directions in paired strands* *produce magnetic fields that partially cancel*. *Specifically*: *external fields produced by Strand A oppose external fields produced by Strand B at corresponding antipodal positions*; *the two fields' contributions to the external field add to zero (or near-zero) at distance*.
>
> *Mainstream electromagnetic property.* *Bifilar coils, twisted-pair cables, balanced transmission lines all use this principle.*
>
> *VBM-tradition counter-winding applies the principle to the doubling-cycle helix specifically.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three engineering principles for counter-winding.*
>
> *Principle one — cancellation is partial, not perfect.* *Real-world counter-winding produces near-cancellation, not perfect cancellation*. *Wire imperfections, geometric variations, frequency-dependent effects all cause residual external field.* *Engineering specification: cancellation ratio (e.g., -40 dB at 1 MHz).*
>
> *Practical applied.* *Don't expect perfect cancellation; specify and measure realistic ratios.*
>
> *Principle two — internal field doesn't cancel.* *Inside the toroidal volume*, *the two strands' fields add (don't cancel) when properly counter-wound*. *External cancellation; internal addition.* *Distinguishing internal from external behaviour is key.*
>
> *Practical applied.* *Different measurement points yield different field contributions.*
>
> *Principle three — frequency response matters.* *At low frequencies, cancellation is high*; *at high frequencies, parasitic capacitances reduce cancellation*. *Engineering specification includes frequency range.*
>
> *Practical applied.* *Test counter-winding at multiple frequencies; characterise frequency response.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three things Powell's framing brings.*
>
> *Thing one — calibrated expectations.* *Counter-winding gives partial-cancellation, not magic.* *Practitioners expect realistic numbers, not impossible ones.*

>
