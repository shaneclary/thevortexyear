## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Lens 6 completed the layout document. Today: *compare VBM counter-wound coils with mainstream bifilar coils*. Where do they sit relative?
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Mainstream bifilar coils.*
>
> *Bifilar coil.* *Two wires wound together as a tight pair*; *typically twisted around each other before winding around the former*. *Used for low-inductance pairs, common-mode chokes, instrument-amplifier inputs.*
>
> *Properties.* *Tight magnetic coupling between strands; near-perfect cancellation of external common-mode fields; low differential inductance; high common-mode inductance.*
>
> *Mainstream variants.* *Twisted-pair cables; toroidal common-mode chokes; balanced transmission lines.*
>
> *VBM counter-wound coils.*
>
> *Counter-wound Rodin Coil.* *Two wires wound on the doubling-cycle helix*, *with the second strand offset by polar pairing*. *Strands occupy antipodal positions around the major circle*; *not tightly twisted together; spatially separated.*
>
> *Properties.* *Counter-coupled at antipodal positions; spatial separation between strands; partial cancellation; specific magnetic field topology determined by doubling-cycle helix.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three structural similarities.*
>
> *Similarity one — both are counter-coupled designs.* *Two strands; opposite-direction current; cancellation properties.*
>
> *Similarity two — both use mainstream electromagnetics.* *Maxwell's equations apply to both*; *standard analysis methods apply.*
>
> *Similarity three — both have measurable cancellation ratios.* *Differential vs common-mode response; mainstream metrics.*
>
> *Three structural differences.*
>
> *Difference one — winding geometry.* *Bifilar: strands tightly paired; minimal spatial separation*. *VBM counter-wound: strands at antipodal positions; significant spatial separation.* *Different magnetic field topologies.*
>
> *Difference two — coupling characteristics.* *Bifilar: very tight coupling; low differential inductance; high cancellation*. *VBM counter-wound: coupling depends on doubling-cycle helix specifics; cancellation is geometry-dependent rather than tight-pair-driven*. *Different engineering profiles.*
>
> *Difference three — design specifications.* *Bifilar: simple 'two wires together'*. *VBM counter-wound: 'two wires per doubling-cycle helix specification'*. *Different specification complexity.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three engineering trade-offs.*
>
> *Trade-off 1 — bifilar gives higher cancellation; counter-wound gives specific field topology.*
>
> *Bifilar*: *if your goal is maximum common-mode rejection*, *bifilar wins*. *Tight pairing maximises cancellation.*
>
> *Counter-wound*: *if your goal is the specific magnetic field structure produced by the doubling-cycle helix*, *counter-wound is required*. *Topology is the goal*; *cancellation is one consequence among others.*
>
> *Trade-off 2 — bifilar simpler to build; counter-wound requires more specification.*
>
> *Bifilar wins on simplicity*; *counter-wound wins on engineering distinctiveness.*
>
> *Trade-off 3 — applications differ.*
>
> *Bifilar applications: common-mode chokes, balanced lines.* *Counter-wound applications: VBM-tradition engineering; possibly distinctive RF or magnetic field applications.*
