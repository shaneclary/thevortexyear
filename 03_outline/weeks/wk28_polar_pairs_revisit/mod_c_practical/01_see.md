>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What 'counter-wound strands' means.*
>
> *Counter-wound design.* *Two (or more) wire strands wound on the same toroidal former*, *with phasing such that current in one strand flows in opposite direction relative to the other at corresponding positions*. *Counter-coupling.*
>
> *VBM-specific counter-wound design.* *Strand A traverses the doubling cycle (1 → 2 → 4 → 8 → 7 → 5).* *Strand B traverses the same cycle but offset such that at each major-circle position, B carries current in the opposite direction to A.*
>
> *Specifically, Strand B starts at the polar partner of A's start — i.e., at position 8.* *As both strands traverse their cycles, polar-paired positions carry counter-direction currents.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three engineering features of counter-wound designs.*
>
> *Feature one — current-direction antipodality.* *At each polar-pair position*, *the two strands carry currents flowing in opposite directions*. *(1, 8) pair: at position 1, current goes left; at position 8, current goes right (or vice versa).*
>
> *Feature two — magnetic field interaction.* *Counter-direction currents produce magnetic fields that interact*. *Constructive interaction at certain points; destructive at others.* *Specific field topology.*
>
> *Feature three — common-mode rejection.* *External magnetic interference affecting both strands equally* *cancels at their differential output*. *Mainstream balanced-design property.*
>
> *Three features; mainstream electromagnetics applied to specific VBM winding pattern.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Visual — what it looks like.*
>
> *Two coloured wires* (red and blue) *wound on the decorated torus former*. *Red traces the helix path through positions 1 → 2 → 4 → 8 → 7 → 5*. *Blue traces the same path but offset 180° on the major circle* — *Blue at 8 when Red at 1; Blue at 7 when Red at 2; etc.*
>
> *At each major-circle position, the two strands occupy antipodal angular slots.* *Visible as colour-segregated pattern around the torus.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three engineering implications.*
>
> *Implication one — multi-strand wire procurement.* *Two strands → twice the wire length*. *Engineering planning.*
>
> *Implication two — measurement methodology change.* *Measurements take place across the differential — i.e., voltage between Strand A's leads and Strand B's leads, not absolute*. *Differential-measurement technique.*
>
> *Implication three — design parameter expansion.* *Single-strand: one design parameter set (turns, dimensions, etc.).* *Counter-wound: additional parameters (relative phasing, lead arrangement, differential vs absolute connection).* *More to specify; more design freedom.*
