## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Lens 1 set up the rig. Today: *count axial pulses per second* in a driven coil and articulate frequency relationships.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Axial pulse rates.*
>
> *Drive circuit can produce axial currents at any frequency*; *practical ranges depend on application*.
>
> *Common drive frequency choices.*
>
> *DC (0 Hz) — steady current.* *Useful for static field measurement; baseline.*
>
> *Low AC (1-100 Hz) — quasi-static.* *Power-line frequency analogue; behaves like DC for many measurements*.
>
> *Audio (100 Hz - 20 kHz) — audible range.* *Many oscilloscope-friendly measurements; convenient for demonstrations*.
>
> *RF (20 kHz - several MHz) — radio frequencies.* *Resonance measurements; antenna behaviour*.
>
> *VHF/UHF (>30 MHz) — high frequencies.* *Specialised measurements; advanced setups required*.
>
> *Five frequency ranges; engineering choice depends on coil resonance and measurement goals.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Frequency relationship to coil cycle.*
>
> *The wound coil has its own resonance frequency (LC frequency) determined by inductance and parallel/series capacitance*. *For a typical 60mm × 15mm Rodin Coil with self-capacitance, resonance is in MHz range*; **`[ORG-INPUT]`** *for foundation-specified resonance ranges*.
>
> *Axial drive at multiples of the coil's resonance frequency*: *6× resonance, for instance, gives 6 axial pulses per cycle*.
>
> *6 axial pulses per cycle* corresponds to *axial drive frequency = 6 × wound-coil-cycle frequency*.
>
> *If wound coil cycles at 100 Hz, 6 axial pulses per cycle = 600 Hz axial drive*.
>
> *Mathematical: pulse rate = N × cycle rate, where N is the desired pulses-per-cycle*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three engineering considerations.*
>
> *Consideration 1 — synchronisation.* *Whether axial drive is synchronised with wound coil cycle determines what behaviour emerges*. *Synchronised pulses have constructive / destructive phase relationships; unsynchronised create beat patterns*. *Engineering design choice.*
>
> *Consideration 2 — pulse shape.* *Axial drive can be sinusoidal, square wave, triangle, or pulse train*. *Different shapes produce different effects*. *Engineering specification.*
>
> *Consideration 3 — duty cycle for pulse trains.* *Pulse trains have on-time fraction (duty cycle)*. *Different duty cycles produce different time-averaged effects*. *Engineering parameter.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three count examples.*
>
> *Example 1 — DC drive (0 pulses per second).* *Steady current; no pulses; baseline reference.*
>
> *Example 2 — synchronous 6-per-cycle drive.* *Axial drive at 6× wound-coil resonance frequency; 6 pulses per cycle; coupled to doubling-cycle's 6-fold structure.* *Foundation-recommended for canonical demonstrations*; **`[ORG-INPUT]`**.
>
> *Example 3 — broadband modulated drive.* *Axial drive frequency-modulated across a range*; *measures frequency-dependent coil response*. *Diagnostic measurement.*
>
> *Each example has specific count; specific engineering purpose.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three observations on the count.*
>
> *Observation one — pulse rates are engineering choices.* *Drive circuit allows arbitrary rates*; *practitioners choose per measurement goal*. *Mainstream signal-generator capability.*
>
> *Observation two — synchronisation with cycle structure is foundation-canonical.* *6-per-cycle drive corresponds to doubling-cycle's 6-fold structure*; *foundation specifications guide.* **`[ORG-INPUT]`**
>
> *Observation three — measurement protocols specify rates.* *Q3 Wks 30+ will define measurement protocols including specific drive frequencies and pulse rates*; *today's lens previews.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Practical guidance.*
>
> *For first-time axial-drive measurements*: *DC and low AC (60 Hz mains-derived)* is *easiest*. *No synchronisation challenges; widely available drive sources.*
>
> *For canonical Rodin Coil measurements*: *6 × wound coil resonance frequency*. *Synchronous drive; foundation-canonical.* **`[ORG-INPUT]`** *for specifics.*
>
> *For diagnostic measurements*: *frequency-swept drive*. *Maps coil response across frequencies; richer data.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Count axial pulses per second per drive frequency choice.* DC, low AC, audio, RF, VHF/UHF — each appropriate for different measurements. *Synchronous 6-per-cycle drive is foundation-canonical for VBM-tradition measurements.* *Engineering choice; foundation specifications guide.*"
