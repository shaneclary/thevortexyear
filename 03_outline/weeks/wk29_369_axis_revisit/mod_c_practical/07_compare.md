## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Lens 6 set up the mutual-inductance experiment. Today: *compare two drive modes — axial drive (through axis wire) vs surface drive (through wound coil)*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Surface drive.*
>
> *Surface drive*: *current is supplied directly to the wound coil's terminals*; *flows through the doubling-cycle helix*. *Standard inductor operation.*
>
> *Properties.* *Wound coil acts as a primary winding*; *full coil current flows in the helix*; *magnetic field produced by coil current through coil geometry*. *Mainstream coil drive.*
>
> *Used for*: *direct coil energisation, resonance experiments, basic inductor function.*
>
> *Axial drive.*
>
> *Axial drive*: *current is supplied through the axis wire*; *flows through the central hole; doesn't flow through wound coil directly*; *induces effects in wound coil through mutual coupling*. *VBM-tradition specific to Rodin Coil's axial-modular design.*
>
> *Properties.* *Wound coil acts as secondary*; *induced EMF appears across coil terminals*; *coil's own current is an induced response, not directly applied*. *Modulation source distinct from coil itself.*
>
> *Used for*: *triggering experiments, resonance probing, mutual-inductance characterisation.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three structural similarities.*
>
> *Similarity one — both produce magnetic fields.* *Different field topologies, but both energetic.*
>
> *Similarity two — both inductively couple.* *Surface drive: self-inductance of coil. Axial drive: mutual inductance between axis and coil.* *Same physics, different geometric arrangement.*
>
> *Similarity three — both characterisable with standard instruments.* *Voltmeters, oscilloscopes, signal generators apply to both.* *Mainstream measurement equipment.*
>
> *Three structural differences.*
>
> *Difference one — primary vs secondary role.* *Surface drive: coil as primary. Axial drive: coil as secondary.* *Inductance role differs.*
>
> *Difference two — coupling mode.* *Surface drive: direct current in coil. Axial drive: induced current through mutual flux.* *Different coupling.*
>
> *Difference three — engineering applications.* *Surface drive: standard inductor / resonator usage.* *Axial drive: triggering / pulsing / modulation usage.* *Different functional roles.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three engineering trade-offs.*
>
> *Trade-off 1 — coupling efficiency.* *Surface drive: 100% coupling (current is direct).* *Axial drive: partial coupling (mutual inductance < self-inductance generally).* *Different efficiency.*
>
> *Trade-off 2 — control flexibility.* *Surface drive: one drive parameter (coil current).* *Axial drive: independent axial-current parameter (frequency, amplitude, modulation), separate from any wound-coil response.* *Axial drive offers richer control.*
>
> *Trade-off 3 — application target.* *Surface drive: maximises coil's electromagnetic output.* *Axial drive: enables trigger/pulse/modulation patterns that surface drive can't produce easily.* *Different goals; both legitimate.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three combinations possible.*
>
> *Combination 1 — surface drive only.* *Standard mainstream inductor operation.*
>
> *Combination 2 — axial drive only.* *Trigger / modulation source; wound coil is passive secondary.*
>
> *Combination 3 — both surface AND axial drive simultaneously.* *Most flexible; full control of both drive modes; modulation possibilities richest.* **`[ORG-INPUT]`** *for canonical multi-drive specifications.*
>
> *Three combinations; engineering choice depends on experiment.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three observations on the comparison.*
>
> *Observation one — Rodin Coil's distinctive feature is supporting both drive modes.* *Mainstream toroidal coils don't typically have integrated axial drive*; *Rodin Coil's modular axis enables it.* *Engineering distinctive.*
>
> *Observation two — measurements should test both.* *VBM-distinctive claims about axial drive* require *measurement of axial-mode-only and combined-mode behaviour*. *Both modes characterised; comparison meaningful.*
>
> *Observation three — frequency response matters.* *Surface drive's frequency response* (resonance, etc.) *combines with axial drive's frequency response* in non-trivial ways. *Two-dimensional frequency-response space.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Useful framing. *Surface drive supplies current directly to the wound coil; axial drive supplies current through the central axis wire, inducing effects in the wound coil through mutual inductance. They produce different couplings, control modes, and engineering applications. The Rodin Coil's modular axial drive supports both modes; combined-mode operation (surface and axial drive simultaneously) provides richest control. Engineering experiments can characterise each mode separately and combinations.*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Axial drive and surface drive are distinct engineering modes for Rodin Coil*: *primary vs secondary; direct vs induced; standard vs trigger.* *Both characterisable with mainstream measurement; combinations enable richest experiments. Wk 33+ builds will use both.*"
