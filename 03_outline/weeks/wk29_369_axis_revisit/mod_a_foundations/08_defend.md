## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The objection. *VBM presentations sometimes invoke a Tesla quote about 3, 6, and 9: 'If you only knew the magnificence of the 3, 6 and 9, then you would have a key to the universe.' Did Tesla actually say this? Even if he did, doesn't using it overclaim Tesla's endorsement of Marko Rodin's specific framework? Citation rigor matters; using disputed quotes erodes credibility.*
>
> *The objection is sincere; quote-citation deserves care.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *The honest defence.*
>
> *The Tesla quote's authenticity is contested. Use it carefully — citing the quote with the caveat that it's traditionally attributed but not historically verified, and noting that even if authentic, Tesla's interest in 3, 6, 9 doesn't constitute endorsement of any specific subsequent framework. Tesla's broader interest in numerical resonances is documented; his specific endorsement of VBM is not. Honest citation preserves credibility.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three layers in the defence.*
>
> *Layer one — quote authenticity.* *The 3-6-9 quote is widely attributed to Tesla but lacks definitive primary-source citation*. *Multiple Tesla biographies don't include it; the quote may be apocryphal or modern misattribution.* *Honest acknowledgment: contested authenticity.*
>
> *Layer two — Tesla's documented numerical interests.* *Tesla was documented to have interests in numerical patterns, resonances, and certain numbers (notably 3, 6, 9 in some accounts of his behaviour, e.g., counting steps in threes)*. *Some basis for the 3-6-9 association; not invention.*
>
> *Layer three — endorsement vs association.* *Even if Tesla had numerical interests in 3-6-9*, *this doesn't constitute endorsement of Marko Rodin's specific Vortex-Based Mathematics framework*. *Association ≠ endorsement; honest distinction.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What can be defended.*
>
> *Defend 1.* *Tesla's documented numerical interests*. *Multiple sources reference his attention to specific numbers and patterns*; *baseline historical fact.*
>
> *Defend 2.* *General resonance between Tesla's interests and VBM's centring on 3, 6, 9.* *Not coincidence; Tesla's interest predates and may have influenced subsequent frameworks*.
>
> *Defend 3.* *The framing 'Tesla had numerical interests including 3, 6, 9; VBM also centres on 3-6-9 axis structure'* is *honest historical framing*. *Connection without endorsement claim.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What can't be defended.*
>
> *Can't 1.* That the specific quote is verified. *It isn't; contested authenticity.*
>
> *Can't 2.* That Tesla endorsed Rodin's framework. *Tesla died in 1943; Rodin's framework developed after.* *Tesla couldn't have endorsed VBM specifically.*
>
> *Can't 3.* That Tesla's interest in 3-6-9 corresponds exactly to Rodin's 3-6-9 axis treatment. *Tesla's interest is numerological / interest-based; Rodin's framing is mathematical-engineering*. *Different specifics.*
>
> *Honest acknowledgment of citation limits.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Honest articulation to a citation-skeptical audience.*
>
> *Articulation.* 'The 3-6-9 quote attributed to Tesla — "if you only knew the magnificence of the 3, 6 and 9, then you would have a key to the universe" — is widely circulated but lacks definitive primary-source citation. The quote may be apocryphal or modern misattribution. Use it carefully: cite with the caveat about authenticity; don't claim Tesla endorsed Marko Rodin's framework (he died in 1943, before VBM developed; endorsement is impossible). Tesla's documented numerical interests, including some attention to 3, 6, 9 and to resonance phenomena, are part of historical record; these resonate with VBM's centring on 3-6-9 axis structure. The honest framing is: Tesla had numerical interests; VBM-tradition emerged later and centres on 3-6-9 axis structure; the connection is historical resonance, not endorsement. Citation rigor preserves credibility; loose citation erodes it.'

The honest framing is historical resonance (Tesla's interests align with VBM's 3-6-9 centring), not endorsement. Use the quote, if at all, with the caveat about authenticity.
