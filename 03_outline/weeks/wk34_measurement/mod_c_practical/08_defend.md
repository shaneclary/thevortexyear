## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The objection. *Cross-rig replication is engineering norm. But practitioners may have different equipment, different skills, different motivations. Some might shape findings to confirm framework; others to deny. How can replication ethics be honest given practitioner variation?*
>
> *The objection is sincere; replication ethics deserve clarification.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *The honest defence.*
>
> *Honest replication ethics requires: practitioners report what they measured, not what they expected; document methodology and conditions thoroughly; share both confirming and deviating findings; submit data to foundation for aggregation. Variation between practitioners is expected and informative — different rigs have different signal-to-noise, different parasitic effects, different practitioner skills. Aggregate data across many practitioners' honest measurements yields engineering knowledge that single rigs can't provide. Replication ethics is the framework's empirical engagement.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three layers in the defence.*
>
> *Layer one — individual ethics.* *Each practitioner reports honestly*: *raw measurements, conditions, methodology, observations*. *Engineering integrity*.
>
> *Layer two — methodology disclosure.* *Methodology and conditions documented*; *enables others to assess and replicate*. *Engineering transparency*.
>
> *Layer three — community aggregation.* *Multiple practitioners' honest data aggregated*; *engineering knowledge accumulates*; *community-level insight emerges from individual contributions*. *Tradition continuity*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What can be defended.*
>
> *Defend 1 — individual honesty enforceable through practice.* *Practitioners trained to measure-first, interpret-later*; *report all findings*; *engineering hygiene*. *Practice norm*.
>
> *Defend 2 — methodology disclosure verifiable.* *Documented methodology can be reviewed*; *cross-rig comparison possible*; *engineering checks*. *Verification.*
>
> *Defend 3 — community aggregation clarifies signal.* *Multi-practitioner data + variation* yields *robust knowledge*; *outliers identifiable; mainstream vs distinctive content distinguishable*. *Statistical engineering*.
>
> *Defend 4 — replication is mainstream engineering.* *VBM-tradition replication ethics share mainstream norms*; *not idiosyncratic*; *engineering standard.*
>
> *Defend 5 — framework reputation depends on replication.* *Without honest cross-rig replication, framework reputation suffers*; *with it, reputation grows*. *Long-term reputation.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What can't be defended.*
>
> *Can't 1.* That all practitioners always measure honestly. *Some don't*; *individual variation*. *Honest acknowledgment.*
>
> *Can't 2.* That replication results will always confirm framework. *They may not*; *honest reporting includes negative results*; *framework's empirical claims tested*.
>
> *Honest acknowledgment.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Honest articulation to a sceptical engineer.*
>
> *Articulation.* 'Honest replication ethics is engineering integrity at individual, methodological, and community levels. Individual practitioners report what they measured (raw data, conditions, methodology); methodology disclosure enables review and replication; community aggregation across practitioners' honest measurements yields robust knowledge. Variation between practitioners is expected — different rigs, equipment, skills — and informative when documented. Aggregate data clarifies signal vs noise; mainstream vs distinctive content distinguished. Replication ethics is mainstream engineering norm; VBM-tradition shares it. Without honest replication, framework reputation suffers; with it, framework's empirical claims become testable through community engagement. The framework's engineering integrity depends on this practice; honest reporting is engineering ethics, not just preference.'
>
> Around 145 words.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Why this honest acknowledgment is the right defence.*
>
> *Reason 1 — engineering credibility.* *Honest replication ethics preserves credibility*.
>
> *Reason 2 — community trust.* *Cross-rig data sharing requires trust; honest practice builds trust*.
>
> *Reason 3 — empirical engagement.* *Framework's empirical claims tested through honest replication*; *integrity central*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Calibrated answer.*
>
> *Honest replication ethics: individual reports raw data; methodology disclosed; community aggregates honestly; variation expected and informative. Mainstream engineering norm applied to VBM-tradition. Without honest replication, framework reputation suffers; with it, empirical claims testable. Engineering integrity at individual, methodological, community levels.*
>
> Around 60 words.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Defend honest replication ethics*: *individual honesty + methodology disclosure + community aggregation*. *Engineering norm; framework reputation depends*; *integrity central*. *Practitioners reporting honestly contribute to framework's empirical engagement*."
