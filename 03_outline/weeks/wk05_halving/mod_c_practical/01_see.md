## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "If the Doubling Circuit is the canonical Rodin Coil winding pattern, the Halving Circuit is the *reverse-direction* winding. Same form factor, same six-vertex cycle, opposite traversal direction.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What does this look like on a real torus? Visually, the wire still spirals around the donut. But the direction of the spiral reverses. Where the doubling-direction coil's wire moves clockwise around the major axis, the halving-direction coil's wire moves counter-clockwise. The geometry is the *mirror image* across some plane through the torus's centre.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three visual differences between the two windings.
>
> *Difference one — the wire's spiral handedness.* Doubling-direction: right-handed spiral, like a standard screw thread. Halving-direction: left-handed spiral, like a reverse-thread bolt. A wound coil immediately shows you which traversal you used.
>
> *Difference two — the start-of-traversal vertex sequence.* Doubling: 1 → 2 → 4 → ... Halving: 1 → 5 → 7 → ... The wire's *first move* tells you which direction. From vertex 1, if the next position is 2, you're winding doubling-direction. If the next is 5, halving.
>
> *Difference three — the antipodal-pair pattern.* Both directions visit the same six vertices, so the polar pairs (1-8, 2-7, 4-5) are present in both. But the *order in which the wire visits the pairs* differs. We'll come back to this in Wk 6 when we cover polar pairs explicitly.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Why build a reverse-direction coil? Three uses, briefly previewed.
>
> *Use one — comparison testing.* If you wind both directions and measure their electromagnetic properties, any differences are direction-specific. Same geometry, opposite direction = a controlled experiment for what direction does to the field.
>
> *Use two — counter-wound assemblies.* In mainstream RF engineering, counter-wound coils are used for noise cancellation. A doubling-direction coil and a halving-direction coil wound on the same form (or on stacked forms) can produce specific cancellation effects. Q3 will explore this.
>
> *Use three — phased multi-strand designs.* Wk 35's multi-strand build uses some strands wound doubling-direction and others halving-direction. The phasing is what produces the more complex field structure.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Today is preview. The actual reverse-winding build is part of Wk 35. For now, you should be able to recognise the difference between a doubling-direction and a halving-direction Rodin Coil by sight."
