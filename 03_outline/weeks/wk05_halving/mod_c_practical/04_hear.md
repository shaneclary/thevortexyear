## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

**[ORG-CLIP]**

> *"When you wind two coils with opposite direction on the same form — or stack them — the magnetic field components from each interact. Some components add. Some cancel. The cancellation is direction-dependent. A doubling-direction coil produces a field with a specific spatial signature; a halving-direction coil produces the mirror signature. Stack them and the symmetric components cancel; the asymmetric components remain. This is one of the design tools mainstream RF engineering already uses — counter-wound chokes, balanced transformers, things that need specific cancellation. The Rodin Coil's doubling-halving pair is a particularly clean example of the technique because the two windings are exact mirror images at the geometric level. The cancellation is correspondingly precise. If you wind only one direction, you get one set of field characteristics. If you wind both and combine, you get a controllable cancellation that you cannot get from either alone. Builders who want to explore the field structure should build both."*
> — Randy Powell, paraphrased. **Source:** P1-7, P1-13.
