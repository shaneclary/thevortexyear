## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Three scenarios where direction choice matters.
>
> *Scenario one — your first build.* The canonical Wk-33 first build uses *doubling-direction*. Why: the standard reference build is doubling, so your measurements compare against published data and other builders' reports. If you use halving-direction for your first build, you have no baseline to compare against. Recommendation: doubling-direction for the first build. Build in halving-direction only after you have a doubling-direction coil to compare against.
>
> *Scenario two — controlled comparison.* Once you have a doubling-direction coil, building a halving-direction one on the same form factor lets you isolate direction effects. Identical wire, identical turns, identical core, opposite direction. Any measured differences attribute to direction alone. This is the standard scientific approach to isolating a variable. Recommendation: build the comparison coil if you have time; it's the most informative second build.
>
> *Scenario three — multi-strand designs.* Wk 35's three- and six-strand builds combine doubling-direction and halving-direction strands on the same form. The phasing — which strands are which direction — is a design parameter that affects the resulting field structure. Some configurations cancel symmetric field components; others reinforce them. Recommendation: follow the spec for your chosen multi-strand design rather than improvising. We'll cover the canonical phasings in Wk 35.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One more practical note. Direction is *not* a free parameter for a single-strand coil that you want to compare to existing data. The published Rodin Coil data assumes doubling-direction. If you wind halving-direction and compare your measurements to published values, you'll find systematic differences that aren't necessarily errors — they're direction effects.
>
> Direction *is* a meaningful design parameter for multi-strand and stacked configurations. There, the choice is part of the design.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> So the three scenarios cover the practical decision tree. *First build: doubling. Comparison study: build both. Multi-strand: follow spec.* The decisions are determined by what you're testing, not by aesthetic preference."
