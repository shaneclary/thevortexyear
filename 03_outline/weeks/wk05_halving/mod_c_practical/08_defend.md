## 0. Authoring notes
Fifteenth Defend. The objection: *if counter-winding is just standard RF practice, the Rodin halving direction isn't novel — it's a re-naming of bifilar.* Honest answer: the halving direction's *specific winding pattern* (cycle-following) is what makes it Rodin-specific. Bifilar coils don't typically follow the doubling cycle; they wind in geometric symmetry. The Rodin halving direction inherits the cycle structure plus the counter-winding principle.

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The objection. *Mainstream RF has counter-wound coils. The Rodin halving direction is just a re-naming of standard bifilar technique. There's nothing novel here.*
>
> The objection is partially correct. The *counter-winding principle* — winding opposite directions for specific cancellation effects — is mainstream. Marko didn't invent it.
>
> What the objection misses.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Bifilar coils don't follow the doubling cycle.* A standard bifilar coil winds two wires in parallel, with one direction reversed, in *geometrically simple* patterns — usually parallel turns on a cylindrical or toroidal core, with no cycle-following pattern. The two wires sit beside each other, with one running 'forward' and one 'backward', but the *winding path* is geometrically straightforward.
>
> The Rodin halving-direction coil follows the *same cycle* as the doubling-direction coil — the path 1 → 5 → 7 → 8 → 4 → 2 → 1, exact mirror of 1 → 2 → 4 → 8 → 7 → 5 → 1. The cycle structure is what's Rodin-specific. The counter-winding principle is mainstream.
>
> So the Rodin halving direction is a *cycle-structured counter-wind* — using both the cycle pattern (Rodin's contribution) and the counter-winding principle (RF engineering's). The combination is Rodin-specific even though both ingredients are not.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> So the calibrated answer to *halving is just bifilar?*:
>
> *Counter-winding is mainstream RF technique. The Rodin halving direction adds the cycle structure on top — the wire follows the doubling cycle reversed, not a geometrically simple counter-wind. The combination is Rodin-specific; the counter-wind alone isn't.*
>
> Forty-one words.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One more thing worth carrying. When an RF engineer asks if your build is bifilar, the answer is *yes, with cycle-following winding*. The *yes* respects the engineering tradition; the *with cycle-following* notes the addition. That phrasing doesn't dismiss either side."
