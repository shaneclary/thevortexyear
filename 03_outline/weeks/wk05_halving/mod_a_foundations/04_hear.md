**[ORG-CLIP]**

> *"Doubling and halving are not two operations. They are one operation, traced in two directions. The path on the Symbol — one to two to four to eight to seven to five — is the same path you trace if you start at one and halve. You just walk it the other way. When I see students treat doubling and halving as separate ideas, I know they are not yet seeing the structure. The Symbol is a closed loop. There are two directions you can walk a closed loop. That is all halving is — the doubling cycle walked backward. The math underneath this is simple: five is the inverse of two in modular nine arithmetic. Multiply by two, then by five, you get back where you started. The two operations cancel. They are inverses. They are the same loop in two directions. Hold that as one fact, not two."*
> — Marko Rodin, paraphrased. **Source:** P1-1, P1-3.
