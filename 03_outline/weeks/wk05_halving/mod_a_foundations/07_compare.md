## 0. Authoring notes
Doubling-halving as multiplicative-inverse pair, compared to addition-subtraction as additive-inverse pair. Both are operations and their inverses. Different group structures, but the *pair-with-inverse* pattern is shared.

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Standard arithmetic has a familiar pair: *addition and subtraction*. They're inverses — adding 1, then subtracting 1, gives back the original number. *(+1) ∘ (−1) = identity*.
>
> The Symbol has its own pair: *doubling and halving*. Also inverses — doubling, then halving, gives back the original. *(×2) ∘ (×5) = ×10 ≡ identity (mod 9)*.
>
> Two operation-inverse pairs. Same structural role, different operations.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Where they differ:
>
> *Addition-subtraction* operates on the *additive* group of integers. Adding any non-zero step eventually visits every integer (or every residue class, in mod-9 arithmetic). The cycle structure is *exhaustive* — additive operations don't leave anything out.
>
> *Doubling-halving* operates on the *multiplicative* group of `(ℤ/9ℤ)*`. The pair leaves out the non-units {0, 3, 6} entirely. They live in different parts of the Symbol — the side cycles. Multiplicative operations are *selective*; addition isn't.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Where they're similar:
>
> Both pairs preserve the cycle's elements when applied iteratively. Doubling iterates through {1, 2, 4, 5, 7, 8} forever. Adding 1 iterates through all 9 residues forever. Both are deterministic, periodic, complete on their respective domains.
>
> Both pairs have a *unit* in their cycle. Doubling has 1 as the multiplicative identity (×1 leaves everything fixed). Addition has 0 (+0 leaves everything fixed). The unit is where the cycle returns to.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One observation about the multiplicative-additive distinction. Most physical processes are described by *differential equations* — additive in nature. Speeds add. Forces add. Adding-and-subtracting is the natural language of physics-as-we-write-it.
>
> Multiplicative operations show up in a different register: scaling, growth, compounding. Bacterial growth doubles. Inflation multiplies. Decibels are logarithmic — multiplicative in disguise. The doubling cycle's cycle structure is the structure of *multiplicative* processes, which is part of why Marko points at it as a candidate for describing energy flow that *grows or decays* rather than *adds or subtracts*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Whether the multiplicative-vs-additive distinction maps onto a physical-vs-mathematical one is contested. The standard view treats both as equally available descriptions. Marko prefers the multiplicative reading — argues physical reality has multiplicative structure underneath, with additive structure as a consequence. We'll meet his version of this claim more directly in Q3."
