## 0. Authoring notes
*The elegance of one path, two directions.* The contemplative beat for symmetry.

## 1. Outcome
By the end of these 6 minutes, the student has spent quiet time with the unity of doubling and halving as a single bidirectional path.

## 2. Hook (0:00–0:30)
*[On screen: the figure-eight, slowly traced one way, then the other. Both directions on the same line.]*

> *(slow)* "One path. Two directions. Sit with that."

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)
> "Lens 5 of 9. Six minutes."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30) — *contemplative*

> *(slow)* "You walked the path one way last week. *One, two, four, eight, seven, five.*
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> This week you walked it the other way. *One, five, seven, eight, four, two.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> The path didn't change. You walked it backwards.
>
> *[long pause — 12 seconds]*
>
> Most things have a *direction*. A river flows downstream. A clock runs forward. A book reads left to right.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> The Doubling Circuit doesn't. It's a closed loop. There's no upstream, no forward, no left-to-right. There are *two directions you can walk it*, and walking either gives a complete tour of the same six vertices.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Marko's framing — *one operation, two directions* — is mathematically precise. The cyclic group `(ℤ/9ℤ)*` has two generators, 2 and 5, which are *inverses* of each other. Their orbits walk the same cycle in opposite directions. Same group structure, two named operations.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> What does this *feel* like? Some students notice that holding doubling and halving as *one thing with two directions* is simpler than holding them as *two operations to learn*. The cognitive load drops. The Symbol's six-vertex cycle becomes a single object with bidirectional reading, like a loop of music that plays in either order.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Other students notice something else. The pair of operations forms a *symmetry* — the kind of symmetry physicists and mathematicians use as a clue that something fundamental is going on. When you find an operation that pairs with its inverse to leave the underlying object invariant, you've found a *group action*. That's what we have here: a group action on the Symbol, with doubling and halving as the two generators.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> A symmetry is a fingerprint of structure. The Doubling Circuit's bidirectional traversal is a fingerprint of the underlying group structure of `(ℤ/9ℤ)*`. Whether that fingerprint points at something physical — Marko's claim — or only at something mathematical — the standard view — is the question we'll be circling all year.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Today, just sit with the unity. Doubling and halving are not two ideas. They are one cycle, walked two ways."
