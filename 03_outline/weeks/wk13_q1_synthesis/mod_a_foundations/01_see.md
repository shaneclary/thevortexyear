## 0. Authoring notes
First lesson of Q1's closing week. The visual library — twelve diagrams replayed.

## 1. Outcome
Student can recognise and name the twelve canonical Q1 diagrams at a glance.

## 2. Hook (0:00–0:30)
*[Twelve diagrams arranged in a 3×4 grid, one per Q1 week, each labelled with its week number.]*

> "Twelve weeks. Twelve diagrams. Six minutes."

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)
*[Carry-over: yesterday's bridge closed Wk 12 with the spiral fully lit through Wk 12.]*

> "Lens 1 of 9, Module A, Week 13. We open Q1's closing week by seeing what you now own visually."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The twelve diagrams of Q1, in order. Each appears now in the grid in front of you.
>
> *Wk 1 — the spiral wheel.* Fifty-two weeks laid out as a circle, four quarter arcs in different colours. The map of the year.
>
> *Wk 2 — the Symbol of Enlightenment.* Nine numbers on a circle, 9 at the top, 1 to 8 clockwise. The static diagram every other concept hangs from.
>
> *Wk 3 — the digital-root field.* Numbers 0 to 99 in a 10×10 grid, coloured by their digital root. The hidden periodicity in plain numerals.
>
> *Wk 4 — the doubling circuit.* The figure-eight tracing 1 → 2 → 4 → 8 → 7 → 5 → 1 on the Symbol. The first dynamic diagram.
>
> *Wk 5 — the halving circuit.* The same figure-eight traced backwards: 1 → 5 → 7 → 8 → 4 → 2 → 1. The doubling's mirror.
>
> *Wk 6 — the polar pairs.* Three chords across the Symbol: 1↔8, 2↔7, 4↔5. Each pair sums to 9.
>
> *Wk 7 — the family number groups.* Three coloured triads: {1, 4, 7}, {2, 5, 8}, {3, 6, 9}. The cosets, visually.
>
> *Wk 8 — the 3-6-9 pulse.* The 3↔6 oscillation with 9 fixed. The orthogonal axis.
>
> *Wk 9 — powers of ten.* Numbers 10, 100, 1000 reducing to 1, the residue 10ᵏ ≡ 1 mod 9 made visual.
>
> *Wk 10 — the nine multiplication series.* Nine separate circles, each showing the orbit of a different multiplier. The full map of cyclic structure.
>
> *Wk 11 — phyllotaxis with primes.* A sunflower-style spiral with 25 prime positions highlighted by family colour.
>
> *Wk 12 — Marko at fifteen.* A photograph or sketch of the discovery moment. The biographical anchor.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three observations.
>
> *Observation one.* Each diagram builds on its predecessor. Wk 4 needs Wk 2. Wk 5 needs Wk 4. The grid in front of you isn't twelve independent images; it's a single image fragmented across twelve weeks.
>
> *Observation two.* Every diagram fits on a single page. Q1 has no diagram requiring 3D rendering or computer assistance. Each is hand-drawable. The whole foundation can be reproduced with paper and pencil.
>
> *Observation three.* The diagrams *together* are a vocabulary. When you encounter Q2's geometric turn next week, the new shapes will be built out of these twelve. You won't be starting fresh; you'll be lifting these diagrams into 3D.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One observation. Most students cannot name all twelve at first sight today. By Friday they can. The recognition is *fast* once the names are attached. Spend the week looking."
