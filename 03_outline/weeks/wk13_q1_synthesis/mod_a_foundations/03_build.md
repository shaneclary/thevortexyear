## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Open your notebook. Fresh page. Date it. Title it *Integrated Symbol — Q1 Reference*.
>
> Six layers. Add them in order.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Layer 1 — the base.* Draw the circle. Place 9 at the top, then 1 through 8 clockwise. Use a pencil so the underlying skeleton stays light.
>
> *Layer 2 — the doubling circuit.* Switch to a coloured pen. Trace 1 → 2 → 4 → 8 → 7 → 5 → 1 as a continuous line. The figure-eight emerges. Label the colour *DOUBLING* in your legend.
>
> *Layer 3 — the halving circuit.* Different colour. Trace 1 → 5 → 7 → 8 → 4 → 2 → 1. It overlaps the doubling but in reverse. Label the colour *HALVING*.
>
> *Layer 4 — the polar pairs.* Third colour. Draw three chords: from 1 to 8, from 2 to 7, from 4 to 5. Each chord crosses the centre. Label the colour *POLAR PAIRS*. Annotate that each chord sums to 9.
>
> *Layer 5 — the family groups.* Use three small circles or coloured dots placed *next to* the numerals. {1, 4, 7} get one colour-dot. {2, 5, 8} get a second. {3, 6, 9} get a third. Note in your legend that each family is a coset of 3ℤ/9ℤ.
>
> *Layer 6 — the 3-6-9 axis.* Highlight the three points 3, 6, and 9 with a heavier outline or a star. Draw a curved arrow showing the 3↔6 oscillation. Mark 9 as the fixed point. Annotate *the 3-6-9 axis is orthogonal to the doubling-halving circuit*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What you now have on one page. The Symbol with five Q1 structures simultaneously visible. *Doubling* and *halving* as opposing flows. *Polar pairs* as crossing chords. *Family groups* as colour assignments. The *3-6-9 axis* as an emphasised triad orthogonal to the figure-eight.
>
> Pin this page somewhere visible. It will become your most-used reference for Q2 and beyond.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three deliberate constraints in this build.
>
> *Constraint one.* Layers go on in order. Doubling first, then halving, then polar pairs, then family colour-codes, then the 3-6-9 emphasis. The ordering reflects the conceptual layering. If you skip ahead, the diagram becomes hard to read.
>
> *Constraint two.* Use a real legend in the corner. Five colour swatches with labels. Without the legend the diagram looks like ornament; with it, the diagram is a map.
>
> *Constraint three.* This is a *reference*, not a *finished artwork*. Pencil first, then ink. If you make a mistake, redraw on a fresh page. Don't fight a bad first attempt.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> If you can't finish the diagram in the next sixty seconds, finish it tonight. The diagram is a working tool for Q2 onward. You'll be unhappy without it.
>
> The eleven prior weeks were preparation. This page is the synthesis."
