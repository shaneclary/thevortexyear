> *Apply 1 — digital roots.* Sum the digits of 142857: 1+4+2+8+5+7 = 27. Reduce: 2+7 = 9. The digital root is 9.
>
> *Apply 2 — the doubling circuit.* Compute 142857 × 2 = 285714. The digits are *the same six digits in a different order*. The order is 2 → 8 → 5 → 7 → 1 → 4. Note: 1 → 4 → 2 → 8 → 5 → 7 → 1 in the original. The doubling rotates the digit-cycle.
>
> *Apply 3 — the halving circuit.* Compute 142857 × 5 = 714285. Same digits, rotated again — the halving (× 5 mod 9) order. The doubling and halving circuits manifest *as integer multiplications* on the cyclic number.
>
> *Apply 4 — polar pairs.* The digits {1, 4, 2, 8, 5, 7} are the units of ℤ/9ℤ, in doubling order. The non-units {3, 6, 9} are *absent*. The cyclic number lives entirely on the doubling circuit.
>
> *Apply 5 — family groups.* The digits split into two of the three families. Family {1, 4, 7} contributes {1, 4, 7}. Family {2, 5, 8} contributes {2, 5, 8}. Family {3, 6, 9} contributes nothing. The cyclic number is *coset-restricted*.

> *Apply 6 — 3-6-9 axis.* The 3-6-9 family is exactly what the cyclic number lacks. The axis sits *outside* the cyclic structure, just as it does on the Symbol.
