> "Today's count is *self-administered*. The number you arrive at is private. It calibrates how solid your foundation is heading into Q2.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Twelve proofs from Q1. Open your build log. Read each title; ask honestly whether you could reproduce the proof in five minutes on a blank page, given only the statement.
>
> *Proof 1.* The doubling orbit of 2 in (ℤ/9ℤ)* has length 6. *Reproducible: ☐ yes, ☐ partially, ☐ no.*
>
> *Proof 2.* For every digit a ∈ {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8}, a + (9 − a) ≡ 0 mod 9. *Reproducible: ☐ yes, ☐ partially, ☐ no.*
>
> *Proof 3.* The set {3, 6, 9} is closed under addition modulo 9 and forms the unique subgroup of order 3 in ℤ/9ℤ. *Reproducible: ☐.*
>
> *Proof 4.* The cosets of 3ℤ/9ℤ in ℤ/9ℤ are {0, 3, 6}, {1, 4, 7}, {2, 5, 8} — the family number groups. *Reproducible: ☐.*
>
> *Proof 5.* For any non-negative integer n, the digital root of n equals (n mod 9) for n not divisible by 9, and equals 9 for n divisible by 9. *Reproducible: ☐.*
>
> *Proof 6.* For every k ≥ 0, 10ᵏ ≡ 1 mod 9. *Reproducible: ☐.*
>
> *Proof 7.* In ℤ/9ℤ, the group of units (ℤ/9ℤ)* has order 6 and consists of {1, 2, 4, 5, 7, 8}. *Reproducible: ☐.*
>
> *Proof 8.* The orbit of 5 under multiplication in (ℤ/9ℤ)* is the halving cycle (1, 5, 7, 8, 4, 2), and is the inverse of the doubling orbit. *Reproducible: ☐.*
>
> *Proof 9.* The Pisano period of the Fibonacci sequence modulo 9 is 24. *Reproducible: ☐.*
>
> *Proof 10.* The cyclic number 142857 satisfies 142857 × n = a rotation of the digit string for n ∈ {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}. *Reproducible: ☐.*
>
> *Proof 11.* The number of primes ≤ 100 is 25, and they distribute non-uniformly across digit-root families. *Reproducible: ☐.*
>
> *Proof 12.* The product of any element of {1, 4, 7} with any element of {2, 5, 8} lies in {2, 5, 8} (modulo 9). *Reproducible: ☐.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Tally. *How many checked 'yes'?*
>
> Honest interpretations.
>
> *Twelve out of twelve.* You're well-prepared for Q2. Few students reach this level after Q1; most reach it after Q2 in retrospect.
>
> *Eight to eleven.* Solid. The gaps you have are localised and easily filled.
>
> *Four to seven.* Moderate. Pick the proofs you don't have yet and re-watch the relevant Build (Lens 3) lesson before next week.

>
> *[pause]*

> One observation. Most students underestimate themselves on this audit. *Partially* often becomes *yes* once you actually attempt the proof. Don't let modesty trick you. Try the proof on paper before deciding the box is empty.

> The audit is private. The number is yours. Use it to calibrate, not to grade."
