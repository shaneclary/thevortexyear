## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Q1 has covered substantial mathematics, in pieces. Today the pieces become a single tree. Root, branches, leaves.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *The root.* One claim, taken as the starting point of the entire foundation.
>
> *Root claim.* The integers form a ring; reduction modulo 9 is a well-defined ring homomorphism; the image is the cyclic group ℤ/9ℤ.
>
> Everything in Q1 sits below this root. *If you accept the root*, you accept the rest as derivations. The root is itself standard mathematics — proven in any introductory abstract-algebra textbook.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three primary branches.*
>
> *Branch 1 — additive structure.* The additive group of ℤ/9ℤ is itself cyclic of order 9. From this branch grow: the polar pairs (Wk 6), the family number groups as cosets of the subgroup 3ℤ/9ℤ (Wk 7), and the 3-6-9 axis as the unique subgroup of order 3 (Wk 8).
>
> *Branch 2 — multiplicative structure.* The multiplicative group (ℤ/9ℤ)* is cyclic of order 6. From this branch grow: the doubling circuit as the orbit of 2 (Wk 4), the halving circuit as the orbit of 5 (Wk 5), and the nine multiplication series as orbits of all multipliers (Wk 10).
>
> *Branch 3 — base-10 interaction.* The fact 10 ≡ 1 mod 9 connects ℤ/9ℤ to ordinary decimal numerals. From this branch grow: the digital root operation (Wk 3), scale invariance (Wk 9), and the cyclic-number structures like 142857 (Wk 13's apply lesson).
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Leaves.* The terminal results. Each is a specific verifiable claim.
>
> *Leaf — doubling order.* The orbit (1, 2, 4, 8, 7, 5) has length 6.
>
> *Leaf — polar sum.* For each pair (a, 9-a), a + (9-a) ≡ 0 mod 9.
>
> *Leaf — family additive closure.* {3, 6, 9} is closed under addition mod 9.
>
> *Leaf — digital-root invariance.* For any non-negative integer n, *n mod 9* equals the iterated digit sum.
>
> *Leaf — scale orbit.* {1, 10, 100, 1000, …} all reduce to 1 mod 9.
>
> *Leaf — cyclic number.* 142857 × k for k ∈ {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6} produces six rotations of the same digit string.
>
> *Leaf — Pisano period 24.* The Fibonacci sequence mod 9 has period 24.
>
> *Leaf — phyllotactic primes.* Primes ≤ 100 distribute across digit-root families with quantifiable bias.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three observations.
>
> *Observation one.* The tree has *one root, three primary branches, dozens of leaves.* The shape is hierarchical. Every leaf can be traced back to the root through one of three branches.
>
> *Observation two.* The tree is *finite and complete*. Q1 didn't leave dangling claims. Every result has a derivation; every derivation reaches the root.
>
> *Observation three.* The tree is *closed in itself*. To extend it — to introduce new leaves — Q2 will add a new branch: *geometric structure*. The torus, the helical doubling, the antipodal pairs in 3D will hang from a new fourth branch we add next quarter.
>
