## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Today's count is hands-on. Open your build log and tally.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Tally 1 — diagrams.* Count fully-drawn diagrams. Symbol of Enlightenment drawings count. Doubling-circuit traces count. Polar-pair annotated diagrams count. Phyllotactic spirals count. Each separate diagram is one tally mark.
>
> Q1's Build lessons asked for at least *fifteen distinct diagrams*: the three Symbol drawings (Wk 2), digital-root grid (Wk 3), doubling circuit (Wk 4), halving circuit (Wk 5), polar pairs (Wk 6), family-colour Symbol (Wk 7), 3-6-9 emphasised Symbol (Wk 8), scale-invariance worked example (Wk 9), nine multiplication-series Symbols (one for each multiplier — that's nine alone, but most students draw three or four), phyllotactic spiral (Wk 11), Marko's first-coil plan (Wk 12), Wk 13 integrated Symbol (Mod A this week).
>
> *Realistic target.* Twelve to twenty diagrams in a strong portfolio. Three to seven is thin.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Tally 2 — written reflections.* Count one-sentence reflections from the *Try it* lessons (Lens 5 across each module). Q1 had thirty-six Try-it reflections (twelve weeks × three modules); a good student has ten to thirty captured.
>
> *Tally 3 — paper plans.* Count parts lists, build plans, layered-build sketches with legends. The Wk 12 first-coil paper plan is one. The Wk 13 integrated Symbol is another. Most students will have one or two paper plans by now.
>
> *Tally 4 — calibrated-answer paragraphs.* Count the verbatim paragraphs you've memorised from Lens 8 lessons (Defend) — the *Is this real math?* answer, the *isn't it just numerology?* answer, the sceptical-friend paragraph. Q1 had thirty-eight calibrated answers; you don't need all of them, but having three to five committed is realistic.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Total tally.* Add the four counts. A robust Q1 build log has *roughly thirty to fifty distinct artefacts* by week 13.
>
> *Forty plus.* Strong. You've engaged with most prompts. Q2 won't be a stretch.
>
> *Twenty to forty.* Solid. You've engaged regularly without exhausting every prompt. Q2 begins on a good footing.
>
> *Ten to twenty.* Light. You'll need to engage more deeply with Q2's hands-on prompts to compensate.
>
> *Under ten.* You've been watching without making. Q2 has explicit physical builds (the geometric strips, the torus models). Without practice making, the builds will be frustrating.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One observation about the counting.
>
> *Quality matters more than count.* Three carefully-drawn Symbols outweigh ten rushed ones. The counting is a *first-order* indicator, not the actual measure. Use the count to find gaps, not to grade.
>
> Another observation. The build log will *triple* in size during Q3 (the engineering quarter). Q1's portfolio is the seed. Don't compare your Q1 log to what's coming; compare it to the prompts Q1 actually gave you.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *If your tally is light, spend this week filling in.* Re-do the Wk 4 doubling diagram. Add the Wk 7 family colour-codes if you skipped them. The course doesn't penalise late entries — it just stops scaffolding for prompts you didn't engage with.
>
> Q2 begins next week. The build log carries forward."
