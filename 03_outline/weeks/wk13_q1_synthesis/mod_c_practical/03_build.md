## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Q2 opens next week with a hands-on exercise: turn the Symbol you've been drawing on a circle into a *strip* — a long, thin rectangle with the same arithmetic. Then later, that strip will close into a *torus*.
>
> Today's lesson prepares you. Cut the strip in advance.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Materials.* You need:
>
> Plain paper. A4 or US-letter is fine. *White or off-white,* not coloured — you'll be writing on it.
>
> A ruler. Twelve inches or thirty centimetres.
>
> A pencil and an eraser.
>
> Coloured pens — the same five colours you've been using for the integrated Symbol.
>
> Scissors or a sharp craft knife with a cutting mat.
>
> A blank build-log page reserved for Wk 14.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Cut the strip.*
>
> Take a sheet of paper. Cut a strip approximately *three centimetres wide and twenty-five centimetres long*. The exact dimensions aren't critical — Q2 will use a couple of variants — but this is a sensible default.
>
> If your paper is shorter than 25 cm in any direction, tape two strips end-to-end. Use clear tape so you can still write on the paper.
>
> Lay the strip flat on the workbench. *This will be your first geometric object next week.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Mark a starter grid.*
>
> Using the ruler, divide the strip into 9 equal sections lengthwise. Each section is approximately 2.7 cm. Use a light pencil mark at each division point. Don't ink it; you'll trace over later.
>
> Number the sections 1 through 9 along the strip, with the section labels matching the Symbol's positions. Position 9 will end up adjacent to position 1 when the strip is bent into a circle — that's the torus closure that Wk 16 makes explicit. For now, 9 is at one end, 1 at the other, with 2 through 8 in between.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Reserve a Wk 14 build-log page.*
>
> Open your build log. Skip a page after this week's entries. Title the new page *Wk 14 — From Circle to Strip — preparation*. Tape or paperclip the cut strip to that page so it's ready.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What you've done. *You've cut and prepared the substrate that Wk 14's first build needs.* Most students arrive at Wk 14 unprepared and lose the first half-lesson scrambling for paper. You're not them.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three deliberate constraints.
>
> *Constraint one.* Paper, not card stock. The strip will be folded, taped, and possibly cut in Wk 14-16. Card is too stiff.
>
> *Constraint two.* No special equipment. Q2's geometric exercises stay accessible. If you can't cut paper accurately by hand, that's fine — the geometry forgives small errors.
>
> *Constraint three.* Only the substrate today, *not the geometric work itself*. Save the doubling-on-strip for Wk 14's actual lesson. This is logistics, not pedagogy.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical observation. The strip looks unimpressive — a ruler-marked rectangle. By the end of Q2, that strip (or one like it) will become the surface of a torus, with the doubling circuit on its surface. Today is preparation; the transformation comes."
