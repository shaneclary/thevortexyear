> *"I drew the circle. I placed the nine numbers. I started at one and I doubled. Two. Four. Eight. Then sixteen, and I had to reduce — seven. Then fourteen, reduce — five. Then ten, reduce — one. I had returned to the beginning. The path my pencil had drawn was a figure-eight on its side. The infinity sign. I sat with the paper for a long time. The figure had emerged from the simplest possible operation — doubling and reducing — applied to the smallest possible substrate — nine numbers on a circle. And out of that came the symbol that mathematics has used for infinity since the seventeenth century. The figure was already there, waiting in the structure of the numbers, and I had only walked a path that revealed it. I have been walking that path for fifty years and the figure still surprises me when I look at it. The cycle never decays. The figure never closes wrong. The infinity that lives in our number system was always there. I drew the line that lets you see it."*
> — Marko Rodin, paraphrased from RodinAerodynamics.org and lecture material. **Source:** P1-1, P1-3. **Permission status:** to be confirmed by org before publication.
