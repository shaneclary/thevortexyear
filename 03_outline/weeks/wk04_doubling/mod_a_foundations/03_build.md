## 0. Authoring notes
Three pencil traces of the Doubling Circuit on three printed Symbols. Same imperative voice as Mod-A wk-2 Lesson 3. The hand learns the figure-eight by repetition.

## 1. Outcome
By the end of these 6 minutes, the student has traced the Doubling Circuit three times on three printed Symbols.

## 2. Hook (0:00–0:30)
*[On screen: three printed Symbols, blank, side by side.]*

> "Three Symbols. Three traces. Six minutes. Pencil ready."

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)
> "Lens 3 of 9. We've seen the trace and computed the cycle. Today we draw it three times. Repetition is the lesson."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

*[Camera close on hand and Symbol. Three traces in real time.]*

> "Open your notebook. You should have at least one fresh page available, or three printed copies of the Symbol from your course materials.
>
> First trace. Place pencil at 1. Move to 2. To 4. To 8 — across the circle. To 7 — across again. To 5. Back to 1. The figure-eight is on the page.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> Second trace. Same gesture. Notice that this time your hand moves more confidently. The crossing in the centre is cleaner. The two lobes look more balanced.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> Third trace. By now your hand makes the figure without consulting your eye. The motion has become familiar. Some students find the third trace takes about half the time of the first.
>
> *[pause — show the three traces side by side]*
>
> Look at your three figure-eights. They aren't identical. The first probably has rougher curves. The third is cleaner. The progression is your hand internalising the figure. By Week 13, when we cover phyllotaxis and the multiplication series, you'll be drawing this trace casually as part of other diagrams. Today: just the three.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Why does this matter? The Doubling Circuit is the most-traced figure in this entire course. By Q3, when you wind a Rodin Coil in Week 33, the helical path on the torus follows this same cycle — lifted into 3D. The hand that has traced the figure-eight a dozen times on paper has an easier time tracing it with copper wire on a torus former. The repetition isn't make-work. It's preparation.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Date each trace. *Trace one, drawing a, drawing b, drawing c, today's date.* The three drawings, dated, go in your build log under *Diagrams*."

## 5. Try it (4:30–5:30)
> "Pause. If you didn't trace along — pause now and trace the cycle three times on three printed Symbols. The cycle goes 1, 2, 4, 8, 7, 5, back to 1. Sixty seconds plus."

*[Pause card on screen for 60+ seconds.]*

> "Welcome back. Three traces, dated. The figure-eight is in your hand."
