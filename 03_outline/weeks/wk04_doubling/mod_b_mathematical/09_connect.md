## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Module B opened with the orbit. *Powers of 2 modulo 9: 1, 2, 4, 8, 7, 5, 1.* The Doubling Circuit, formally, is the orbit of 2 under multiplication in `(ℤ/9ℤ)*`.
>
> From there, the proof. Lagrange's theorem says the order divides the group's size. The group has 6 elements; the order divides 6. Compute *2¹, 2², 2³* — none equals 1. The smaller divisors are ruled out. The order is exactly 6.
>
> Then the full table. Six elements, six orders. Two generators (2 and 5), one subgroup of order 3 ({1, 4, 7}), one of order 2 ({1, 8}), the identity. The complete multiplicative structure of `(ℤ/9ℤ)*` on one notebook page.
>
> Then Marko spoke on the cycle's permanence. *The pattern is non-decaying in a way no physical pattern is.* The mathematical content of *non-decaying* is *deterministic on a finite set*; Marko reads it interpretively as evidence that the cycle is more fundamental than physical structure.
>
> Then a contemplative beat. The cycle's substrate-independence held against the question of what it would mean for a pattern to never decay.
>
> Then we applied. The cycle as a hypothesis about physical systems — electromagnetic oscillators (testable in Q3 with the Rodin Coil) and biological cycles (less rigorously tested). Honest about what's tested and what's contested.
>
> Then the comparison. `(ℤ/9ℤ)*` is a standard object in undergraduate algebra. VBM adds the visualisation, the geometric extensions, and the physical hypothesis. The algebra is standard; the framework around it is not.
>
> Then the calibrated answer to *standard mod-9 result, what's the significance?* Forty-five words about where the significance does and doesn't lie.
