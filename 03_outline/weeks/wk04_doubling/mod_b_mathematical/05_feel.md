## 0. Authoring notes
Sit with the cycle's permanence as evidence of structure. The mathematical lens established it formally (the order of 2 is exactly 6, never 5, never 7); Marko named it non-decaying. Today: what does it feel like to hold a pattern that doesn't change?

## 1. Outcome

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30) — *contemplative, paced, sparse*

> *(slow)* "Most things change.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> A signal weakens over distance. A song's notes fade between speakers. A planet's orbit, given enough millennia, decays.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> The Doubling Circuit doesn't.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> You can compute it once. You can compute it a thousand times. You can compute it a million times. The cycle gives you the same six steps every iteration. *1, 2, 4, 8, 7, 5, 1, 2, 4, 8, 7, 5*. The thousandth iteration looks identical to the first. The millionth identical to the thousandth.
>
> *[long pause — 12 seconds]*
>
> What does that feel like, as a property?
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Most patterns we know carry the residue of their substrate. Music carries the room it's played in. Speech carries the speaker. The Doubling Circuit carries nothing. It has no substrate. The math doesn't care whether you compute it on paper, in your head, on a calculator, in 1655 or in 2026. The output is the same.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Marko reads this as *evidence*. The pattern's substrate-independence and non-decay aren't ordinary properties; they're the kind of properties you'd expect from something *more fundamental* than physical structure. He doesn't say physics is wrong; he says the math underneath physics has a permanence physics itself doesn't produce.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> A formal mathematician hears *non-decaying* and translates it as *deterministic*. The Doubling Circuit is a deterministic function from `(ℤ/9ℤ)*` to itself; iterating a deterministic function on a finite set gives a periodic orbit. Of course it doesn't decay. Decay isn't an option for finite-state deterministic systems.
>
> Both readings are valid. The mathematician's reading explains the *why*. Marko's reading asks *whether the why points at something*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Sit with the question for a moment. The pattern doesn't decay. The mathematician says *of course*. Marko says *and isn't that worth noticing*.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> What you brought to this lesson is your own answer to the question. The course teaches the math regardless of which answer you give."
