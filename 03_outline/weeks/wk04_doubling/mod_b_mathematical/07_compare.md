Place `(ℤ/9ℤ)*` and the orbit of 2 in the broader context of algebraic number theory. Show that this group is the multiplicative group of integers mod 9, that its structure is well-known, and that what's distinctive about VBM is the visualisation and the interpretation, not the underlying algebra.

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Standard algebra. `(ℤ/9ℤ)*` — the multiplicative group of units modulo 9 — is a textbook example introduced in undergraduate abstract algebra. It's used to illustrate cyclic groups, generators, subgroup lattices, and order calculations. Most undergraduates meet this group within their first year of formal algebra study. It's not exotic; it's a standard exercise.
>
> Standard treatment: prove the group is cyclic (it is), find its generators (2 and 5), enumerate its subgroups (orders 1, 2, 3, 6), classify orders of elements (1, 2, 3, 6), apply Lagrange's theorem (we did so in Lesson 2). Done. Move on to the next example.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What VBM adds.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> First — *visualisation*. The standard treatment leaves the group as a list of elements with a multiplication table. VBM places the elements on a circle (the Symbol) and traces the doubling action as a figure-eight on the circle. The visualisation isn't required for the algebra to work — but it changes how the structure is *seen*. The figure-eight that emerges is information the multiplication table doesn't display.
>
> Second — *connection to other structures*. Standard algebra studies `(ℤ/9ℤ)*` as one example among many. VBM connects this specific group to the Rodin Number Map (Q2), the ABHA Torus (Q2), the Rodin Coil (Q3), and a metaphysical interpretation about the structure of reality. The connections aren't part of standard algebra; they're VBM's contribution.
>
> Third — *physical hypothesis*. As we discussed in Lesson 6, VBM treats the doubling cycle's structure as physically instantiated, not just symbolically interesting. Standard algebra has no such hypothesis; the algebraic objects exist as mathematical objects without further claims.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What VBM does *not* add to standard algebra. The algebraic content. The proofs of cyclicity, the orders of elements, the subgroup structure — all of these are derived in standard treatments. VBM doesn't extend the algebra; it interprets it.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Useful framing for talking to a working algebraist: *VBM uses `(ℤ/9ℤ)*` as a substrate. The algebra is standard. The visualisation, the geometric extensions in Q2, and the physical hypothesis are VBM's contributions on top of the standard treatment.* That sentence respects the algebra and is honest about where the novelty lives.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> A consequence worth carrying. When critics describe VBM as *just modular arithmetic dressed up*, they are correct *about the algebra*. The algebraic content is well-known. They are wrong if they conclude there's *nothing else* — the visualisation and the physical hypothesis are real, even if contested. The honest answer is: the math is standard, the geometric and metaphysical extensions are not."
