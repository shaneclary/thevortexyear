## 0. Authoring notes
First physical handling of the cycle in 3D. Mock-up on a torus former with string or thread, no copper yet. The student traces the doubling path with their hands on a real torus shape. Preview of the Week 33 build session.

## 1. Outcome
By the end of these 6 minutes, the student has traced the Doubling Circuit on a physical torus former with string or thread.

## 2. Hook (0:00–0:30)
*[On screen: a torus former on a desk. A spool of string. A pencil. A piece of paper marked with vertex positions.]*

> "Torus former. String. The cycle in your hands."

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)
> "Lens 3 of 9. The first 3D mock-up. No copper yet — just string. Six minutes."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

*[Camera follows the host's hands around a torus former.]*

> "What you need: a torus former (download the STL from course materials and 3D-print, or use a wooden donut-shape — kitchen-supply stores sell them, or any donut-shaped object you have around). A length of string or thread. A pencil. A small piece of paper with the nine vertex positions marked.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Step one — mark the vertices on the former. Take the paper template from course materials and wrap it around the torus's outer equator. Use a pencil to mark the nine equally-spaced positions on the former itself. The 9 goes at the top of the donut. Numbers 1 through 8 mark clockwise around.
>
> *[long pause for marking]*
>
> Step two — tape one end of the string at vertex 1.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Step three — wrap the string from vertex 1 to vertex 2. Take the path that crosses the torus's surface, not the path through the air. The string lies on the donut's surface, looping over from 1 to 2.
>
> Step four — from 2, wrap to 4. Step five — from 4, wrap to 8. Step six — from 8, wrap to 7. Step seven — from 7, wrap to 5. Step eight — from 5, return to 1.
>
> *[pause — show the completed traversal]*
>
> The string traces a helical path around the torus. Six segments per traversal. The string crosses the torus surface in a way that the figure-eight on flat paper *suggested* — but couldn't show directly. Now the path is in 3D, on a real surface, in your hands.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Step nine — wrap a second traversal in a different colour string, offset slightly so you can see both. The two traversals lie close to each other but not on top. Step ten — third traversal, third colour. Step eleven — fourth traversal, fourth colour.
>
> *[pause — show four traversals on the former, four colours]*
>
> Look at what you've made. Four colours of string, each tracing the same Doubling Circuit on the same torus, slightly offset from each other. This is the *exact path* a copper wire will take in Week 33 — except the wire will be one continuous strand, not four offset strings. The four-colour mock-up is your *visualisation* of what the build will look like.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One thing the mock-up shows that diagrams don't. *The string is under tension*. Wrapping under tension teaches your hands what the wire will feel like. By Week 33 your hands have already done this gesture, just with string. The build session goes faster."
