## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The Doubling Circuit, on the Symbol, is the path *1, 2, 4, 8, 7, 5, back to 1*. Six steps. A figure-eight crossed in the centre of a circle.
>
> Now imagine the circle isn't flat. Imagine it's been wrapped onto the surface of a torus — a donut-shaped form. The vertices that were on a flat circle are now on a 3D surface. The lines that connected them are now arcs across the torus.
>
> *[pause — animation: flat Symbol wraps to torus surface]*
>
> The Doubling Circuit, traced on this 3D surface, becomes a *helix*. Specifically: a path that spirals around the torus, visiting the six vertices of `(ℤ/9ℤ)*` in their doubling order, returning to its starting point after one full traversal.
>
> That helix is what a copper wire follows when you wind a Rodin Coil.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> The connection. The math you've been studying — the cycle 1-2-4-8-7-5 — is the *winding instructions* for the coil. *Place the wire at vertex 1. Run it to vertex 2. Run it to vertex 4. Run it to vertex 8. Continue to 7. Continue to 5. Return to 1.* Six wire-segments per traversal. The wire visits every vertex of the multiplicative group, in a single closed loop.
>
> A typical first build does *four full traversals* — four times through the cycle. That's twenty-four winding moves, ending at the same starting vertex. The result is a coil with the Doubling Circuit's structure embedded in copper.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Why does this matter beyond aesthetics? Two reasons.
>
> *First — the geometry is constrained.* You can't choose a different winding pattern and call it a Rodin Coil. The pattern is the cycle 1-2-4-8-7-5; deviation from that pattern produces a different object. The math we proved this week is the spec.
>
> *Second — the resulting coil has specific predicted properties.* Marko's hypothesis (Lens 6 of Mod B yesterday) is that the coil's electromagnetic behaviour reflects the cycle's six-fold structure. Whether that prediction holds is what the Q3 measurements test. The coil is the testbed.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Today is preview. Week 16 will formalise the wrap from flat Symbol to torus. Week 32 will give the full topological specification of the winding. Week 33 is the build. We're showing the destination now so the foundation between here and there has a destination to point at."
