## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The canonical first Rodin Coil — the one we'll build in Week 33 — has these specifications. Write them in your build log under *Builds — Wk 33 spec*.
>
> *Turns per traversal.* 24. The Doubling Circuit visits six vertices, but a single physical traversal of the torus surface requires four loops between consecutive vertices on the cycle, each crossing about a quarter of the torus. Four loops × six vertices = 24 winding moves per traversal. *(`[ORG-INPUT]` — confirm canonical traversal count for the org's preferred build spec.)*
>
> *Number of traversals.* 4. The first build does four full traversals — four times through the cycle. This produces a coil dense enough to measure but light enough to wind in one session.
>
> *Total turns.* 96. Twenty-four per traversal, four traversals, ninety-six total.
>
> *Wire length.* About three metres. Depends on torus size. For a torus with major diameter 100 mm and minor diameter 25 mm — the canonical first-build dimensions — three metres of wire covers ninety-six turns. Plus an extra metre for terminations and slack. Total: four metres of wire to acquire.
>
> *Wire gauge.* AWG 22 for the canonical build. Roughly 0.6 mm diameter copper. Heavy enough to hold its shape, fine enough to wind by hand.
>
> *Build time.* Six hours, broken into chunks. Two hours of preparation (form check, wire cutting, position marking). Three hours of actual winding. One hour of termination, measurement, and logging. A first-time builder typically takes longer; a third coil takes about four hours total.
>
> *Total cost.* Around thirty dollars at the time of recording — torus former (3D printed from a course-supplied STL, or wood-turned at a local shop) plus enameled copper wire plus solder and small connectors.
>
> *[pause — show the spec card on screen]*
>
> Six numbers to remember. *Twenty-four turns per traversal. Four traversals. Ninety-six total turns. Four metres of wire. Six hours. Thirty dollars.* The coil is small, cheap, and quick. The structure underneath it is the cycle you've been studying for four weeks.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One observation about the numbers. *Ninety-six*. Reduce: 9 + 6 = 15, then 1 + 5 = 6. Vertex 6. The total turn count for the canonical build has digital root 6. We saw this in Wk 3 Mod-C Lesson 6 — the milestone-check trick. Turn 24 reduces to 6, turn 48 reduces to 3, turn 72 reduces to 9, turn 96 reduces to 6. Four checkpoints. The Doubling Circuit's verification structure is built into the build's turn count by design."
