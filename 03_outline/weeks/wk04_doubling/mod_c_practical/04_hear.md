> *"The first time I wound a Rodin Coil, I wound it wrong. I lost track of the cycle around turn forty. The wire was tight, my eyes were on the next vertex, and I forgot which traversal I was on. I had to unwind and start over. The second coil I wound, I lost count differently — I got the cycle order right but lost the strand spacing. The third coil came out clean. By the tenth coil, my hands knew what to do. The doubling cycle had moved from my notebook to my hands. What I want to tell new builders is two things. First — your first coil will probably have errors. That's fine. Build it anyway. The errors teach you. Second — by the third or fourth coil, the cycle becomes a hand-knowledge, not a mind-knowledge. The math is in your fingers, and you can wind without consciously checking the next vertex. That hand-knowledge is what builders bring that mathematicians often don't. Don't underestimate the value of having traced this circuit a hundred times in copper. The math you have studied changes when you have built it."*
> — Randy Powell, paraphrased from Red Ice Radio Hour 1 and TEDxCharlotte 2010 lecture material. **Source:** P1-7, P1-13. **Permission status:** to be confirmed by org before publication.
