## 0. Authoring notes
First sketch of a Rodin Coil winding plan. The student produces a worksheet — a step-by-step plan of which vertex to wire to next — that they'll use as a reference during the Week 33 build. Concrete and useful.

By the end of these 6 minutes, the student has produced a step-by-step winding plan for the canonical Wk-33 build, ready to use during the build session.

> "Twenty-four rows. Each one a winding step. Today we write the plan."

> "Lens 6 of 9. Apply. We're producing the worksheet you'll bring to Wk 33's build. Six minutes."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Open your notebook. Or open a fresh page in your build log under *Builds*. Title it *Wk 33 winding plan*.
>
> Below the title, draw a table with three columns: *step, from vertex, to vertex*. Number the rows 1 through 24 — that's one full traversal of the Doubling Circuit at four loops per vertex.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Fill the table.
>
> Step 1: from 1 to 2.
> Step 2: from 2 to 4.
> Step 3: from 4 to 8.
> Step 4: from 8 to 7.
> Step 5: from 7 to 5.
> Step 6: from 5 to 1.
>
> The first six steps complete one cycle. Now four-loop the cycle.
>
> Step 7: from 1 to 2 (cycle 2).
> Step 8: from 2 to 4.
> ... continue through step 12 (1 again).
> Step 13: from 1 to 2 (cycle 3).
> ... continue through step 18.
> Step 19: from 1 to 2 (cycle 4).
> ... continue through step 24.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Twenty-four rows. Four cycles of six steps each. The plan is the wire's path through the build session.
>
> Beside each step, leave a small column for *check mark*. During the actual build, you tick each row as you complete it. The plan becomes a real-time progress indicator.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Now add the milestones. After step 6, draw a horizontal line and write *Cycle 1 complete — verify with digital-root: turn 6 = vertex 6*. We covered this in Wk 3 Mod-C Lesson 6 — the milestone-check trick. The cycle 6 turn lands on vertex 6 because *6 mod 9 = 6*. After step 12, write *Cycle 2 complete — verify: turn 12 = vertex 3*. (12 mod 9 = 3.) After step 18, *Cycle 3 complete — turn 18 = vertex 9*. After step 24, *Cycle 4 complete — turn 24 = vertex 6*.
>
> *[pause — show the completed worksheet]*
>
> The full Wk 33 winding plan, ready. When you sit at the workbench in Week 33, you have this worksheet open on your desk. You wind, tick, verify, repeat. The plan turns the build into a checked-off sequence. It catches errors before they propagate.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One observation. The plan is the same across builds. Every Rodin Coil following the canonical four-loop spec has these twenty-four steps. The plan is *reproducible* — your worksheet matches another builder's worksheet matches Marko's worksheet. That's an engineering virtue, not a coincidence."
