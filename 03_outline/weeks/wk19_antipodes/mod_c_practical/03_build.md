## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Today's build is a *plan* for a two-strand counter-wound mini-coil. Two strands is the simplest counter-wound configuration; building it before the six-strand canonical gives valuable practice with multi-strand technique.
>
> Open your build log to a fresh page. Title it *Q3 Mini-Build — Two-Strand Counter-Wound Coil*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Section 1 — Concept.*
>
> Two strands wound simultaneously on the torus former, both following the doubling helix path, but starting at antipodal positions and connected so currents flow in opposite directions.
>
> Strand A starts at column 0, row 0 (value 1). Strand B starts at column 12, row 3 (value 8). *Antipodal partners.*
>
> Both strands follow the doubling helix path. Strand A traces (0, 0), (1, 1), (2, 2), …, (23, 5). Strand B traces (12, 3), (13, 4), (14, 5), (15, 0), (16, 1), …, (11, 2).
>
> *Both wires close at their respective starting points after 24 turns.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Section 2 — Materials.*
>
> *Two* lengths of magnet wire, AWG 24, *different colours* if available (e.g., red and green, for visual distinction). 1 m each.
>
> Four terminal posts (or four insulated through-holes): A_start, A_end, B_start, B_end.
>
> Soldering iron, solder, flux.
>
> Multimeter for resistance and continuity verification.
>
> *Section 3 — Procedure.*
>
> *Step 1.* Cut wire A (red) and wire B (green) to ~1 m each. Strip 1 cm of enamel from each end.
>
> *Step 2.* Solder wire A start to terminal A_start at column 0, row 0.
>
> *Step 3.* Solder wire B start to terminal B_start at column 12, row 3.
>
> *Step 4.* Wind both wires *simultaneously* — one cell at a time, alternating which wire you advance. Wire A goes through cells (0, 0), (1, 1), (2, 2), … Wire B goes through (12, 3), (13, 4), (14, 5), …
>
> *Step 5.* Continue for all 24 cells. Wire A ends at (23, 5). Wire B ends at (11, 2).
>
> *Step 6.* Solder wire A end to terminal A_end. Solder wire B end to terminal B_end.
>
> *Step 7.* Verify visually: both wires follow the doubling helix path. Both close. Both occupy antipodal cells throughout.
>
> *Step 8.* Verify electrically: resistance from A_start to A_end ≈ 1.5 Ω. Same for B_start to B_end. Continuity check between A and B: should *not* be continuous (they're separate wires).
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Section 4 — Counter-current connection.*
>
> *To run currents in opposite directions:*
>
> Connect A_start to power supply +V terminal.
>
> Connect A_end to power supply ground (or +V if testing differently).
>
> Connect B_start to power supply ground.
>
> Connect B_end to power supply +V.
>
> *Result:* current flows column-0-to-column-23 in wire A, and column-12-to-column-11 in wire B. *Opposite directions through antipodal positions.*
>
> Alternative: use a single power supply with both wires connected so they form a *closed loop* — current enters wire A at A_start, exits at A_end, enters wire B at B_end, exits at B_start. This is the *bifilar series* connection.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Section 5 — Verification.*
>
> Energise the coil at low current (< 100 mA). Use a hand-held magnetic field probe (or a compass) to test the field shape.
>
> *Expected.* Field should be reduced compared to a single-strand coil at the same current. *Counter-currents partially cancel.*
>
> *If field is similar to single-strand.* Check connections — wires may be co-current rather than counter-current.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What you've planned. *A two-strand counter-wound mini-coil with measurement protocol.* Q3's Wk 35 (Multi-strand) will use a similar template scaled to six strands.
>
> Three observations.
>
> *Observation one.* Two strands is *simpler* than six but introduces the counter-wound concept fully. The technique scales.
>
> *Observation two.* The connections matter. Wrong terminal connections give co-current rather than counter-current.
>
> *Observation three.* The mini-build is *optional* for Q3. The canonical build is six-strand four-traversal. The two-strand single-traversal mini is for *practice and intuition*."
