> *Practical takeaways.*
>
> *Takeaway 1 — wind two wires simultaneously, not in sequence.* Winding strand A entirely first, then strand B, makes precision difficult. *Wind both at once*, alternating cells.
>
> *Takeaway 2 — colour-code from the start.* Two wires of distinguishable colours prevent confusion. Even if both are visible at the start, after a few turns it gets hard to tell them apart without colour distinction.
>
> *Takeaway 3 — verify connections before energising.* Counter-current connection is set at the terminals. *Wrong connections give co-currents and waste the structural work.*
>
> *Takeaway 4 — measure first.* Before claiming the coil produces interesting effects, verify the basic resistance and continuity. *Most surprising claims trace back to measurement errors.*
>
