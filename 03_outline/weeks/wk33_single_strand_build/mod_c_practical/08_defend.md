## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The objection. *I built my first coil; I see imperfections. Will measurements show distinctive content with imperfect coil? Should I rebuild before measuring?*
>
> *The objection is sincere; first-build anxiety is real.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *The honest defence.*
>
> *First coils are rarely perfect; that's the engineering norm, not failure. Build well enough that measurements can yield interpretable data; document imperfections so measurements are interpretable in context. Don't rebuild for perfectionism; rebuild for engineering iteration if first build is fundamentally non-functional. Most first builds yield engineering data; iteration through second builds improves; honest reporting is what matters.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three layers in the reassurance.*
>
> *Layer one — engineering norm.* *First builds in any engineering tradition vary in quality*; *iteration is standard practice*; *imperfect first builds are normal output*. *Mainstream practice.*
>
> *Layer two — measurements work with imperfect coils.* *Measurement instruments don't require perfect coils*; *they measure whatever's there*; *interpretation handles imperfections through documentation*.
>
> *Layer three — practitioner agency.* *Practitioner decides when to proceed and when to rebuild*; *not pressured to rebuild for cosmetic concerns*; *not pressured to proceed if fundamental issues*. *Mature engineering choice.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three indicators that first build is sufficient for measurement.*
>
> *Indicator 1 — DC continuity passes.* *Multimeter shows continuous wire path through coil*; *no broken wire or open joint*. *Foundational.*
>
> *Indicator 2 — winding visible matches helix path approximately.* *Six positions identifiable; doubling-cycle order recognisable*; *significant deviations within tolerance*. *Visual.*
>
> *Indicator 3 — terminations functional.* *Both wire ends connect to terminal block; instrument cables can attach*; *measurement-ready*. *Engineering.*
>
> *Three indicators; if all met, first build is measurement-ready*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three indicators that first build needs rework.*
>
> *Indicator 1 — DC continuity fails.* *Broken wire or open joint*; *no electrical path*; *measurement impossible*. *Rebuild needed.*
>
> *Indicator 2 — winding pattern fundamentally wrong.* *Wire wound continuously around tube (mainstream toroidal pattern instead of doubling-cycle)*; *not a Rodin Coil*. *Rebuild needed.*
>
> *Indicator 3 — terminations fail.* *Cannot connect instruments*; *no measurement possible*. *Repair or rebuild.*
>
> *Three indicators; if any present, rebuild before measurement*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What can be defended.*
>
> *Defend 1.* *Most first builds meet measurement criteria.* *Imperfections within tolerance*; *measurement-ready*.
>
> *Defend 2.* *Imperfections documented enable interpretation.* *Wk 34 measurements interpreted in context*; *not invalidated by imperfections*.
>
> *Defend 3.* *Iteration is engineering norm.* *Second builds improve*; *first builds are not final*.
>
> *Defend 4.* *Practitioner agency respected.* *Practitioner decides per their build state and confidence*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Honest articulation to a concerned practitioner.*
>
> *Articulation.* 'Your first coil is rarely perfect; that's the engineering norm, not failure. Most first builds meet measurement criteria: DC continuity passes; winding pattern recognisable; terminations functional. With these three indicators met, your coil is measurement-ready; document imperfections in your build log; proceed to Wk 34 measurements. If any of three rebuild indicators are present (continuity fail, fundamentally wrong pattern, termination fail), repair or rebuild before measurement. Otherwise, proceed; engineering iteration follows; second builds improve. Honest reporting of imperfections enables interpretation; perfectionism leads to abandonment; reasonable engineering proceeds with documented imperfect coils. Most practitioners worry more than necessary; first builds typically work for measurement.'
>
> Around 145 words.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Why this honest acknowledgment is the right defence.*
>
> *Reason 1 — engineering reality.* *First builds vary; iteration is norm*; *acknowledgment honors reality.*
>
> *Reason 2 — measurement methodology.* *Imperfections documented enable interpretation*; *measurements proceed.*
>
> *Reason 3 — practitioner support.* *Anxiety reduces engineering quality*; *reassurance enables proceeding*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Defend imperfect first builds*: *engineering norm; measurements work with documented imperfections; iteration improves second builds*. *Three measurement-ready indicators (continuity, recognisable pattern, terminations); three rebuild indicators (continuity fail, wrong pattern, termination fail)*. *Most first builds proceed; perfectionism counterproductive*."
