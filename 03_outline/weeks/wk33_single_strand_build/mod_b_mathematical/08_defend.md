## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The objection. *Math is for specification, not for diagnosing mistakes. If a build has issues, you find them by inspection or measurement, not by math. Why claim math 'says where mistakes show up'?*
>
> *The objection is sincere; math's role in diagnosis deserves clarification.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *The honest defence.*
>
> *Math says where mistakes show up because measurement-and-prediction comparison reveals build deviations. The math predicts specific measurable values (resonance frequency, inductance, harmonic content); when measurements diverge from predictions, the divergence-pattern points to specific build issues. Math doesn't directly diagnose mistakes — it provides predictions against which measurements reveal mistakes. Diagnostic chain: math → predictions → measurements → comparison → mistake-localisation.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three diagnostic patterns.*
>
> *Pattern 1 — wrong resonance frequency.* *Math predicts f_res based on inductance + capacitance*. *If measured f_res is significantly off, possible causes: wrong turn count; wrong dimensions; wrong wire properties*. *Math + measurement narrow possibilities.*
>
> *Pattern 2 — missing harmonic content.* *Math predicts 6-fold harmonic content from doubling-cycle structure*. *If 6 × f_res content absent, possible causes: cycle order wrong; positions skipped; coil-build doesn't realise cycle structure correctly*. *Diagnostic.*
>
> *Pattern 3 — unexpected harmonic content.* *Math doesn't predict prominent content at certain frequencies*. *If unexpected peaks appear, possible causes: wire crossings creating short paths; non-canonical winding pattern; capacitive coupling unexpected*. *Diagnostic.*
>
> *Three patterns; mistake localisation through math + measurement*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What can be defended.*
>
> *Defend 1.* *Math provides predictions.* *Specific quantitative predictions for measurable quantities*; *comparison framework*.
>
> *Defend 2.* *Measurement vs prediction divergence reveals mistakes.* *Direction and magnitude of divergence narrow possible causes.*
>
> *Defend 3.* *Diagnostic chain is mainstream engineering.* *Standard practice in many engineering domains*; *math + measurement = diagnosis*.
>
> *Defend 4.* *Diagnostic value is conditional on accurate predictions.* *If math is wrong, predictions are wrong*; *but math accuracy can be tested through control coils*; *bootstrapping confidence*.
>
> *Defend 5.* *Math doesn't diagnose alone.* *Inspection, measurement, intuition all contribute*; *math is one tool among several*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What can't be defended.*
>
> *Can't 1.* That math diagnoses mistakes directly. *Math doesn't see the coil*; *math predicts; measurement sees*; *comparison reveals*.
>
> *Can't 2.* That every divergence points to a unique mistake. *Different mistakes can produce similar divergences*; *math + measurement narrow but don't always uniquely identify*.
>
> *Honest acknowledgment.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Honest articulation to a sceptical builder.*
>
> *Articulation.* 'Math says where mistakes show up indirectly. Math provides specific quantitative predictions: resonance frequency, harmonic content, magnetic field topology. These are predictions for measurable quantities. When you measure your built coil and compare to predictions, divergences reveal where the coil departs from canonical specification. Different mistakes produce different divergence patterns. Wrong turn count shifts resonance frequency; cycle-order errors affect 6-fold harmonic content; wire crossings affect parasitic capacitance and unexpected harmonics. Math + measurement together = diagnostic; math alone doesn't diagnose. Engineering chain: math → predictions → measurements → comparison → mistake localisation. This is mainstream engineering practice; VBM applies it specifically.'
>
> Around 130 words.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Why this honest acknowledgment is the right defence.*
>
> *Reason 1 — clarifies math's role.* *Math provides predictions; measurement reveals; comparison diagnoses*.
>
> *Reason 2 — preserves engineering credibility.* *Math + measurement combined is engineering norm*; *VBM applies standard practice*.
>
> *Reason 3 — engineering iteration enabled.* *Diagnostic chain enables targeted second-build improvements*; *iteration converges on canonical*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Calibrated answer.*
>
> *Math says where mistakes show up indirectly: math provides predictions; measurement reveals; comparison localises mistakes. Three example diagnostic patterns: wrong resonance frequency (turns / dimensions issue); missing 6-fold content (cycle-order issue); unexpected harmonics (parasitic / crossing issues). Math alone doesn't diagnose; math + measurement combined does. Engineering chain: math → predictions → measurements → comparison → mistake-localisation. Standard engineering practice.*
>
> Around 80 words.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Defend math's diagnostic value*: *math provides predictions; measurements reveal; comparison localises mistakes*. *Three diagnostic patterns (resonance, missing harmonics, unexpected harmonics)*. *Engineering chain: math → predictions → measurements → comparison → mistake-localisation*. *Standard engineering practice*."
