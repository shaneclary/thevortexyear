## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Lens 6 set up coil's existence. Today: *compare your first build with expert / foundation reference builds*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Expected differences between first and expert builds.*
>
> *Difference 1 — winding uniformity.* *Expert: uniform tension, even spacing, clean transitions. First build: variable tension, uneven spacing, rough transitions*. *Practice develops uniformity*.
>
> *Difference 2 — visual cleanliness.* *Expert: clean wire paths, no kinks, polished appearance. First build: possibly minor kinks, less polish*. *Aesthetics improve with practice*.
>
> *Difference 3 — termination quality.* *Expert: precise solder, clean connections, professional terminations. First build: possibly less polished termination*. *Soldering skill develops with practice*.
>
> *Three differences; expected and normal*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three observations on the comparison.*
>
> *Observation one — comparison reveals expected gaps.* *First builds shouldn't match expert builds*; *expectation of equivalence is unrealistic*.
>
> *Observation two — comparison identifies improvement areas.* *Specific deviations* indicate *specific practice areas for second build*; *targeted skill development*.
>
> *Observation three — first build has its own value.* *Despite imperfections, first build is artefact of practitioner's specific Q3 engagement*; *biographical value beyond aesthetic comparison*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three healthy comparison practices.*
>
> *Practice 1 — note specific imperfections, not overall judgment.* *'Position 4 winding has gap' is informative*; *'this build is bad' is not*. *Specific feedback enables iteration.*
>
> *Practice 2 — compare to canonical, not perfect.* *Foundation expert builds are well-executed canonical specs, not idealised perfection*; *target is canonical, not impossible*.
>
> *Practice 3 — celebrate completion alongside identifying improvements.* *Build completion is event*; *imperfections don't diminish completion*; *both honest engagement*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three engineering implications of comparison.*
>
> *Implication 1 — measurement may differ from prediction.* *First build's imperfections* affect *measured response*; *measurement results compared to prediction may show variation from expert-build expected*. *Documented context.*
>
> *Implication 2 — second build benefits from comparison.* *Identifying first-build issues* informs *second-build improvements*; *iterative development*.
>
> *Implication 3 — foundation collaboration informed.* *Practitioners reporting first-build measurements* should *include build-quality assessment*; *cross-rig context*. *Engineering transparency*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three honest assessments.*
>
> *Assessment one — first build is reasonable engineering target.* *Working coil, identifiable winding, terminations functional*; *that's enough for first build*.
>
> *Assessment two — perfectionism is counterproductive.* *Pursuing perfection in first builds leads to abandonment*; *good-enough first build leads to second build leads to better builds*. *Iteration mindset*.
>
> *Assessment three — community helps calibration.* *Other practitioners' first builds* (visible through foundation, online communities) *show range of first-build quality*; *normalises expectation*. *Community context*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Useful framing. *First Rodin Coil builds vary in quality compared to expert / foundation reference builds. Three expected differences: winding uniformity, visual cleanliness, termination quality. First builds aren't expected to match expert builds; iteration produces improvement. Comparison should be specific and informative, not perfectionistic. Engineering context and biographical value coexist; measurement context informed by build quality.*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Compare honestly with expert builds*: *expected differences in uniformity, cleanliness, terminations*. *Specific imperfection notes inform second-build improvement*. *Completion has value beyond aesthetic comparison*; *iteration is normal*."
