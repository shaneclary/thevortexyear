>
> *What to count during winding.*
>
> *Count 1 — cycle position.* *At each pause, what cycle position is current?* *(1, 2, 4, 8, 7, 5)*. *Verify position against winding chart*.
>
> *Count 2 — completed cycles.* *Each full doubling-cycle traversal = 1 cycle complete*. *Track total cycles wound.*
>
> *Count 3 — major-circle turns.* *(Per cycle = 1 major turn for canonical winding.)* *Major circle traversals.*
>
> *Count 4 — minor-circle turns.* *Per cycle = n minor turns (canonical n = 2)*. *Minor circle traversals.*
>
> *Count 5 — wire length used.* *Approximately measure remaining wire vs original*; *catches wire-shortage issues early*.
>
> *Five counts; engineering hygiene through tracking*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Counting rhythm during winding.*
>
> *Approach 1 — count aloud as winding.* *'Position 1; position 2; position 4; ...'* . *Continuous counting; verbal*.
>
> *Approach 2 — count between pauses.* *Wind position-to-position; pause briefly; verify count*. *Discrete counting; less verbal*.
>
> *Approach 3 — counting after stage.* *Wind without counting; verify count after each stage*. *Less interruption; potential for missing errors mid-process*.
>
> *Three approaches; practitioner choice per personality*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three observations on counting.*
>
> *Observation one — counting catches errors early.* *If wire skips a position, count diverges from chart*; *catch in process; correct before continuing*.
>
> *Observation two — counting reduces anxiety.* *Knowing where you are in the build* reduces *uncertainty*; *promotes focus.*
>
> *Observation three — counting becomes automatic with practice.* *First builds require deliberate counting*; *experienced builders count subconsciously*; *engineering muscle-memory*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three engineering uses of counting.*
>
> *Use 1 — quality control.* *Counts match chart → build is on-spec*; *deviation indicates error*. *In-process verification.*
>
> *Use 2 — progress tracking.* *Knowing what cycle you're on; what fraction complete*; *psychological benefit; planning.*
>
> *Use 3 — documentation.* *After build, record final counts in notebook*; *engineering record*. *Documentation aid.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three potential counting issues.*
>
> *Issue 1 — losing count mid-winding.* *If interrupted, may forget count*. *Mitigation: pause notes; visual position indicators; helix markings*.
>
> *Issue 2 — miscounting positions.* *Easy to confuse position 7 and position 5 (look similar; both off-axis)*. *Mitigation: explicit verification against chart; colour-coded markings*.
>
> *Issue 3 — over- or under-counting due to fatigue.* *Long builds tire counting accuracy*. *Mitigation: take breaks; verify counts during breaks; resume with attention*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three counting practices to adopt.*
>
> *Practice 1 — verify cycle complete.* *After each cycle, count: did I do 6 positions?* *Cycle integrity check.*
>
> *Practice 2 — count at major checkpoints.* *Halfway through cycle (3 positions); fully through cycle*. *Multi-checkpoint counting*.
>
> *Practice 3 — record counts at completion.* *Final count in notebook*: *cycle count, total positions, wire length used*. *Documentation*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Count turns as you go*: *5 quantities (cycle position, completed cycles, major turns, minor turns, wire length)*. *Three counting approaches (continuous, discrete, post-stage)*. *Engineering hygiene; in-process verification; documentation*. *First-build counting deliberate; experienced builders automatic*."
