## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The objection. *First builds inevitably have mistakes. Wrong positions wound; tension uneven; wires crossing accidentally; soldering issues. The framework's specifications don't accommodate mistakes. Should practitioners discard imperfect builds and start over?*
>
> *The objection is sincere; build-mistake handling deserves clarification.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *The honest defence.*
>
> *Most build mistakes are recoverable through repair, partial rewinding, or accepting documented deviation. The framework accommodates imperfect builds because real builds have variation; specifications include tolerances. Recovery practices exist for common mistakes; documented deviation enables Wk 34 measurements to interpret results in context. Discarding imperfect first builds is rarely necessary; iteration through repair / partial rebuild is usually preferable.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three categories of common build mistakes.*
>
> *Category 1 — winding errors.*
>
> *Wrong position*: *wire wound to incorrect position*; *not on canonical helix path*. *Common.*
>
> *Recovery*: *unwind from last verified position; rewind correctly*; *minor wire bend tolerable*. *Recovery time: 5-15 min per error*.
>
> *Skipped position*: *wire bypasses one position in cycle*; *cycle incomplete or distorted*. *Less common.*
>
> *Recovery*: *unwind to before skip; insert missed position; rewind*. *Recovery time: 10-30 min*.
>
> *Crossing*: *wire crosses itself unintendedly*; *accidental short or distortion*. *Common.*
>
> *Recovery*: *separate the crossing if possible; or unwind to before crossing*. *Recovery time: 5-20 min*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Category 2 — termination errors.*
>
> *Cold solder joint*: *insufficient heat or flux during soldering*; *intermittent or no electrical connection*. *Common.*
>
> *Recovery*: *re-solder with adequate heat / flux*; *test continuity*. *Recovery time: 5-15 min*.
>
> *Reverse polarity*: *wire entry / exit swapped at terminal block*. *Sometimes.*
>
> *Recovery*: *swap connections at terminal block*. *Recovery time: 2-5 min*.
>
> *Insulation issue*: *exposed wire at unintended point; potential short with adjacent winding*. *Common.*
>
> *Recovery*: *insulate with electrical tape or heatshrink; or rewind to repair*. *Recovery time: 5-15 min*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Category 3 — material / former issues.*
>
> *Former damage*: *crack or warp during winding*. *Rare.*
>
> *Recovery*: *if minor, continue with documentation; if major, rebuild on new former*. *Recovery time: variable*.
>
> *Wire breakage*: *wire snap during winding*. *Rare with appropriate gauge.*
>
> *Recovery*: *splice with solder if reasonable; or restart winding with new wire*. *Recovery time: 30-60 min for splice; 1-3 hr for restart*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What can be defended.*
>
> *Defend 1.* *Most mistakes are recoverable.* *Three categories with specific recovery procedures*.
>
> *Defend 2.* *Documented deviation is acceptable.* *If recovery isn't perfect; document the variation; Wk 34 measurements interpret in context*.
>
> *Defend 3.* *Iteration is engineering norm.* *Mistakes happen; recovery happens; second builds improve*. *Standard engineering practice.*
>
> *Defend 4.* *Specifications include tolerances.* *Real builds have manufacturing variation*; *specifications acknowledge this; honest engineering*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Honest articulation to a sceptical or worried practitioner.*
>
> *Articulation.* 'Build mistakes are normal in engineering; first builds especially. Most mistakes are recoverable through specific recovery procedures: unwind to last verified position and rewind for winding errors; re-solder for cold joints; insulate or rewind for insulation issues. Document any deviations from canonical specification; Wk 34 measurements interpret results in context of build quality. Discarding imperfect first builds is rarely necessary; recovery and iteration are preferable. The framework accommodates imperfect builds because real builds always have variation; specifications include tolerances. First-build mistakes are learning opportunities, not framework failures; second builds improve.'
>
> Around 110 words.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Defend mistake recovery*: *most mistakes recoverable through specific procedures (rewinding, re-soldering, insulating)*; *documented deviation acceptable*; *iteration is engineering norm*. *Three categories of common mistakes with recovery procedures*. *First builds aren't expected to be flawless*."
