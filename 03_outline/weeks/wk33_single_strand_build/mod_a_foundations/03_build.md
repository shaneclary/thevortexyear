> *The actual winding — step-by-step.*
>
> *Step 1 — connect entry.* *Take wire's first end; insert into terminal block at entry position; tighten or solder*. *Wire's free end now extending from former's entry point*.
>
> *Step 2 — position 1 anchor.* *Locate position 1 on the former* (already marked from Wk 27 Mod C). *Pull wire so it touches the former at position 1*. *Anchor wire firmly there with finger or temporary tape*.
>
> *Step 3 — wind to position 2.* *From position 1, wire follows the helix path to position 2 — major angle 60°, minor angle 120° (n = 2)*. *Trace the helix line on the former with the wire*; *maintain consistent tension*; *anchor at position 2*.
>
> *Step 4 — wind to position 4.* *Same procedure; helix path to position 4*. *Major angle 120°, minor angle 240°*.
>
> *Step 5 — wind to position 8.* *Helix path to position 8*. *Major angle 180°, minor angle 360° = 0° (modulo)*.
>
> *Step 6 — wind to position 7.* *Helix path to position 7*. *Major angle 240°, minor angle 120°*.
>
> *Step 7 — wind to position 5.* *Helix path to position 5*. *Major angle 300°, minor angle 240°*.
>
> *Step 8 — close cycle to position 1.* *Helix path back to position 1*; *wire returns to start of cycle*. *Cycle 1 complete*.
>
> *[Repeat steps 2-8 for additional cycles if multi-cycle build].*
>
> *Step 9 — secure exit.* *Wire's second end pulled to exit position*; *insert into terminal block at exit position*; *tighten or solder*.
>
> *Step 10 — verification.* *Visual check: wire path matches helix markings; no crossings unintended; both ends secured.* *Photograph completed coil*.
>
> *Ten steps total; central winding work in steps 2-8 (and repeats)*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three observations on actual winding.*
>
> *Observation one — pace develops naturally.* *First few positions awkward; rhythm emerges*; *don't force speed*. *Quality over speed.*
>
> *Observation two — pause as needed.* *Tension fatigue, position confusion, distraction* warrant *brief pause*; *resume when settled*. *Patience.*
>
> *Observation three — anchor between positions.* *Don't try to wind continuously without anchoring*; *finger or tape at each position prevents wire slippage*. *Engineering technique*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three potential issues during winding.*
>
> *Issue 1 — wire kink or fold.* *Bent wire that doesn't lay flat*; *catch immediately; gently straighten or accept minor imperfection*.
>
> *Issue 2 — position drift.* *Wire crossing wrong position*; *unwind to last verified position; rewind correctly*.
>
> *Issue 3 — wire breakage.* *Rare with appropriate gauge; can occur with thin wire or rough handling*; *splice with solder if reasonable; otherwise cut and re-do*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Hand-winding is 10 steps*: *entry connection; 6 helix-position windings (cycle); cycle repeat if multi-cycle; exit connection; verification*. *Pace develops naturally; patience; anchor between positions*. *First Wk 33 build produces first physical Rodin Coil*."
