## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Today's build is the slow, patient labelling of every cell on your paper torus. By the end of the lesson (or this evening), the torus will have all 144 values written on it.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Materials.*
>
> Your paper torus from Wk 16 (or the cardstock-reinforced version from Wk 16 Mod C if you upgraded).
>
> A fine-point pencil or marker.
>
> The Number Map (printed grid from Wk 15) for reference.
>
> Optional: coloured pens for previewing the family colours (Layer 2).
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Step 1 — orient the torus.* Lay or hold the torus so you can see one section at a time. Identify column 0, row 0 — your starting cell. This should be on the *outer surface* of the doughnut at one specific 'top' position.
>
> *Step 2 — label cell (0, 0).* The cell holds value 1 (since 2⁰ = 1). Write *1* in the cell with a fine-point pencil.
>
> *Step 3 — label row 0 across all columns.* Move column-by-column. Cell (0, 1) → 1. Cell (0, 2) → 1. Etc. All 24 cells in row 0 hold the value 1. Write *1* in each.
>
> *Step 4 — label row 1.* The 24 cells in row 1 each hold value 2. Write *2* in each.
>
> *Step 5 — label rows 2 through 5.* Continue the pattern.
>
> Row 2 cells: 4. Row 3 cells: 8. Row 4 cells: 7. Row 5 cells: 5.
>
> *Step 6 — verify.* Count: 24 ones, 24 twos, 24 fours, 24 eights, 24 sevens, 24 fives. Total: 144. Sum: 24 × (1 + 2 + 4 + 8 + 7 + 5) = 24 × 27 = 648.
>
> *Sanity check via digital root:* sum of all cells mod 9 = 648 mod 9 = 0. (Because 6 + 4 + 8 = 18, 18 mod 9 = 0.) ✓
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Practical tips.*
>
> *Tip one.* Use a sharp pencil. Cells are small (under 1 cm² typically). Sloppy writing makes the labels unreadable.
>
> *Tip two.* Work in 6-cell columns first if you find rows confusing. Each column always reads top-to-bottom: 1, 2, 4, 8, 7, 5.
>
> *Tip three.* If the torus is hard to handle while you write, *unfold one segment at a time* (if your torus uses Method 2 segment-and-tape from Wk 16 Mod C). Label the unfolded segment, then re-fold.
>
> *Tip four.* Photograph each section as you complete it. The build log gets a sequence of photos showing the progression from blank to fully-labelled.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Step 7 — preview Layer 2 (family colours).*
>
> If you have time, lightly colour the cells you've labelled.
>
> Family 1 cells (rows 0, 2, 4 — values 1, 4, 7): one colour. Light shading is enough.
>
> Family 2 cells (rows 1, 3, 5 — values 2, 8, 5): a different colour.
>
> Don't colour intensely; the labels need to remain readable. *Light highlighting only.*
>
> Layer 2's full treatment comes in Mod C of this week. Today is just a preview.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What you've built. *Layer 1 of the ABHA Torus is now in place on your paper torus.* Every cell has a value; every value can be read.
>
> Three observations.
>
> *Observation one.* The labelling is *tedious*. That's the point. Repetition burns the structure into memory.
>
> *Observation two.* The labelled torus is now *readable in 3D*. You can pick up the torus, look at a cell, and know its value without referring to the grid. This is the embodied vocabulary Mod A's Lens 1 was preparing.
>
> *Observation three.* The labelling will *fade with handling*. By Q3 your labels may be partially worn. *Re-labelling is a normal maintenance task* of the working torus."
