## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Last week's torus was *bare* — a topological surface with cells but no annotations. This week's torus is *decorated* — the same surface with VBM's specific structures marked on it. The decorated version is what Marko has named the *ABHA Torus*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Four annotation layers* turn a bare torus into the ABHA Torus.
>
> *Layer 1 — cell digit-roots.* Each of the 144 cells holds a value 1, 2, 4, 5, 7, or 8 (the units of ℤ/9ℤ that the doubling cycle visits). Each cell's value is written or marked on it. The bare torus had cells but no values; the ABHA Torus has labelled cells.
>
> *Layer 2 — family colour regions.* Cells are coloured by family. Family 1 = {1, 4, 7} in one colour. Family 2 = {2, 5, 8} in another. Family 3 = {3, 6, 9} would be a third — *but doesn't appear on this torus*; the doubling-only grid omits family 3.
>
> *Layer 3 — polar pair antipode markings.* Each polar pair (1, 8), (2, 7), (4, 5) appears at antipodal positions. Each pair is connected by a line through the torus's body — a *physical chord* between antipodal cells, often drawn with a coloured thread.
>
> *Layer 4 — 3-6-9 axis as orthogonal direction.* The 3-6-9 axis is the *third* axis through the torus's centre, orthogonal to both the major and minor circles. On the ABHA Torus, this axis is marked with a vertical line passing through the doughnut hole.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What the four layers do together.*
>
> They convert the bare torus into a *workspace*. With the layers in place, every Q1 structure has a *physical location* on the torus.
>
> *Q1 doubling cycle* → Layer 1 cells, traced sequentially around the torus.
>
> *Q1 polar pairs* → Layer 3 antipodal markings.
>
> *Q1 family groups* → Layer 2 coloured regions.
>
> *Q1 3-6-9 axis* → Layer 4 orthogonal axis.
>
> *Each algebraic structure becomes a geometric structure on the surface.* The torus is now an embodied reference for Q1's content.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Why call it the ABHA Torus.*
>
> *ABHA* is the Bahá'í greeting Marko encountered at age 15 (Wk 12). Its Abjad numerology reduces to 9 — the foundational number of VBM. Marko named the structured torus *ABHA* in honour of that origin story.
>
> The name signals that this torus is *not just a generic torus* but the specific structured object that VBM uses. Other structured tori exist (in physics, mathematics, engineering); the ABHA Torus is one specific structuring with the four layers above.
>
> The name has Marko's biographical and spiritual significance. The math doesn't depend on the name; the name is a tradition marker.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three observations.
>
> *Observation one.* The bare torus and the ABHA Torus are the *same surface*. The decorations don't change the topology. They make the structure *legible*.
>
> *Observation two.* The decorations are *modular*. You can have the cell labels without the family colours; the family colours without the antipode threads; etc. Each layer is independent.
>
> *Observation three.* The full ABHA Torus with all four layers is *the canonical Q2 reference object*. Wks 18-26 will refer back to it repeatedly.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Hold the bare paper torus from Wk 16 in mind. By the end of this week, it will be decorated with all four layers. The decoration happens across Lens 3 (Build) and Mod C of this week."
