## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The ABHA Torus has multiple count structures. Today we tally each.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Count 1 — total cells.* 6 rows × 24 columns = *144 cells*. Same as the flat grid; the wrap doesn't change the cell count.
>
> *Count 2 — distinct values.* The cells hold values 1, 2, 4, 5, 7, 8. *Six distinct values*. Each value appears 24 times across the torus (once per column at one specific row).
>
> *Count 3 — family-1 cells.* Family 1 = {1, 4, 7}. Three values × 24 occurrences each = *72 cells*. These are colored one way.
>
> *Count 4 — family-2 cells.* Family 2 = {2, 5, 8}. Three values × 24 = *72 cells*. Colored another way.
>
> *Count 5 — family-3 cells.* Family 3 = {3, 6, 9}. *Zero cells* — the doubling-only torus omits this family.
>
> Sanity check: 72 + 72 + 0 = 144. ✓
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Count 6 — polar pair antipode endpoints.*
>
> Three polar pairs: (1, 8), (2, 7), (4, 5).
>
> Each pair has *two cells* on the torus where the pair's values live. But each value appears 24 times. So the pair's value-1 endpoints are on 24 cells, and the value-8 endpoints are on another 24 cells.
>
> *Total antipode-pair endpoints* across the torus: 3 pairs × 24 occurrences × 2 endpoints per pair = *144*.
>
> Wait — that's the same as the total cell count, because every cell *is* one endpoint of an antipodal pair.
>
> *Antipodal pair count* on the torus: 144 / 2 = *72 distinct antipode pairs*.
>
> Two endpoints per pair × 72 pairs = 144 cells. ✓
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Count 7 — 3-6-9 axis points.*
>
> The 3-6-9 axis runs through the torus's central hole, perpendicular to both the major and minor circles.
>
> Two intersection points with the torus surface: where the axis enters the doughnut hole at the *top* and *bottom* of the torus.
>
> The axis itself doesn't have cells (cells are on the surface, axis is through the hole), but the *endpoints of the axis on the surface* are special: they're the cells where the axis would touch if extended.
>
> Numerically: *two surface points* lie exactly on the axis-of-symmetry direction (top and bottom 'pole' points).
>
> Plus: the *six positions* of the family-3 elements (3, 3, 6, 6, 9, 9 — counted with multiplicity if you trace the axis through the body) form a small subgroup-like structure that the orthogonal axis passes through.
>
> Practically, *6 axis-related points*: 2 surface poles + 4 'axis-near' cells.
>
> The exact count is convention-dependent. The takeaway: *the 3-6-9 axis is a small substructure with about 6 distinguished points*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Count 8 — winding cycle length.*
>
> The doubling cycle on the torus visits every cell that holds doubling-cycle values: 144 cells. Walking the cycle traverses 144 cells before returning to start.
>
> *Total cycle length: 144 cells.* Equivalent: 24 columns × 6 row-shifts = 144 cell-visits.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three numbers worth memorising for the ABHA Torus.
>
> *144.* Total cells; total cycle length.
>
> *72.* Cells per family (1 and 2); also polar pair count.
>
> *6.* Distinct values on the torus; rows; family-3 substructure size.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One observation. The ABHA Torus is *small but non-trivial*. With 144 cells, every structure of Q1 has a finite enumerable instantiation. The torus is *finite mathematics in physical form*.
>
> Practical takeaway. *Annotate the counts on the back of your bookmark.* 144 cells, 72 family count, 6 distinct values. The numbers will recur in Q2 and especially in Q3 when wire counts derive from these surface-structure numbers."
