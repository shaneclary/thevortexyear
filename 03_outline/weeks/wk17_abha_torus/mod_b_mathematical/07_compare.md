## 0. Authoring notes
S¹ × S¹ decorated vs flat. The torus seen as a topological object versus as a flat fundamental domain — what is gained and lost.

## 1. Outcome
Student can articulate the trade-offs between studying VBM on the flat strip versus on the toroidal surface.

## 2. Hook (0:00–0:30)

> "Decorated torus or flat strip? Same data. Six minutes."

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)
> "Lens 7 of 9. Compare torus and strip as study surfaces."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "*Toroidal study.* Use the decorated torus (paper artefact in Q2; 3D-printed former in Q3) as the working surface. Read coordinates, paths, symmetries directly off the 3D object.
>
> *Flat-strip study.* Use the printed 6×24 grid (pinned to the wall) as the working surface. Read the same data, but on a 2D representation that includes the periodic boundary identifications implicitly.
>
> Both are *valid representations* of the same mathematical object. The choice between them affects what's easy to see.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three structural similarities.
>
> *Similarity one — same cell content.* Each cell holds the same value (digit-root) on both representations. Computations using cell values give identical results.
>
> *Similarity two — same coordinate system.* (col, row) labels each cell on either surface. Coordinate arithmetic works identically.
>
> *Similarity three — same symmetries.* The same automorphism group acts on both. Rotations and antipodal involutions transfer cleanly between representations.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three structural differences.
>
> *Difference one — wrap visibility.* On the strip, the wrap is *implicit* — the left and right edges identify, but you have to remember this. On the torus, the wrap is *automatic* — there are no edges.
>
> *Best for the strip.* Quick visual scanning of all 144 cells in one glance. The strip is *flatter* in the sense of being entirely visible at once.
>
> *Best for the torus.* Path tracing without edge-jumping. A path on the torus continues smoothly across what would have been the strip's edge.
>
> *Difference two — orientation.* On the strip, *up and down* and *left and right* are well-defined. On the torus, all directions are equivalent (the surface is uniform). The strip has *built-in orientation*; the torus doesn't.
>
> *Best for the strip.* Comparing rows or columns visually. The strip's organisation makes the family-stripe pattern obvious.
>
> *Best for the torus.* Demonstrating that no position is privileged. The torus *literally* doesn't favour any cell over any other (up to symmetry).
>
> *Difference three — physical handling.* The strip is paper on a wall. The torus is a 3D object in your hands.
>
> *Best for the strip.* Quick reference during desk work. The wall display is always there.
>
> *Best for the torus.* Embodied learning. Physical handling teaches in ways the strip can't.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *When to use each.*
>
> *Use the strip when:* doing arithmetic computation; surveying the whole 144-cell pattern; communicating with someone unfamiliar with the framework (the strip looks more like a familiar table).
>
> *Use the torus when:* tracing paths; contemplating symmetries; communicating with another VBM practitioner (who'll appreciate the physicality); or in Q3 when the torus is the actual coil substrate.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Useful framing. *The strip and the torus are companions, not competitors. They show the same mathematical object from different angles. The decorated torus emphasises wholeness; the flat strip emphasises flatness. Each invites different kinds of attention.*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Use both.* The strip on the wall and the torus in hand serve different purposes. By Q3, you'll have *both physical artefacts and a mental ability to translate between them*. The translation skill is itself one of Q2's deliverables.
>
> A small note. Practitioners who only ever study the strip miss the torus's geometric features (antipodes as physical opposites, paths as helices). Practitioners who only ever study the torus may struggle with bulk-pattern surveys. *Both are useful; both are recommended.*"
