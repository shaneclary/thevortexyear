## 0. Authoring notes
Count winding positions on the torus. The marked positions, the unmarked positions, and what each represents.

## 1. Outcome
Student can count the marked positions on a properly-prepared torus former and explain what each count represents.

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Today we count the marks on the prepared torus former. Each count represents something specific; getting the counts right is part of correctness.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Count 1 — angular column marks.* 24 marks around the major circumference. Includes column 0 (special, marked with a triangle or arrow), columns 1 through 23 (light tick marks).
>
> Total: *24 column marks.* Each is an angular position 15° from its neighbours.
>
> *Count 2 — row dividers.* 6 circles around the minor circumference. The 6 rows are separated by 5 circumferential lines (dividers between adjacent rows) plus the implicit row-0/row-5 wrap around the minor circle.
>
> Equivalently: *5 explicit row dividers* + *6 row positions* = *11 marks total* if we count both. The conventional count: *6 row positions* (the cells), with *5 dividers* as auxiliary.
>
> *Count 3 — antipodal pair markers.* 3 polar pairs: (1, 8), (2, 7), (4, 5). For each pair, two endpoint markers on the surface.
>
> Total: *3 pairs × 2 endpoints = 6 antipodal markers.*
>
> If using threads through the centre: *3 threads* connecting endpoints.
>
> *Count 4 — orientation arrow.* 1 arrow showing winding direction.
>
> *Count 5 — start position triangle.* 1 triangle marking column 0, row 0.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Total essential marks.* 24 + 6 + 6 + 1 + 1 = *38 marks*. (Or about 34 by the conservative count earlier.)
>
> The number isn't critical; the structure is. *Each mark category serves a function*; the count just says how many marks are in each.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Optional decoration counts.*
>
> *Cell labels.* 144 marks. One value per cell.
>
> *Family colour zones.* 144 small coloured zones (one per cell), or 6 colour-bands (one per row), depending on choice.
>
> *Strand entries/exits.* 12 marks per six-strand build (6 strand-starts × 6 strand-ends? or 6 starts + 6 ends = 12). For a single-strand build: 2 marks (start and end).
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What the counts tell you.*
>
> *Tell-thing one.* The torus is *not heavily decorated*. With essential marks only, you have under 40 marks on a 144-cell surface. Decoration density is low; most of the surface is unmarked.
>
> *Tell-thing two.* The marks are *positioned for use*. Column marks are at angular positions where wire turns happen. Row dividers are where strand boundaries fall. Antipode markers are where polar pairs are verified. Each mark has an operational role.
>
> *Tell-thing three.* Marks are *not the structure*. The structure exists whether or not the marks are present. The marks just *display* the structure for human perception. *A perfectly accurate coil could be wound on an unmarked former by an experienced builder*; the marks are aids to perception.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three observations.*
>
> *Observation one.* The mark count is *small enough to add by hand* in 15-30 minutes. No special equipment needed. *Marking is accessible.*
>
> *Observation two.* The mark count *grows* if you add optional decorations. A heavily-decorated coil teaching former might have 200+ marks (cells, colours, strand assignments, etc.). A working former typically stays minimal.
>
> *Observation three.* The marks *can fade* with handling. Re-marking is part of former maintenance. Permanent marker fades less than pencil but is harder to correct.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Plan to spend 30 minutes marking your former when you receive it.* Bring tools: fine-tip permanent marker, ruler with mm marks, protractor for angular precision."
