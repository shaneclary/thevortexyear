## 0. Authoring notes
Apply lens for an axiom-introduction theme means: trace one of the five claims forward through the year and show what it pays for. We pick claim two — *the decimal system is natural law* — because it's the load-bearing premise that makes the next 51 weeks coherent. Walk it forward, link by link, until the student sees the chain. Avoid throwing all five claims at the wall; pick one, do it well.

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Claim two: the decimal system is natural law, not human invention. Why does this load-bear? Walk the chain.
>
> **Link one.** If base 10 is privileged, then the digital-root operation — sum the digits, repeat — is privileged too. It's the natural reduction of a number to its mod-9 residue. Claim two earns claim three: digit-summing reveals stable structure.
>
> **Link two.** The doubling map under digital root produces the cycle 1-2-4-8-7-5. If base 10 weren't privileged — if we worked in base 12 or base 7 — the orbit would be different. Different period, different cycle, different structure. The non-decaying cycle that Marko reads as physically meaningful exists *because* of base 10. Claim four rests on claim two.
>
> **Link three.** The numbers 3, 6, 9 sit outside the cycle precisely because of how the orbit of 2 partitions the units modulo 9 — and 9 is the modulus *because* base 10. In base 8 the trinary structure dissolves. Claim five rests on claim two.
>
> **Link four.** Claim two carries forward into Q2. The Rodin Number Map is a 6×24 grid of digital roots — 6 because the doubling cycle is six long, 24 because of polar-pair and family-group LCMs. Wrap the grid into a torus, and you get the ABHA Torus. Every step is forced by the digit-root structure. Without claim two, the torus geometry isn't natural — it's an arbitrary choice.
>
> **Link five.** Claim two carries into Q3. The Rodin Coil winding follows the doubling helix. The helix exists because the cycle exists. The cycle exists because of digital roots. Digital roots exist as the natural reduction because base 10 is privileged. Knock out claim two and the coil's geometry is no longer derivable — it's just one choice among many.
>
> *[pause — show all five chain-links lit]*
>
> Five consequences from one claim. That's what *load-bearing* means. Most of what you'll learn this year follows if claim two holds. Most of what's contestable in VBM concentrates *in* claim two — because if base 10 is just a human convention, the rest of the structure becomes a curiosity rather than a description. The course presents claim two as Marko's premise. The arithmetic that follows is yours to verify."
