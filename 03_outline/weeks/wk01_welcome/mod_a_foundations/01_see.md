## 0. Authoring notes
First lesson of the year. Sets the *visual* of the journey before any math. Show the whole-year map: 52 weeks as a spiral, four quarters, three modules per week, nine lessons per module. Don't teach VBM yet — orient. By the end of this lesson the student should *see* the shape of the year and feel they can do this.

## 1. Outcome
By the end of these 6 minutes, the student can describe the year-long course visually: 52 weeks, 4 quarters, 3 modules per week, 9 lessons per module.

## 2. Hook (0:00–0:30)
*[On screen: a single static image — the full 52-week spiral as a coloured wheel.]*

> "This is your year. Fifty-two weeks. Four seasons. Three lenses each week. Nine angles each module. By the time you reach the centre of this wheel, you'll see numbers the way Marko Rodin sees them. Let's look at it together."

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)
> "We're starting where every good map starts: with the territory. Before we open a single piece of math, I want you to see the full shape of where we're going. This is lesson 1 of 9 in your first module. You'll do nothing today but watch and look. That's the work."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

*[Animation: the spiral builds outward from the centre. As each ring appears, label the four quarters in turn.]*

> "Look at the wheel. Four quarters, like a year of seasons.
>
> The outer ring — Quarter One — is **Foundations**. Thirteen weeks. We learn the language: digit roots, the doubling circuit, the polar pairs, the families of three. Everything you'll ever need to read Rodin's diagrams, you learn here.
>
> The next ring in — Quarter Two — is **The Geometric Turn**. We rise from flat numbers into three dimensions. The torus appears. The Decoqubit appears. The shapes you've been hearing about become the shapes you can hold.
>
> The third ring — Quarter Three — is **Spiral Back & Engineering**. This is where we revisit the deepest concepts from Quarter One, but now you have the geometry of Quarter Two. The Doubling Circuit you met in Week Four becomes the winding pattern of a Rodin Coil in Week Twenty-Seven. You'll meet it again with new eyes.
>
> The centre ring — Quarter Four — is **Synthesis, Application, Mastery**. Cross-tradition connections. Tesla. Larson. Haramein. Applications to DNA, to propulsion, to spirit. And your capstone — the artefact you'll have built by the end of this year, that you can show anyone."

*[Animation: zoom into a single week. Show three modules splay out, then nine lessons within one module.]*

> "Inside each week, three modules. Module A is Foundations — the lens you're watching right now. Module B is Mathematical — for when you want the proof, the derivation, the rigor. Module C is Practical — for when you want to build, measure, apply.
>
> Inside each module, nine lessons. Six minutes each. Same nine angles every time, in the same order. By the end of this week you'll know all nine. By the end of Week Five you'll feel them as second nature."

## 5. Try it (4:30–5:30)
> "Pause the lesson. Find a piece of paper. Draw a circle. Inside the circle, draw four arcs — your four quarters. That circle is your year. We'll add to it as we go. Don't write anything inside the arcs yet. We're just sketching the shape. Take thirty seconds and come back."

*[Pause card on screen for 30 seconds.]*

> "Welcome back. Look at what you drew. That's the territory. Every week from here on, you'll add a line to that map. Keep it somewhere visible."

## 6. Bridge (5:30–6:00)
> "Next lesson — the second of nine — we count. We're going to look at the numbers of your commitment: how many lessons, how many hours, what your week looks like in real time. Same theme: welcome. New lens: count."

*[Closing card: "Lesson 1 of 9 — See. Next: Count."]*
