## 0. Authoring notes
Lens 3 is *Build*. For Week 1, the build is laughably simple: assemble your year-long study kit. Notebook, pencils, the printed Symbol of Enlightenment, a folder for the build log. The point isn't the kit — it's the act of preparing. Students who set up a deliberate study space stay; students who try to "just watch on the phone in bed" drop out by week 6.

## 1. Outcome
By the end of these 6 minutes, the student has a study kit assembled (or a plan to assemble it within 24 hours): a notebook, two coloured pencils, a printed copy of the Symbol of Enlightenment, and a build-log folder.

## 2. Hook (0:00–0:30)
*[On screen: four objects laid out on a wooden table. A notebook. Two coloured pencils. A printed circle of nine numbers. A simple folder.]*

> "Four things. That's your kit for the next six months. Then we'll add a few more in Quarter Three. Today's lesson: the table you'll work at, and the four things on it."

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)
> "Lesson 3 of 9. We saw the year. We counted it. Now we build the surface where the year happens. The build lens is about *making it physical*. For now, that means assembling the simplest possible kit. Six minutes."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

*[Camera slowly pans across the four items as the host names each one.]*

> "**Item one: a notebook.** Hardcover or softcover, lined or unlined, doesn't matter. What matters is that it's *only* for this course. Don't share it with another project. The notebook is the place your hand learns. You'll draw the Symbol of Enlightenment more times than you can imagine. You'll work the doubling circuit by hand. You'll sketch the torus. The notebook is your second teacher.
>
> **Item two: two coloured pencils.** Two different colours. Could be red and blue. Black and red. Any two. We use one for the doubling direction and one for the halving direction — you'll see why in Week Four. Keep them with the notebook.
>
> **Item three: the Symbol of Enlightenment, printed.** The download is in your course materials — the link is in this lesson's notes. Print it once. Print it large enough to lay your palm on. Pin it where you'll see it during the day. The symbol becomes your peripheral vision over the course of the year. It moves from a curiosity to a familiar friend.
>
> **Item four: a folder labelled 'Build Log'.** Physical or digital — your choice. This is where you keep records. Photos of your hand-drawn diagrams. Notes from Lens 4 — the lessons where you hear from Marko Rodin and Randy Powell. By Quarter Three, when you build a Rodin Coil, the build log fills with measurements and photographs. Start it empty, today."

*[Camera holds on the assembled kit.]*

> "That's the entire kit until Week Twenty-Seven. In Quarter Three, when we build a Rodin Coil, you'll need a few more things — a torus form, magnet wire, a soldering iron, a multimeter. We'll give you a buying list eight weeks before you need it. Don't worry about that today. Today: notebook, pencils, printed Symbol, build-log folder."

## 5. Try it (4:30–5:30)
> "Pause. Make a list right now of what you need to acquire. If you have a notebook on the shelf, write 'notebook ✓'. If you don't, write 'notebook — buy by Friday'. Same for the pencils. Print the Symbol of Enlightenment from this lesson's downloads, or note 'print Symbol — Friday'. Set up the build-log folder — even just a manila folder in a drawer, even just a notes app on your phone called 'Rodin Build Log'. One minute."

*[Pause card on screen for 60 seconds.]*

> "Welcome back. Whatever's on your list as 'buy' or 'print' — do it within twenty-four hours. Don't let this turn into a nice idea you didn't follow through on."

## 6. Bridge (5:30–6:00)
> "Next lesson — Hear from Marko. We've spent three lessons looking at the *course*. Now we'll spend six minutes with the *person*. You'll hear Marko Rodin in his own voice, telling you what this work is for. You don't have to take notes. Just listen."

*[Closing card: "Lesson 3 of 9 — Build. Next: Hear from Marko."]*
