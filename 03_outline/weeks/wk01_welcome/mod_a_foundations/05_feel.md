## 0. Authoring notes
Lens 5 is *Feel* — the contemplative / philosophical register. For Week 1, the question is: *what does it mean to spend a year with numbers?* Don't preach. Don't promise transformation. Just open the question and let the student sit with it. Treat this lesson the way the best meditation app would: sparse, paced, leaves room. Tone borrowed from contemplative practice but not branded as religious. The student's relationship with the spiritual framing is theirs to build — the course doesn't push them.

## 1. Outcome
By the end of these 6 minutes, the student has spent quiet time considering their personal reason for taking this course, and can articulate it in one sentence.

## 2. Hook (0:00–0:30)
*[On screen: a single black field. The Symbol of Enlightenment, very small, in the centre. Nothing moves. No text yet.]*

> *(spoken slowly)* "A year. With numbers. Why?"

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)
> "Lesson 5 of 9. The lens called Feel. Six minutes. Not for new information — for sitting with a question. If you've never used a course this way, that's okay. If contemplative space feels foreign, that's okay too. Stay with me."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30) — *contemplative, sparse, paced*

*[Visual: the Symbol of Enlightenment grows slowly, very slowly, across these 3.5 minutes. Numbers appear one at a time, with long silences between.]*

> *(slow, measured)* "Most things you'll learn this year, you could find in books. The Doubling Circuit is in books. The polar pairs are in books. The diagrams are in books.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> What books can't give you is the year of your attention. Three hours a week, for fifty-two weeks, on the same body of work — that's not a thing you can speed up. It's a thing you can only *do*.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> Marko Rodin says numbers are alive. He doesn't mean it as a metaphor. He means: every number you'll see in this course is doing something. Not symbolising — doing.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> What does it mean to spend a year with something that is doing? You don't study the rain by reading about it. You stand in the rain. The work this year is to stand in something that is happening, and pay close enough attention to notice what it is doing.
>
> *[longer pause — 10 seconds, just the visual]*
>
> Some students come to this work for the math. Some come for the engineering — they want to build a coil, they want to test the claims, they want to measure. Some come for what Marko calls Spirit. Some come because someone they love brought them. Some come because they read about it once at three in the morning on the internet and they couldn't shake it.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> All of those are reasons.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Take a breath. Ask yourself: why are *you* here?
>
> *[long silence — 15 seconds]*"

## 5. Try it (4:30–5:30)
> *(softer)* "In your notebook, write one sentence. The one that begins: 'I'm taking this course because ___.' One sentence. Don't second-guess it. Don't make it sound impressive. Just true. Sixty seconds."

*[Pause card on screen for 60 seconds, with the still Symbol of Enlightenment behind.]*

> "Welcome back. That sentence is going to change across the year. Some weeks it will sharpen. Some weeks it will surprise you. Come back to it. Cross things out. Rewrite. The sentence is alive too."

## 6. Bridge (5:30–6:00)
> *(returning to normal tempo)* "Next lesson — Apply. We've felt our way to the why. The next lens looks at the where. Where does a year of this work actually take people? Lens 6: apply."

*[Closing card: "Lesson 5 of 9 — Feel. Next: Apply."]*
