## 0. Authoring notes
Lens 9 is *Connect* — the synthesis lens. For Week 1, the back-look is the eight lessons just completed; the forward-look is Week 2 (the Symbol of Enlightenment). The lens repeats every week and every week it does the same job: zoom out to the year. By Week 5 the student feels the spiral; the connect lessons are how that feeling gets built. Don't summarise mechanically — narrate the journey.

## 1. Outcome
By the end of these 6 minutes, the student can describe what they've learned this week in one sentence and name what's coming in Week 2.

## 2. Hook (0:00–0:30)
*[On screen: the 52-week spiral wheel returns. Week 1 is highlighted. Week 2 — adjacent — pulses softly.]*

> "We're at the close of your first week. You've already done eight lessons. Let me show you what you've actually built — and where we go from here."

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)
> "Lesson 9 of 9. The lens that closes every module by zooming out: looking back at where we've been, forward to what's next. This is the lens that turns six minutes plus six minutes plus six minutes — into one course."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

*[Visual: the eight previous lessons replayed as a single mosaic — eight thumbnails, one per lens.]*

> "Look at the eight tiles on screen. That's your Module A. Eight lenses, every one of them now done.
>
> *[pointing to each]*
>
> Lens 1 — *See*. You looked at the year. You drew a circle and the four arcs.
>
> Lens 2 — *Count*. You put three hours on your calendar. You committed.
>
> Lens 3 — *Build*. You set up a study kit. Notebook, pencils, printed Symbol, build-log folder.
>
> Lens 4 — *Hear from Marko*. You sat with three and a half minutes of Rodin in his own voice. You wrote down a phrase that stayed with you.
>
> Lens 5 — *Feel*. You answered a quiet question. *Why am I here?* In one sentence.
>
> Lens 6 — *Apply*. You picked which of three year-end artefacts excites you most.
>
> Lens 7 — *Compare*. You sketched the four-column map. Math, physics, meditation, this course.
>
> Lens 8 — *Defend*. You memorised one calibrated sentence to give your sceptical friend.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Eight things, all yours. None of them required prior knowledge of vortex-based mathematics — you could have done all eight without ever having heard the name Marko Rodin before this week. That's deliberate. Week One isn't math. Week One is *the table being set*. By the time we open the math next week, you have a notebook to work in, a calendar that holds three hours, a phrase from Marko, a reason of your own, an artefact you're working toward, a placement on the map, and a defended position. That is what one week of preparation looks like."

*[Visual: zoom out; Week 2 lights up. Show the 52-week spiral with Week 1 done and Week 2 ready.]*

> "Next week — the Symbol of Enlightenment. The first piece of math. Three modules, twenty-seven lessons. Module A's first lesson next week is *See* — and what you'll see is the symbol you printed and pinned this week. We won't waste a minute. The notebook you set up gets used Monday. The pencils get used Monday. By the end of next week the Symbol of Enlightenment is no longer something on your wall — it's something in your hand."

## 5. Try it (4:30–5:30)
> "Pause. Open your notebook. On a fresh page, write the date and the title 'Week One — done'. Below it, write three things — any three — from the eight lessons that you want to remember. Just three. Sixty seconds."

*[Pause card on screen for 60 seconds.]*

> "Welcome back. That fresh page is the start of your year. Every Lens 9, every week, you'll come back to write the closing entry. By week fifty-two, that notebook is fifty-two short reflections long. It's your record of who you became across this year."

## 6. Bridge (5:30–6:00)
> "Module A is done. Next session — Module B. Same theme — Welcome — Numbers Are Living — through the *mathematical* lens. We open with first axioms: what does it mean for a number to be 'alive'? See you there."

*[Closing card: "Lesson 9 of 9 — Connect. Module A complete. Next: Module B, Lesson 1 — See."]*
