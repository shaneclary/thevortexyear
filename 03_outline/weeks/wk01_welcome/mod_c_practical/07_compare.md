## 0. Authoring notes
The honest comparison: passive watcher vs. active builder. Both are real student paths. The course supports either. But the outcomes are different and the student should choose with eyes open. No moralising. No shaming. Just the facts about what each path produces by Week 52.

## 1. Outcome
By the end of these 6 minutes, the student can describe the difference between watcher and builder student paths in concrete terms — what each produces, what each understands, what each can do.

## 2. Hook (0:00–0:30)
*[On screen: two columns. Left header: *Watcher*. Right header: *Builder*. Both empty.]*

> "Two paths through this course. Both real. Both honest. Different outcomes. Today: the comparison without judgement."

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)
> "Lens 7 of 9. We have three graduate stories from the previous lesson — all builders. Today the comparison: what does the watcher path look like at Week 52? What does the builder path look like? Six minutes."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Start with the watcher. The watcher follows the course on the lesson schedule — three modules a week, twenty-seven lessons. They watch the videos. They read the materials. They take some notes. They don't draw. They don't compute. They don't wind a coil. By Week 52 the watcher knows what VBM is. They can describe the Doubling Circuit. They can explain the polar pairs. They have a coherent picture of the field. If you ask them about the Rodin Coil, they can talk about it for ten minutes.
>
> Now the builder. The builder follows the same course on the same schedule. They watch the videos. They read the materials. They draw the Symbol every week, by hand, and date it. They work the practice exercises. They wind a coil in Week 33. They measure it. They keep a build log. By Week 52 the builder also knows what VBM is — and they have a notebook full of diagrams, two coils on a shelf, a build log, and a capstone document.
>
> *[pause — show the two columns now filled]*
>
> Look at the comparison. The watcher's understanding is real. They aren't a fraud. If you tested them on the material, they'd answer correctly. But there's a quality of knowledge they don't have access to. They can't draw the Symbol from memory at speed. They've never felt the doubling circuit close on a piece of paper. They've never pulled a measurement from a coil and compared it to a prediction. Their knowledge is shape-correct and texture-thin.
>
> The builder's understanding is the same shape, with texture. The builder has felt the circuit close. The builder has held a paper torus that they folded themselves. The builder has been wrong on a measurement and figured out why. That's a different kind of knowing.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Two things to note. First: the course doesn't penalise the watcher. You can choose to watch this year and call it a success. Many students do. The course is rigorous as a watching experience. Second: the watcher path produces no portfolio. There's no notebook, no coil, no capstone. The knowledge is real but invisible. If you want to point to evidence at Week 52 — for yourself, for the org, for an employer — the builder path is the one that produces it.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Most students start as watchers and become builders sometime in Quarter Two. Some start as builders and stay builders. A few stay watchers all year and report afterwards that they wish they'd built. Almost no one regrets having built."
