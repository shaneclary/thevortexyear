## 0. Authoring notes
Mod A Lesson 3 was kit assembly. Mod B Lesson 3 was transcribing the five claims. Mod C Lesson 3 is the build log structure — the empty container that catches everything from now until Week 52. Imperative voice throughout. The act of structuring the log is the lesson; the explanation lives in the doing.

## 1. Outcome
By the end of these 6 minutes, the student has set up a build log with five labelled sections, ready to receive content from Week 2 onwards.

## 2. Hook (0:00–0:30)
*[On screen: a folder, open and empty, lying flat. A pen rests beside it. No headers yet.]*

> "An empty folder. Five labels. That's the lesson. Open your folder."

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)
> "Lens 3 of 9. We've named the artefacts and the milestones. Now we build the container. The build log is what holds your year. Today we set it up."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

*[Camera close on a hand writing folder labels and dividers on plain card stock.]*

> "Open your build-log folder. The one you set up in Module A Lesson 3. If it's a physical folder, take out a piece of card stock or five sheets of plain paper. If it's a digital folder, open it and prepare to make five subfolders. The structure works the same either way.
>
> Section one. Label it *Reflections*. This holds your closing-reflection entries from Lens 9 of every module. Three entries per week, fifty-two weeks, one-hundred-fifty-six entries by Week 52. They are short — three sentences each — but together they form a journal of how your understanding evolves.
>
> *[pause for the student to make the first label]*
>
> Section two. Label it *Diagrams*. This holds photographs of the hand-drawn diagrams from your notebook — one photograph per week, taken at the end of the week, dated. By Week 52 you have fifty-two snapshots of how your ability to draw VBM diagrams developed. The progression is its own teaching.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Section three. Label it *Calculations*. This holds the worked examples from Lens 2 lessons — your hand-computed digit-roots, your traced doubling circuits, your reductions. Most go directly in the notebook; the build log holds photographs of the most useful pages, indexed.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Section four. Label it *Builds*. Empty for now. By Week 33 it holds the bill of materials for your first coil, the winding-session photographs, the measurement table. By Week 35 it holds the second build's documentation. By Week 39 it holds bench-test reports. The Builds section grows fast in Quarter Three.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Section five. Label it *Capstone*. Empty until Week 50. At Week 50 the proposal goes here. At Week 51 the draft goes here. At Week 52 the final bound document goes here.
>
> *[pause — show the five labelled sections, complete and empty]*
>
> Five sections. The container of your year is now structured. Every other Lens 3 from here on adds something to it. Don't add to it today — today is just the structure. Empty file folders, ready to fill."

## 5. Try it (4:30–5:30)
> "Pause. Look at your folder. Are the five labels written cleanly? Is the structure findable in three seconds? If not, fix it now. The container will outlast the contents — make it one you'll want to open. Sixty seconds."

*[Pause card on screen for 60 seconds.]*

> "Welcome back. The folder waits. Starting next week, it begins to fill."
