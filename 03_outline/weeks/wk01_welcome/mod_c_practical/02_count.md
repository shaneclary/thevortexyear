## 0. Authoring notes
The four milestones — weeks 13, 26, 39, 52 — and what each demands. Concrete deliverables, no fluff. Lens 2 leans numerical-precise. Each milestone gets one paragraph: what's due, what shape it takes, what the student should already have on the date.

## 1. Outcome
By the end of these 6 minutes, the student can name the four milestones of the year and what's due at each.

## 2. Hook (0:00–0:30)
*[On screen: a horizontal timeline. Weeks 1 → 52. Four marker points: 13, 26, 39, 52.]*

> "Four checkpoints across the year. Week 13. Week 26. Week 39. Week 52. Each has a deliverable. Today: what each one demands."

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)
> "Lens 2 of 9. Last lesson we showed the three artefacts at Week 52. Today we count the four checkpoints between now and then. Each milestone earns a notebook entry and a small portfolio update."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "**Week 13 — end of Quarter One.** Foundations done. By Week 13 your notebook holds twelve hand-drawn Symbols, one per week, each annotated with that week's concept. The Doubling Circuit is traced. The polar pairs are coloured. The family number groups are marked. You have the five claims of VBM written by hand on a single page near the front of the notebook. You have one closing-reflection entry per module — that's thirty-nine reflections by Week 13. Total notebook pages: roughly forty. Total time invested: thirty-five hours.
>
> **Week 26 — end of Quarter Two.** Geometric turn done. By Week 26 your notebook adds thirteen more weeks of drawings, this time three-dimensional. A flat 6×24 number map, hand-drawn. A paper torus you folded yourself, with the digit-roots written across its surface. A Decoqubit projection, sketched. The five claims page now has a sixth annotation — your own observation about how the geometry rests on them. The portfolio still holds no physical coil — but the build log is initialised, with the bill-of-materials list for the canonical first build of Week 33 already placed at the front. Total notebook pages: roughly eighty. Total time invested: seventy hours.
>
> **Week 39 — end of Quarter Three.** Engineering done. By Week 39 the table holds your first physical artefact: a single-strand Rodin Coil, wound on a torus former, with leads exposed and labelled. The build log is fifteen to twenty pages: the bill of materials, photographs from the winding session, the bench-test measurements (DC resistance, inductance, self-resonant frequency, plus whatever else you chose to capture). A second coil — multi-strand or phased — sits beside the first if you took the optional second-build path. The notebook now totals roughly one-hundred-twenty pages. Total time invested: a hundred and ten hours.
>
> **Week 52 — end of year.** Capstone deposited. By Week 52 the bound capstone document sits next to the notebook and the coils. Twenty to fifty pages. Photographs, diagrams, measurements, reflection. Submitted to the Rodin and Powell organisation's capstone portfolio — your name in the table of contents. The notebook holds fifty-two closing-reflection entries, one per week, dated. Total time invested: a hundred and forty hours. The year is done.
>
> *[pause — show the four milestones lit on the timeline]*
>
> Four checkpoints. Each one is photographable. Each one is small enough to hit without panic. The course schedules a synthesis week at each quarter boundary so you can pause, take stock, and adjust. If you're behind by Week 13, you fix it before Week 26. The course is forgiving of pace; it's unforgiving of skipping."
