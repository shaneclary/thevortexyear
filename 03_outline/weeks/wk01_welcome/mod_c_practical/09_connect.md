## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

*[Visual: a quiet animation of Module C's eight lessons, then the wider view of Week One complete.]*

> "Module C opened by showing you what's on the table at Week 52 — the three artefacts of a finished portfolio. A notebook, two-hundred pages of dated diagrams and reflections. A Rodin Coil wound on a torus former, with a build log full of measurements. A bound capstone document, twenty to fifty pages, deposited in the org's student portfolio. The three artefacts are the shape of where the year lands.
>
> From there, you counted the four checkpoints between now and then. Week 13 ends Foundations. Week 26 ends the Geometric Turn. Week 39 ends Engineering. Week 52 deposits the capstone. Each milestone is photographable and small enough to hit without panic.
>
> Then you set up the build log. Five sections, labelled and empty. Reflections. Diagrams. Calculations. Builds. Capstone. The container of your year is now structured, ready to fill from Week 2 forward.
>
> Then you heard Randy Powell speak on the practical implications. Three and a half minutes of him in his own voice. You added a *Randy* heading to your collected-quotes page and wrote the line that landed for you. Two voices on one page now — Marko and Randy.
>
> Then a contemplative beat. *Learn or do?* The two modes of taking this course. You wrote one sentence about which mode you're currently in. The course supports either path; you should know which you've picked.
>
> Then three graduate stories. The antenna replicator, the teacher, the integrator. Three different capstones from past students, all real, all in the org's portfolio. Each one shows what *doing* this work for a year actually produced.
>
> Then the honest comparison. Watcher and builder paths laid side by side. Both real. Both supported. Different outcomes. The watcher knows; the builder knows-with-texture and produces evidence.
>
> Then the calibrated answer to the technical-confidence objection. Twenty-eight words about how the build curriculum scaffolds across thirty-two weeks. The objection is reasonable. The answer is specific.
>

The five claims of VBM are written on a page near the front of your notebook. The build log has its five sections labelled. You have heard Marko twice and Randy once. You have written three closing reflections, one per module. The year has begun.
