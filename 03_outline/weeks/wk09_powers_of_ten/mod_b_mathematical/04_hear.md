## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

**[ORG-CLIP]**

> *"The decimal system is not a human invention. I have said this many times and I will say it again. We did not choose ten because it was convenient. We chose ten because the structure of reality has ten in it. Look at the human body — ten fingers, ten toes, two arms with five digits each. Look at the structure of the chemical elements — the periodic table organises by tens, with the noble gases marking the closures. Look at the structure of the integers themselves — modulo nine gives you the cleanest periodic structure of any modulus. Base ten is *found*, not chosen. The scale invariance under digital-root reduction is the *consequence* of base ten's privileged position. If base ten were arbitrary, scale invariance would be too. Because base ten is natural law, scale invariance is the *fingerprint* of that natural law. The fingerprint is what you see when the law shows itself."*
> — Marko Rodin, paraphrased. **Source:** P1-1, P1-3.
