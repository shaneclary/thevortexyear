## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Q2 (Wk 16) wraps the 6×24 Rodin Number Map onto a torus. The wrap requires the grid to *close cleanly* — the right edge of the grid identifies with the left edge, the top with the bottom.
>
> The horizontal closure depends on scale invariance.
>
> Here's why. The grid has 24 columns. The columns are indexed by powers of 10 (effectively): *c = 0, 1, 2, ..., 23*. After 24 columns, the wrap identifies column 24 with column 0.
>
> If the columns weren't *constant under digital-root reduction*, the wrap would fail. Column 24 would have different cells from column 0, and identifying them would create discontinuities.
>
> Because scale invariance holds — *10ᶜ ≡ 1 (mod 9)* for all *c* — every column has the same content. So *column 24 equals column 0* automatically (and column 25 equals column 1, etc.).
>
> The wrap closes. The torus exists.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Without scale invariance, you couldn't build the ABHA Torus from the Rodin Number Map. The wrap would produce a *cone* (with a cap) or an *open cylinder* (with two distinct edges) instead of a closed torus.
>
> Scale invariance is *what makes the wrap into a torus* rather than some other surface.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> So the scale invariance you've been studying is structurally essential, not decorative. Q2's geometric work depends on it. Q3's coil engineering depends on Q2's torus, which depends on the wrap, which depends on scale invariance.
>
> The *whole curriculum* from Wk 9 onward rests on the proof you saw yesterday: *10ᵏ ≡ 1 (mod 9)*. One congruence, much consequence.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. When you see the Rodin Number Map next quarter — or any of the geometric structures that follow — recognise that the *column-constant property* is what allows the geometric structures to close cleanly. The arithmetic and the geometry are tied together by this one congruence.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One observation worth carrying. *Marko's stronger claim* — that base 10 is privileged — would mean that the ABHA Torus is the *natural* geometric closure of arithmetic, not an arbitrary construction. The standard mathematical view holds that any base could produce its analogous closure structure. Both views agree the wrap-to-torus mechanism is real for base 10; they disagree on whether other bases would produce equally significant structures."
