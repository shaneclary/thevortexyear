## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The objection. *The scale invariance under digital-root reduction is a property of base 10. In binary, the analogous operation would reduce numbers mod 1 (since 2 - 1 = 1), which is trivial. In base 12, mod 11 — different invariance, different scale-preservation. Marko's scale invariance is base-10-dependent, just like the doubling cycle.*
>
> The objection is technically correct. The scale invariance under digital-root reduction is a property of *base-b arithmetic where the modulus is b−1*. In base 10, modulus is 9; powers of 10 reduce to 1; scale invariance holds.
>
> In base 12, modulus is 11; powers of 12 reduce to 1; analogous scale invariance under casting-out-elevens holds.
>
> So the *abstract scale invariance* is base-independent — every base has its analogous property. What's *specific to base 10* is the *modulus 9*, with its specific composite structure (9 = 3²) and specific orbit structure under doubling.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> So the calibrated answer to *scale invariance only works because base 10?*:
>
> *Scale invariance under digital-root reduction holds in any base — every base b has it under casting-out-(b−1). What's base-10-specific is the modulus 9 and its rich composite structure, which produces the specific Doubling Circuit, Polar Pairs, Family Groups, and 3-6-9 Pulse we study. Scale invariance is general; the specific patterns above it are base-10-specific.*
>
> Forty-eight words.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One nuance. Marko's stronger claim — that base 10 is *non-arbitrary* — is what makes the base-10-specific patterns interesting. Even granting scale invariance is base-independent, the *specific patterns* that scale-invariantly survive (Doubling Circuit, Polar Pairs, etc.) are base-10. If base 10 is privileged in some way that goes beyond convention, those specific patterns *are* fundamental. If base 10 is purely conventional, the patterns are just *one example* of a general structure.
>
> The course doesn't settle this question. It teaches the math (which is sound regardless) and presents Marko's framework (which is contested) and lets you decide.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. When you describe scale invariance to someone, lead with *digital-root operation is scale-invariant in any base under analogous casting-out*. Then, if relevant, mention that *the specific patterns we study are base-10's version of this structure*. The two-step framing distinguishes general from specific."
