> *"Size does not change the pattern. This is one of the most beautiful things about the Symbol. A grain of salt has a chemistry. A galaxy has a chemistry. Both are made of atoms in specific configurations. The Symbol's patterns — the doubling circuit, the polar pairs, the families — work the same on the count of atoms in a salt grain and the count of stars in a galaxy. The math is the same. The structure is the same. The scale is irrelevant. When physicists describe the universe, they often need different equations at different scales — quantum mechanics for the small, general relativity for the large, classical mechanics for the in-between. The Symbol is *unified across scales*. The same vertices hold the same structures whether you're counting things you can see with your eyes or things that fill the night sky. I find this scale-freeness to be evidence — not proof — that the Symbol describes something fundamental. Whatever it describes, it does not care about size."*
