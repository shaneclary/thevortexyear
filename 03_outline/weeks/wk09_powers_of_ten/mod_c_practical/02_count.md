## 0. Authoring notes
Resonance ratios are preserved across coil scales. Specifically, the ratio of resonant frequencies between strands or harmonics depends on the *winding pattern*, not on the absolute size.

## 1. Outcome
Student can compute expected resonance ratios for Rodin Coils at any scale.

## 2. Hook (0:00–0:30)
*[Three coils with their measured resonant frequencies; ratios identical across scales.]*

> "Frequency changes with size. Ratios stay the same. Six minutes."

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)
> "Lens 2 of 9. Resonance at scale."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "When you scale a Rodin Coil up or down, the *absolute* resonant frequency changes — smaller coils ring at higher frequencies. But the *ratios* between resonances are preserved.
>
> Specifically: a Rodin Coil has a base resonant frequency *f₀* and harmonics at specific multiples — typically *2f₀*, *4f₀*, *6f₀* (the trigger frequency), and so on.
>
> Scale the coil down by a factor of *k* (linear dimension reduction). The base frequency rises by approximately *k* (or *k²*, depending on which dimension dominates). All the harmonics rise by the same factor. The *ratios* between them stay constant.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Concrete example.
>
> *Medium coil (200 mm major).* Base resonance *f₀ ≈ 30 MHz*. Harmonics at *60 MHz*, *120 MHz*, *180 MHz* (the 6f trigger frequency). *(Numbers approximate; actual values depend on specific winding and core.)*
>
> *Small coil (100 mm major, half-scale).* Base resonance *f₀ ≈ 60 MHz*. Harmonics at *120 MHz*, *240 MHz*, *360 MHz*. *Same ratios.*
>
> *Large coil (400 mm major, double-scale).* Base resonance *f₀ ≈ 15 MHz*. Harmonics at *30 MHz*, *60 MHz*, *90 MHz*. *Same ratios.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Why? Because the coil's *electrical structure* — number of turns, winding pattern, relative dimensions — determines the *frequency-ratio pattern*. Absolute frequency depends on absolute dimensions. Scale changes absolute dimensions but not the ratio pattern.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical implication for testing. If you're investigating frequency-ratio patterns predicted by the math (e.g., the 6f trigger frequency), you can do the test at *any* scale. Build a small cheap coil; measure ratios; the predicted patterns appear at scaled frequencies but with identical ratios.
>
> This is *scale-invariant testing*. Useful for quick experiments and for verifying predictions before committing to a full-scale build.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Build log entry. *Resonance ratios for Wk 33 build: f₀ : 2f₀ : 4f₀ : 6f₀ = 1 : 2 : 4 : 6. (Independent of scale.)*"
