## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

**[ORG-CLIP]**

> *"I have built Rodin Coils at sizes from a few centimetres to half a metre. The behaviours are recognisable across all of them. The smaller coils ring at higher frequencies; the larger ones ring at lower; the ratios stay the same. What this means for builders is that you can do useful experimental work at small scales — verify the cycle structure, test the harmonic ratios, check your measurement protocols — before you commit to a full-scale build. I recommend new builders start small. A 4-inch coil costs less than a meal at a restaurant. You can build it in a Saturday. You can measure it on a basic bench. You can publish your results. If something interesting shows up, scale up. If nothing does, you've spent thirty dollars and a day. The math is the same at every scale; the cost of being wrong is much lower at small scales. Build small. Build often. Then build big when the small builds tell you something worth following up."*
> — Randy Powell, paraphrased. **Source:** P1-7, P1-13.
