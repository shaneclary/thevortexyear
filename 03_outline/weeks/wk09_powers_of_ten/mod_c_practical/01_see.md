## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Imagine three Rodin Coils:
>
> *Small coil.* 4-inch (≈100 mm) major diameter. Pocket-sized. Wound on a small 3D-printed former. Total wire: ≈3 metres.
>
> *Medium coil.* 8-inch (≈200 mm) major diameter. Standard reference size. Wound on a wooden former. Total wire: ≈6 metres.
>
> *Large coil.* 16-inch (≈400 mm) major diameter. Tabletop demo size. Wound on a fabricated metal former. Total wire: ≈12 metres.
>
> *[pause — show the three side by side]*
>
> All three follow the *same winding pattern* — the Doubling Circuit on a torus. The 24-position helix is identical in geometry; only the *scale* differs.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What's the same across all three:
>
> *Number of turns.* All three have 24 winding positions per traversal, 4 traversals total, 96 turns.
>
> *Geometry.* All three are toroidal coils with the cycle-following helix.
>
> *Mathematical structure.* Same Doubling Circuit, same Polar Pairs, same Family Groups, same trigger axis at 3-6-9.
>
> *What's different across all three:*
>
> *Wire length.* Proportional to size — small uses 3 m, medium 6 m, large 12 m.
>
> *Inductance.* Scales roughly with the form's volume.
>
> *Resonant frequency.* Scales inversely with size — smaller coils ring at higher frequencies.
>
> *Build time.* Roughly proportional to wire length — small builds in 4 hours, large in 8.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> The principle: *the math is scale-invariant; the physical parameters scale with size*.
>
> A builder can choose any scale and the cycle structure is preserved. The choice depends on practical considerations: budget, available tools, intended use. The math doesn't care.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Recommendation. *First build: small or medium*. Smaller coils are cheaper, faster, and forgiving of mistakes. Once you've built one successfully, you can scale up if you want. Builders who try to start at large scale often waste materials on first-build errors.
>
> The Wk-33 canonical first build is medium-scale (around 100-mm major / 25-mm minor diameter). Optimised for first-time success."
