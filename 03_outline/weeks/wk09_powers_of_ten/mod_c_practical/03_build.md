## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Based on your resources and intent, choose a scale for the Wk-33 first build. Three options.
>
> *Small (≈100 mm major / 25 mm minor diameter).*
>
> Cost: ≈$30. Time: ≈6 hours. Materials: 3D-printed PETG former, 4 m AWG-22 magnet wire, basic terminations.
>
> Pros: cheapest, quickest, forgives mistakes. Most students start here.
>
> Cons: smaller resonant frequencies are higher (less audible without amplification); inductance is lower (harder to measure with cheap multimeters); physical handling is finickier (small turns require care).
>
> Recommendation: most first-time builders. The Wk-33 canonical specification is this size.
>
> *Medium (≈200 mm major / 50 mm minor diameter).*
>
> Cost: ≈$80. Time: ≈10 hours. Materials: wooden or larger 3D-printed former, 8 m wire, slightly heavier gauge.
>
> Pros: larger inductance is easier to measure; lower resonant frequencies are audibly testable; physically robust.
>
> Cons: more wire, more time, more cost.
>
> Recommendation: for builders with bench equipment they want to use; for second builds.
>
> *Large (≈400 mm major / 100 mm minor diameter).*
>
> Cost: ≈$200. Time: ≈20 hours. Materials: machined or fabricated metal former, 16 m wire, heavier gauge for current capacity.
>
> Pros: substantial visual presence; high inductance; suitable for power-electronics experiments.
>
> Cons: significantly more expensive; long winding sessions; mistakes cost more.
>
> Recommendation: for advanced builders or research applications.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Decision tree.
>
> *First-time builder, casual interest.* Small. $30 buys you a coil and a build log entry. If you abandon the project later, you've not spent much.
>
> *First-time builder, serious interest.* Medium. The extra cost and time pay back in measurement quality and physical robustness. Most committed students choose this.
>
> *Returning builder, second coil.* Medium or Large depending on what you tested first.
>
> *Researcher with specific question.* Large or specialised scale matched to the experiment.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Add your choice to the build log under *Wk 33 spec*. Note the major and minor diameters, the wire length, and the cost. The decision is now committed."
