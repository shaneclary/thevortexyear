## 4. Body (1:00–4:30) — *contemplative*

> *(slow)* "Wind a 4-inch coil. Twenty-four turns per traversal. Four traversals. Ninety-six turns total.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Wind a 16-inch coil. Twenty-four turns per traversal. Four traversals. Ninety-six turns total.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> The hand does the same gesture in both. The fingers trace the same cycle. The number of moves is the same. The *pattern* is identical.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What's different is *physical*. The 16-inch coil takes longer to wind because the wire has more distance to cover per turn. The hands move further per turn. The body works harder.
>
> But the *pattern of the work* — the sequence of moves, the cycle of vertices, the verification milestones — is the same.
>
> *[long pause — 12 seconds]*
>
> A musician plays the same scale on a piccolo and on a contrabassoon. The notes are the same; the breath is different; the body relates differently to the instrument. The *music* is recognisable across the size difference.
>
> A Rodin Coil at 4 inches and at 16 inches has the same relationship. The math is the same. The body's relationship to the work differs.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What this *feels* like, contemplatively. The hands learn the *pattern* once and apply it across sizes. The pattern lives in the hands, not in the specific physical scale. Once you've wound a few coils at any size, you can wind one at any other size with minor adjustments.
>
> Marko's framing — *the math is scale-invariant* — translates into *the hand-knowledge is scale-invariant*. Practitioner skill transfers across sizes.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> Sit with the experience of skill that doesn't care about size. The pattern lives in the gesture; the gesture works at any scale.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> When you wind your first coil in Wk 33, your hand learns the pattern. When you wind your second at a different size — if you do — the pattern is already there. The size is the new thing; the pattern stays.
>
> The math's scale invariance is mirrored by the hand's scale invariance. One pattern. Many sizes. The same dance."
