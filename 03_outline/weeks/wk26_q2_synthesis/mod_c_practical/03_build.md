## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Lens 2 counted Q3 readiness; Item 7 (decorated coil former) is the most build-specific. Today: *build it*. *Polished; decorated; Q3-ready.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What a coil former is.*
>
> A *coil former* is a *toroidal physical structure* around which wire is wound to make a coil. *It defines the coil's shape — major radius, minor radius, cross-section.* The Rodin Coil specifically uses a torus-shaped former with the doubling-cycle helix as winding guide.
>
> *Q3 will wind wire around your former*. Today: build the former and decorate it.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Materials.*
>
> *Material option A — paper-mache torus.* Inexpensive; build from scratch; takes a day or so to dry between layers. Result: lightweight; surface accepts marker decoration; suitable for low-current demos.
>
> *Material option B — pre-made wooden torus former.* Available from VBM-tradition suppliers; ready immediately. Result: heavier; durable; surface accepts paint decoration.
>
> *Material option C — 3D-printed torus former.* Available from VBM-tradition suppliers or printable from open files. Result: precise dimensions; durable; surface accepts marker or paint.
>
> *Material option D — metal-pipe-bent torus former.* For higher-current applications. Result: durable; functional for serious engineering tests.
>
> **`[ORG-INPUT]`** for foundation-recommended specifications and supplier referrals.
>
> *Most beginners use option A or B.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Decoration scheme.*
>
> *Decoration 1 — mark the doubling cycle.* Around the torus's major circumference, mark positions for the doubling cycle. *9 positions evenly spaced (or 12 if including 3-6-9).* Mark 1, 2, 4, 8, 7, 5, with the helix winding from 1 to 5 across the torus.
>
> *Decoration 2 — mark the 3-6-9 axis.* Mark 3, 6, 9 positions. Place these on a vertical-axis line through the torus.
>
> *Decoration 3 — mark winding starting points.* The Rodin Coil winding starts at specific points. Mark these clearly.
>
> *Decoration 4 — colour-code per polar-pair group.* Use one colour for (1, 8); another for (2, 7); another for (4, 5); another for the 3-6-9 axis. *Visual coding makes structure obvious.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Build steps (paper-mache option).*
>
> *Step 1.* Make a torus form. Inflate a balloon to a doughnut shape (a balloon-animal helper) or use a styrofoam torus from craft store as armature.
>
> *Step 2.* Apply 3-4 layers of paper-mache (paper strips dipped in flour-water glue), drying between layers. *Result: rigid toroidal shell.*
>
> *Step 3.* Sand smooth. Prime with white paint if desired.
>
> *Step 4.* Mark and decorate per scheme above. *Use permanent markers, paint, or labels.*
>
> *Step 5.* Seal with clear varnish for durability.
>
> *Total time: 3-5 days (mostly drying).* Plan accordingly.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three observations on the build.*
>
> *Observation one — physical artefact.* *Hand-built physical artefacts engage spatial cognition*. The torus former in your workspace *teaches the Torus shape* in ways diagrams can't. *Q3 builds on this spatial cognition.*
>
> *Observation two — decoration matters.* *The doubling-cycle markings, polar-pair colours, and 3-6-9 axis annotations* turn the former into *a teaching artefact*. *The former isn't just a coil-shape former; it's a visualisation of the framework's geometry.*
>
> *Observation three — Q3-prep complete with this build.* Once the decorated former is in your workspace, *you have the most Q3-specific item ready*. *Lens 3's build closes the most prominent Q3-readiness gap.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *If you don't build today.*
>
> *That's fine.* The build can happen this week or early in Q3. *The framework doesn't gatekeep on artefact-presence.* *But the artefact will help; build when you can.*
>
> *Some practitioners outsource to VBM-tradition suppliers* (option B or C). *Saves the build time; standard practice.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Build the polished decorated torus former.* Paper-mache or pre-made; mark doubling cycle, polar pairs, 3-6-9 axis; colour-code; varnish. *Q3-prerequisite; teaches Torus shape spatially; ~3-5 days build time.*"
