## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Lens 3 built the decorated former. Today: *apply Q2 to a small mini-build* — a side project that puts Q2 content to work in a specific concrete object.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Why a mini-build.*
>
> *Reason 1 — practice integration.* *Q2's content is many objects (Symbol, Map, Torus, Axis).* *Mini-builds force integration: which objects does this build use?*
>
> *Reason 2 — Q3-prep.* *Q3 will be larger builds (Rodin Coil and beyond).* *Mini-builds rehearse the integration of mathematical content with physical construction* at smaller scale.
>
> *Reason 3 — fun.* *Side projects are pleasant.* *Sustained engagement benefits from occasional smaller builds for variety.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three mini-build options.*
>
> *Option 1 — 9-spoked wheel.* Build a wheel with 9 spokes evenly spaced. *Mark each spoke with a digit 1-9.* *Apply: the polar-pair antipodality (Wk 15 Map of Numbers); the 3-6-9 axis (vertical through 9 and 3+6); the doubling cycle (rotational order).*
>
> *Materials.* Cardboard or wood; 9 dowels; markers. *Time: 1-2 hours.*
>
> *Apply observations.* The wheel makes *polar pair antipodality visible physically*: opposite spokes are 1-8, 2-7, 4-5. *Spinning the wheel reveals the doubling-cycle order in motion.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Option 2 — 3-6-9 axis pendulum.* Build a pendulum with three weights at heights 3, 6, 9 (in any unit). *Apply: the 3-6-9 axis (Wks 19-20); axis-of-polarity dynamics; resonance between heights.*
>
> *Materials.* String, three weights, support. *Time: 30 min - 1 hour.*
>
> *Apply observations.* *The pendulum's motion under various perturbations reveals 3-6-9 dynamics.* *Especially: the 3-and-6 weights tend to oscillate against the 9 weight*; *axis-of-polarity comes alive in motion.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Option 3 — sphere with polar antipodes.* Decorate a sphere (foam, wood, paper-mache) with the digits 1-9 placed antipodally per polar-pair convention. *Apply: polar pairs visualised in 3D; Mobius-style reflections; the Map of Numbers extended to a sphere instead of a circle.*
>
> *Materials.* Foam ball; markers. *Time: 30-60 min.*
>
> *Apply observations.* *On a sphere, polar pairs at antipodal points look strikingly meaningful.* *Spatial cognition reinforces the antipodal property.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three observations on mini-build practice.*
>
> *Observation one — small builds teach big lessons.* *A 9-spoked wheel with markers teaches polar-pair antipodality more vividly than any diagram*. *Embodiment helps.*
>
> *Observation two — variety matters.* *Different mini-builds expose different framework content*. *Wheel teaches polar pairs and cycle; pendulum teaches axis dynamics; sphere teaches 3D antipodality.* *Multiple builds give multiple perspectives.*
>
> *Observation three — Q3-prep through small steps.* *Q3 will require integrating Q1+Q2 content with physical construction*. *Mini-builds at this scale prepare for the integration; reduces Q3 startup friction.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Pick one option.*
>
> *Option 1 (wheel)* — recommended for spatial visualisation; moderate time investment.
>
> *Option 2 (pendulum)* — recommended for dynamic content; lighter time investment.
>
> *Option 3 (sphere)* — recommended for 3D extension of polar-pair concepts; light time investment.
>
> *Or pick your own*. *Any small build that uses Q2 content qualifies.* *Constraints: you understand the framework content the build uses; the build is doable in your workspace.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Practical note.*
>
> *Mini-builds are optional Q3-prep, not Q3-prerequisite.* If time is tight, *finishing the decorated former (Lens 3)* takes precedence. *Mini-builds are bonus practice for practitioners with capacity.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Pick a mini-build that uses Q2 content.* Wheel, pendulum, or sphere; or your own choice. *Build at small scale; rehearse Q1+Q2 integration; prepare for Q3.* Optional but valuable."
