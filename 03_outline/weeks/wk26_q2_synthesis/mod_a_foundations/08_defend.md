## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The challenge. *A friend asks: 'You've been studying Vortex Mathematics for six months. What is it? Why are you spending time on it? Should I take it seriously?'*
>
> *The friend is skeptical, possibly dismissive, possibly genuinely curious. Standard situation.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *The honest defence — about three minutes spoken.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Open with what it is.*
>
> *'Vortex-Based Mathematics, or VBM, is a framework developed by Marko Rodin starting in his teens. It uses modular arithmetic mod 9 — that is, every multi-digit number is reduced to a single digit by adding its digits, repeatedly, until you get one digit. Out of this simple operation come patterns, geometric structures, and a coherent framework that I've been studying for the first half of the year.'*
>
> About 45 seconds.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Acknowledge what's contested.*
>
> *'The framework has parts that are mainstream mathematics — modular arithmetic, group theory, toroidal geometry. It also has parts that are speculative — claims about consciousness, cosmology, and engineering applications that haven't been rigorously verified. I engage at the layer where claims have appropriate epistemic support: the math I treat as math; the engineering I treat as testable; the metaphysics I treat as Marko's interpretation.'*
>
> About 45 seconds.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *State what you've learned.*
>
> *'In Q1, I learned modular arithmetic mod 9 deeply — the doubling cycle (1-2-4-8-7-5), polar pairs (numbers that sum to 9), family groups. I built the Symbol of Enlightenment from scratch. In Q2, I extended into geometry: the ABHA Torus (the framework's central toroidal object), helical winding patterns, the 3-6-9 axis structure, deeper objects like the Decoqubit and the Holographic Hypersphere. The framework has Bahá'í-tradition biographical roots; the math is mainstream-defendable.'*
>
> About 60 seconds.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *State what comes next.*
>
> *'The second half is engineering and integration. Q3 covers the Rodin Coil — a specific toroidal coil structure based on the doubling-cycle pattern. Engineering work has measurement protocols and benchmarks; that's what makes engineering claims testable. Q4 will integrate the whole year.'*
>
> About 30 seconds.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Address why you're spending time on it.*
>
> *'I find the framework intellectually engaging. It gives me content I don't get in standard math curricula — modular cycles, geometric encodings, deep symmetries. The depth-over-breadth approach suits how I want to engage. The framework has limitations and unproven claims; I'm honest about those. The work I'm doing isn't trying to replace standard math; it's adding a particular kind of geometric-numerical perspective I find valuable.'*
>
> About 45 seconds.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Total: about three minutes.* Honest; specific; calibrated; not defensive.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three reasons this articulation works.*
>
> *Reason 1 — specific content.* Naming actual content (mod-9, doubling cycle, ABHA Torus, etc.) *demonstrates substance*. Generic gestures don't.
>
> *Reason 2 — epistemic layering.* Acknowledging *what's mainstream and what's speculative* preserves credibility. Pretending all is verified would lose credibility.
>
> *Reason 3 — personal honesty.* Saying *why you're spending time on it* — intellectual engagement, distinctive content, depth-over-breadth — *names your own values*. Friends respect honesty about values.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What this articulation isn't.*
>
> *Isn't 1.* Recruiting. *You're not trying to convert your friend.*
>
> *Isn't 2.* Defending all claims. *You're acknowledging what's not yet defendable.*
>
> *Isn't 3.* Apologising. *You're not framing the engagement as embarrassing.*
>
> *Honest, calibrated, personal.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Articulate VBM to a sceptical friend in about 3 minutes: what it is, what's contested, what you've learned, what's next, why you spend time on it. Specific content; epistemic layering; personal honesty. Don't recruit; don't defend everything; don't apologise. Calibrated articulation builds trust.*"
