## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Lens 6 set up first sighting of the future coil. Today: *compare it with mainstream RF coil designs*. Where does Rodin Coil winding sit?
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three mainstream RF coil patterns.*
>
> *Pattern 1 — air-core solenoid.* *Cylindrical former (or no former); wire wound in even helical turns*. Used in resonant RF circuits, antennas, wireless charging. *Properties: simple geometry; calculable inductance; tunable by adjusting turns/length.*
>
> *Pattern 2 — toroidal RF coil.* *Toroidal ferrite or air-core former; wire wound around the small-circle*. Used in RF chokes, EMI filters, broadband baluns. *Properties: contained magnetic field; low external interference; high inductance per unit volume.*
>
> *Pattern 3 — basket-weave or Litz coil.* *Specialised winding patterns minimising AC losses at high frequencies*. Used in RF and inductive-coupling systems. *Properties: reduced skin-effect losses; specific high-frequency optimisation.*
>
> *Three mainstream patterns; well-characterised.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *The Rodin Coil pattern relative to these.*
>
> *Relation to Pattern 1 (solenoid).* *Rodin Coil is toroidal, not cylindrical. Different topology.* *Less direct comparison.*
>
> *Relation to Pattern 2 (mainstream toroidal).* *Rodin Coil shares toroidal substrate.* *Differs in winding specifically: 6 discrete positions per cycle vs continuous winding around tube.* *Closest mainstream relative.*
>
> *Relation to Pattern 3 (basket-weave/Litz).* *Both involve specialised winding patterns.* *Litz optimises for AC losses; Rodin optimises for VBM-tradition specifications.* *Different optimisation targets.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three structural similarities.*
>
> *Similarity one — wire-wound coils.* All four (solenoid, mainstream toroidal, basket-weave, Rodin) are *wire-wound electromagnetic coils*. *Standard physical category.*
>
> *Similarity two — calculable inductance.* All four have *calculable inductance from geometry and wire properties*. *Mainstream EM analysis applies to each.*
>
> *Similarity three — measurement protocols.* *DC resistance, inductance, resonance, magnetic field — same protocols apply to all four.* *Comparison meaningful.*
>
> *Three structural differences.*
>
> *Difference one — winding-pattern distinctiveness.* *Rodin Coil's 6-position helix is unique among the four*; *the others have either simple-helical or continuous winding*. *Distinctive winding.*
>
> *Difference two — purpose.* *Mainstream RF coils target specific functions* (resonance, filtering, impedance matching, low loss). *Rodin Coil targets VBM-tradition specifications*; *function relative to mainstream is open empirical question.*
>
> *Difference three — empirical characterisation.* *Mainstream RF coils have decades of measured-vs-predicted comparisons; characterisation is mature.* *Rodin Coil characterisation is developing; Q3 contributes to it.* *Different maturity.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three open questions for Q3.*
>
> *Question 1 — does the doubling-cycle winding produce distinctive RF behaviour?* *Resonance frequency relative to predicted; Q-factor; insertion loss; bandwidth. To be measured.*
>
> *Question 2 — for what RF applications might the Rodin Coil compete?* *If distinctive behaviour exists*, *which mainstream applications could the Rodin Coil substitute or complement?* *Open.*
>
> *Question 3 — does Rodin Coil performance match VBM-tradition predictions?* *Foundation predictions about coil performance need to be tested*. *Q3 will compare.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three honest assessments.*
>
> *Assessment one — Rodin Coil isn't categorically distinct.* *It's a wire-wound toroidal coil with specific winding pattern.* *Mainstream EM applies.* *Distinctive behaviour, if any, is quantitative not categorical.*
>
> *Assessment two — testable through standard protocols.* *Same measurements as RF coils*; *direct comparison possible.* *No special instruments needed.*
>
> *Assessment three — characterisation is in-progress.* *Q3 doesn't conclude characterisation*; *it advances it*. *Long-term work; ongoing research.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Useful framing. *The Rodin Coil is a wire-wound toroidal coil with a specific winding pattern (doubling-cycle helix; 6 positions per cycle). It shares structural similarities with mainstream RF coils — toroidal substrate, calculable inductance, standard measurement protocols. It differs in winding-pattern specifics from both mainstream toroidal coils and from basket-weave / Litz coils. Whether the specific winding produces distinctive RF behaviour is an open empirical question; Q3's measurements will contribute. The comparison is calibrated; mainstream EM analysis applies; characterisation is developing.*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Compare Rodin Coil with mainstream RF coils.* Toroidal substrate shared with mainstream toroidal RF coils; specific winding distinct. *Standard EM analysis applies; measurement protocols transfer; characterisation developing through Q3.*"
