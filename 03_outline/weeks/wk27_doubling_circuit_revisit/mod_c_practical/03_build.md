## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Lens 1 set up the winding plan; Lens 2 counted turns. Today: *physically mark the helix path* on your decorated former. *Wk 33-prep.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Materials.*
>
> *Item 1.* Decorated torus former (Wk 26 Mod C Lens 3).
>
> *Item 2.* Fine permanent marker (different colour from existing decorations). *Black, dark blue, or red works.*
>
> *Item 3.* Reference: your winding plan (Lens 1) and turn count (Lens 2).
>
> *Item 4.* Optional: thin painter's tape for masking guide lines.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Six steps to mark the helix.*
>
> *Step 1 — identify position 1 (entry).*
>
> Convention: outer equator at θ = 0°. *Mark with a small dot or labelled '1'.*
>
> *Step 2 — calculate position 2's location.*
>
> *(θ, φ) = (60°, 60° × n).* For n = 2: (60°, 120°). *Translate to physical (x, y, z) using torus formula; locate on former.*
>
> *Mark position 2; label '2'.*
>
> *Step 3 — connect positions 1 and 2 with helix arc.*
>
> *Draw a smooth curve* on the former surface from position 1 to position 2, *advancing 60° major and 120° minor along the way*. *Practice; the line need not be perfect first try.*
>
> *Step 4 — repeat for positions 4, 8, 7, 5.*
>
> Each from previous: 60° major, n × 60° minor. *Draw arcs; label positions.*
>
> *Step 5 — close the cycle.*
>
> *From position 5*, advance 60° major + n × 60° minor, returning to position 1. *Verify closure.* *Mark closure with annotation if you're winding multiple cycles.*
>
> *Step 6 — annotate.*
>
> *Add labels:*
>
> *'Helix path — single cycle'.*
>
> *Each position's number (1, 2, 4, 8, 7, 5).*
>
> *Direction arrow showing winding direction.*
>
> *Entry and exit points clearly marked.*
>
> *Six steps; clean marked helix; ready for wire.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three observations on physical marking.*
>
> *Observation one — first attempt is rough.* *Hand-marking on a curved surface is tricky*. *Re-tracing with cleaner lines is normal*. *Don't aim for perfection on first attempt.*
>
> *Observation two — colour-coding helps.* *Use one colour for cycle 1, another for cycle 2 (if multi-cycle), etc.* *Visual differentiation aids winding.*
>
> *Observation three — verify before sealing.* *If varnishing the former, mark the helix first, then varnish*. *Marker will smear if varnished after.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *If you don't have the decorated former yet.*
>
> *Mark on a paper torus instead* (from Wk 17). *Same procedure; smaller scale; rehearsal.*
>
> *Order or build the decorated former this week if possible*. *Wk 33 will need it.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three things the marked helix does.*
>
> *Thing one — wire-winding guide.* *In Wk 33, the marker shows where wire goes*. *No improvising; structured winding.*
>
> *Thing two — visualisation aid.* *The marked helix is itself a visual artefact*. *Workspace decoration; teaches geometry incidentally.*
>
> *Thing three — communication.* *Practitioners can show others the marked former*; *the helix path is visible without explanation*. *Engineering communication.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Mark the helix path on your decorated former.* Six steps; six positions labeled; closure verified; entry/exit annotated. *Wk 33-prep complete after this.* *If former isn't ready, use paper torus as rehearsal substitute.*"
