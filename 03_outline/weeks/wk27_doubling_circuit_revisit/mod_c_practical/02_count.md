## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Lens 1 set up the winding plan. Today: *count winding turns* — specific numbers for your Wk 33 build.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What 'winding turn' means here.*
>
> A *winding turn* is *one full helical-loop traversal of the wire*. For the doubling-cycle helix:
>
> *One cycle = 6 discrete steps = 1 major-circle progression + n minor-circle wraps*.
>
> *Total wire length per cycle* depends on R, r, n.
>
> *Total turns required* depends on number of cycles to wind.
>
> *Each is calculable.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Worked example — typical decorated former.*
>
> *Former specifications.*
>
> *R = 60 mm.* (Major radius.)
>
> *r = 15 mm.* (Minor radius.)
>
> *Wire diameter d = 1.0 mm.*
>
> *n = 2.* (Canonical minor turns per major; **`[ORG-INPUT]`**.)
>
> *Number of cycles to wind = 1.* (Single-cycle for first build.)
>
> *Calculations.*
>
> *Major-circle circumference.* 2πR = 2π × 60 mm ≈ 377 mm.
>
> *Minor-circle circumference.* 2πr = 2π × 15 mm ≈ 94 mm.
>
> *One cycle's wire length* (helical arc length, approximation): approximately *√(major² + (n × minor)²) = √(377² + 188²) ≈ √(142,129 + 35,344) ≈ √177,473 ≈ 421 mm*. *About 42 cm of wire per cycle.*
>
> *For 1 cycle:* wire length ≈ 42 cm.
>
> *For 3 cycles:* wire length ≈ 126 cm ≈ 1.3 m.
>
> *For 9 cycles (a fuller coil):* wire length ≈ 3.8 m.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Turn count interpretation.*
>
> *Each cycle has 6 discrete positions but is 1 full helical 'turn' in the major-circle direction with n minor-circle 'turns'.*
>
> *Single-cycle coil: 1 major turn + 2 minor turns (with n = 2).*
>
> *Three-cycle coil: 3 major turns + 6 minor turns.*
>
> *Nine-cycle coil: 9 major turns + 18 minor turns.*
>
> *Turn counts depend on what's being counted (major or minor); both are needed for engineering specification.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three observations on the count.*
>
> *Observation one — counts are dimensional.* *The count depends on R, r, d, n, and cycles.* *Different formers give different turn counts.* *Specifications matter.*
>
> *Observation two — wire length follows mechanically.* *Once turn counts are known, wire length is direct calculation*. *Procurement specification.*
>
> *Observation three — multi-cycle gives finer winding.* *More cycles = more wire on the same former = more turns per unit volume = different inductance characteristics.* *Engineering trade-off.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three uses of the count.*
>
> *Use 1 — wire procurement.* *Order the calculated wire length plus 10-20% margin*. *Avoid running short mid-winding.*
>
> *Use 2 — winding planning.* *Practitioners know how many cycles to wind before starting*; *no improvising.* *Reproducible.*
>
> *Use 3 — performance prediction.* *Inductance scales with turn count squared* (for solenoid-like coils; toroidal helix relations are similar). *Predicted inductance follows from turn count.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *For your specific build.*
>
> *Step 1.* Measure your decorated former: R, r.
>
> *Step 2.* Choose canonical n (per **`[ORG-INPUT]`** or your foundation source).
>
> *Step 3.* Choose number of cycles to wind. *Beginner: 1-3 cycles. Intermediate: 9 cycles (matches the full doubling cycle plus a bit). Advanced: 18+ cycles for full coil.*
>
> *Step 4.* Compute wire length using the formula. *Procurement spec: ordered length × 1.15 (margin).*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three pieces of advice for the count.*
>
> *Advice 1 — start small.* *First Wk 33 build: single-cycle coil*. *Tests construction technique without large wire commitment.*
>
> *Advice 2 — record specifics.* *Note your specific R, r, d, n, cycles, wire length in your notebook*. *For Wk 33 reference; for performance comparison.*

> *Advice 3 — verify with a foundation source.* *If foundation specifications differ from your computation*, defer to foundation values. **`[ORG-INPUT]`** is appropriate when in doubt.
>
> *[pause]*
>
