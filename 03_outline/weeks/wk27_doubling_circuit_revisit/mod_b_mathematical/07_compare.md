## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Lens 6 applied the unification. Today: *compare two mainstream mathematical treatments* of the toroidal helix.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *The topological treatment.*
>
> *Topology* sees the helix as *a closed curve on a torus surface*. Properties:
>
> *Property 1 — winding numbers.* The helix has *(major, minor) winding numbers* — one full major loop and n minor loops per cycle. *Topological invariants.*
>
> *Property 2 — homotopy class.* *The helix represents an element of the torus's fundamental group (Z × Z)*. *Specifically, the (1, n) element.*
>
> *Property 3 — knot type.* *The helix, viewed in 3D, is a (1, n) torus knot when n and 1 are coprime, or a (1, n) torus link otherwise*. *Knot theory provides classification.*
>
> *Topological treatment is purely geometric / algebraic; no physics.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *The electromagnetic treatment.*
>
> *EM* sees the helix as *a wire carrying current*. Properties:
>
> *Property 1 — magnetic field.* Current in the helix produces *a magnetic field*; the field's structure depends on helix geometry. *Calculable from Biot-Savart law.*
>
> *Property 2 — inductance.* *The helix has self-inductance L*; *L depends on geometry, wire properties, and turn count*. *Standard formulas.*
>
> *Property 3 — resonance.* *In an LC circuit*, *the helix-as-inductor pairs with capacitance to give resonance frequency f = 1 / (2π√(LC))*. *Mainstream EM analysis.*
>
> *Electromagnetic treatment is physical; uses topology indirectly through geometry.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three structural similarities.*
>
> *Similarity one — same underlying object.* *Both treatments describe the same physical helix*. *No conflict; complementary.*
>
> *Similarity two — geometry shared.* *Both use the helix's geometric coordinates (θ, φ, n)*. *Topology uses for invariants; EM uses for field calculations.*
>
> *Similarity three — predictive power.* *Both make verifiable predictions*. *Topology: knot classification, homotopy class. EM: inductance, resonance, field strength.*
>
> *Three structural differences.*
>
> *Difference one — abstraction level.* *Topology is more abstract — invariants, classes, group elements*. *EM is more concrete — currents, fields, voltages*. *Different levels.*
>
> *Difference two — verification methods.* *Topology verifies through proof / classification*. *EM verifies through measurement* (oscilloscopes, multimeters, magnetometers). *Different verification.*
>
> *Difference three — engineering relevance.* *Topology informs structural choices* (winding number, knot type). *EM informs functional performance* (inductance, resonance, efficiency). *Both relevant for Rodin Coil engineering.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Where the doubling-cycle helix sits in each.*
>
> *In topology.* It's a (1, n) torus knot/link with n = canonical foundation specification. *Specific element of the torus's fundamental group.* *Mainstream classifiable.*
>
> *In EM.* It's a *toroidal coil with specific helical winding pattern*. *Inductance and resonance can be calculated from (R, r, n, wire diameter, turn count).* *Mainstream analyzable.*
>
> *Both treatments accommodate the doubling-cycle helix without modification.* *Mainstream content; layer 1.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three observations.*
>
> *Observation one — both treatments are useful for VBM engineering.* *Topology gives structural specifications; EM gives functional predictions*. *Q3 will use both.*
>
> *Observation two — neither treatment is VBM-specific.* *VBM uses these as standard tools.* *No proprietary mathematics; mainstream tools applied.*
>
> *Observation three — Rodin Coil distinctive features may need additional treatment.* *If Rodin Coil performance is distinctive in ways mainstream EM doesn't predict*, *additional analysis tools may be needed*. *Open question; Q3 measurements will inform.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Useful framing.* *The doubling-cycle helix is treatable in both topology (as a torus knot/link with specific winding numbers) and electromagnetism (as a toroidal coil with calculable inductance and resonance). Both treatments are mainstream and apply without modification. Topology gives structural specifications; EM gives functional predictions. Both inform Q3 engineering.
