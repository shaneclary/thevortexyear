## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Today we count the hardware components a complete axial-drive Rodin Coil rig requires.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Component 1 — torus former.* The 3D-printed (or wooden) torus, marked with the 5 essential marks plus helix line. *Already required for any Rodin Coil work.* Cost: $5-50 depending on source.
>
> *Component 2 — surface windings.* The wire wound on the toroidal surface. Single-strand, six-strand, or other configuration. *Already required.* Cost: $5-15 in magnet wire.
>
> *Component 3 — axial wire.* New for axial drive. Insulated wire, AWG 18-22, passing through the central hole. *Length: about 30 cm* (15 cm above the torus + 15 cm below, leaving extra for clamping and termination). Cost: under $1.
>
> *Component 4 — axial supports.* Non-conductive clamps or stands to hold the axial wire centred. Could be wooden dowels, plastic stands, or custom 3D-printed supports. *Functional cost: $2-20 depending on solution.*
>
> *Component 5 — toroidal power supply.* DC or low-frequency AC supply for the surface windings. Variable voltage 0-12 V; current up to 1 A typical. *Cost: $20-60 for a basic bench supply.*
>
> *Component 6 — axial power supply.* Separate supply for the axial wire. Same specs as toroidal supply. Independent control. *Cost: $20-60. (If you already have one, you can multiplex; for serious work, separate supplies are recommended.)*
>
> *Component 7 — measurement instruments.* Multimeter ($20), oscilloscope ($100-300 for entry-level), magnetic field probe ($50-200). *Total: $170-520 depending on quality.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Total component count: 7 main components.*
>
> *Total cost: $250-700* for a complete axial-drive rig.
>
> *Compared to a basic surface-only Rodin Coil (Wk 33's first build): about $50-100 total.* The axial drive *roughly doubles the rig cost* but adds the second electrical degree of freedom.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Optional / advanced components.*
>
> *Component 8 — function generator.* For driving AC waveforms instead of DC. *$50-150.*
>
> *Component 9 — phase-locked loop.* For synchronising toroidal and axial drives. *$30-100.*
>
> *Component 10 — magnetic-field-mapping rig.* Mechanised positioning system for systematic field measurements. *$200-1000+ for serious work.*
>
> *Component 11 — Faraday cage / shielded environment.* For low-noise measurements. *$100-500 in materials.*
>
> *These are advanced items*; the basic 7-component rig is enough for Wk 33-35 builds.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three observations.
>
> *Observation one.* The component list is *long but not exotic*. Most components are off-the-shelf electronics. No specialised parts required.
>
> *Observation two.* The cost is *manageable for hobbyist builders* — total under $500 for an entry-level rig with axial drive. Compared to specialised research equipment, this is small.
>
> *Observation three.* The components *accumulate over time*. You don't need everything at once. Wk 33 needs items 1, 2, 5 (surface-only). Wk 34 adds item 7 (measurement). Q3 Wk 30+ adds items 3, 4, 6 (axial drive). *Build the rig piece by piece*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Plan rig acquisition over the next 10-12 weeks.* Don't try to assemble everything at once.
>
> Suggested phased plan:
>
> *Phase 1 (Wks 20-26): basic surface-only rig.* Components 1, 2, 5. Total cost: ~$50-100.
>
> *Phase 2 (Wk 27-32): add measurement.* Component 7. Total cost: ~$150-300.
>
> *Phase 3 (Wk 30-35): add axial drive.* Components 3, 4, 6. Total cost: ~$25-80.
>
> *Total over 16 weeks:* $225-480 spread out manageably."
