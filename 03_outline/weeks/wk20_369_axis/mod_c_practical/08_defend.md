## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The objection. *Adding axial drive doubles the rig's complexity. Cost goes up; setup time goes up; potential failure points multiply. Why bother — can't builders just stick with surface windings?*
>
> The objection is fair for *learning builds*. For *full-framework engineering*, both surface and axis are needed.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three reasons builders need both.*
>
> *Reason one — the framework specifies both.* Q1 established (ℤ/9ℤ) as the carrier. Q2 placed the units on the surface and the non-units on the axis. *Both pieces are part of VBM's complete geometry.* A surface-only build realises only half the framework.
>
> *Reason two — claimed effects depend on the interaction.* Marko's stronger claims about Rodin Coil behaviour involve the *interaction* between toroidal windings and axial drive. Specific cancellation patterns, claimed non-Hertzian fields, energy claims — *these require both components energised*. Without axial drive, the relevant claims aren't being tested.
>
> *Reason three — comparability with foundation experiments.* Foundation-published measurements often involve axial-drive configurations. Builders comparing their results need matching configurations. *Surface-only builds aren't comparable to axial-driven results.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *When surface-only is enough.*
>
> *Sufficient 1 — first build.* Wk 33's single-strand build is surface-only. *Don't add axial drive there*; the build is for learning winding technique.
>
> *Sufficient 2 — basic measurement.* Initial measurements of resistance, inductance, basic field shape can be done with surface-only configurations. *Foundational measurements work.*
>
> *Sufficient 3 — comparison baseline.* Surface-only acts as the *baseline* against which axial-driven measurements are compared. *You need both, but you can build them sequentially.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *When axial drive is essential.*
>
> *Essential 1 — Aetheron-related experiments.* Wk 30 onward focuses on axial drive's role in claimed Aetheron effects. *Without axial drive, you can't test these claims.*
>
> *Essential 2 — full canonical configuration.* The complete Rodin Coil rig (per foundation specifications) includes both. *Skipping axial drive produces an incomplete rig.*
>
> *Essential 3 — multi-strand interaction studies.* Some advanced experiments involve interaction between counter-stranded surface coils and axial drive. *These require both components.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> So the calibrated answer to *do builders need both*:
>
> *VBM's framework includes both the toroidal surface (carrying multiplicative units) and the orthogonal axis (carrying family-3 non-units). A complete rig realises both. Surface-only builds are sufficient for first builds, basic measurements, and baseline comparisons. Axial drive is essential for Aetheron-related experiments, full canonical configuration, and advanced multi-strand interaction studies. Builders should plan for both, building sequentially: surface first, axis added when the framework's claims about axial behaviour become the focus of investigation.*
>
> Around eighty words.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Plan the full rig but build it incrementally.*
>
> Phase 1 — surface only (Wk 33-35).
>
> Phase 2 — measurement infrastructure (Wk 34).
>
> Phase 3 — axial drive added (Wk 30 in Q3 timeline; Wk 35 in build sequence).
>
> By Q3's end, you have the complete rig.
>
> One more note. *The full rig is a research instrument*, not a casual coil. It supports systematic investigation of VBM's engineering claims. Builders who skip the axial drive miss the framework's signature experiments. *Don't skip it permanently; the timing is just question.*"
