## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Today's build is a *plan* for installing the axial wire on the future 3D-printed former. The actual installation happens in Q3 (Wk 30+). Today: write the procedure.
>
> Open your build log to a fresh page. Title it *Q3 Wk 30 — Axial Wire Installation Plan*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Section 1 — materials needed.*
>
> Axial wire: AWG 22 insulated copper, 30 cm length.
>
> Two non-conductive supports: 1 cm × 1 cm wooden blocks with a small hole drilled through, OR 3D-printed brackets, OR plastic clamps.
>
> Mounting base: a small wooden or plastic plate large enough to hold the torus and the axial-wire endpoints stable. About 15 cm × 15 cm.
>
> Hot glue or non-conductive epoxy.
>
> *Section 2 — installation procedure.*
>
> *Step 1 — orient the torus former.* Place the torus on the mounting base, doughnut hole pointing up.
>
> *Step 2 — pass the axial wire.* Thread the AWG 22 wire through the central hole. Both ends should protrude — about 15 cm above the torus and 15 cm below the base.
>
> *Step 3 — attach upper support.* Place one support 5-10 cm above the torus. Pass the wire through the support's hole. The support should be *stable* but not gripping the wire tightly.
>
> *Step 4 — attach lower support.* Same on the other side, 5-10 cm below the base.
>
> *Step 5 — adjust centring.* Slide the wire so it's *centred* in the torus's hole. The wire should pass through the *exact centre* of the doughnut hole, perpendicular to the torus's plane.
>
> *Step 6 — verify alignment.* Look down through the torus from above. The wire should appear at the centre of the doughnut hole. Same view from below.
>
> *Step 7 — tighten or fix.* Glue the supports to the mounting base if needed for stability. Don't glue the wire itself; you'll need to remove it for measurements.
>
> *Step 8 — strip and prepare ends.* Strip 1 cm of insulation from each wire end. These will connect to the axial power supply.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Section 3 — verification.*
>
> *Verify 1 — centring.* The wire is centred in the torus's hole. Visual check from above and below.
>
> *Verify 2 — perpendicularity.* The wire is perpendicular to the torus's plane. Use a small bubble level or visual check.
>
> *Verify 3 — non-contact.* The axial wire does *not touch* any surface winding. The wire is electrically isolated from the toroidal coil.
>
> *Verify 4 — connection.* Both ends of the axial wire are accessible for connection to a power supply.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Section 4 — common installation errors.*
>
> *Error 1 — wire not centred.* Check from multiple angles. If off-centre, slide the wire until centred.
>
> *Error 2 — wire touches the torus.* Insulation should prevent shorts, but contact damages the surface windings over time. Use spacers if needed.
>
> *Error 3 — supports unstable.* If the supports tilt or shift, the wire moves during measurement. Use enough glue or larger supports.
>
> *Error 4 — wire too short.* The wire should extend several centimetres above and below the torus for stable connection. Cut a longer length than you think you need.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What you've planned. *A complete axial-wire installation procedure.* When the 3D-printed former arrives in Q3, this plan tells you what to do.
>
> Three observations.
>
> *Observation one.* The installation is *simple* — about 15 minutes once the materials are gathered.
>
> *Observation two.* The supports are *the critical part*. A stable, centred wire makes measurement reproducible. *A wobbly axial wire makes everything noisier.*
>
> *Observation three.* This is *Q3 prep*, not Q3 itself. Wk 30 will introduce the axial drive in context. The plan stays in your build log until then."
