## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "*Solenoid axis.* The axis through the centre of a solenoid (cylinder coil). The wire wraps *around* this axis in a uniform helix. The axis is the *direction of the magnetic field* the solenoid produces.
>
> Used for: directing magnetic fields, generating electromagnets, MRI bores.
>
> *3-6-9 axis (Rodin Coil).* The axis through the central hole of a torus, perpendicular to the toroidal plane. The wire wraps *around the toroidal surface* (not around this axis). The axis carries family 3 in VBM's framework.
>
> Used for: VBM-specific structural framework, axial-drive coil designs (Q3).
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three structural similarities.
>
> *Similarity one — both are perpendicular to the coil's primary winding.* The solenoid's axis is perpendicular to each turn of its helix. The Rodin's 3-6-9 axis is perpendicular to the toroidal surface where the wire wraps.
>
> *Similarity two — both are linear (1D).* Each axis is a 1D line. Both have a *direction* (positive end vs negative end).
>
> *Similarity three — both connect to magnetic field structure.* Each axis is *related to* the magnetic field of its respective coil — though differently.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three structural differences.
>
> *Difference one — relationship to wire path.* Solenoid axis: *the wire winds around it*. Rodin 3-6-9 axis: *the wire doesn't wind around it* — the wire wraps around the toroidal surface, while the 3-6-9 axis passes through the central hole *empty of wire*.
>
> *Difference two — magnetic field along the axis.* Solenoid: *strong magnetic field along the axis* (this is the solenoid's main feature). Rodin Coil: *the field along the 3-6-9 axis depends on the wind pattern* — for counter-wound canonical configurations, the axial field is reduced (cancellation).
>
> *Difference three — algebraic content.* Solenoid axis: *no specific algebraic content* — it's a geometric direction. Rodin 3-6-9 axis: *carries family 3 of (ℤ/9ℤ)* — it has *VBM-structural meaning*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What each axis is for.*
>
> *Solenoid axis.* Direction of magnetic field. Used in EM applications where you want a field aligned with a specific direction.
>
> *3-6-9 axis.* (a) Geometric placeholder for family 3 in the math. (b) In Q3 axial-driven coils: the direction of *axial drive* — current that flows *along* the axis (through a central wire), distinct from the coil's surface windings.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *A subtle distinction.*
>
> *In a single-strand Rodin Coil*, the magnetic field along the 3-6-9 axis is approximately what a small toroidal inductor would produce — a closed-loop field around the surface, with axial component.
>
> *In a counter-wound multi-strand Rodin Coil*, axial-direction field components are partially cancelled. The axial direction shows *less* field than for a uniform coil.
>
> *In an axial-driven Rodin Coil* (Wk 30 onward), a *separate wire passes along the 3-6-9 axis*, carrying its own current. This adds an *active* axial drive distinct from the toroidal windings.
>
> *Three configurations, three different relationships between the 3-6-9 axis and EM behaviour.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Useful framing. *Solenoid axes are about field direction. Rodin 3-6-9 axes are structural placeholders for the family-3 algebraic complement of the toroidal surface — and, in axial-drive configurations, become active EM components carrying their own current.*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Don't conflate the two axes.* The solenoid axis and the Rodin 3-6-9 axis serve different roles in their respective frameworks. *VBM uses the same geometric concept (a perpendicular line) for a different structural purpose than mainstream coil design uses it.*"
