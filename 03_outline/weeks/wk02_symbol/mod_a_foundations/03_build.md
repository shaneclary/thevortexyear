Wk 02 — Theme: The Symbol of Enlightenment — Lens 3: Build — Module A (Foundations)
Runtime: 6:00. Author: draft. Status: Draft.

---

## 0. Authoring notes
Three drawings of the Symbol, by hand, in one sitting. Imperative voice. The lesson is the drawing, not the explanation. The producer should resist over-cutting — long takes of pen on paper carry the lesson. By the end, the student has three Symbols on a single notebook page, and the third looks better than the first.

## 1. Outcome
By the end of these 6 minutes, the student has drawn three Symbols of Enlightenment in succession on a single notebook page.

## 2. Hook (0:00–0:30)
*[On screen: a fresh notebook page. Three blank circle outlines, lightly pencilled, ready to receive numbers.]*

> "Three drawings. One page. That's the lesson. Pencil in hand."

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)
> "Lens 3 of 9. We drew one Symbol yesterday. Today we draw three. Three more times the hand learns the placement. The lesson is in the doing — don't watch this one passively."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

*[Camera close on the host's hand. Three sequential drawings, in real time.]*

> "First drawing. Circle. Mark the top — that's 9. Place 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8 clockwise around the circle. Don't aim for perfect. Aim for done.
>
> *[long pause — let the host complete the first drawing in real time, ~60 seconds. Student should be drawing along.]*
>
> Second drawing. Same gesture. Circle, 9 at the top, 1 through 8 clockwise. Notice your hand. By the second drawing, you're already faster. The placement is already familiar. You don't have to think about where 4 goes — your hand is starting to know.
>
> *[long pause — second drawing, ~60 seconds]*
>
> Third drawing. Circle. 9. The eight numbers around. By the third one your hand makes the diagram without quite consulting you. That's the point. The Symbol is going to be drawn dozens of times across the year. The faster your hand internalises the placement, the more attention you have for the *content* of each drawing — what we're marking on it, what we're tracing on it, what we're observing.
>
> *[long pause — third drawing, ~60 seconds]*
>
> Look at your three. The first probably has uneven spacing. Maybe the 5 is too close to the 6. Maybe the 9 isn't quite at the top. The third is cleaner. Don't compare them as performances. Compare them as evidence — the hand is learning. By Week 13, your Symbol will look like the printed reference. By Week 26, it will look like Marko's. The drawing is the apprenticeship.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Beside the third Symbol, write the date. Beside the first, write *first try*. The page now has three Symbols, dated, and a label that lets you orient it later when you flip back."

## 5. Try it (4:30–5:30)
> "Pause. If you didn't draw along — pause now and draw three Symbols on a fresh page. This is the only lesson where I ask you to stop and do the work before continuing. Sixty seconds is enough for one. Three minutes for all three. Take what you need."

*[Pause card on screen for at least 60 seconds; producer leaves space for student's actual drawing time.]*

> "Welcome back. Whether you drew zero or three — the next lesson assumes the page exists. Catch up if needed."

## 6. Bridge (5:30–6:00)
> "Next lesson — Hear from Marko. We've drawn the Symbol three times today. The next lens hands you Marko speaking about *his* first drawing of the Symbol. Different page, same gesture."

*[Closing card: "Lesson 3 of 9 — Build. Next: Hear from Marko."]*
