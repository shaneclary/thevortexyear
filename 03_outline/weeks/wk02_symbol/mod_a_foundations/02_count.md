## 0. Authoring notes
The "why nine, not ten" question. Mod A leans accessible — present the answer without invoking modular arithmetic by name (that lives in Mod B). The clean answer: under digit-root reduction, 0 and 9 collapse to the same value, so the Symbol uses 9 as the canonical representative. Show this with a small worked example. The student should leave knowing why ten numbers would be redundant.

## 1. Outcome
By the end of these 6 minutes, the student can answer the question *why does the Symbol have nine numbers and not ten?* in one sentence.

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Most circular number diagrams use ten — clocks have twelve, stopwatches have sixty, but a base-ten diagram naturally suggests ten positions. The Symbol uses nine. Why?
>
> Here's the answer in one piece. Under digit-root reduction — the operation we use everywhere in VBM, summing digits until one digit remains — the number 10 reduces to 1. The number 100 reduces to 1. The number 1,000 reduces to 1. Every power of ten collapses to 1. So a position labelled 10 would just be the same as the position labelled 1. Putting both on the diagram would be redundant.
>
> What about 0? Under digit-root reduction, 0 stays 0 — but in VBM the operation treats 0 and 9 as the same point. Reducing 9 gives you 9. Reducing 18 gives you 9. Reducing 27 gives you 9. Multiples of 9 always reduce to 9. The number 0, in the same operation, behaves as the absence of multiplicity — and Marko folds 0 into the position labelled 9. Hence: 9 at the top, where 0 would be on a counter.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> So the Symbol carries nine numbers because nine is exactly the number of *distinct values* the digit-root operation produces. Try it. Take any whole number. Reduce. The result is one of {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9}. Not 10. Not 0. Not 11. Nine outcomes. The Symbol shows you each one, in its place, around the circle.
>
> *[pause — show a worked example]*
>
> Quick example. Take the number 47. Sum the digits: 4 + 7 = 11. That's still two digits, so reduce again: 1 + 1 = 2. So 47 lives at position 2 on the Symbol. Take the number 999. Sum: 9 + 9 + 9 = 27. Reduce: 2 + 7 = 9. Position 9. Take 1,000,000. Sum: 1 + 0 + 0 + 0 + 0 + 0 + 0 = 1. Position 1.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three different scales. Three single-digit answers. Each one finds its place on the Symbol. The Symbol is the smallest diagram that can hold every digit-root in the universe. Adding a tenth position wouldn't add information — it would just duplicate something already there. Marko's claim is that this isn't an accident of base-10 notation; it's evidence that the decimal system has a natural shape, and the shape happens to be nine-fold."
