## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

*[Visual: the Mod-A spread of Week 2 walks past — eight lesson cards, briefly each.]*

> "Module A opened with you and the pencil. You drew the Symbol of Enlightenment on a fresh page — circle, 9 at the top, 1 through 8 clockwise. The first dated drawing of fifty-two you'll have by Week 52.
>
> From there, we counted. The Symbol uses nine numbers, not ten, because under digit-root reduction the count produces nine distinct residues — and zero collapses to nine, the position at the top of the circle. You wrote *zero* underneath the 9 on your drawing. The diagram is whole, not missing a number.
>
> Then three drawings on one page. Repetition by hand. The pencil starts to know the placement of the numbers before the mind has to think about it. The third drawing was already cleaner than the first.
>
> Then Marko spoke. Three and a half minutes of him telling the story of his own first drawing — fifteen years old, the Bahá'í name Abha reducing to nine, the figure-eight tracing of the Doubling Circuit, the feeling of looking at something already there. You linked his date to yours on the same notebook page.
>
> Then a contemplative beat. The Symbol now lives on your wall, in your notebook, in your peripheral attention. Decoration, curiosity, co-thinker, companion — you wrote the word that's true today.
>
> Then we showed how the Symbol stores the year. Three uses. The Doubling Circuit traced as a path. The polar pairs marked as three diameters. The family number groups coloured as three triads. By Week 26 the Symbol on your wall could carry most of the first half of the course, on one page.
>
> Then the comparison. The enneagram is adjacent but not identical. Mandalas are radially symmetric; the Symbol isn't. Clocks count to twelve; the Symbol counts to nine because of digit-root structure. You wrote four distinguishing words in your notebook.
>
> Then the calibrated answer to *isn't this just numerology?* The patterns are derived, not asserted. The metaphysics is contested; the math isn't. Twenty-six words. Yours.
>
> *[pause — show the spiral wheel with Week 1 fully lit and Week 2 Mod A lit]*
>
> Module A done. The Symbol is no longer abstract — you've drawn it three times, dated it, used it, defended it. Tomorrow Module B opens. Same theme, mathematical lens. *Why this placement? Why nine? What is this object, formally?* The math behind everything you sketched today."
