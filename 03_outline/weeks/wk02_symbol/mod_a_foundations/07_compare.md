## 0. Authoring notes
Place the Symbol of Enlightenment beside three other circular nine-fold or numbered figures — the Gurdjieff enneagram, sacred-geometry mandalas, the standard clock face. Show overlap and divergence honestly. The Symbol is *adjacent to* these figures, not identical to any of them, and the differences matter for what each one teaches.

## 1. Outcome
By the end of these 6 minutes, the student can describe one specific way the Symbol differs from each of: the enneagram, a sacred-geometry mandala, and a clock face.

> "Four circular diagrams. They look related. They aren't the same. Today: what makes the Symbol *the* Symbol."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "**The enneagram.** A nine-pointed figure used in the Gurdjieff tradition for self-development work and personality typology. It has nine vertices, like the Symbol. It usually places the *9* at the top, like the Symbol. It has internal connecting lines — most commonly a triangle through 9-3-6 and a hexagonal figure connecting 1-4-2-8-5-7. Marko, in his Enneatruth materials, argues the enneagram and the Symbol are *the same diagram* under rotation — that the figures Gurdjieff drew and the figures Marko draws encode the same structure differently. We'll examine the claim carefully in Week 21. For now, the safe statement: the Symbol and the enneagram are visually similar and structurally adjacent. They aren't identical until a specific rotation is applied.
>
> **A sacred-geometry mandala.** Mandala traditions span Tibetan Buddhism, Hindu yantra practice, Islamic geometric art. Most mandalas are *radially symmetric* — they look the same when rotated by some fraction of a full circle. The Symbol of Enlightenment is *not* radially symmetric. Rotate it by 40° (one ninth of a circle) and you get a different diagram, with different number placements. The Symbol's content depends on the labels at each vertex, not just the figure. Mandalas tend to value the figure as a whole; the Symbol values the *named places* on the figure.
>
> **The clock face.** A circle with twelve numbers, with 12 (or 0) at the top, going clockwise. The Symbol shares the orientation convention — 9 at the top, like 12 — and shares the clockwise reading. But the clock face has twelve positions for time-keeping reasons. The Symbol has nine for digit-root reasons. The clock's circle is *cyclical at twelve*; the Symbol's circle is *cyclical at nine*. If you doubled positions on a clock face under digit-summing, you wouldn't get the closed Doubling Circuit — you'd get something else, because mod-12 has different orbit structure than mod-9.
>
> *[pause — show the four figures again, with their distinguishing properties highlighted]*
>
> Three comparisons. Three specific differences. The Symbol is *adjacent* to all three figures and *identical* to none of them. The next time someone tells you it looks like a clock or a mandala or an enneagram, you can answer: similar, but not the same, and here's why.
>
> One more thing. The Symbol is unusual in being *labelled with whole numbers*. Most circular spiritual figures use letters, glyphs, or unlabelled positions. The labelling is part of what makes the Symbol legible to a mathematician — and is part of what makes Marko's claim that the figure encodes mathematical structure fall in a different register than mandala or clock comparisons."
