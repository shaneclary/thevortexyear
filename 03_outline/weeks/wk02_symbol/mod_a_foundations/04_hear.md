**[ORG-CLIP]** *— canonical clip selection. Candidate from Marko's recorded teachings:*

> *"I was fifteen years old. I had been studying the writings of Bahá'u'lláh. I came across the name Abha — the Most Great Name in the Bahá'í tradition — and noticed that under the Abjad numerology used in Arabic and Persian texts, the name reduces to the number nine. I wondered what would happen if I drew nine numbers around a circle. I took a piece of paper and a pencil and I drew nine. I started at the top. I went around clockwise. I traced the doubling — one to two to four to eight, then sum the digits, seven, then five, then back to one. The line moved like a figure-eight on its side. I had the feeling — not a thought, a feeling — that I was looking at something that had always been there, that someone, somewhere, had drawn it before me, and that it would still be there if no one ever drew it again. I have spent the rest of my life trying to put words around that feeling. The Symbol is what came out of that page."*
> — Marko Rodin, paraphrased from RodinAerodynamics.org and lecture material. **Source:** P1-1, P1-3. **Permission status:** to be confirmed by org before publication.
