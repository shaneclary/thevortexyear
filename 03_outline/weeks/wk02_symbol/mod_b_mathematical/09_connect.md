## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

*[Visual: calm walk through eight Mod-B lesson cards, then zoom to the spiral.]*

> "Module B opened by adding arrows to the Symbol. The Symbol is a *group action diagram* — a drawing that shows nine vertices and the function — *doubling, then digit-summing* — that maps each vertex to another. You traced the doubling action: a six-cycle through the units coprime to 9, a two-cycle through 3 and 6, and a self-loop at 9.
>
> Then we counted. The set ℤ/9ℤ has nine elements, and the Symbol uses the label 9 where a textbook uses 0 — a representation choice, not a different set. You wrote both representations on a notebook page so you can read either notation when you encounter it.
>
> Then we constructed. Compass and protractor, or the printed template — the Symbol is a regular nonagon with labelled vertices, with the placement determined by 40° rotations from 9 at the top. You constructed one carefully, with vertices placed by geometry rather than estimation.
>
> Then Marko spoke on placement. *9 at top because we read top-down. Clockwise because clockwise.* Two reasons, in his voice, on a notebook page beside the construction.
>
> Then a contemplative beat. The Symbol's nine vertices hold the digit-root reductions of every whole number. Infinity compressed to nine points. You wrote one sentence about what you noticed.
>
> Then we showed how the Symbol functions as a reference object. Three derivations from later in the year — the Doubling Circuit, the Polar Pairs, the torus wrap — all point back to the Symbol's specific placement. The Symbol is mathematical notation, not illustration.
>
> Then the comparison. The Symbol next to a standard textbook Cayley diagram of ℤ/9ℤ. Three differences: labelling, orientation, pre-loaded structure. You can now translate between Marko's notation and the textbook's.
>
> Then the calibrated answer to *the placement is arbitrary*. The abstract group is invariant under relabelling, but the visual figures the action draws on the diagram depend on the placement. Forty words. Yours.
>
