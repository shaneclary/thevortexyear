> *"The placement is not arbitrary. I have been asked many times — could you put 9 at the bottom, or 1 at the top? You could draw the diagram differently, but you would lose the alignment with the natural reading direction. We read from top to bottom; we trace clockwise as the natural movement of time on a clock face. When the doubling circuit is traced — 1 to 2 to 4 to 8 to 7 to 5 — the figure that emerges is a figure-eight on its side, the symbol of infinity. That figure only emerges when 9 is at the top and the numbers run clockwise. Move 9 to the bottom and the figure rotates; you can still trace it, but you have introduced a coordinate transformation between yourself and the natural orientation. The placement aligns the Symbol with the reading and the writing, with the way the human hand and the human eye already move."*
