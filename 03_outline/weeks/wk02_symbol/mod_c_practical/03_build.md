## 0. Authoring notes
The act of installation. Imperative voice. The wall print goes on the wall. The notebook insert goes in the notebook. The pocket card goes in the pocket. Three small acts, in sequence. Production: long shots of the host doing each install — pinning, slipping, pocketing.

## 1. Outcome
By the end of these 6 minutes, the student has installed the three Symbol prints in their three locations.

## 2. Hook (0:00–0:30)
*[On screen: a wall, a notebook, a pocket — three empty spaces. A pin, a paperclip, a wallet — three tools.]*

> "Three placements. Three small acts. Six minutes. Stand up."

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)
> "Lens 3 of 9. The prints have arrived. Today we install. The lesson is the doing. Don't watch — do."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

*[Camera follows the host through three install actions, in real time.]*

> "**Action one — wall print.** Take the A3 print. Pick the spot you decided yesterday. Hold the print at that spot for ten seconds. Imagine walking past it three times a day for a year. Does the spot work? If yes, pin or tape it up. If no, choose a better spot now. Pin it.
>
> *[long pause for action]*
>
> **Action two — notebook insert.** Open your notebook to the front cover. Slip the A5 insert into the inside-front-cover pocket if your notebook has one, or use a paperclip or a small piece of tape to fix it to the inside-front cover. The insert should stay in place when you open and close the notebook. Test by closing and reopening. If it falls out, secure it better.
>
> *[long pause for action]*
>
> **Action three — pocket card.** Take one of your business-card-sized Symbols. Slide it into your wallet, your phone case, the inner pocket of a daily bag, or wherever you carry small cards. Place a second copy somewhere different — a glove compartment, a kitchen drawer, the desk you work at. Spread them across two or three locations so the Symbol shows up in different parts of your day.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Look around. The Symbol is now in three places in your life. The wall. The notebook. The pocket. By tomorrow morning you will have walked past, opened, or touched the Symbol three or four times without thinking about it. By Week 13 it will be invisible the way familiar things become invisible. By Week 26 it will be in your peripheral attention without you having to invite it. By Week 52, when the year ends, you'll take down the wall print and find the rectangle of unfaded paint behind it. That's what a year of presence looks like.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Don't add anything to the Symbols today. Don't annotate yet. The blank, clean Symbols start the year. Annotations come at their weeks — the Doubling Circuit traced in Week 4, the polar pair lines in Week 6, the family group colours in Week 7. Today: the canvas is hung."

## 5. Try it (4:30–5:30)
> "Pause. If you haven't pinned the wall print, pin it now. If the insert isn't in the notebook, put it in. If the pocket card isn't on you, pocket it. Sixty seconds for whichever installation hasn't happened yet."

*[Pause card on screen for 60 seconds.]*

> "Welcome back. Three Symbols, three places, installed. The space is set."
