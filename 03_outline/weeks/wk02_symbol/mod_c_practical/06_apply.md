## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The Rodin Coil is a copper conductor wound on a toroidal — donut-shaped — form, following a specific winding pattern. The pattern is not arbitrary. It's derived from the Symbol of Enlightenment.
>
> Here's the chain.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> The Symbol places nine numbers on a circle in a specific order. Q2 of this course wraps that placement onto a torus surface, producing the Rodin Number Map. Each cell of the map is a digit-root, computed from the Symbol's structure. The map's row periodicity comes from the Doubling Circuit's six-step closure, visible on the Symbol. The map's column structure comes from powers of ten reducing to 1 — also a Symbol fact.
>
> Q3 takes the wrapped map and turns it into a wiring chart. Each cell on the torus surface corresponds to a position where copper wire crosses. The wire's path follows the Doubling Circuit — 1 to 2 to 4 to 8 to 7 to 5 to 1 — *as a helix on the torus*. That helix is the Doubling Circuit you traced on the flat Symbol, lifted into three dimensions and followed by the wire.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> So the Rodin Coil's winding is the Symbol's Doubling Circuit, embodied in copper. When you wind your first coil in Week 33, your hands are tracing the same path that your pencil traced in Week 4 — only this time the path lives on a 3D toroidal surface and is made of wire.
>
> *[pause — show the Symbol-to-coil chain visually]*
>
> The Symbol is, structurally, the *canonical face* of the Rodin Coil. The coil is the Symbol made physical. This isn't analogy or metaphor — it's a direct geometric correspondence. Marko draws the Symbol on a workshop wall before he winds a coil because the Symbol is what the coil is going to be a physical instance of. You're pinning the Symbol on your wall now for the same reason: by Week 33, when you wind, you'll have looked at the Symbol enough times that the helical path will already feel familiar.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One more way to put it. If the Symbol is a piece of mathematical notation — as Module B argued — then the Rodin Coil is what you get when you *render* that notation in copper. The notation doesn't change. The medium does."
