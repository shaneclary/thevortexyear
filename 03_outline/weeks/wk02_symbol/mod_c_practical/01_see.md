## 0. Authoring notes
The Symbol moves from the page into the student's living space. Mod A had them draw it. Mod B had them construct it formally. Mod C makes it a *physical fixture*. Three sizes — wall poster, notebook insert, pocket card — with three different roles in the year's practice.

## 1. Outcome
By the end of these 6 minutes, the student can describe three sizes the Symbol gets printed at and the use case for each.

## 2. Hook (0:00–0:30)
*[On screen: three Symbols laid out — large (A3), medium (A5), small (business-card). Each captioned with a one-word use: *wall*, *notebook*, *pocket*.]*

> "Three sizes. Three places. The Symbol leaves the page and enters your space."

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)
> "Lens 1 of 9, Module C. Practical lens. We've drawn the Symbol and constructed it. Today we put it to work — at the right size, in the right place."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "**Size one — the wall print.** A3 or larger. The Symbol on your wall is the version you walk past. It enters your peripheral attention without demanding it. Marko keeps a large Symbol in his workshop. Powell keeps one above his lecture board. The wall print's job is to be *present* — to remind you, in the corner of your eye, that this work is going on. Print it once. Pin it where you'll be every day — above your desk, beside your kitchen, on the back of your bedroom door. Don't put it somewhere you'll never look.
>
> **Size two — the notebook insert.** A5 or smaller, slipped inside the front cover of your notebook. The notebook insert is the version you open when you sit down to work. You consult it as a reference — to check a polar pair, to trace a doubling step, to verify a vertex placement. The notebook Symbol's job is to *eliminate friction*. Without it, you'd flip through pages or open a tab. With it, the Symbol is one cover-flip away.
>
> **Size three — the pocket card.** Business-card or wallet-card size. The pocket Symbol is the version you carry. You won't use it most days. But when you find yourself sitting in a coffee shop or on a train and the morning's lesson surfaces in your mind, you can pull out the card and look. The pocket Symbol is for the in-between moments when the work would otherwise drop. Some students laminate it. Some keep it in a wallet pocket. Some hold it pinned inside a phone case.
>
> *[pause — show the three sizes again, with use cases highlighted]*
>
> Three sizes. Three contexts. Each one is the Symbol in the role its size makes possible. Course materials include printable PDFs at all three sizes — the link is in this lesson's notes. Print them today, or order them — vendors will print and ship for a few dollars. The investment is small; the friction reduction across the year is large.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One more thing. The three sizes aren't a flex. They're how Marko and Randy teach in person. Marko's workshops have a giant Symbol painted on a wall. Each student gets a notebook-sized Symbol in their materials. Each student is given a wallet card. The same diagram, three placements, three relationships. We're reproducing the workshop format at home."
