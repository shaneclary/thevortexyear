## 0. Authoring notes
Numerical-precise lesson on the practical print-and-place specs. Specific sizes, materials, vendors. Lens 2 owns its specificity. The student should leave with a buying or printing list ready to execute.

## 1. Outcome
By the end of these 6 minutes, the student can list the print specs for the wall, notebook, and pocket Symbols.

> "Three sizes. Three specs. Today: numbers."

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "**Wall print.** A3 size — that's 297 by 420 millimetres, or roughly 12 by 17 inches. Print on matte paper, 200 to 250 GSM weight — heavy enough not to curl, light enough not to require professional mounting. Black-on-white is the classical option. Some students prefer a coloured background — earthy beige, slate grey — that reduces visual aggression. Print at 300 DPI minimum. The download is in your course materials, ready to send to a printer.
>
> **Notebook insert.** A5 size — 148 by 210 millimetres, or roughly 6 by 8 inches. Card stock, 180 to 250 GSM. Two-sided printing is useful: clean Symbol on one side, annotated reference (with the Doubling Circuit pre-drawn, polar pair lines, family group colours) on the other. The annotated side becomes useful from Week 4 onwards. The clean side stays available for fresh annotations. Trim with a craft knife or paper guillotine — the cleaner the cut, the longer the card holds up in your notebook.
>
> **Pocket card.** Business-card size — 85 by 55 millimetres, or about 3.5 by 2 inches. Heavy card stock, 350 GSM, with a glossy or matte laminate. Round-corner if possible — the corners are what wear first in a wallet. Same two-sided treatment as the notebook insert: clean Symbol on one face, reference annotations on the other. Vendors will print fifty cards for under twenty dollars; that gives you spares to leave in book pockets, on the dashboard, in your travel kit.
>
> *[pause — show a spec sheet with all three specs side by side]*
>
> Total cost for all three sizes, printed by a vendor, is typically thirty to fifty dollars. Total cost if you have a home printer with decent paper is under ten. Time investment: an hour, including ordering and trimming.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One specification that matters across all three sizes: *the placement convention*. The 9 must be at the top, the 1 through 8 must run clockwise, and the spacing must be even. Course-supplied PDFs already enforce this. If you generate your own — for instance, by printing a clean Symbol you've drawn — verify the placement against the course PDF before printing fifty copies. Misplaced numbers ship to fifty cards and disappoint."
