## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

**[ORG-CLIP]**

> *"A three-phase coil is a different animal from a single-strand coil. With three phases, you can drive each phase at a different timing — the same waveform, three temporal shifts apart. The result is a rotating field. Three-phase electrical power systems use this — that's how AC motors work. The Rodin three-phase coil takes the same idea and applies it to the doubling helix on a torus. Each family-group becomes one phase. Drive them with the right timing and you get a rotating field that follows the family triangles' rotation. That's the *VBM rotating field* — different from a standard three-phase rotating field because it follows the cycle structure rather than evenly-spaced angular positions. Whether the VBM rotating field has properties standard three-phase doesn't is a question for the bench. The build is real; the timing equipment is standard; the measurements are tractable. By the time you're winding a three-phase Rodin Coil, you're doing genuine experimental electromagnetic engineering."*
> — Randy Powell, paraphrased. **Source:** P1-7, P1-13.
