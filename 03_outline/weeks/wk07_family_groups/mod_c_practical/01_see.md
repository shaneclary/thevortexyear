## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "A three-phase Rodin Coil uses the three Family Number Groups as the three phases. Each family's vertices become one strand's start positions; each strand visits the doubling cycle independently.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Phase 1 — Family 1.* A strand starts at vertex 1, then visits 4, then 7. Three start positions in family one. Each start position spawns a Doubling-Circuit traversal. Three traversals make up phase 1.
>
> *Phase 2 — Family 2.* Strands start at 2, 5, 8. Three more traversals. Phase 2.
>
> *Phase 3 — Family 3.* Strands at 3, 6, 9. But family three's vertices aren't on the doubling cycle — 3 ↔ 6 oscillate, 9 is fixed. So phase 3 isn't a doubling-cycle traversal; it's a *static* phase that oscillates rather than spirals.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What does this look like physically? Nine strands total — three per phase. Phase 1 and phase 2 strands wind the doubling helix from different starting points; phase 3 strands oscillate at the 3-6 axis or stay at the 9 vertex.
>
> The three-phase build is *more complex* than the six-strand polar-pair build (Wk 35). Where the polar-pair build has six strands paired into three antipodal pairs, the family-phase build has nine strands organised into three triads.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> The family-phase build isn't part of the standard Wk-33/Wk-35 progression. It's an *advanced* build that the course covers in Wk 35's optional advanced section. We're previewing it now because the family structure makes the build's phasing rationale visible.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What three-phase windings give you. Three-phase RF circuits — like three-phase electrical power systems — can produce specific field structures that single-phase or two-phase systems can't. The three-phase Rodin Coil's promise is that the family-group structure gives a *VBM-specific* three-phase configuration that's distinct from standard three-phase electrical engineering.
>
> Whether the configuration produces measurable advantages over standard three-phase configurations is one of the engineering questions Q3 examines."
