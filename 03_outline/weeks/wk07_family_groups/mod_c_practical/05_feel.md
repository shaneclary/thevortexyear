## 0. Authoring notes
*Three is a different unit than pair.* Three-strand handwork as a different bodily skill than pair handwork.

## 1. Outcome
Student has spent quiet time with the bodily difference between two and three.

## 2. Hook (0:00–0:30)
*[Three coloured strands moving in synchrony.]*

> *(slow)* "Two hands. Three strands. One rhythm. Sit with that."

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30) — *contemplative*

> *(slow)* "Two strands divides naturally between two hands. Left and right. Each hand gets one. The arrangement is bilateral.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> Three strands has no such natural division. Two hands can't each take 1.5 strands. The arrangement is ternary, and ternary doesn't fit human bilaterality cleanly.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three-strand winding asks the body to *hold a rhythm of three* with two hands. Like a waltz: 1-2-3, 1-2-3, 1-2-3 — danced with two feet. The two-hand body manages a three-count by *cycling*, not by dividing.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> What this feels like in practice. The hand picks up strand A, advances it; picks up strand B, advances it; picks up strand C, advances it; then back to A. The cycle of three is the rhythm. The body learns to cycle in threes.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Musicians know this rhythm well. Three-against-two polyrhythms, waltz time, triplets in 4/4. The body can hold a three-count, but it requires a *different* kind of attention than two-counts.
>
> *[long pause — 12 seconds]*
>
> Marko's framing — *families as fields, three of them* — has this bodily echo. Three is not just *more than two*. Three is *a different unit*, with its own rhythm, its own bodily implications.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Mathematicians know this too. Cyclic groups of order 3 have *no nontrivial subgroups except the identity and themselves*. They're *prime-order* groups; they're *atomic* in a sense that order-2 and order-4 groups aren't. The number 3 is structurally distinguished.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Sit with the cycle of three. The three families. The three strands. The three-count rhythm.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> By Wk 35, when you wind a three-phase Rodin Coil with nine strands organised into three triads, the cycling-by-three rhythm will be in your body. The Symbol's family structure will have become a bodily skill.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three is a different unit. Two pairs are not the same as one triad. The body learns each rhythm separately."
