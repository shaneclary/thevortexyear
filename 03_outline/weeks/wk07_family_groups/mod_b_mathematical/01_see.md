## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The Family Number Groups have a clean algebraic description: they are the *cosets* of the subgroup 3ℤ/9ℤ in the additive group ℤ/9ℤ.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Vocabulary check.
>
> *Subgroup.* A subset of a group that's closed under the group operation and contains the identity. In ℤ/9ℤ, the subgroup *3ℤ/9ℤ* consists of {0, 3, 6} — the multiples of 3 modulo 9. (Recall: in our representation, 0 = 9, so the set is {3, 6, 9}.)
>
> *Coset.* For a subgroup *H* of a group *G* and an element *a* in *G*, the *left coset* *aH* is the set {*a + h* : *h* ∈ *H*}. Cosets partition the group into equal-sized subsets.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Compute the cosets of 3ℤ/9ℤ in ℤ/9ℤ.
>
> *Coset 0* (also written *0 + 3ℤ*): {0, 3, 6} = {3, 6, 9}. The subgroup itself, also called the *trivial coset*. **Family three.**
>
> *Coset 1* (also written *1 + 3ℤ*): {1, 4, 7}. Take 1 and add each element of {0, 3, 6}: *1+0=1, 1+3=4, 1+6=7*. **Family one.**
>
> *Coset 2* (also written *2 + 3ℤ*): {2, 5, 8}. Take 2 and add each: *2+0=2, 2+3=5, 2+6=8*. **Family two.**
>
> *[pause — show the three cosets on the Symbol]*
>
> Three cosets. Three families. Same partition.
>
> The cosets are determined by *residue modulo 3*. Coset 0 contains elements ≡ 0 (mod 3); coset 1 contains elements ≡ 1 (mod 3); coset 2 contains elements ≡ 2 (mod 3). This is just the standard residue partition mod 3, applied to elements of ℤ/9ℤ.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Why is this useful? Cosets are the *atoms* of group quotient structure. The quotient group *ℤ/9ℤ / (3ℤ/9ℤ)* is itself a group with three elements — isomorphic to ℤ/3ℤ. The Family Number Groups are the elements of this quotient group.
>
> So the family structure is *the quotient ℤ/9ℤ / 3ℤ*. Three elements, additive structure: *family 1 + family 2 = family 3*, *family 1 + family 1 = family 2*, etc. The families themselves form a small group of order 3.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One observation. The quotient structure isn't visible in the family-group enumeration alone. It's visible when you compose families: adding any element of family 1 to any element of family 2 gives an element of family 3 (always). This *additive closure* is what makes the families a quotient group, not just a partition."
