## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Open your notebook. Draw a 3×3 table. Label rows and columns *F₁, F₂, F₃* (or by representative: *1, 2, 0*). The entry at row *i*, column *j* is the family that contains *aᵢ + aⱼ* mod 9.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Compute each entry.
>
> *F₁ + F₁* (any element of F₁ plus any element of F₁): *1 + 1 = 2*. Vertex 2 is in F₂. Entry: F₂.
>
> *F₁ + F₂*: *1 + 2 = 3*. Vertex 3 is in F₃. Entry: F₃.
>
> *F₁ + F₃*: *1 + 0 = 1*. Vertex 1 is in F₁. Entry: F₁. (Adding the trivial coset to anything gives the original.)
>
> *F₂ + F₁*: *2 + 1 = 3*. F₃. Entry: F₃.
>
> *F₂ + F₂*: *2 + 2 = 4*. F₁. Entry: F₁.
>
> *F₂ + F₃*: *2 + 0 = 2*. F₂. Entry: F₂.
>
> *F₃ + F₁*: *0 + 1 = 1*. F₁. Entry: F₁.
>
> *F₃ + F₂*: *0 + 2 = 2*. F₂. Entry: F₂.
>
> *F₃ + F₃*: *0 + 0 = 0*. F₃. Entry: F₃.
>
> *[pause — show the completed 3×3 table]*
>
> Read the table.
>
> *F₃ is the identity element*. Adding F₃ to any family leaves it unchanged. (Because F₃ contains 0.)
>
> *F₁ + F₂ = F₃*. Family one and family two are *additive inverses* in the quotient group. They cancel to the identity.
>
> *F₁ + F₁ = F₂*. *F₁ + F₁ + F₁ = F₃*. So F₁ has order 3 in the quotient group.
>
> *F₂ + F₂ = F₁*. *F₂ + F₂ + F₂ = F₃*. F₂ also has order 3.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> The quotient group *ℤ/9ℤ / 3ℤ ≅ ℤ/3ℤ*. Three elements, cyclic, generated by either F₁ or F₂.
>
> The Family Number Groups, considered as elements of the quotient group, form a cyclic group of order 3. This is the *higher-level structure* on top of the partition.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One observation. The quotient group's identity is the family containing 0 — *family three*. That's why family three is special: it's the *identity* of the family quotient. Adding family three to anything is the identity operation.
>
> Marko's framing of family three as the *spirit field* aligns with this — family three is the *neutral* family, the one that doesn't move things. Mathematically: it's the identity coset."
