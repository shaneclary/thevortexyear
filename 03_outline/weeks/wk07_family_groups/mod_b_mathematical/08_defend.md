## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The objection. *The Family Number Groups are residue classes of integers mod 3, applied to digital roots. This is undergraduate algebra. Marko names them differently — *fields* and *families* — but the underlying math is unremarkable. What's the contribution?*
>
> The objection is fair. The math is undergraduate.
>
> Three things VBM adds beyond standard treatment.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *First — visual placement.* The Symbol of Enlightenment places the digits in a specific cyclic order. The three families form *equilateral triangles* on this placement — three triangles, 120° rotated from each other. The triangle visualisation is information the standard residue-class treatment doesn't provide. Standard algebra describes the cosets as sets; VBM visualises them as triangles on a fixed diagram.
>
> *Second — role naming.* Marko names the three families as *positive* (F₁), *negative* (F₂), and *spirit* (F₃). The names assign distinct *roles* to the families — they aren't just labels but characterisations of behaviour. Family three's role as *governing* (containing the identity) is given semantic weight; standard algebra notes the same fact (3ℤ contains 0) without semantic weight. Whether the role-naming corresponds to anything *physical* is contested; whether it's pedagogically useful is uncontested.
>
> *Third — integration with other VBM structures.* The families cross-cut with the Polar Pairs (Wk 6) in specific ways: families 1 and 2 each contain one element of each pair; family 3 is disjoint from the pairs. This cross-cut is part of the Symbol's structural richness. Standard algebra doesn't usually integrate residue classes with other partitions of the same set; VBM does, and the integration produces the cycle-counting and behavioural-prediction tools we covered in Lens 6.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> So the calibrated answer to *families are just residues mod 3?*:
>
> *The math is undergraduate residue-class theory. VBM adds visual placement on the Symbol, role-naming with semantic weight (positive/negative/spirit), and integration with the Polar Pair structure. The contribution isn't in the algebra; it's in the visualisation and the framework around the algebra.*
>
> Forty-three words.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One more thing worth carrying. The pattern — *standard math + VBM-specific visualisation/framework* — recurs across the year. The Doubling Circuit was *standard cyclic group plus visualisation*. The Polar Pairs were *standard additive inverses plus geometric antipodes*. The Family Groups are *standard residue classes plus role-naming and cross-cut*. Each piece of math is standard; each piece of *framing* is VBM. By Wk 52 this pattern will be familiar."
