## 0. Authoring notes
Family membership predicts which doubling-cycle component a number lives in. Family three numbers never enter the Doubling Circuit; family one and family two numbers do.

## 1. Outcome
Student can predict, from a number's family, which cycle-component it lives in under doubling.

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Family membership tells you, in advance, which doubling-cycle component a number will live in.
>
> Recall the doubling map's three components from Wk 4:
> - The big six-cycle through {1, 2, 4, 5, 7, 8}.
> - The two-cycle on {3, 6}.
> - The fixed point at 9.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Compare to the family structure:
> - Family one: {1, 4, 7}. All in the doubling circuit.
> - Family two: {2, 5, 8}. All in the doubling circuit.
> - Family three: {3, 6, 9}. Two in the 3-6 oscillation, one fixed at 9.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> The map is direct. *Numbers in families one and two enter the doubling cycle. Numbers in family three stay in the side cycles.*
>
> Practical use. Given any whole number, you can predict its doubling behaviour in three steps:
>
> 1. Reduce to digital root.
> 2. Identify family (residue mod 3 of the digital root).
> 3. Predict behaviour: family 1 or 2 → enters doubling cycle; family 3 → stays in side cycles.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Example. Take 1989 (the year). Digital root: *1 + 9 + 8 + 9 = 27 → 2 + 7 = 9*. Family three. Prediction: doubling 1989 keeps you at vertex 9 forever (since 9 is the fixed point of family three). Verify: *1989 × 2 = 3978*. Reduce: *3 + 9 + 7 + 8 = 27 → 9*. Yes, lands at 9.
>
> Example. Take 2026. Digital root: 1. Family one. Prediction: 2026 enters the doubling cycle. Verify: *2026 × 2 = 4052*. Reduce: *4 + 0 + 5 + 2 = 11 → 2*. Yes, the doubling moved 2026 from vertex 1 to vertex 2 — into the cycle.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> So the family structure is a *fast classifier* of numbers by behaviour. Once you've internalised the three families, you can predict any number's doubling fate in seconds.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One use case. Counting iterations of doubling on a long number. If you're working with a list of numbers and need to identify which ones would *escape* the doubling cycle (none — every digital root has a fixed cycle behaviour) versus which would *land at the fixed point 9*, family-three membership is the indicator. The family is the predictor."
