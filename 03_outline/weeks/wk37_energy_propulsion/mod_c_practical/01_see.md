> *Element 1 — calorimetric containment.* *Insulated chamber containing the coil and load*; *measures total thermal output as a check on electrical-only measurement*; *provides energy-balance closure pillar*. *Course-typical setup: no calorimetry*. *Lab-style: ~$500-$2000 calorimetric chamber or DIY insulated-box equivalent*.

> *Element 3 — calibrated load with thermal imaging.* *Resistive load with documented R(f) and thermal coefficient*; *thermal imaging or thermocouple monitoring*. *Course-typical: simple resistive load*. *Lab-style: instrumented load with thermal cross-check*.
>
> *Element 4 — dual-channel network analyser.* *Allows simultaneous measurement at two ports (input and output) over swept frequency*; *eliminates time-correlated artefacts*. *Course-typical: single-channel VNA*. *Lab-style: dual-channel VNA*.
>
> *Element 5 — EMI-shielded enclosure.* *Faraday-cage or anechoic-chamber containment*; *eliminates broadcast EM and ambient EMI from measurement*; *isolates ambient harvest loophole*. *Course-typical: open lab*. *Lab-style: shielded enclosure*.
>
> *Element 6 — multiple replicate measurements.* *Each measurement repeated 3-10 times*; *statistical analysis of variance*; *outlier detection*. *Course-typical: single measurement*. *Lab-style: replicate-with-statistics*.
>
> *Six elements; lab-style rigor specified*.
