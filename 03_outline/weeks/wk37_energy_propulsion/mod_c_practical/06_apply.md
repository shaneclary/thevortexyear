## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Replication produces a record. *Today: how to write it honestly*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Six sections of the replication record.*
>
> *Section 1 — Source citation.* *Original claim: who, when, where, what was claimed*. *Specific reference (video URL, article, lecture)*; *exact wording of original claim*; *configuration of original setup*. *Powell's pillar 2 (attribution); ~0.5-1 page*.
>
> *Section 2 — Setup specification.* *Replicator's reconstructed setup; documented deviations from source; reason for any deviations*; *coil specification, instrumentation, environment*. *Powell's pillar 1 (fidelity); ~2-3 pages*.
>
> *Section 3 — Methodology.* *Measurement protocol, calibration procedures, error analysis, replicate-count, environment-control measures*. *Powell's pillar 3 (methodology equal-or-better); ~1-2 pages*.
>
> *Section 4 — Results.* *Per-frequency, per-condition measurements; raw data; computed quantities; uncertainty per measurement*. *~3-5 pages with tables and figures*.
>
> *Section 5 — Conclusions.* *Honest assessment of what was found vs. what was claimed*; *agreement / disagreement / ambiguity; magnitude of difference; possible explanations for differences (methodology, environment, build deviations)*. *Powell's pillar 4 (honest reporting); ~1-2 pages*.
>
> *Section 6 — Limitations and follow-up.* *What this replication does not establish; what additional measurement would clarify*; *replication targets for future work*. *~0.5-1 page*.
>
> *Six sections; total ~8-15 pages*; *engineering replication record*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three honesty-in-record principles.*
>
> *Principle one — record raw data, not just summaries.* *Appendix includes original measurements (V, I, T, etc.) before computational manipulation*; *raw data is immune to summary bias*; *replicators-of-the-replicator can re-analyse*; *engineering transparency*.
>
> *Principle two — state what didn't go right.* *Calibration failures noted; instruments that drifted; sessions that had to be repeated; assumptions that proved wrong*; *engineering process honest, not airbrushed*.
>
> *Principle three — distinguish observation from interpretation.* *'Closure check shows 8% gap' is observation*; *'closure gap suggests measurement uncertainty rather than anomaly' is interpretation*; *both reported, distinguished*; *engineering precision*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three potential outcomes and replication-record framings.*
>
> *Outcome 1 — confirms source claim.* *Framing*: *'Replicated [source]'s claim of [specific result] under [reconstructed conditions]; measured [matching result] within [uncertainty]; consistent with claim'*. *Engineering implication*: *one positive replication; community confidence increment*.
>
> *Outcome 2 — disconfirms source claim.* *Framing*: *'Attempted replication of [source]'s claim of [specific result] under [reconstructed conditions]; measured [different result]; possible explanations include [methodology, environment, build differences]; claim not confirmed at this configuration'*. *Engineering implication*: *one disconfirming data point; calibrates community expectation*.
>
> *Outcome 3 — replication not feasible.* *Framing*: *'Attempted to identify Quadrant-1 catalog claim suitable for practitioner-bench replication; documentation gaps in [specific claims]; instrument requirements beyond bench in [other claims]; replication not currently feasible at this scope'*. *Engineering implication*: *foundation feedback on documentation gaps; engineering-honest scope acknowledgment*.
>
> *Three outcomes; all engineering contributions*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three engineering uses of the replication record.*
>
> *Use one — community knowledge.* *Replication records combined produce engineering literature on Rodin Coil claims*; *aggregate knowledge*; *foundation may collect and curate*. **`[ORG-INPUT]`** for foundation policy.
>
> *Use two — capstone integration.* *Replication record is capstone-quality engineering deliverable*; *Wk 50-52 capstone work uses it as input or extension*.
>
> *Use three — professional credibility.* *Documented replication work demonstrates engineering professionalism*; *CV-quality engineering deliverable*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three engineering judgments at record stage.*
>
> *Judgment one — completeness vs. brevity.* *Engineering record needs sufficient detail for replicators-of-the-replicator*; *too brief: insufficient for community use; too verbose: lost in detail*; *engineering balance*.
>
> *Judgment two — interpretation calibration.* *Conclusions should match measurements with appropriate uncertainty*; *no over-claim either direction; no over-qualifying that buries result*; *engineering precision*.
>
> *Judgment three — sharing or holding.* *Replication record may be shared with foundation, VBM community, broader engineering community, or held private*. **`[ORG-INPUT]`** for canonical sharing policy.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Apply replication record as 6-section engineering deliverable: source, setup, methodology, results, conclusions, limitations. Three honesty principles (raw data, what-didn't-go-right, observation-vs-interpretation). Three outcome framings (confirm, disconfirm, not-feasible). Three uses (community knowledge, capstone, professional credibility). Three judgments (completeness, calibration, sharing).* *Engineering deliverable per Powell's four pillars*."
