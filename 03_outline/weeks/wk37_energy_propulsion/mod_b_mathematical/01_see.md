## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Energy claims need quantitative analysis. *Today: introduce the energy-balance diagram as the mathematical tool*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What an energy-balance diagram shows.*
>
> *An energy-balance diagram is a labelled flow diagram showing all input and output energy streams of a system over a time interval*.
>
> *Standard convention*: *system represented by a box; energy inputs as arrows entering; energy outputs as arrows exiting; each arrow labelled with energy quantity (joules, watts) and source/destination*.
>
> *Conservation expressed*: *sum of inputs = sum of outputs + change in stored energy*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Six energy streams of a Rodin Coil setup.*
>
> *Stream 1 — electrical input (P_in).* *Power supplied by signal generator or amplifier; measured at coil feed terminals; typically positive (into system)*.
>
> *Stream 2 — radiated electromagnetic output (P_rad).* *Power radiated as EM waves into space; positive (out of system)*; *measurable indirectly via received-power on probe*.
>
> *Stream 3 — thermal loss (P_loss).* *Power dissipated as heat in conductors and dielectrics; positive (out of system as low-quality thermal energy)*; *measurable via calorimetry or temperature rise*.
>
> *Stream 4 — output to load (P_load).* *Power delivered to load (resistor, lamp, antenna, etc.)*; *positive (out of system)*; *measurable at load terminals*.
>
> *Stream 5 — ambient energy input (P_ambient).* *Power harvested from ambient sources (cosmic noise, broadcast EM, thermal environment)*; *typically near-zero for laboratory setup; can be positive in unusual conditions*; *measurable indirectly via comparison setups*.
>
> *Stream 6 — change in stored energy (ΔE_stored).* *Change in energy stored in coil's reactive elements (L, C); over a long time average, ΔE_stored = 0; over short transients, can be nonzero*; *measurable via instantaneous voltage and current product integrated over time*.
>
> *Six streams; energy-balance frame complete*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *The energy-balance equation.*
>
> *Conservation*: *P_in + P_ambient = P_rad + P_loss + P_load + dE_stored/dt*.
>
> *Steady state* (averaged over many cycles): *P_in + P_ambient = P_rad + P_loss + P_load*.
>
> *Conventional measurement* (no ambient): *P_in = P_rad + P_loss + P_load*.
>
> *Apparent overunity claim* (output appears > input): *typically arises when measured P_load > measured P_in*; *requires investigation of P_ambient (was ambient harvested?), P_loss measurement, P_rad measurement*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three things energy-balance diagram makes visible.*
>
> *Visible 1 — all streams.* *Forces accounting of every energy flow*; *no hidden flows*; *engineering rigour*.
>
> *Visible 2 — measurement points.* *Each arrow corresponds to a specific measurement*; *measurement protocol fully specified by diagram*; *engineering specification.*
>
> *Visible 3 — claim location.* *Apparent-overunity claim points to specific stream where conventional measurement is questionable (typically P_in or P_load)*; *targeted investigation possible*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three engineering uses of energy-balance diagrams.*
>
> *Use one — claim evaluation.* *Public claim of overunity is evaluated by drawing its diagram and identifying which stream is inadequately characterised*; *engineering analysis tool*.
>
> *Use two — measurement protocol design.* *Comprehensive measurement protocol covers all six streams*; *energy-balance diagram is the protocol-specification document*; *engineering design.*
>
> *Use three — replication standardisation.* *Different practitioners drawing energy-balance diagrams in same convention can compare their methodologies*; *replication community-coherent*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three caveats on energy-balance.*
>
> *Caveat one — quasi-instantaneous vs. cycle-averaged.* *Some claims involve transient effects (e.g., output spike during particular phase of cycle)*; *cycle-averaged balance may obscure transient*; *both timescales sometimes need analysis*.
>
> *Caveat two — narrow-band vs. broadband.* *Some 'output' may be in frequency bands not accounted for by narrow-band measurement*; *broadband measurement (or specific multi-band) may be required*; *engineering-instrument capability*.
>
> *Caveat three — coupling complexity.* *Multi-strand coils have complex internal energy flows between strands*; *full balance may require strand-level resolution*; *engineering complexity scales with coil structure*.
>
> *[pause]*
>

>

>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *See energy-balance diagrams as the mathematical tool for evaluating energy claims. Six streams (electrical input, radiated EM, thermal loss, load output, ambient input, stored-energy change); conservation equation; three uses (claim evaluation, protocol design, replication standardisation); three caveats (timescale, frequency-band, coupling complexity).* *Mathematical machinery for energy-claim engagement*."
