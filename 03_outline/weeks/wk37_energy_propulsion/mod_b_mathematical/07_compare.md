## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Energy claims may survive in some forms but not others. *Today: classify*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three loopholes (claim-consistent-with-conservation paths).*
>
> *Loophole 1 — ambient energy harvesting.* *Coil structure receives EM, thermal, or mechanical energy from environment*; *output power exceeds nominal 'input' power because environmental input is real but unmeasured*; *no conservation violation*; *engineering distinct from overunity*.
>
> *Engineering test*: *measure ambient power at test location with no coil; compare to claimed output*; *if ambient ≥ claimed-overunity, loophole consistent*. *Quantitative: per Lens 2's bound (~1 mW typical urban), accommodates only sub-mW claims*.
>
> *Engineering implication*: *small-power overunity claims (sub-mW) could be ambient harvesting; large-power claims (multi-watt) cannot*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Loophole 2 — narrow-band concentration.* *Coil acts as narrow-band filter on broadband ambient*; *concentrates broadband ambient into measurement-frequency band*; *appears to amplify but is filtering*; *no conservation violation*.
>
> *Engineering test*: *measure ambient power-spectrum-density vs. coil's measurement-bandwidth filtering characteristic*; *integrated ambient × filter shape ≈ apparent 'extra' power*.
>
> *Engineering implication*: *narrow-band concentration explains some apparent overunity in narrow-band measurements; broadband measurement disambiguates*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Loophole 3 — thermal/mechanical conversion.* *Coil converts environmental thermal energy or mechanical vibration to electrical*; *thermoelectric, piezoelectric, magnetostrictive analogues*; *no conservation violation*.
>
> *Engineering test*: *measure thermal gradient or mechanical vibration at coil location; compute extractable energy via known conversion mechanisms; compare to apparent 'extra' power*.
>
> *Engineering implication*: *plausible only at very small power scales (mW to µW)*; *typical Rodin Coil claims exceed this*; *limited applicability*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Two direct conflicts (require extraordinary evidence).*
>
> *Conflict 1 — true overunity from nothing.* *Output power exceeds all measurable input streams (electrical input + ambient) with no environmental energy source identifiable*; *would require new physics admitting energy creation*.
>
> *Engineering status*: *no rigorous evidence of this in current catalog*; *requires extraordinary evidence; current evidence does not constitute extraordinary*.
>
> *Engineering implication*: *cannot rule out in principle*; *can rule in only with extraordinary methodology + replication*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Conflict 2 — momentum violation without ejection.* *Net force on coil-system exceeds force on environment from any identifiable interaction; coil moves without pushing external mass*; *would require new physics admitting net momentum creation*.
>
> *Engineering status*: *no rigorous evidence in current catalog*; *requires extraordinary evidence; current evidence does not constitute extraordinary*.
>
> *Engineering implication*: *cannot rule out in principle*; *can rule in only with extraordinary methodology + replication*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three engineering moves the loophole/conflict comparison enables.*
>
> *Move one — claim classification.* *Each catalog entry classified by which loophole(s) could conservatively explain it OR which direct conflict it directly invokes*; *engineering categorisation*.
>
> *Move two — measurement focus.* *If loophole-applicable, measurement focuses on ambient quantification (loophole 1), broadband vs. narrow-band (loophole 2), or thermal/mechanical environment (loophole 3)*; *measurement design specific*.
>
> *Move three — verdict calibration.* *Loophole-consistent claims have lower extraordinary-evidence bar; direct-conflict claims have higher bar*; *catalog verdicts (Lens 2) reflect this*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What the comparison establishes.*
>
> *Establishment one — most plausible-conservative explanations of overunity claims involve loopholes, not direct conservation violation*. *Engineering parsimony favours loophole-explanations where applicable*; *direct-conflict explanations require extraordinary evidence*.
>
> *Establishment two — for high-power claims (multi-watt overunity), loopholes have insufficient capacity*; *high-power claims are direct-conflict territory*; *engineering bar correspondingly high*.
>
> *Establishment three — current catalog evidence does not constitute extraordinary*. *Documentation gaps, replication scarcity, methodology questions all reduce evidence quality*; *neither loophole nor direct-conflict claims are currently engineering-established*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three engineering judgments at compare stage.*
>
> *Judgment one — apply parsimony cautiously.* *Engineering parsimony favours simpler explanations; loopholes are simpler than direct conservation violation*; *but parsimony is heuristic, not logic*; *measurement settles when methodology is rigorous*.
>
> *Judgment two — claim-magnitude maps to evidence-bar.* *Sub-mW claims need less extraordinary evidence than multi-watt claims*; *engineering bar scales with claim magnitude*; *engineering proportionality*.
>
> *Judgment three — both sides honestly engaged.* *Acknowledge what each loophole could explain and what each direct conflict would require*; *honest two-sided engagement*; *engineering integrity*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Compare loopholes (ambient harvest, narrow-band concentration, thermal/mechanical conversion — conservation-consistent) with direct conflicts (true overunity, momentum violation — require extraordinary evidence). Engineering moves: claim classification, measurement focus, verdict calibration. Most plausible-conservative explanations favour loopholes where applicable; high-power claims are direct-conflict territory; current catalog evidence is not extraordinary.* *Honest two-sided engagement.*"
