## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The challenge. *Vague claims and vague evidence both corrupt engineering. 'Coil shows energy gain' is vague; 'coil shows 25% output-greater-than-measured-input at 1 MHz with single-sample measurement' is specific. Engineering requires the second. Defend the specificity ethic.*
>
> *The challenge is engineering's fine-grained discipline*; *the reply is the specificity framework*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *The honest engineering defence.*
>
> *Engineering integrity requires that claim-language and evidence-language be specific. The four questions — what was measured, with what precision, under what conditions, replicable — must be answered for any claim to be engineering-evaluable. Vague claims and vague evidence both fail integrity; both undermine cumulative engineering knowledge; both are correctable by specificity discipline.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Question 1 — what was measured?*
>
> *Specific*: *'P_load measured as RMS voltage across 50-Ω resistive load at 1 MHz'*.
>
> *Vague*: *'output power'*.
>
> *Engineering test*: *given the measurement description, can a second practitioner reproduce the measurement?* *If yes, specific*; *if no, vague*.
>
> *Specificity components*: *physical quantity (voltage, current, power)*; *integration mode (RMS, peak, mean)*; *frequency or bandwidth*; *load specification*; *measurement instrument*; *temperature, etc.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Question 2 — within what precision?*
>
> *Specific*: *'P_load = 2.5 ± 0.13 W (95% confidence)'*.
>
> *Vague*: *'about 2.5 W'*.
>
> *Engineering test*: *is uncertainty quantified per source and propagated?* *If yes, specific*; *if no, vague*.
>
> *Specificity components*: *instrument-precision contribution*; *systematic error sources*; *combined uncertainty per standard error-propagation*; *confidence level stated*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Question 3 — under what conditions?*
>
> *Specific*: *'at 1 MHz operating frequency, coil at 50 cm height above outdoor ground plane, 22°C ambient, no broadcast tower within 5 km, environment EMI background measured at -110 dBm/Hz'*.
>
> *Vague*: *'in the lab'*.
>
> *Engineering test*: *can someone determine whether their measurement conditions match yours?* *If yes, specific*; *if no, vague*.
>
> *Specificity components*: *test frequency / waveform / drive level*; *environment (location, surroundings, EMI, weather)*; *coil configuration (which strand fed, etc.)*; *temperature; humidity*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Question 4 — replicable?*
>
> *Specific*: *'Test setup specification document available; methodology document available; raw data available; second practitioner with same setup expected within ±2 dB'*.
>
> *Vague*: *'others should be able to do the same'*.
>
> *Engineering test*: *is the path from claim to replicable test setup explicit?* *If yes, specific*; *if no, vague*.
>
> *Specificity components*: *availability of setup spec; methodology document; raw-data archive; expected reproducibility tolerance; replication targets identified*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three things specificity ethic enables.*
>
> *Enable one — peer-review-friendly engagement.* *Specific claims can be peer-reviewed*; *vague claims can be praised or dismissed but not engineering-engaged*; *engineering progress through specificity*.
>
> *Enable two — refinement and update.* *Specific claims can be refined as evidence accumulates*: *'60% efficiency at f_res with 6-strand multi-strand build at 1.5 m above ground'* is more refinable than '60% efficiency'*; *engineering knowledge precision improves*.
>
> *Enable three — community knowledge accumulation.* *Specific claims combine into engineering literature; aggregate measurements triangulate; community knowledge advances*; *vague claims don't aggregate*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three things specificity prevents.*
>
> *Prevent one — over-claiming through ambiguity.* *Claims that gain plausibility through vagueness lose plausibility under specificity*; *engineering integrity preserved*.
>
> *Prevent two — under-engagement through over-generality.* *Claims dismissed as 'implausible' on their general form may be engineering-tractable in specific form*; *honest engagement through specificity*.
>
> *Prevent three — community confusion.* *Specific claims have unambiguous interpretation*; *vague claims invite multiple incompatible interpretations*; *community discourse clearer with specificity*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What can be defended.*
>
> *Defend 1.* *Engineering integrity requires specificity in both claim and evidence*. *Defendable*.
>
> *Defend 2.* *Four-question framework (what, precision, conditions, replicable) is comprehensive*. *Defendable*.
>
> *Defend 3.* *Specificity enables peer review, refinement, community knowledge accumulation*. *Defendable*.
>
> *Defend 4.* *Vague language harms both believers and skeptics by preventing engineering engagement*. *Defendable*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What can't be defended.*
>
> *Can't 1.* *Specificity-as-pedantry.* *Specificity that exceeds engineering need is wasted effort*; *appropriate specificity matches claim-magnitude and engineering context*; *not over-specification*.
>
> *Can't 2.* *Specificity-without-honesty.* *Specific-but-fabricated detail is worse than honest vagueness*; *specificity ethic requires honesty foundation*.
>
> *Can't 3.* *Specificity-claims-validity.* *Specific claims aren't automatically true; specificity is necessary but not sufficient for engineering claim*; *engineering integrity proper*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Honest articulation to a sceptical engineer.*
>
> *Articulation.* 'Engineering integrity requires specificity in both claim-language and evidence-language. For any energy or propulsion claim, four questions must be answerable: what was measured (physical quantity, integration mode, instrumentation), within what precision (uncertainty quantified and propagated), under what conditions (frequency, configuration, environment), and replicable (setup documented, methodology available, expected reproducibility tolerance). Specific claims can be peer-reviewed, refined, and combined into engineering literature; vague claims cannot. Vague language harms believers and skeptics equally by preventing engineering engagement. Specificity is necessary for engineering integrity; specificity-with-honesty is the ethic; over-specification beyond engineering need or specificity-without-honesty are not engineering virtues.'
>
> Around 130 words.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Calibrated answer.*
>
> *Be specific. What was measured? Within what precision? Under what conditions? Replicable? Specificity enables peer review, refinement, community knowledge. Vague claims and vague evidence both fail engineering integrity. Specificity is necessary; specificity-with-honesty is the ethic.*
>
> Around 50 words.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Defend specificity as engineering-integrity-required. Four questions: what, precision, conditions, replicable. Three enables (peer review, refinement, community knowledge); three prevents (over-claiming, under-engagement, confusion).* *Specificity-with-honesty is the engineering ethic.*"
