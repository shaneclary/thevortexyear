## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Today's build is a *constructive derivation*. Starting from the two generators 2 and 10 acting on ℤ/9ℤ, we build the 6×24 grid step by step.
>
> Open your notebook. Title the page *Number Map — generator construction*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Setup.* Two generators:
>
> *Vertical generator: g_v = 2.* Acts on ℤ/9ℤ by multiplication: g_v(x) = 2x mod 9.
>
> *Horizontal generator: g_h = 10.* Acts on ℤ/9ℤ by multiplication: g_h(x) = 10x mod 9 = x mod 9 (because 10 ≡ 1).
>
> The starting cell holds the value 1 (the multiplicative identity).
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Step 1 — vertical action.* Apply g_v repeatedly starting from 1:
>
> g_v(1) = 2.
>
> g_v(2) = 4.
>
> g_v(4) = 8.
>
> g_v(8) = 16 ≡ 7.
>
> g_v(7) = 14 ≡ 5.
>
> g_v(5) = 10 ≡ 1.
>
> The orbit closes after six applications. *Six rows, generated by g_v acting on the starting value 1.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Step 2 — horizontal action.* Apply g_h to any cell value:
>
> g_h(1) = 1.
>
> g_h(2) = 2.
>
> g_h(4) = 4.
>
> Etc.
>
> The horizontal generator is the identity on ℤ/9ℤ. Each application returns the same value.
>
> *Horizontal orbit length is 1* — the trivial orbit.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Step 3 — combined action.* The grid is the *Cartesian product* of the two orbits:
>
> Rows: 6 distinct values (orbit of g_v).
>
> Columns: 1 distinct value (trivial orbit of g_h).
>
> Total distinct cell values: 6 × 1 = 6. Total cells: 6 × 24 = 144 (with repetitions).
>
> *Each row contains 24 copies of the same value*, because the horizontal generator doesn't change values. *Six distinct values total*, one per row.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Step 4 — Cayley-diagram interpretation.* The grid can be seen as a *Cayley diagram* of the action of the abelian group ⟨g_v, g_h⟩ on ℤ/9ℤ. Vertices are cells; edges between adjacent cells correspond to applications of g_v (vertical edges) or g_h (horizontal edges).
>
> *Cayley structure.*
>
> Vertical edge from (r, c) to (r+1, c): cell value goes from 2ʳ to 2ʳ⁺¹ — it's actually multiplied by 2.
>
> Horizontal edge from (r, c) to (r, c+1): cell value stays the same.
>
> So the *vertical edges encode the doubling action*; the *horizontal edges encode the trivial scale action*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Step 5 — generalisation insight.* If g_h were *not* trivial (e.g., we changed the modulus or the base), the horizontal edges would carry non-trivial action, the rows would not be constant, and the grid would be richer.
>
> *Wk 16 will replace the horizontal generator with the Fibonacci recurrence*, which has period 24 mod 9. The replacement makes the horizontal direction non-trivial. The grid's *full content* will then emerge.
>
> Today's grid is the *trivial-horizontal-generator* version. *Wk 16's grid is the rich version.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three observations.
>
> *Observation one.* The grid's structure is *forced* by the choice of generators. Different generators produce different grids.
>
> *Observation two.* The *Cayley-diagram* framing connects the grid to mainstream group theory. The Number Map is a Cayley diagram of an abelian group acting on a small ring.
>
> *Observation three.* The grid is *complete* — every cell is reachable from the starting cell via finite combinations of g_v and g_h. The construction terminates at finite period.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *The grid's cells are not arbitrary entries; they are the orbit of a starting element under a generated group action.* This framing supports both the visualisation and the upcoming wrap to a torus."
