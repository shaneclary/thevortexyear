## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The mathematical grid you've drawn (Mod A) and proved (Mod B) becomes a *coil-layout chart* with a few engineering annotations layered on. Today's lens shows what the chart looks like in working form.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Six annotation layers* turn the math grid into a builder's chart.
>
> *Annotation 1 — angular positions.* Above the grid, label each column 0 through 23 with the *angular position* on the torus. Column 0 = 0°. Column 1 = 15° (since 360°/24 = 15°). Column 12 = 180°. Column 23 = 345°.
>
> *Annotation 2 — strand assignments.* For a six-strand coil, each row corresponds to a strand. Label rows 0-5 with strand identifiers (e.g., S0 through S5, or by colour: red, orange, yellow, green, blue, violet).
>
> *Annotation 3 — wind sequence numbers.* Number the cells in the order they're wound. Starting at column 0, row 0 = 1. Move to column 1, row 1 (the next row in the doubling cycle) = 2. And so on. The wind-sequence number tells the builder which cell to wind next.
>
> *Annotation 4 — traversal markers.* For a four-traversal coil, mark which traversal each cell belongs to. Traversal 1: columns 0-23 with one row pattern. Traversal 2: columns 0-23 with the next row pattern. (The doubling cycle of period 6 shifts by one row per 24-column traversal, so 4 × 6 = 24 cells per column requires 4 traversals.)
>
> Wait — let me recount. Six strands × twenty-four columns = 144 cell-positions per traversal. Four traversals × 144 = 576 wind events total. Distinguish *cell positions* (geometric) from *wind events* (each strand passing through each position).
>
> *Annotation 5 — termination markers.* Where each strand starts (terminal A) and ends (terminal B). On a typical Rodin Coil, all six strands enter at one position and exit at another, with their wind paths interleaved in between.
>
> *Annotation 6 — physical orientation reference.* An arrow showing the torus's orientation in space — *up*, *outer-edge-of-doughnut*, *winding-direction*. Without orientation, the chart is ambiguous.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What the annotated chart looks like.* The 6×24 grid, now densely annotated, is what Powell's bench refers to as a *winding chart*. It has:
>
> Math content (the cell values and the doubling cycle).
>
> Engineering content (angular positions, strand identifiers, sequence numbers, traversal markers, terminations, orientation).
>
> The engineering content layers *on top of* the math; it doesn't change the math.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three observations.
>
> *Observation one.* The annotations are *modular*. You don't need them all for every coil. Single-strand coils don't need strand colour-codes; they need strand identifiers only.
>
> *Observation two.* The annotations are *standard*. Different builders may use different colours for strands but the structural pattern of annotations is consistent across the foundation's published charts. Charts are exchangeable.
>
> *Observation three.* The annotations are *added at the bench*, not at the design table. The math grid is universal; the engineering chart is *build-specific*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Don't try to add all the engineering annotations today.* Wk 33 will introduce them in the context of the canonical six-strand coil build. Today's lens is a preview — see what the chart will become — not a deliverable.
>
> Tape the un-annotated grid into your build log alongside the bookmark from Wk 14. The grid is now your reference; engineering decoration comes later."
