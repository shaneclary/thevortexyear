## 0. Authoring notes

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Today's build is the slow, deliberate filling of the grid you scaffolded last week. The act of writing each cell — even though every row holds a single repeated value — embeds the structure in your hand and eye.
>
> The exercise will take fifteen to twenty minutes. Don't rush it.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Materials.* Your build log open to the scaffolded 6×24 grid. A sharp pencil. Optional: coloured pencils for family-coding.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Step 1 — fill row 0.* Write *1* in every cell of row 0. Twenty-four ones, left to right. Do them all; don't shortcut by writing a few and assuming the rest.
>
> *Step 2 — fill row 1.* Twenty-four twos.
>
> *Step 3 — fill row 2.* Twenty-four fours.
>
> *Step 4 — fill row 3.* Twenty-four eights.
>
> *Step 5 — fill row 4.* Twenty-four sevens. (Note: 16 mod 9 = 7.)
>
> *Step 6 — fill row 5.* Twenty-four fives. (Note: 32 mod 9 = 5.)
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Step 7 — colour-code by family.*
>
> Use one colour to lightly shade rows 0, 2, 4. These are family 1 — values 1, 4, 7.
>
> Use a different colour to shade rows 1, 3, 5. These are family 2 — values 2, 8, 5.
>
> Leave it uncoloured (or shade in a third colour) where rows would belong to family 3 — *but no rows hold family-3 values, so this third colour goes unused on this grid*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Step 8 — annotate.*
>
> In the right margin: row 0 = 2⁰; row 1 = 2¹; row 2 = 2²; row 3 = 2³; row 4 = 2⁴ ≡ 7; row 5 = 2⁵ ≡ 5.
>
> In the top margin: column c = 10ᶜ ≡ 1 (with the note that column index doesn't change cell values).
>
> In the bottom margin: family colour key.
>
> *Step 9 — title and date.* Title the page *Wk 15 — The Rodin Number Map (filled)*. Date it.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What you've drawn. *144 cells filled by hand, colour-coded by family, with the row, column, and family annotations in the margins.* The grid is now in your build log as a working reference.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three observations on the act.
>
> *Observation one.* Filling repeated values feels *tedious*. That's the point. The repetition burns the row-by-row structure into long-term memory in a way that print or computer-drawing wouldn't.
>
> *Observation two.* The colour coding makes the *family alternation* visible. Reading down the rows alternates between family 1 (rows 0, 2, 4) and family 2 (rows 1, 3, 5). The visual rhythm confirms what Q1's family-group analysis predicted.
>
> *Observation three.* The *blank* family-3 colour is itself informative. The fact that you have an unused colour in your legend signals that *something is missing from this grid*, and Wk 17-20 will introduce the structures that bring family 3 in.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *The hand-filled grid is the canonical Q2 reference page.* Pin it next to (or in the same area as) the integrated Symbol from Wk 13. The two diagrams are the *Q1 reference* (Symbol, 1D) and the *early-Q2 reference* (grid, 2D)."
