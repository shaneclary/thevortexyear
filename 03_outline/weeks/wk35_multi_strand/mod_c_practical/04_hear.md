> Powell, on multi-strand: *'When you wind multi-strand, every choice you make becomes part of the coil's specification. Strand count is a choice. Phase offsets are choices. Counter-winding is a choice. Wire diameter is a choice. Each choice has measurable consequences. The discipline of multi-strand engineering is making choices deliberately and documenting them so that someone else can replicate or modify your work.'*

> *Layer-1 framing — engineering practice*. *Defendable; mainstream engineering principle*.

> *Powell on the four major builder's choices.*
>
> *Choice one — strand count.* Powell: *'Three strands or six? Three for a family group; six for the full doubling cycle. Three is faster to build, lighter on measurement, easier to debug. Six is the full realisation but takes 5+ hours and 27 measurements. Pick by how much time you have and what you want to test.'*
>
> *Choice two — phasing.* Powell: *'Equispaced is the default and what we recommend for first multi-strand builds. Non-equispaced phasings exist for specialised purposes — beam-shaping for antenna work — but they're advanced. Don't depart from equispaced unless you know why.'*
>
> *Choice three — counter-winding.* Powell: *'Counter-winding adds common-mode rejection but increases build complexity. For straightforward characterisation, skip counter-winding on first multi-strand builds. Add it later when you have a specific application that benefits.'*
>
> *Choice four — wire material and gauge.* Powell: *'Course-canonical is 1.0 mm enamelled copper magnet wire. Heavier gauge for high-current applications; lighter for compact builds. Stick to canonical for replicability unless you have an application reason.'* **`[ORG-INPUT]`** for canonical wire spec.

> *Powell on documenting choices.*
>
> Powell: *'A multi-strand coil is a specification document plus a physical artefact. Without the document, the artefact can't be replicated, modified, or honestly compared with other builds. Build log columns: strand count, phase offsets per strand, counter-wind decision per strand, wire spec, anomalies per strand, final continuity check per strand, total session time, builder identification.
