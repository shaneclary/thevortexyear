## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "*Mainstream quantum computing.* A field of computer science / physics. Uses quantum-mechanical effects to perform computation. Uses qubits (2-state systems) as the basic unit. Major research programs at Google, IBM, IonQ, Microsoft, academic labs. *Tens of thousands of researchers; hundreds of billions invested.*
>
> *Decoqubit-inspired computing.* Speculative direction within VBM. Would use higher-dim toroidal-lattice structure as computing substrate. *No existing research program; speculative framings only.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three structural similarities.
>
> *Similarity one — both seek information-processing advantages from non-classical structures.* Mainstream QC uses quantum mechanics; Decoqubit uses higher-dim toroidal structure. Both step beyond standard classical computing.
>
> *Similarity two — both involve multi-state primitives.* QC: qubits with 2 basis states (or qudits with d states). Decoqubit: 10-state structural elements.
>
> *Similarity three — both have strong claims and active uncertainty.* Mainstream QC has demonstrated quantum supremacy in narrow tasks but full-scale quantum computers don't yet exist. Decoqubit-inspired computing has *no demonstrated implementations*; full claims await development.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three structural differences.
>
> *Difference one — research stage.* Mainstream QC is *mid-stage research* with demonstrated proofs-of-concept. Decoqubit-inspired computing is *concept-stage* with no implementations.
>
> *Difference two — physical basis.* Mainstream QC has clear physical implementations (superconducting qubits, trapped ions, photonics). Decoqubit-inspired computing has no clear physical implementation; it's structural / mathematical.
>
> *Difference three — peer review.* Mainstream QC publishes in peer-reviewed journals with thousands of citations. Decoqubit material is published primarily in foundation-tradition contexts.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What the comparison reveals.*
>
> *Reveal 1.* Mainstream QC and Decoqubit-inspired computing are *at different research stages*. Mainstream is engineering; Decoqubit is conceptual.
>
> *Reveal 2.* The two might converge, eventually. *If Decoqubit specifications mature into specific computing structures*, the field could engage them as alternative or supplementary computing models.
>
> *Reveal 3.* Currently, *Decoqubit-inspired computing isn't competitive with mainstream QC* in any specific demonstrated application. *Aspirations exceed implementations.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *How to discuss this honestly.*
>
> *Honest framing 1.* 'Decoqubit-inspired computing is a speculative research direction within VBM. It hasn't been implemented; mainstream QC is decades ahead in implementation.'
>
> *Honest framing 2.* 'If Decoqubit specifications mature, the framework could inform alternative computing architectures. Whether the resulting systems would compete with QC is unclear.'
>
> *Honest framing 3.* 'Mainstream QC and Decoqubit-inspired computing are at different stages and aren't directly comparable. Each engages different communities and traditions.'
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Useful framing. *Mainstream quantum computing is a mid-stage research program with demonstrated proofs-of-concept. Decoqubit-inspired computing is a concept-stage speculation within VBM. They share an interest in non-classical information processing but operate at different stages and rely on different principles. Direct comparison is currently asymmetric — mainstream has implementations; Decoqubit doesn't.*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Don't claim Decoqubit-inspired computing as competitive with mainstream QC.* It's not — yet. *Engage as research direction; track mainstream QC for context; don't overclaim.*
>
> One more practical note. Some VBM enthusiasts overclaim Decoqubit-inspired computing as superior to mainstream QC. *This is unsupported by evidence and harms the framework's credibility.* The honest engagement is *forward-looking exploration*, not *competitive replacement*."
