## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "A timeline of Marko Rodin's work, approximate dates.
>
> *Circa 1973.* Discovery moment. Marko at fifteen draws the Symbol. Begins documenting patterns.
>
> *1970s-80s.* Personal study and notebook accumulation. Marko refines the framework. No major publications.
>
> *Late 1980s-90s.* Marko begins giving public lectures and teaching workshops. The Rodin Coil concept emerges. Initial coil prototypes are built.
>
> *Late 1990s-2000s.* Internet age. VBM material spreads online via early websites and video. Followers form a community of independent practitioners.
>
> *2000s-2010s.* Randy Powell becomes a prominent advocate. TEDxCharlotte 2010 talk brings VBM to wider audience (later removed by TED, increasing its reach). Multiple builders worldwide replicate Rodin Coils.
>
> *2010s-2020s.* Continued growth in independent-research community. Marko publishes *Sanctified Mirrors* (2023) with co-author Art Tawanghar. Spirit Density Mapping framework introduced.
>
> *2026 (today).* VBM is a recognisable independent-research tradition with thousands of practitioners worldwide and an extensive online presence. Mainstream academic mathematics and engineering largely ignore the work. Marko continues teaching from Maui.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Major numerical milestones.
>
> *Time from discovery to public lectures*: roughly 15-20 years (1973 → late 1980s).
>
> *Time from discovery to first prominent advocate*: roughly 30 years (Powell era starts ~2003).
>
> *Time from discovery to Sanctified Mirrors*: 50 years (1973 → 2023).
>
> Marko's work has unfolded slowly. The framework discovered at fifteen has been refined over five decades.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One observation. The VBM community is *small but durable*. Without academic institutional support, the work has grown through independent practitioners, online communities, and a few advocates. The growth pattern is unusual for a mathematical framework — most academic mathematics either dies young or gets institutionalised within a decade. VBM's persistence outside academia is itself notable."
