> *"I was fifteen years old. I was studying the writings of Bahá'u'lláh. I came across the name Abha — the Most Great Name in the Bahá'í tradition — and noticed that under Abjad numerology, the letters reduced to the number nine. I wondered what would happen if I drew nine numbers around a circle. I took a piece of paper and a pencil. I drew. I started at the top with nine. I went clockwise — one, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight. Then I started doubling. One to two to four to eight, and then I had to reduce sixteen, which gave me seven, then five, then one. Six steps and I was back at the start. The line on the page was a figure-eight on its side. I sat looking at it. The infinity sign was on my page, drawn from the simplest possible operation. I had not invented it. I had only walked the path that the structure of the numbers forced. That afternoon at fifteen years old changed the rest of my life."*
> — Marko Rodin, paraphrased. **Source:** P1-1, P1-3.
