## 4. Body (1:00–4:30) — *contemplative*

> *(slow)* "Most mathematical discoveries happen to people who have spent decades in training.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> A fifteen-year-old usually hasn't.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> Marko, at fifteen, had not done a graduate degree. He had not read research papers. He had not been inducted into the consensus of mathematicians. He was a teenager studying scripture and playing with numbers.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What he found was a pattern. Drawn from one of the simplest possible operations on the smallest possible substrate. The pattern is the *figure-eight*. He had it on his page within an afternoon.
>
> *[long pause — 12 seconds]*
>
> What does this *feel* like, contemplatively? Some students experience an *opening* — discovery isn't gatekept by credentials. Anyone with a pencil and curiosity can draw the figure-eight. The math is accessible.
>
> Other students experience *suspicion* — surely real discovery requires more than a teenager's afternoon. Surely the Symbol's significance, if it has any, would have been picked up by professional mathematicians by now.
>
> Both responses are honest. The biographical fact — *Marko was fifteen* — doesn't itself argue for or against the work's significance. It just is.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Marko's framing emphasises the simplicity. The Symbol is *available* to anyone. He didn't invent it; he drew it; the drawing was forced by the operation. Anyone repeating the operation gets the same drawing.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> Sit with the simplicity. The Symbol is on your page right now (you just drew it in Lens 3). The same Symbol Marko drew at fifteen. The same figure-eight. The same closure.
>
> The math doesn't care about your age or credentials. The operation gives the same result to a fifteen-year-old and a fifty-year-old. The *significance* of the result is what's at stake; the result itself is universally available.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What you're sitting with is *democratic mathematics* — math that's accessible to anyone with paper and pencil. Most mathematics isn't this democratic. The Symbol is.
>
> Whether democratic mathematics is *as deep* as institutional mathematics is the question Marko's biography quietly poses. Today, we sit with the question without resolving it."
