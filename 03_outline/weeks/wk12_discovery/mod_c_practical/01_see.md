## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The diagram came first. The coil came next. By the late 1980s Marko had moved from circle-and-pencil into copper.
>
> What you're looking at — *or what the foundation's photograph will show* — is a hand-wound prototype. A wooden form, roughly grapefruit-sized, lathed into a torus shape. Magnet wire, AWG 22 or thereabouts, wound by hand around the form following the doubling-circuit path the diagram dictates.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Five things to notice.
>
> *One — the form is wood.* Not 3D-printed plastic. Not machined aluminium. Lathe-turned hardwood, available to anyone with a basic shop. The first prototype could be built without specialist equipment.
>
> *Two — the wire is single-strand magnet wire.* Insulated copper, single conductor, hand-soldered at the terminations. No proprietary materials. The coil is reproducible from a hardware-store parts list.
>
> *Three — the winding follows the doubling path on the surface.* Position 1, then 2, then 4, then 8, then 7, then 5, then back to 1 — traced *over* the wooden torus rather than just drawn on a flat circle. The diagram lifted off the page and onto a 3D surface.
>
> *Four — there's no electronics on the first build.* No drive circuit, no signal generator, no measurement gear. Just the wound coil, two leads, and Marko's hands. Electrical characterisation came later.
>
> *Five — the build is documented but not published as a kit.* Marko sketched his winding in his notebooks; he didn't release a shop drawing for years. The first coil was a *one-off*, not a product.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What this prototype is *not*. It is not the canonical six-strand, four-traversal Rodin Coil that the foundation now distributes. That came later, after refinement. The first coil was simpler — fewer strands, fewer turns, looser tolerances. It is the *seed* of the engineering, not the final form.
>
> What it *is*. It is the moment the math became hardware. Up to this point Marko had a diagram. From this point he had an object that other people could hold, photograph, measure, and reproduce.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One observation worth sitting with. The first coil was built by *one person*, by hand, without funding, without a lab. Every Rodin Coil that exists today traces back to that wooden form. The lineage is direct."
