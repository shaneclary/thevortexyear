## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "*Two hundred* is an estimate. The exact figure isn't catalogued, and the foundation will supply a ratified number for the production cut. **`[ORG-REVIEW]`**. But the order of magnitude is informative on its own.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Let's break the count.
>
> *Conference talks.* Marko has spoken at consciousness, alternative-physics, and sacred-geometry conferences for roughly thirty-five years. Estimate one to three appearances per year over that span — call it sixty to a hundred conference talks.
>
> *Workshops.* Multi-day workshops, taught directly, hands-on. Estimate one to four per year — call it thirty to fifty across his career.
>
> *Public lectures and demonstrations.* University talks, public-library appearances, regional gatherings. Estimate fifty to a hundred.
>
> *TEDxCharlotte 2010.* One — but with disproportionate reach because of the platform.
>
> *Podcast and interview appearances.* The largest category by raw count, and the one that grew fastest after 2015. Estimate sixty to a hundred and twenty.
>
> Add them. Two hundred is a defensible round number.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What does the *ratio* tell us?
>
> *Ratio one — talks per year.* Two hundred over thirty-five years is roughly six per year. Less than one per quarter. Marko is not a circuit speaker. He teaches when invited; he doesn't tour.
>
> *Ratio two — talks to publications.* Two hundred talks against eight to twelve major papers and books — twenty talks per publication, *roughly*. Marko's output is heavily oral. Most of what he has taught lives in recordings, not in print. This shapes what students inherit: video and audio dominate the corpus.
>
> *Ratio three — talks to coil prototypes.* Two hundred talks against several dozen documented coil builds in fifty years — call it ten talks per coil. The teaching scales faster than the hardware.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Why this matters for your study.
>
> *Most of the canonical material is recorded speech.* If you want depth, you watch and listen. The course's Lens 4 (*Hear from Marko*) leans on this corpus precisely because the corpus is *mostly* talks.
>
> *Quality varies across talks.* Two hundred lectures, given across thirty-five years, will not all be equally polished. The course has curated. Lens-4 clips are selected for clarity and accuracy, not for being his most-quoted moments.
>
> *Some talks contradict others.* Marko's framing has evolved across decades. When two clips disagree, the later one usually reflects his current view. The course flags such evolutions where they matter.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Two hundred talks* means you don't run out of primary material. It also means you choose carefully. The course has done that selection for you across the year — but you're free to dig deeper into the foundation's archive."
