## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

**[ORG-CLIP]**

> *"At fifteen, I drew the figure-eight. I did not yet have the word *axiom*. The first thing I wrote down — perhaps a year or two later, after staring at the diagram for many months — was the sentence *the doubling cycle is non-decaying*. That was my first axiom-like statement. *Non-decaying* — meaning the cycle goes forever without losing information. I came to that wording because I had run the doubling thousands of times and never seen the cycle break. The number of iterations a finite-state computation can survive without breaking — for a deterministic system, infinitely many. But I did not know group theory. I thought the cycle's permanence was a *physical fact*, not just a mathematical one. The first axiom — *the doubling cycle is non-decaying* — was simultaneously a math claim and a physics claim, in my mind. I have since learned to separate them. The math is forced; the physics is hypothesised. I write the axiom now with both layers, and let the listener distinguish."*
> — Marko Rodin, paraphrased. **Source:** P1-1, P1-3.
