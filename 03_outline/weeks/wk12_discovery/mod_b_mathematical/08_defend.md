## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The objection. *VBM's mathematical claims should be subject to scepticism because Marko isn't an academic mathematician. The math could be wrong; without peer review, we can't know.*
>
> The objection conflates two issues.
>
> *Issue one — is the math correct?* This question is *verifiable* by anyone with relevant mathematical knowledge. Reading the proofs and checking them is independent of credentials.
>
> *Issue two — is the framework significant?* This question depends on judgment about what mathematical work is *important* — which is partly social, requires institutional context, and is influenced by peer review.
>
> The two issues are different. Mainstream peer review primarily addresses *issue two* (gatekeeping for what's worth attention). Issue one (mathematical correctness) is *technically verifiable* by anyone who reads the proofs.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What's verified about VBM math.
>
> *The doubling cycle (1, 2, 4, 8, 7, 5).* Standard. Anyone with basic group theory can verify this is the orbit of 2 in `(ℤ/9ℤ)*`.
>
> *The polar pairs sum to 9.* Standard. Direct arithmetic.
>
> *The family groups are cosets of 3ℤ/9ℤ.* Standard. Direct group theory.
>
> *Scale invariance via 10ᵏ ≡ 1 mod 9.* Standard. Two-line induction proof.
>
> *Pisano period mod 9 is 24.* Standard. Computable.
>
> All of these are *verifiable*. None requires special credentials. Anyone with high-school or undergraduate math can check them.
>
> What's *not verified by mainstream channels.*
>
> *Whether VBM's framework reveals fundamental physical structure.* This is the contested interpretation. Different question.
>
> *Whether the Rodin Coil's claimed properties exceed standard EM theory's predictions.* This requires bench measurement, not mathematical verification.
>
> *Whether base 10 is natural law.* This is metaphysical and not subject to mathematical proof.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> So the calibrated answer to *the math could be wrong without peer review*:
>
> *The mathematical content — group theory, modular arithmetic, cyclic structure — is verifiable by anyone with appropriate training. It doesn't require Marko's credentials to be checked. The interpretive claims (physical, metaphysical) are different and properly subject to scepticism. The course teaches the verifiable math; presents interpretation as Marko's framework.*
>
> Forty-six words.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. When discussing VBM math with academic mathematicians, *invite them to verify* the math directly. The proofs are short and standard; verification doesn't require trust. *Distinguish* the math from the interpretive framework, which has different epistemic status."
