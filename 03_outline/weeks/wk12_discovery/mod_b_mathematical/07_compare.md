## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "*Mathematics discovered.* The Platonist view. Mathematical objects exist independently of human minds; mathematicians *discover* them rather than invent them. The figure-eight is *out there* (in some abstract sense); Marko found it.
>
> *Mathematics invented.* The constructivist or formalist view. Mathematical objects are constructions of human minds operating with formal systems. The figure-eight isn't *out there*; it's a consequence of human-chosen rules applied to human-chosen symbols.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three differences.
>
> *Difference one — origin of objects.* Platonist: objects pre-exist. Constructivist: objects are built.
>
> *Difference two — what mathematicians do.* Platonist: see what's there. Constructivist: build what's allowed.
>
> *Difference three — necessity vs. contingency.* Platonist: theorems are necessary truths. Constructivist: theorems are necessary given chosen axioms; axioms are themselves contingent.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Marko's biographical framing aligns with Platonism. *I drew what was already there.* The figure-eight existed before Marko found it; he just attended.
>
> Mainstream academic mathematics is mostly *non-committal* on the discovery-vs-invention question. Practitioners do mathematics without resolving the philosophy. Both views allow productive work.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Where the distinction matters for VBM.
>
> If math is *discovered*, then VBM's Symbol describes structure that exists independently of Marko. The structure has *natural* significance whether or not anyone notices it.
>
> If math is *invented*, then VBM's Symbol is a particular construction that's mathematically valid (the math works) but not necessarily *significant in some absolute sense*. Its significance depends on what we choose to do with it.
>
> Marko's stronger claims — that VBM describes physical reality, that base 10 is natural law — fit Platonism more naturally than constructivism. Constructivists could grant the math while resisting the metaphysical interpretation.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Useful framing. *Marko's discovery-style narrative aligns with mathematical Platonism. The math itself works under either philosophy. The interpretive claims (VBM as physical reality) are stronger if Platonism is correct; weaker if constructivism is.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. You don't need to settle the philosophy of mathematics to learn VBM. The math holds either way. Whether you interpret the math as *discovery of pre-existing structure* or *interesting consequence of chosen rules* affects how much weight you give to Marko's metaphysical claims, but doesn't change the math itself."
