## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Mod A engaged the Bahá'í-tradition foundation. Mod B engaged the math. Mod C turns to *the practical engineering of naming itself*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Naming in engineering.*
>
> Engineers regularly face naming decisions: *what to call a function, a class, a module, a device, a framework*. Each name has *consequences*: who uses it, how it's perceived, what audiences it attracts and repels.
>
> *VBM faced the same naming decision* with its central torus. Marko chose 'ABHA Torus'. *Today's lens engages that choice as engineering decision.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Four candidate names for the central VBM torus.*
>
> *Candidate 1 — ABHA Torus.* Bahá'í-tradition-rooted; biographical-honest; tradition-committed.
>
> *Candidate 2 — Vortex Torus.* Generic-sounding; secular-friendly; loses biographical-cultural information.
>
> *Candidate 3 — VBM-Torus.* Framework-internal; technical; disconnects from origins.
>
> *Candidate 4 — Marko-Torus.* Personal-attribution; loses tradition-context.
>
> *Each has trade-offs.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Trade-off analysis.*
>
> *ABHA Torus.*
>
> *Pros:* honors source; biographical-honest; symbolic-rich; aspirational.
>
> *Cons:* may alienate secular audiences; carries religious overlay; requires acknowledgment.
>
> *Vortex Torus.*
>
> *Pros:* secular-friendly; technically descriptive; broadly accessible.
>
> *Cons:* loses biographical-cultural context; flattens framework's character; risks anonymising Marko's path.
>
> *VBM-Torus.*
>
> *Pros:* technically clear; framework-internal coherence.
>
> *Cons:* erases historical-cultural origins; less memorable; abstract.
>
> *Marko-Torus.*
>
> *Pros:* attribution-clear.
>
> *Cons:* personalises what's larger than person; loses tradition; potentially uncomfortable for Marko.
>
> *Trade-offs make the choice non-trivial.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Marko's choice — ABHA Torus.*
>
> Why this choice over the alternatives?
>
> *Reason 1 — biographical truth.* The framework's seed *was* Bahá'í-numerology-mediated. *Generic naming would erase truth.*
>
> *Reason 2 — symbolic richness.* ABHA's superlative-glory meaning supports the torus's aspirational role. *Generic names lose this.*
>
> *Reason 3 — tradition-respect.* Naming honors source. *Marko commits to the cultural lineage.*
>
> *Reason 4 — distinctiveness.* 'ABHA Torus' is *memorable and distinctive*; generic names are forgettable.
>
> *Trade-offs accepted.* Some audience alienation; some religious overlay. *In exchange, biographical truth, symbolic richness, tradition respect, distinctiveness.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three observations on naming as design decision.*
>
> *Observation one — every name has trade-offs.* No naming option is *purely advantageous*. Engineering naming decisions involve choosing which trade-offs to accept.
>
> *Observation two — naming shapes audience.* The chosen name *partly determines who comes to the framework*. Bahá'í-rooted naming *invites students who can engage it; some students screen out*. *Audience-shaping is a design feature.*
>
> *Observation three — substitute names possible.* Engineers using the math under different names *don't break the math*. The name is invariant-under-substitution at the math level. *Practical accommodation built in.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Where Mod C goes.*
>
> Across nine lessons: *naming as design decision* (today); *count occurrences of '9' in Bahá'í texts* (Lens 2); *print the Abjad table; pin it for reference* (Lens 3); *Powell on respecting the spiritual lineage* (Lens 4); *naming as commitment* (Lens 5); *use the name as contemplative anchor* (Lens 6); *naming traditions in scientific work* (Lens 7); *builders free to use the math under any name* (Lens 8); *naming respected, build is yours* (Lens 9).
>
> *Mod C engages the practical / engineering side of naming consistently.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Naming is engineering decision; trade-offs matter.* Marko's ABHA Torus choice trades audience-alienation for biographical-truth, symbolic-richness, tradition-respect, distinctiveness. *Engineers may substitute names without breaking the math; the design decision can be revisited per audience.*"
