## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Mod B heard Marko on the naming choice; today: Powell on the *engineering practitioner's* posture toward the tradition origin.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *The question Powell engages.* How does an engineer — possibly secular, possibly from a different tradition, possibly indifferent to religious questions — *engage VBM's Bahá'í-rooted naming and origins respectfully*?
>
> *Powell's answer is nuanced.* Three principles run through it.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Principle one — acknowledge without adopting.*
>
> Engineers can *acknowledge* the framework's Bahá'í-tradition roots *without adopting* Bahá'í tradition themselves. *Acknowledge that Marko's path was Bahá'í-mediated; that the names trace to Bahá'í tradition; that 9 is institutionally important in Bahá'í tradition.* This is *factual acknowledgment*, not adoption.
>
> *Practical applied.* When introducing VBM to colleagues or students, *say where the names come from*. 'ABHA Torus is named after a Bahá'í-sacred word; the math is mod-9 modular arithmetic.' *Honest one-line summary; no need to elaborate.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Principle two — use the names as given.*
>
> When working *within VBM-tradition contexts* (with practitioners, in tradition-aware materials), *use the canonical names*. 'ABHA Torus, not Vortex Torus'. *Respects the tradition's vocabulary; honors Marko's choice; prevents confusion.*
>
> When working *outside VBM-tradition contexts* (with secular engineers, in technical specifications), *substitute names if useful*. 'A 9-fold-symmetric toroidal structure with X properties'. *Practical engineering communication; math is invariant.*
>
> *Both modes are legitimate; the key is awareness of audience.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Principle three — don't trivialise.*
>
> The names ABHA and Bahá-as-9 are *sacred in their tradition*. *Treating them casually — as mere labels, as decorative — is disrespectful.* Engineers may engage at math level without religious commitment, but *should not actively trivialise* the tradition source.
>
> *Practical applied.* Don't make jokes about ABHA. Don't dismiss Bahá → 9 as 'just numerology'. *Engage with the same respect you'd offer to any other tradition's sacred terms*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three things Powell's posture brings.*
>
> *Thing one — engineering accessibility.* Engineers who can acknowledge-without-adopting can use the framework. *No religious test for engagement.*
>
> *Thing two — tradition respect.* The framework's Bahá'í source is *honored*, not erased. *Practitioners of the tradition encounter respectful engineering.*
>
> *Thing three — community sustainability.* By respecting the tradition while remaining accessible, *VBM's community can include both Bahá'í practitioners and secular engineers* without conflict. *Mixed communities are sustainable when respect is mutual.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three practical examples of Powell's posture.*
>
> *Example one — the printed Abjad table* (Lens 3). The context note acknowledges Bahá'í provenance. *Honest disclosure; no trivialisation.*
>
> *Example two — substitute names in technical writing.* If publishing a paper on toroidal field structures using VBM math, *can use the math under any name*. The paper doesn't need to be about Bahá'í tradition unless the tradition is relevant.
>
> *Example three — collaborator dialogue.* When collaborating with someone unfamiliar with Bahá'í tradition, *introduce the naming honestly*. 'These names come from Bahá'í tradition through Marko Rodin's biography; the math is mainstream; we can use either canonical or substitute names depending on context.'
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What Powell's posture isn't.*
>
> *Isn't 1.* Religious-test engagement. *No requirement to adopt Bahá'í tradition.*
>
> *Isn't 2.* Erasure of origins. *No pretending the framework is religion-neutral.*
>
> *Isn't 3.* Trivialisation. *No casual use of sacred terms as decoration.*
>
> *Three things Powell's posture is*: *acknowledge, use-as-given-in-context, don't-trivialise*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Respect the spiritual lineage by acknowledging it, using canonical names within context, and avoiding trivialisation.* Engineers may engage at math level without religious commitment; the tradition source is honored, not erased. *Sustainable mixed-community engineering practice.*"
