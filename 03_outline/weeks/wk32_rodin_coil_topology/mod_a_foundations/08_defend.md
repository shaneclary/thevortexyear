## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The objection. *VBM specifies the doubling-cycle helix as the Rodin Coil winding pattern. But other winding patterns are possible: simple helical (mainstream-toroidal); evenly-distributed; randomly-distributed; any of dozens of conceivable patterns. Why this specific pattern? Couldn't another pattern produce equivalent or better results?*
>
> *The objection is sincere; engineering choice deserves justification.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *The honest defence.*
>
> *The doubling-cycle winding is the canonical Rodin Coil pattern because it instantiates the framework's mathematical structure (the doubling cycle in (Z/9Z) under multiplication by 2). Other patterns would produce different coils, not Rodin Coils. The framework's name and tradition specify this pattern as the canonical one. Engineering credibility through specification consistency: Rodin Coil = doubling-cycle-helix-winding; other patterns are different coils. Foundation specifications fix the canonical; replication across practitioners requires fixed specification.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three layers in the defence.*
>
> *Layer one — definitional.* *'Rodin Coil' is defined by its specific winding pattern*; *changing pattern produces a different coil*; *specification preservation is what makes 'Rodin Coil' a meaningful term*. *Definitional consistency.*
>
> *Layer two — framework-derived.* *Doubling cycle is the framework's central object*; *Rodin Coil instantiates the cycle physically*; *winding pattern is mathematically motivated, not arbitrary*. *Math-derived specification.*
>
> *Layer three — VBM-tradition canonical.* *Marko's framework specifies this winding*; *VBM-tradition's reference implementations all use this winding*; *replication across practitioners depends on specification stability*. *Tradition-canonical.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What can be defended.*
>
> *Defend 1 — definitional consistency.* *'Rodin Coil' specifies a particular winding*; *meaningful term has fixed specification*.
>
> *Defend 2 — math-derived motivation.* *Doubling-cycle winding instantiates framework's central mathematical object*; *not arbitrary*.
>
> *Defend 3 — replication requirements.* *Multi-practitioner foundation work requires shared specification*; *canonical winding enables replication*.
>
> *Defend 4 — alternative patterns produce different coils.* *Other winding patterns are valid coil designs but aren't Rodin Coils*; *they have their own names, characterisations, applications*.
>
> *Defend 5 — Rodin Coil's distinctiveness IS the winding.* *If we changed the winding pattern, we'd lose Rodin Coil's defining feature*; *distinctiveness preserved through specification*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What can't be defended.*
>
> *Can't 1.* That doubling-cycle winding produces optimal performance for all applications. *Optimality is application-dependent*; *for specific applications, other patterns may outperform*. *Specification ≠ optimality.*
>
> *Can't 2.* That alternative patterns are categorically inferior. *Many winding patterns produce useful coils*; *Rodin Coil is one specific pattern; not the only good pattern*.
>
> *Honest acknowledgment.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Honest articulation to a sceptical engineer.*
>
> *Articulation.* 'The doubling-cycle helix winding is the canonical Rodin Coil pattern because the term "Rodin Coil" specifies this particular winding. Changing the winding pattern would produce a different coil, with its own name, characterisations, and applications — not a "Rodin Coil". The pattern is mathematically motivated: it instantiates the doubling cycle in (Z/9Z) under multiplication by 2, the framework's central mathematical object. Replication across multi-practitioner foundation requires shared canonical specification; without fixed winding, "Rodin Coil" loses meaning. We don't claim doubling-cycle winding is optimal for every application; specification is about identity, not unique optimality. Other winding patterns produce other valid coils with their own merits; Rodin Coil specifies this one pattern. Engineering credibility through specification consistency.'
>
> Around 145 words.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Why this honest acknowledgment is the right defence.*
>
> *Reason 1 — preserves term meaning.* *'Rodin Coil' has specific specification; consistency required for meaning.*
>
> *Reason 2 — preserves replication.* *Multi-practitioner work requires shared specification.*
>
> *Reason 3 — engineering credibility through honest specification.* *Acknowledging specification ≠ optimality; framework-canonical ≠ universally-best.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Calibrated answer.*
>
> *Doubling-cycle helix winding is the canonical Rodin Coil pattern because the term specifies this winding. The pattern is math-derived (instantiates doubling cycle in (Z/9Z)). Alternative patterns produce different coils with their own characterisations. Replication across foundation requires canonical specification. Engineering credibility through specification consistency. We don't claim optimality for all applications; specification is about identity.*
>
