## 4. Body (1:00–4:30) — *contemplative*

> *(slow)* "*Wk 33 begins next week.*
>
> *Practitioners with prepared rigs* will *wind their first Rodin Coil*.
>
> *32 weeks of preparation lead to this*. *Q1's arithmetic; Q2's geometry; Q3's spiral revisits; Aetheron framework; phase-locked rigs*. *Substrate for what comes next.*
>
> *[long pause — 12 seconds]*
>
> Sit with anticipation.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> *Three contemplations on anticipation.*
>
> *Contemplation one — anticipation is not impatience.* *Patient anticipation is engaged with the present while looking toward future*; *unlike impatience, doesn't resent current moment*. *Mature anticipation is presence + projection.*
>
> *Contemplation two — preparation enables the build.* *32 weeks of preparation make Wk 33 build possible*; *without preparation, building is improvisation*. *Anticipation honors preparation.*
>
> *Contemplation three — first build is an event.* *Hand-built first Rodin Coil is biographical event for the practitioner*; *the practitioner who built it is changed*; *first builds become reference for all subsequent builds*. *Significance of first.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What does anticipation feel like?*
>
> Some practitioners notice *quiet excitement.* *Long preparation reaching its first physical artefact*; *energising; gentle.*
>
> Some practitioners notice *uncertainty.* *Will the build go well? Will it match specifications?* *Honest uncertainty.*
>
> Some practitioners notice *responsibility.* *About to commit physical resources (wire, time, attention) to construction*; *engineering responsibility for what's about to be built*.
>
> Some practitioners notice *gratitude.* *To Marko and the framework's developers*; *to teachers and predecessors*; *to themselves for sustained engagement*. *Many sources of gratitude*.
>
> All four are honest.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *A reflection on first artefacts.*
>
> *Most practitioners' first artefacts are imperfect*. *First-time wound coils have winding irregularities, cosmetic flaws, possibly construction errors*.
>
> *That's normal*. *First builds aren't expected to be flawless*; *they're learning experiences*.
>
> *Iteration matters more than first-perfect*. *Wk 33's first build will be followed by reflection, refinement, second build*; *progressively better builds across weeks*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *One more contemplation.*
>
> *Anticipation includes anticipation of difficulty.*
>
> *First builds will encounter challenges*: *materials behaving unexpectedly; tools requiring practice; spatial reasoning being demanded; patience being tested*.
>
> *Anticipating difficulty rather than only success* prepares *practitioner for honest engineering experience*; *failures and successes both expected.*
>
> *Engineering is not magical*; *it's iterative; it's hand-work; it requires showing up*.
>
> *Wk 33 begins this engagement.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Closing reflection.*
>
> *What happens at Wk 33's first build is hard to predict.* *Some practitioners will succeed cleanly; some will encounter difficulties; some will iterate quickly; some will pause and resume*.
>
> *All paths are honest engineering*. *The framework's preparation has been thorough*; *practitioners are equipped to engage*; *outcomes vary*.
>
> *Today's contemplation* honors *the anticipation of an unpredictable but well-prepared next week*. *Hold space for whatever comes*.
>
> *[long pause — 12 seconds]*
>
> *Sit with anticipation.* *Patient; engaged; humble; gratefully uncertain.* *Continue.*"
