> *What does practical anticipation feel like?*
>
> Some practitioners notice *quiet preparation.* *Final hours before building feel different from earlier preparation phases*; *focused; settled.*
>
> Some practitioners notice *gentle excitement.* *Energising rather than agitated*; *long-prepared work approaching its act.*
>
> Some practitioners notice *reverence.* *About to do something specific, real, particular*; *practitioner-and-craft meeting in concrete way*; *gravitas*.
>
> Some practitioners notice *practical patience.* *Days remain; not yet*; *patience for completion of preparation; for time itself*. *Mature anticipation is patient.*
>
> All four are honest.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *A reflection on anticipation in engineering work.*
>
> *Engineering work has rhythms*: *preparation phases (long); building phases (intense, finite); measurement phases (analytical); reflection phases*. *Each has different cognitive engagement.*
>
> *Anticipation between preparation and building* is the *transitional phase*. *Not actively building; not still gathering*; *something in between*.
>
> *Mature engineers learn to inhabit this phase well*: *neither anxious nor rushed; engaged but not yet acting*. *Skill in transitional phases is part of engineering practice.*
>
