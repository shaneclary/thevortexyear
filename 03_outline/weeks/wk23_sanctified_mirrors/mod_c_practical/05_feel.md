## 0. Authoring notes
Live with the mirrors. Contemplative on the experience of having Mirrors as continuing presences in workspace and life.

## 1. Outcome
Student has spent quiet time considering what living with Mirrors as workspace presences entails.

## 2. Hook (0:00–0:30)
*[Slow shot of a workspace with Mirrors integrated into the daily environment, cycle of seasons suggested by changing light.]*

> *(slow)* "Live with the mirrors. Sit with the months ahead."

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)
> "Lens 5 of 9. Six minutes, slower."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30) — *contemplative*

> *(slow)* "An object that lives with you for a year becomes part of who you are. *Not always in dramatic ways.* Sometimes the quiet presence is what matters most.
>
> The Mirrors you've placed in your workspace will be there for the rest of the year. *Months of accumulated glances. Months of background presence.*
>
> *[long pause — 12 seconds]*
>
> Sit with the months ahead.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> *Living with structured visual presence is an old practice.* Religious icons in homes. Ancestor portraits. Family photographs. Posters of admired figures. *Visual artefacts that share daily life with us.*
>
> The Mirrors join this lineage. *Not religious icons (necessarily); not portraits; but in the family of *visual companions*.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three contemplations on living-with-mirrors.
>
> *Contemplation one — the artefact accumulates relationship.* Each glance at the Mirror adds something — a moment of attention, a pattern noticed, a connection made. *Over months, the relationship deepens*.
>
> Some students notice this only in retrospect. *They didn't realise they were forming a relationship with the Mirror until they catch themselves drawing on it from memory*. The relationship was forming below conscious notice.
>
> *Contemplation two — daily presence vs. ceremonial use.* Some traditions use sacred imagery only on specific occasions (rituals, prayers, retreats). Other traditions integrate sacred imagery into daily life. *VBM's Mirror practice is closer to the daily-integration model.*
>
> The choice has consequences. Daily presence makes the imagery *familiar*; ceremonial use keeps it *special*. *Familiarity has its own depth*; specialness has its own intensity. *VBM tends toward the familiar approach.*
>
> *Contemplation three — what changes about you.* Living with Mirrors over months changes the practitioner. *Not the Mirrors.* Pattern recognition sharpens; mathematical structures feel native rather than imposed; the framework's vocabulary becomes second-nature. *Cumulative formation.*
>
> Some students don't notice the change until a moment of comparison — *what would I have not seen six months ago?* The change becomes visible against the comparison.
>
> *[long pause — 12 seconds]*
>
> What does living-with-mirrors feel like?
>
> Some students notice *integration*. The Mirrors become part of the workspace. *Not separate; integrated.*
>
> Some students notice *evolution*. As the practice matures, *the Mirrors look different to you over time*. The same image teaches new things at different stages of practice.
>
> Some students notice *quiet*. The Mirrors don't demand attention; they *invite* it. *Restful presence.*
>
> All three are honest.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> A practical observation. *Most VBM practitioners who live with Mirrors find the practice low-cost and high-yield.* No specific time investment beyond the placement; quiet pedagogical and contemplative benefits over months and years.
>
> *Counter-experiences also occur.* Some students find the Mirrors distracting or unhelpful; they put them away or replace them. *Honest variation in temperament.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One more contemplation. *The Mirrors will outlast many other workspace artefacts.* Posters, calendars, schedules — these come and go. *The Mirrors stay.* They mark the workspace as a VBM-engaged space across the year.
>
> Hold this. Notice that you've placed Mirrors in your environment. Notice they will be there tomorrow, next week, next month. *Quiet companions.*"

## 5. Try it (4:30–5:30)
> *(softer)* "One sentence. *What might living with Mirrors give me over months?* Sixty seconds."
