## 0. Authoring notes
Use mirrors in a practical contemplative session. Specific session structure for Mirror contemplation.

## 1. Outcome
Student can run a structured contemplative session using a Mirror.

## 2. Hook (0:00–0:30)
*[A practitioner sitting quietly with a Mirror; timer visible at 10 minutes.]*

> "A 15-minute contemplative session. Six minutes describing it."

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Today's apply gives a *specific session structure* for Mirror contemplation. About 15 minutes; reproducible; suitable for daily or several-times-weekly practice.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Pre-session setup (1-2 minutes).*
>
> Pick one Mirror. *Today's Mirror is whichever feels appropriate; rotate across sessions.*
>
> Place the Mirror at comfortable viewing distance. *Eye-level if possible.*
>
> Set a 10-minute timer (out of sight or with quiet notification).
>
> Sit comfortably. Hands resting; back supported.
>
> *Optional.* Brief intentional breath — three slow breaths to settle.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Phase 1 — overview gaze (2 minutes).*
>
> Look at the Mirror as a whole. *Don't focus on details yet.* Take in the overall image.
>
> Notice the dominant features. *Where does the eye naturally rest? What's the centre? What's the boundary?*
>
> Don't analyse. Just gaze.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Phase 2 — feature exploration (4 minutes).*
>
> Pick *one* specific feature: a cell, a colour region, a connecting line, a symbol.
>
> Look at it carefully. *What's around it? What's it connected to? What's its position relative to other features?*
>
> Spend a minute or two on this feature. Then *move to another* feature. Spend time with that one.
>
> Two or three features explored carefully in 4 minutes.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Phase 3 — relational attention (2 minutes).*
>
> Now look at *relationships* between features. *Antipodes; cycles; family colour pairings; symmetries.*
>
> The Mirror's structure is a *web of relationships*. Notice the web.
>
> *Don't try to verbalise the relationships.* Just see them.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Phase 4 — return to whole (1 minute).*
>
> Pull back to the overview gaze again. *The whole Mirror, taken in at once.*
>
> Notice if the whole feels different now than it did at the start. *Often it does — features have settled into context.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Phase 5 — close (1 minute).*
>
> Three slow breaths.
>
> Brief mental note: *what struck me? what's worth returning to next session?*
>
> Optional: write the note in your build log.
>
> Stand up, stretch.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Total time: ~10-15 minutes.* Suitable for daily or several-times-weekly practice.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three observations on this session structure.*
>
> *Observation one.* The session is *not contemplation in the deep meditative sense*. It's *structured looking* — a specific way of attending to the visual content of the Mirror. *Lighter than meditation; more focused than casual viewing.*
>
> *Observation two.* The phases *layer*. Overview → details → relationships → overview. *The structure scaffolds attention through different ways of looking at the same image.*
>
> *Observation three.* The session is *adaptable*. Spend more time on features if they're new; more time on relationships if you're consolidating. *Don't be rigid.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Try the session structure for 2-3 weeks.* See if it suits you. *Adapt or drop based on your experience.*
>
> Some students develop their own session styles. *That's appropriate*; the structure is a starting point, not a prescription."
