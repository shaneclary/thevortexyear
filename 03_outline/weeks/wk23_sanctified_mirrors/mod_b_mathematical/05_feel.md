## 0. Authoring notes
Projection as compression. Contemplative on what it means to compress higher-dim into lower-dim and what's preserved versus lost.

## 1. Outcome
Student has spent quiet time with the experience of mathematical compression and what it means to lose information.

## 2. Hook (0:00–0:30)
*[Slow shot of a 3D object's shadow — the shadow being more than the 3D object, less than the 3D object.]*

> *(slow)* "Projection compresses. Sit with what's lost and what's kept."

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)
> "Lens 5 of 9. Six minutes, slower."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30) — *contemplative*

> *(slow)* "Projection is *compression*. A higher-dim object becomes a lower-dim image. *Some information is preserved; some is lost.* The specific preservation pattern is the projection.
>
> *[long pause — 12 seconds]*
>
> Sit with compression.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> Most things in life are compressed when communicated. *We can't transmit our entire mental state to another person; we transmit a compressed version through speech, writing, gesture.* Communication is structured information loss.
>
> Sanctified Mirrors are an analogous structure. *The Decoqubit (or whatever higher-dim structure) can't be fully communicated; it's compressed to 2D images.* The Mirror is what survives compression.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three contemplations on compression.
>
> *Contemplation one — what's lost matters.* When we project a 3D object to 2D, we lose depth information. *Two distinct 3D objects can have the same 2D shadow.* The shadow is ambiguous about depth.
>
> Sanctified Mirrors have similar ambiguity. *The same Mirror could come from different higher-dim structures, in principle.* The Mirror underdetermines what's behind it.
>
> *Contemplation two — what's preserved matters too.* The shadow does preserve *some* information. *2D outline; relative positions; certain relationships.* Within those preserved features, the higher-dim object is *legible* through the shadow.
>
> Sanctified Mirrors preserve specific aspects of the higher-dim VBM structure. *Reading the Mirror skilfully recovers the preserved aspects.*
>
> *Contemplation three — multiple projections give more.* One shadow is ambiguous. *Several shadows from different angles* can disambiguate. *Multiple Sanctified Mirrors collectively encode more of the underlying structure than any single Mirror does.*
>
> The canonical Sanctified Mirror set, taken together, *would encode (most of) the Decoqubit*. Individual Mirrors are partial; the set is more complete.
>
> *[long pause — 12 seconds]*
>
> What does compression-as-mirror feel like?
>
> Some students notice *humility about partial knowledge*. The Mirror is not the structure. *Even careful Mirror study leaves the underlying structure not-fully-known.* This is honest about epistemic limits.
>
> Some students notice *appreciation for the partial*. Even partial knowledge is *real knowledge*. The Mirror gives genuine information; the limitation is the price.
>
> Some students notice *suspicion*. *Maybe the Mirror is too compressed; maybe what's preserved is too thin to be useful.* This suspicion is healthy; engagement reveals whether the Mirror is informative or not for specific purposes.
>
> All three are honest.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> A practical observation. *Mathematical and engineering practice constantly involves compression*. Engineering drawings compress 3D objects to 2D. Mathematical proofs compress complex truths to symbolic chains. Programming compresses behaviour into code. *Compression is a core practice; not an unusual move.*
>
> Sanctified Mirrors are one specific compression practice within VBM. *Familiar in form even if VBM-specific in content.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One more contemplation. *Living with compressed knowledge is the human condition.* Most of what we know about the universe, ourselves, others — comes through compressed representations. *Direct unmediated knowledge is rare.* The Sanctified Mirror practice is one specific instance of this general human condition.
>
> Hold the Mirror. Notice what's preserved. Notice what's lost. *Both are real.*"
