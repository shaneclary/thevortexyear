## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "*Tibetan Buddhist mandala.* Visual artefact representing cosmic/spiritual structure. Used in meditation, ritual, deity-yoga practice. Highly developed; centuries of tradition; specific iconographic conventions.
>
> *Hindu yantra.* Geometric diagram representing deity-form or cosmic structure. Sri Yantra and others. Used in meditation, ritual. Centuries of tradition; specific symbolic vocabulary.
>
> *Sanctified Mirror (Marko).* Visual representation of higher-dim VBM structure. Used in study, contemplation, teaching. Recent (2023); developing tradition; **`[ORG-INPUT]`** for canonical conventions.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three structural similarities.
>
> *Similarity one — geometric structure.* All three are based on geometric structure (mandalas have specific layouts; yantras have geometric shapes; Sanctified Mirrors are projections of mathematical structures).
>
> *Similarity two — contemplative use.* All three are designed for sustained visual contemplation. *Not just decoration; for sitting-with practice.*
>
> *Similarity three — symbolic or representational content.* Each represents something — cosmic structure, deity-form, mathematical/metaphysical structure. *Carry meaning beyond mere image.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three structural differences.
>
> *Difference one — tradition age.* Mandalas: centuries (1000+ years for major Tibetan lineages). Yantras: centuries (Hindu tantric traditions). Sanctified Mirrors: years (since 2023). *VBM is a young tradition by comparison.*
>
> *Difference two — what they represent.* Mandalas: cosmic Buddha-realms or deity-mandalas. Yantras: specific deities or cosmic principles. Sanctified Mirrors: higher-dimensional mathematical structures (Decoqubit and related). *Different content even if formal similarity exists.*
>
> *Difference three — practice context.* Mandalas: integrated with monastic Tibetan Buddhist practice; specific ritual contexts. Yantras: integrated with Hindu tantric practice. Sanctified Mirrors: emerging within VBM-tradition contexts; less stabilised practice context.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What VBM practitioners can learn from older traditions.*
>
> *Learn 1 — sustained engagement matters.* Mandala and yantra traditions teach that *years of practice with the same image* deepens the relationship. *VBM practitioners pursuing Mirror practice can expect long-term cultivation.*
>
> *Learn 2 — community context helps.* Mandala practitioners typically have teachers and lineage. *VBM Mirror practice is more solo; community support evolving.*
>
> *Learn 3 — practice-without-attainment is OK.* Older traditions accept that some practitioners study mandalas without attaining specific spiritual realisations. *VBM Mirror practice doesn't have to deliver dramatic results to be worthwhile.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What VBM offers that older traditions don't.*
>
> *Offer 1 — explicit mathematical content.* Sanctified Mirrors derive from specific mathematical structures (Decoqubit, etc.). *Mandalas and yantras have implicit mathematical structure; Sanctified Mirrors are explicit.*
>
> *Offer 2 — engineering connection.* Sanctified Mirrors connect to Rodin Coil engineering, computing, etc. *Mandalas and yantras typically don't connect to engineering.*
>
> *Offer 3 — contemporary articulation.* Marko's recent publication (*Sanctified Mirrors* 2023) is contemporary; engages contemporary problems. *Less archaic vocabulary than older traditions.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Useful framing. *Sanctified Mirrors are a young addition to the family of structured-visual contemplative traditions. They share formal characteristics (geometric structure, contemplative use, representational content) with older traditions like mandalas and yantras. They differ in tradition age, content (mathematical-structural rather than mythic-symbolic), and engineering connections. Younger tradition; learning from older lineages while developing its own approach.*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Reference older traditions for Mirror-practice technique without claiming Sanctified Mirrors are equivalent.* The traditions are cousins; not identical."
