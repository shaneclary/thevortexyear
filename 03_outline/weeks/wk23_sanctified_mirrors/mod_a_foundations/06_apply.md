## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Today's apply gives a *practical regimen* for using Sanctified Mirrors in daily VBM study.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *The basic regimen.*
>
> *Daily practice — 5-15 minutes.* Sit with one Sanctified Mirror. Look at it without analysing. Just attend.
>
> *Weekly review — 30 minutes.* Compare your sketched Mirror with VBM concepts you've covered in the past week. *What does the Mirror illustrate that you've now learned?*
>
> *Monthly addition — 1-2 hours.* Sketch a new Sanctified Mirror or refine an existing one. *Build a personal collection over time.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Specific use cases.*
>
> *Use 1 — pre-study warmup.* Before opening the build log, sit with the Mirror for 2-3 minutes. *Settles attention; primes the mind for VBM material.*
>
> *Use 2 — concept-anchoring.* When learning a new VBM concept, *find which aspect of the Mirror illustrates the concept*. The Mirror becomes a memory anchor.
>
> *Use 3 — review aid.* When reviewing past material, *use the Mirror as the review framework*. Walk through what you remember, locating each piece in the Mirror's structure.
>
> *Use 4 — contemplative break.* During study sessions, take a 60-second Mirror-sit-with break. *Refreshes attention without disengaging from the material.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Practical setup.*
>
> *Print or sketch the Mirror.* Have a physical copy you can pick up. Digital screens work less well for this practice.
>
> *Place visibly.* Next to your build log; on your wall; on your desk. *Available without effort.*
>
> *Keep clean.* The Mirror gets handled; replace if smudged or torn.
>
> *Multiple Mirrors over time.* Start with one; add more as you develop your practice. *Don't try to use all at once.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What this regimen is and isn't.*
>
> *Is — a study aid.* Helps memorise, anchor, review VBM concepts.
>
> *Is — a contemplative practice.* Settles attention; develops sensitivity to visual structure.
>
> *Isn't — a substitute for math.* The Mirror doesn't *replace* the algebraic content; it *complements* it.
>
> *Isn't — required for everyone.* Some students engage VBM purely through math; others integrate Mirror practice. *Both paths work.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three observations.
>
> *Observation one.* The regimen is *modest*. 5-15 minutes daily; 30 weekly; 1-2 hours monthly. Total time: about 1-3 hours per week. *Manageable.*
>
> *Observation two.* The regimen *grows over time*. Initial sessions feel awkward; later sessions feel natural. *Patience needed.*
>
> *Observation three.* The regimen *layers with other VBM practices*. Hand-drawing diagrams (build log); reading source material; coil-engineering work — all coexist with Mirror practice.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Try the regimen for 2-4 weeks.* If congenial, integrate; if not, drop. *Don't force; don't judge premature.*
>
> The Sanctified Mirror practice is *one of VBM's contemplative-tradition resources*. Engage as fits."
