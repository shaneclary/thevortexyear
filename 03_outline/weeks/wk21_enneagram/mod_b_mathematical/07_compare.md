## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "*Complete graph K_9.* The graph with 9 vertices in which *every* pair of distinct vertices is connected by an edge. K_9 has 9 vertices and *C(9, 2) = 9!/(2! × 7!) = 36* edges.
>
> The Rodin Symbol's edges and the classical enneagram's edges are *subsets* of K_9's 36 possible edges. Today we examine which subsets they are.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *K_9's edge inventory.*
>
> 36 edges total. We can categorise them by how 'far apart' the connected vertices are on the circle (since the 9 vertices sit on a circle).
>
> *Distance 1 (adjacent vertices):* 9 edges. (1-2, 2-3, 3-4, …, 8-9, 9-1.)
>
> *Distance 2:* 9 edges. (1-3, 2-4, …, 9-2.)
>
> *Distance 3:* 9 edges. (1-4, 2-5, …, 9-3.)
>
> *Distance 4:* 9 edges. (1-5, 2-6, …, 9-4.)
>
> Total: 4 × 9 = 36 edges. ✓
>
> Each distance class is a *9-cycle* in itself when traced through.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *The Rodin Symbol's 9 edges within K_9.*
>
> Doubling cycle 6-edges: 1-2 (dist 1), 2-4 (dist 2), 4-8 (dist 4), 8-7 (dist 1), 7-5 (dist 2), 5-1 (dist 4).
>
> Family-3 triangle 3-edges: 3-6 (dist 3), 6-9 (dist 3), 9-3 (dist 3).
>
> *Edges at each distance class within Rodin Symbol.*
>
> Distance 1: 2 edges (1-2, 8-7). *Out of 9 in K_9.*
>
> Distance 2: 2 edges (2-4, 7-5).
>
> Distance 3: 3 edges (3-6, 6-9, 9-3, the family-3 triangle).
>
> Distance 4: 2 edges (4-8, 5-1).
>
> Total: 2 + 2 + 3 + 2 = 9 edges. ✓
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *The classical enneagram's 9 edges within K_9.*
>
> Outer cycle 6-edges: 1-4 (dist 3), 4-2 (dist 2), 2-8 (dist 3), 8-5 (dist 3), 5-7 (dist 2), 7-1 (dist 3).
>
> Inner triangle 3-edges: 3-6 (dist 3), 6-9 (dist 3), 9-3 (dist 3).
>
> *Edges at each distance class within classical enneagram.*
>
> Distance 1: 0 edges.
>
> Distance 2: 2 edges (4-2, 5-7).
>
> Distance 3: 7 edges (1-4, 2-8, 8-5, 7-1, 3-6, 6-9, 9-3).
>
> Distance 4: 0 edges.
>
> Total: 0 + 2 + 7 + 0 = 9 edges. ✓
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Comparison: edges at each distance.*
>
> Distance 1: Rodin 2; classical 0.
>
> Distance 2: Rodin 2; classical 2.
>
> Distance 3: Rodin 3; classical 7.
>
> Distance 4: Rodin 2; classical 0.
>
> *Different distributions.* Both subgraphs have 9 edges total but distribute them differently across the distance classes.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What this comparison reveals.*
>
> *Reveal 1.* The two subgraphs *occupy different parts of K_9*. They overlap on the family-3 triangle (3 edges) but otherwise don't share edges.
>
> *Reveal 2.* The classical enneagram is *distance-3-heavy*. Most of its edges connect vertices 3 apart on the circle. The Rodin Symbol *spreads* its edges across distances 1, 2, 3, 4.
>
> *Reveal 3.* The two figures *complement* each other in some sense. Edges Rodin uses (distances 1, 2, 4) are mostly *not* used by the enneagram. *Together they cover more of K_9 than either alone.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Useful framing. *Both the Rodin Symbol and the classical enneagram are 9-edge subgraphs of K_9. They share the family-3 triangle but use different distance classes for their unit-cycles. Graph-isomorphic as abstract graphs; geometrically distinct as subsets of K_9.*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Visualise the two figures as different colourings of K_9.* The complete graph K_9 has 36 edges; each subgraph picks 9. Different selections produce visually different figures with the same abstract structure."
