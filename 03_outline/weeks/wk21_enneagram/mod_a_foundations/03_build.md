## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Today's build is a hand-drawn comparison artefact. You draw both figures on the same circle of nine vertices, in different colours, to see their relationship directly.
>
> Open your build log to a fresh page. Title it *Symbol vs Enneagram — Both Traced*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Materials.* Pencil, ruler or compass for circle, three coloured pens.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Step 1 — draw the circle.* Use a compass or ruler to draw a clean circle, about 8-10 cm in diameter. Centre it on the page.
>
> *Step 2 — mark 9 evenly-spaced vertices.* Divide the circle into 9 equal arcs (each 40°). Mark the 9 positions. Place 9 at the top; number 1 to 8 clockwise from there.
>
> *Step 3 — Rodin doubling cycle (colour 1).* Use one coloured pen. Draw line segments connecting:
>
> 1 → 2 → 4 → 8 → 7 → 5 → 1.
>
> The shape is a *figure-eight* or *Rodin spiral*.
>
> *Step 4 — classical enneagram outer cycle (colour 2).* Use a different coloured pen. Draw:
>
> 1 → 4 → 2 → 8 → 5 → 7 → 1.
>
> The shape is a *hexagon-like figure* — different from the Rodin doubling cycle.
>
> *Step 5 — classical enneagram inner triangle (colour 3).* Use a third coloured pen. Draw:
>
> 3 → 6 → 9 → 3.
>
> An equilateral triangle inside the circle.
>
> *Step 6 — observe.*
>
> Both cycles use the same six vertices {1, 2, 4, 5, 7, 8} but visit them in different orders. *The two cycles intersect at all six vertices but trace different paths.*
>
> The triangle 3-6-9 is *separate* from the cycles — it lives on the family-3 vertices that the doubling skips.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Step 7 — annotate.*
>
> In the margin, label:
>
> *Colour 1.* Rodin doubling cycle. Source: 2ⁿ mod 9.
>
> *Colour 2.* Classical enneagram outer cycle. Source: digits of 1/7 = 0.142857...
>
> *Colour 3.* Family-3 triangle. Source: subgroup 3ℤ/9ℤ.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Step 8 — photograph and document.* Take a clear photo. Tape into your build log.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What you've drawn. *Both 9-fold figures on the same circle, distinguishable by colour.* The relationship is now visible — same vertex set, different traversal orders.
>
> Three observations.
>
> *Observation one.* The two cycles *cross each other* multiple times within the circle. The crossings are at non-vertex positions; *the cycles don't share edges*, only vertices.
>
> *Observation two.* The triangle 3-6-9 is *common to both interpretations*. Both the classical enneagram and the (extended) Rodin Symbol include this triangle. *Family 3 lives the same place in both traditions.*
>
> *Observation three.* If you imagine the two cycles together (without the triangle), you have *all the edges of K_6* — the complete graph on 6 vertices — minus a few. *The two cycles are complementary in some sense within K_6.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *The hand-drawn comparison makes the relationship between the figures visceral.* Different colours on the same vertices reveal: same set, different paths.
>
> *This is one of Q2's most useful comparison artefacts.* Pin it next to your other diagrams; reference it when discussing VBM with practitioners of other 9-pointed traditions."
