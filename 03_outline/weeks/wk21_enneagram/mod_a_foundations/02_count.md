## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Today we count graph-theoretic properties of the Rodin Symbol and the classical enneagram.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Symbol of Enlightenment with doubling cycle.*
>
> *Vertices: 9.* The nine numbered positions on the circle.
>
> *Edges: 6.* The doubling cycle's edges: 1-2, 2-4, 4-8, 8-7, 7-5, 5-1. *Six edges traversed in cyclic order.*
>
> *Cycles: 1.* One closed loop through six of the vertices (the doubling cycle).
>
> *Family-3 vertices: 3 (positions 3, 6, 9).* Not on the cycle; *isolated* in the doubling-cycle graph.
>
> Note: if we add the family-3 connections (a triangle 3-6-9 and possibly the centre), the count changes. The *unannotated* doubling-cycle figure has 6 edges; with all family-3 connections, more.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Classical enneagram.*
>
> *Vertices: 9.* Same 9 positions on the circle.
>
> *Edges: 9.* Six edges along the outer hexagonal-cycle path (1-4, 4-2, 2-8, 8-5, 5-7, 7-1) plus three edges of the inner triangle (3-6, 6-9, 9-3).
>
> *Cycles: 2.* The outer 6-cycle and the inner 3-cycle (triangle).
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Direct count comparison.*
>
> Vertices: same (9 each).
>
> Edges: 6 (Rodin doubling-only) vs 9 (classical with both cycles).
>
> Cycles: 1 vs 2.
>
> Family-3 connections: absent in Rodin doubling-only; explicit triangle in classical.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *If we add the Rodin Symbol's family-3 axis (Wk 20):*
>
> The Rodin Symbol can be considered as having the three family-3 vertices connected to a central axis (in 3D), but the *2D figure* doesn't show this.
>
> If we add a 2D triangle 3-6-9 to the Rodin Symbol (closing family 3 into a cycle on the surface), we get:
>
> Vertices: 9.
>
> Edges: 6 (doubling) + 3 (triangle) = 9.
>
> Cycles: 2 (doubling 6-cycle + triangle 3-cycle).
>
> *Now the counts match the classical enneagram exactly.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Key insight.*
>
> The *fully-annotated* Rodin Symbol (doubling + family-3 triangle) is *graph-isomorphic* to the classical enneagram. Same number of vertices, same number of edges, same cycle structure.
>
> They differ only in the *labelling order* — which vertex is 1, which is 4, etc. Different labellings give different traversal patterns.
>
> *Up to relabelling, the two figures are the same graph.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three observations.
>
> *Observation one.* The graph isomorphism is *exact* (when we add the family-3 triangle to the Rodin Symbol). Mod B Lens 2 will prove this formally.
>
> *Observation two.* The labelling difference is *significant* even though the graphs are isomorphic. The Rodin labelling reflects the doubling structure of (ℤ/9ℤ)*; the classical labelling reflects the digits of 1/7. *Different mathematical sources produce different labellings of the same graph.*
>
> *Observation three.* The two cycles in the classical enneagram correspond to *two different orbits of (ℤ/9ℤ)*. The 6-cycle is the doubling orbit (or its reflection). The 3-cycle is the family-3 subgroup. *Together they account for all 9 elements of ℤ/9ℤ.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *The Rodin Symbol and the classical enneagram are graph-isomorphic structures with different labellings.* This is a precise relationship — not 'the same' (labellings differ) and not 'unrelated' (graph structure is identical). *They're cousins.*"
