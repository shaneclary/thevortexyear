## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Today we count what gets wound when Q3 brings copper. The numbers come from the helix's structure but expressed in engineering terms.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Per-strand counts.*
>
> *Turns per traversal:* 24. (Each helix-cell is a turn through one angular position.)
>
> *Traversals per strand:* 4. (The canonical Rodin Coil has four traversals — four passes around the helix per strand.)
>
> *Turns per strand:* 24 × 4 = 96.
>
> *Wire length per strand:* 96 turns × ~3 cm per turn = ~288 cm = ~2.9 m. Round up to 3 m for slack.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Per-coil counts (six-strand canonical).*
>
> *Turns total across all strands:* 96 × 6 = 576.
>
> *Wire length total:* 3 m × 6 strands = 18 m.
>
> *Time to wind* (rough estimate): 30 seconds per turn × 576 turns = ~5 hours.
>
> *Materials cost:* about $5-15 in magnet wire, depending on AWG and source.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Single-strand single-traversal counts.*
>
> *Turns:* 24.
>
> *Wire length:* ~75 cm. Round up to 1 m.
>
> *Time to wind:* ~12 minutes.
>
> *Materials cost:* under $1 in wire.
>
> Single-strand is the *first build* in Q3 (Wk 33). The full six-strand four-traversal version comes later.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three engineering implications of the counts.*
>
> *Implication 1 — wire ordering.* Order at least 5 m of wire for a single-strand build, 25 m for a six-strand build. Keep slack for re-do potential.
>
> *Implication 2 — soldering count.* Single-strand: 2 solder joints (start, end). Six-strand: 12 solder joints (6 starts, 6 ends). Higher joint counts mean more failure points; precise soldering matters.
>
> *Implication 3 — measurement scale.* Single-strand resistance ~1.5 Ω (3 m × 0.5 Ω/m). Six-strand parallel: ~0.25 Ω. Multimeter readings should match these expectations; large deviations indicate construction errors.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Verification.*
>
> *Self-check.* Multiply turns × per-turn-length × strand count: 24 × 3 × 4 × 6 = 1728 cm = 17.28 m. Close to my 18 m estimate; the difference is from rounding.
>
> *Reality check.* Foundation-published builds report ~15-20 m total wire for canonical six-strand four-traversal coils with 10 cm major / 2.5 cm minor formers. *My estimate is in range.* ✓
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three observations.
>
> *Observation one.* The numbers are *not large*. A single Rodin Coil uses less than $20 in materials. *Cost isn't a barrier* to entry.
>
> *Observation two.* The wind *time* is the dominant resource. Six-strand four-traversal coils take 5+ hours of focused winding. *Plan a Saturday for it.*
>
> *Observation three.* These numbers are *standardised across builders.* If your wire usage differs significantly from these estimates, something is off — either your former dimensions differ or your wind has errors.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Practical takeaway.* Order materials based on these estimates. For Q3 Wk 33's single-strand build: 1-3 m of AWG 24 magnet wire. For Wk 35's six-strand build: 20-30 m. Plan for both if you intend to do both.
>
> Add the numbers (96 turns/strand, 18 m total, $15 materials) to your build log's materials-planning page."
