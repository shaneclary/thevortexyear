## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The objection. *Phase-locked measurement requires more equipment and analysis than averaged-only. Wouldn't it be simpler to measure aggregate coil behaviour and skip the temporal-structure complexity? If the framework's predictions about phase patterns are wrong, no amount of phase-locking will help. If predictions are right, simple averaged measurements should also detect something. Why bother with the complication?*
>
> *The objection is sincere; engineering simplicity has value.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *The honest defence.*
>
> *Builders should respect temporal structure because the framework's distinctive predictions are temporal. Within-cycle phase pattern, sequential activation, polarity-dependent sequence reversal — these are time-domain predictions. Averaged-only methodology averages out the very pattern being tested. Phase-locked methodology preserves the pattern. Without phase-locked methodology, temporal predictions cannot be empirically engaged. Skipping phase-locking is choosing not to test the framework's distinctive content.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three layers in the defence.*
>
> *Layer one — temporal predictions require temporal measurement.* *Time-domain predictions cannot be tested with averaged-only methodology*. *Methodology must match prediction structure.*
>
> *Layer two — phase-locking is the standard temporal-measurement technique.* *Mainstream signal processing uses phase-locking for time-resolved analysis*; *not exotic; not VBM-distinctive technique*; *standard.*
>
> *Layer three — engineering credibility.* *Engineers who skip appropriate methodology and conclude based on inadequate data* lose *engineering credibility*. *Methodology rigor protects conclusions.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What can be defended.*
>
> *Defend 1.* *Temporal predictions require temporal measurement.* *Mathematical fact about measurement methodology.*
>
> *Defend 2.* *Phase-locking is mainstream signal processing.* *Standard technique; not VBM-distinctive method.*
>
> *Defend 3.* *Aggregate measurements complement, don't substitute, phase-locked.* *Both have roles; aggregate is baseline; phase-locked tests temporal pattern.*
>
> *Defend 4.* *Investment in phase-locked rig is reasonable.* *Modest equipment upgrade from basic oscilloscope to phase-locked oscilloscope*; *one-time cost; reusable across builds.*
>
> *Defend 5.* *Foundation collaboration benefits from phase-locked data.* *Multi-practitioner replication needs comparable methodology*; *phase-locked is the standard for VBM engineering.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What can't be defended.*
>
> *Can't 1.* That phase-locked methodology is required for all VBM-coil engineering. *For DC measurements, baseline characterisation, etc., averaged-only suffices*. *Methodology choice is task-specific.*
>
> *Can't 2.* That every practitioner should immediately invest in phase-locked rig. *Some practitioners may start with averaged-only and progress to phase-locked*; *progression appropriate per resources.*
>
> *Honest acknowledgment of contextual flexibility.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Honest articulation to a sceptical builder.*
>
> *Articulation.* 'You're right that phase-locked methodology is more complex. The reason builders should invest in it is that the framework's distinctive engineering predictions are temporal — within-cycle phase patterns, sequential activation, polarity-dependent sequence reversal. Averaged-only methodology averages out exactly the pattern being tested; it cannot empirically engage temporal predictions. Phase-locking is mainstream signal processing — used in radar, oscilloscope-based measurements, lock-in amplifiers — not VBM-distinctive technique. The investment is modest: phase-locked rig builds on basic oscilloscope and signal generator with phase-trigger capability. Without phase-locked methodology, temporal predictions remain claims rather than empirically engaged hypotheses. Skipping phase-locking is choosing not to test the framework's distinctive content. For aggregate measurements (resonance, Q-factor, DC values), averaged-only suffices; phase-locked is for testing temporal predictions. Both methodologies complement; choice should match measurement goal.'
>

> *Why this honest acknowledgment is the right defence.*
>
> *Reason 1 — methodological appropriateness.* *Methodology must match prediction structure*; *temporal predictions need temporal measurement.*
>
> *Reason 2 — engineering credibility.* *Phase-locked methodology is mainstream*; *applying it makes engineering work credibly engaged.*
>
> *Reason 3 — empirical engagement.* *Without phase-locked methodology, temporal predictions are unfalsifiable in practice*; *with it, they become testable*; *honest engagement requires.*

> *Builders should respect temporal structure because temporal predictions require temporal measurement methodology. Phase-locking is mainstream signal processing — used in radar, oscilloscope-triggered measurements, lock-in amplifiers — not VBM-distinctive. Investment in phase-locked rig is modest; one-time; reusable. Without phase-locked methodology, temporal predictions cannot be empirically tested; with it, they become testable. Aggregate measurements (averaged-only) complement but don't substitute. Methodology choice matches measurement goal.*
