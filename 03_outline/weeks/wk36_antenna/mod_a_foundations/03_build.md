> "Antenna measurement requires a deliberate test setup. *Today: build it*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Six components of the antenna test setup.*
>
> *Component 1 — antenna mast rigs.* *Two non-conducting masts (PVC or fibreglass; ~2-3 m tall)*; *each holds one antenna under test*; *sturdy bases; far-field separation (≥ 5 m) between antennas*. **`[ORG-INPUT]`** for canonical test-range dimensions.
>
> *Component 2 — ground plane.* *Conductive surface beneath antennas*; *standard test practice; minimum size ~λ/2 × λ/2 (where λ is test-frequency wavelength)*; *for HF tests (~3-30 MHz), ground plane is ~5 m × 5 m*; *chicken-wire mesh on plywood is course-canonical*. **`[ORG-INPUT]`** for canonical ground-plane spec.
>
> *Component 3 — feed lines.* *50-Ω coaxial cable from each antenna to instrumentation*; *equal lengths to minimise differential phase shift*; *N-type connectors (lab-grade)*; *cable losses calibrated out via VNA*. **`[ORG-INPUT]`** for canonical cable type / length.
>
> *Component 4 — instrumentation.* *Vector network analyser (VNA) for impedance + return loss measurements (1 Hz–6 GHz typical lab-grade)*; *RF signal generator and power meter for gain measurements*; *spectrum analyser optional*. **`[ORG-INPUT]`** for course-available instruments.
>
> *Component 5 — reference field probe.* *Calibrated antenna probe at known distance for radiated-power reference*; *isotropic probe ideal; loop or short-dipole probe acceptable with calibration*; **`[ORG-INPUT]`** for course probe specification.
>
> *Component 6 — measurement log.* *Antenna test log (parallel to coil build log)*; *records per-frequency, per-antenna, per-orientation measurements*; *engineering deliverable*.
>
> *Six components; antenna test setup specified*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Five-phase test setup build session.*
>
> *Phase 1 — site preparation (~30 min).* *Choose location: ideally outdoor or anechoic chamber; minimise reflective surfaces, conductors, and EMI sources*. *Lay ground plane*; *check level and continuity*.
>
> *Phase 2 — mast assembly (~20 min).* *Erect two masts at agreed separation (~5 m); secure bases; verify vertical alignment with plumb line*.
>
> *Phase 3 — antenna mounting (~30 min).* *Mount reference dipole on mast 1 with documented orientation (typically vertical or horizontal half-wave)*; *Rodin Coil on mast 2 with documented orientation (typically axis-vertical)*. *Centre antennas at same height above ground plane*; *typically λ/4 above ground (~10-25 m at HF, ~1-3 m at VHF)*. **`[ORG-INPUT]`** for canonical antenna height.
>
> *Phase 4 — feed-line connection (~20 min).* *Run feed lines from each antenna to instrumentation bench*; *equal lengths; secure connections; visually verify no damaged shielding or kinked cable*.
>
> *Phase 5 — instrument calibration (~30-45 min).* *Calibrate VNA at antenna-feed reference plane (open/short/load standards)*; *calibrate power meter against known reference*; *log calibration*.
>
> *Total assembly time: ~2-3 hours for first test setup*. *Subsequent tests reuse setup (only antenna swap needed)*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three engineering judgments at test-setup stage.*
>
> *Judgment one — outdoor vs. indoor.* *Outdoor minimises reflective surfaces but introduces weather and EMI variability*; *indoor (large room or anechoic chamber) controls environment but risks multipath*. *Course-canonical likely outdoor with documented conditions*. **`[ORG-INPUT]`** for canonical site choice.
>
> *Judgment two — reference dipole construction.* *Half-wave dipole at test frequency; tunable balun for impedance match; standard textbook-spec reference*. **`[ORG-INPUT]`** for canonical reference dipole.
>
> *Judgment three — mast material.* *Non-conducting (PVC, fibreglass)*; *avoid conductive masts which would couple to antennas and distort patterns*. *Engineering safety.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Build the antenna test setup as a 6-component assembly: mast rigs, ground plane, feed lines, instrumentation, reference probe, measurement log.* *5-phase build (~2-3 hours): site prep, masts, antennas, feeds, calibration.* *Three engineering judgments: site, reference, masts.* **`[ORG-INPUT]`** for all canonical specifications."
