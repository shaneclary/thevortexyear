>
> *[pause]*
>
> *What 'portfolio piece' means.*
>
> A *portfolio piece* is a *documented engineering work-product* a practitioner can present to *organisations, employers, peers, or capstone reviewers*.
>
> *Quality criteria*: *clear specification, reproducible methodology, honest results, defensible conclusions*.
>
> *The Wk 36 antenna comparison test, executed and documented, is a strong portfolio candidate*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Four parts of the engineering deliverable.*
>
> *Part 1 — Setup specification.* *Test-range dimensions; ground-plane spec; antenna-mount specification; reference dipole construction; Rodin Coil specification (linking to Wk 33/35 build logs); instrumentation list with calibration records; environmental conditions during test*. *Engineering specification document; ~3-5 pages*.
>
> *Part 2 — Methodology description.* *Measurement protocol step-by-step; calibration procedures; error analysis approach; data-recording format*. *Engineering methodology document; ~2-3 pages*.
>
> *Part 3 — Results presentation.* *Per-frequency, per-antenna, per-orientation measurements*; *gain table; efficiency table; pattern plots; impedance plots; return loss plots*. *Engineering results document with figures; ~5-10 pages*.
>
> *Part 4 — Conclusions and discussion.* *Comparison summary (Rodin Coil efficiency at test frequencies vs. reference dipole)*; *agreement / disagreement with predictions and claims*; *honest assessment of what the test established and what it didn't*. *Engineering analysis document; ~2-3 pages*.
>
> *Total deliverable: ~12-20 pages with figures*; *substantial portfolio piece*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three engineering quality criteria.*
>
> *Criterion one — reproducibility.* *Setup specification and methodology must be detailed enough that a second practitioner could replicate the test*; *if not, the test isn't engineering*. *Reproducibility check: can someone else build the test setup from your document?*
>
> *Criterion two — calibration honesty.* *Every measurement instrument used must have its calibration documented*; *any uncalibrated measurement flagged as such*; *engineering precision tracked*.
>
> *Criterion three — claim-measurement alignment.* *Conclusions must align tightly with measurements*; *no conclusion should claim more than measurements support*; *engineering integrity*.
>
> *Three quality criteria; engineering portfolio standard*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three uses of the portfolio piece.*
>
> *Use one — capstone deliverable.* *Wk 50-52 capstone work uses earlier portfolio pieces as foundation*; *antenna comparison test is strong capstone-piece candidate*; *or capstone may extend it (e.g., antenna at additional frequencies, or with phased-drive)*.
>
> *Use two — community contribution.* *Honest measurement reports of VBM-engineering claims contribute to the empirical record*; *practitioner shares (with foundation approval) with VBM community or broader engineering community*; *cumulative knowledge*.
>
> *Use three — professional credibility.* *For practitioners pursuing engineering careers (VBM-related or otherwise), the test is a credible engineering deliverable*; *demonstrates measurement competence, honesty, methodology*. *Engineering CV-quality work*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Two engineering judgments at portfolio-piece stage.*
>
> *Judgment one — claim measured, claim assessed honestly.* *Whatever the measurement shows is the result*; *60% confirmed, 40% measured, 75% measured — all are valid results*; *honest reporting required*.
>
> *Judgment two — limitations stated explicitly.* *No measurement is perfect*; *test-range size, instrumentation precision, reference dipole quality all introduce limits*; *limitations stated in methodology and conclusions*; *engineering humility*.
>
> *[pause]*
>

> *Establishes 1.* *Practitioner can specify, build, and execute an antenna measurement test*. *Engineering competence*.

> *Establishes 2.* *Practitioner reports honestly even when results don't match claims*. *Engineering integrity*.

> *Establishes 3.* *Practitioner thinks systematically about reproducibility, calibration, and limitations*. *Engineering maturity*.
>
> *Three competencies; substantial professional development*.
