> "Antenna engineering has many types. *Today: place Rodin Coil among them*.

>
> *Parameter 2 — efficiency (radiated/input power).* *Full-size dipole: ~95%; compact whip: 30-70%; meander-line / chip antenna: 10-40%*.
>
> *Parameter 3 — gain pattern.* *Omnidirectional, directional, or shaped*; *gain in dBi at peak direction*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Comparison 1 — half-wave dipole (reference).*
>
> *Size: λ/2 (e.g., 5 m at 30 MHz, 50 cm at 300 MHz)*.
>
> *Efficiency: ~95%*.
>
> *Pattern: figure-eight in equatorial plane, null at poles*.
>
> *Gain: 2.15 dBi*.
>
> *Mainstream reference; not compact*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Comparison 2 — patch antenna.*
>
> *Size: ~λ/3 (typically planar; PCB-implemented)*.
>
> *Efficiency: 60-80% (substrate losses)*.
>
> *Pattern: directional (broadside to patch); gain ~6-9 dBi*.
>
> *Application: cellular, GPS, WiFi; integrated into devices.*
>
> *Compact and directional; manufacturing-friendly*.

> *Comparison 3 — helical antenna.*

> *Size: variable (axial-mode helix typically ~λ in length, ~λ/3 diameter)*.

> *Efficiency: 70-90%*.

> *Pattern: directional axial; gain 8-15 dBi*; *circular polarisation typical*.

>
> *Three things Rodin Coil shares with these mainstream antennas.*
>
> *Shared one — wire-wound radiating structure.* *Dipole, helical, Rodin Coil all radiate via current in conductive wire*; *patch is exception (planar)*; *fundamental EM principle*.
>
> *Shared two — frequency-dependent behaviour.* *All antennas have operating frequency range*; *efficiency, pattern, impedance all frequency-dependent*; *characterisation across frequency required*.
>
> *Shared three — measurable on standard test bench.* *All four antennas characterisable with same VNA + power meter + reference probe instrumentation*; *no Rodin-specific instrumentation needed*; *engineering accessibility*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three things distinguish Rodin Coil.*
>
> *Distinct one — toroidal geometry.* *Most mainstream antennas are linear (dipole), planar (patch), or cylindrical (helical)*; *Rodin Coil is toroidal*; *unusual antenna geometry*.
>
> *Distinct two — multi-strand phased structure.* *Most mainstream compact antennas are single-conductor*; *Rodin Coil multi-strand introduces phasing complexity*. *Rare in mainstream compact-antenna design*.
>
> *Distinct three — VBM-correspondent design rationale.* *Mainstream antennas designed for performance*; *Rodin Coil designed for VBM correspondence first, performance evaluated by measurement*. *Different design intent.*
>
> *[pause]*

> *Where Rodin Coil sits in the antenna trade-off space.*
>
> *Compact (similar to patch and meander-line antennas in size)*; *efficiency expected mid-range (40-70%, similar to compact whip / patch)*; *pattern shape novel (toroidal multi-strand)*; *VBM-distinguished by design rationale not raw performance*.

>
> *If Marko's 60% doesn't hold*: *Rodin Coil is a pedagogical and VBM-correspondent antenna without competitive performance edge*; *engineering value still real (build skill, measurement skill, framework integration)*.

> *Both outcomes are honest engineering*.
