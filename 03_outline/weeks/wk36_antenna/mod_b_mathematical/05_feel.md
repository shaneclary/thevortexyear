## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Two days ago — *predict gain from topology*. *Yesterday — compute expected impedance match*.
>
> *Today — feel what it is to have made those predictions and be about to test them*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *The embodied stance of engineer-with-hypothesis.*
>
> *A hypothesis is not a wish*. *It's a structured prediction with explicit content: 'Rodin Coil 6-strand at f = 1 MHz should show gain ~3 dBi, impedance ~314 Ω inductive, pattern azimuthal-symmetric'*.
>
> *Standing as engineer-with-hypothesis means*: *the prediction is on record; the test is upcoming; the result will be data*.
>
> *Three textures of this stance.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Texture one — predictions are commitments.*
>
> *Once predictions are written down (Mod B Lens 2-3), they're commitments to specific values*. *The practitioner has staked something*: *the prediction methodology, the assumptions, the parameter values*.
>
> *Standing behind a prediction is engineering accountability*. *If the measurement disconfirms, the practitioner reviews the prediction and learns what went wrong*. *If it confirms, the prediction-method has earned credibility*.
>
> *Either way, the prediction is yours*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Texture two — the test instrument is the arbiter.*
>
> *The VNA, the power meter, the reference probe — these will produce numbers*. *Those numbers settle the question*.
>
> *No amount of prediction-defence can override what the instruments report*.
>
> *Standing as engineer means accepting this asymmetry*: *predictions speak before tests; tests speak last*.
>
> *Engineering humility before instrumentation*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Texture three — outcome is information regardless.*
>
> *The hypothesis-tester does not need confirmation to feel the work is meaningful*.
>
> *Confirmation: prediction-method validated for this case; engineering refinement gradual*.
>
> *Disconfirmation: prediction-method refined; assumptions re-examined; engineering learning sharp*.
>
> *Ambiguous: methodology improved; further measurement specified; engineering iteration*.
>
> *All three outcomes are valuable*. *The embodied stance includes acceptance of all three*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three contrasts with Q1-Q2 mathematical stances.*
>
> *Contrast one — Q1 was solitary mathematical work.* *Practitioners worked through arithmetic; the doubling cycle closed mod 9 by computation; no test against external instrument needed; mathematical truth was internal*.
>
> *Contrast two — Q2 was geometric work.* *Helices on tori; modular labels; geometry verified by working through it; mathematical-structural truth still internal*.
>
> *Contrast three — Q3 is hypothesis-testing work.* *Predictions about physical systems are external claims; instruments arbitrate; truth involves the world*.
>
> *Three stances; engineering practice introduces the third*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *A contemplative observation on the engineer's role.*
>
> *Marko Rodin, in the Hear lessons, has put forth claims*. *Mod B has put those claims into specific testable form*.
>
> *The practitioner now stands*: *not as Marko's defender; not as Marko's debunker; as engineer-with-hypothesis-and-instruments*.
>
> *Engineering doesn't pre-decide*. *Engineering measures*.
>
> *The practitioner's role in this week is to make the measurement, report it, interpret it, defend it*.
>
> *That's engineering identity*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Two reflections to close.*
>
> *Reflection one — hypothesis-testing is engineering's epistemic core.* *All real engineering involves predicting and testing*. *Wk 36 enacts this on Marko's specific claim, but the practice is universal*.
>
> *Reflection two — the embodied stance is calm.* *Predictions are made; tests are coming; results will be data; reporting will be honest*. *Calm engineering anticipation*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Feel the engineer-with-hypothesis stance: predictions are commitments; instruments are arbiters; outcome is information regardless.* *Three textures: prediction-as-commitment, test-as-arbiter, outcome-as-information.* *Engineering identity is the calm stance of measurement-readiness.*"
