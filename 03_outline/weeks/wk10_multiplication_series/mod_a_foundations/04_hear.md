> *"Each of the nine multiplication tables is its own world. Multiply by one and nothing moves. Multiply by two and the doubling cycle traces. Multiply by three and everything collapses to nine. Each multiplier is a different action — a different way the numbers can move under multiplication. What I find beautiful is that all nine actions live on the same Symbol, with the same nine vertices. The Symbol is the *substrate*; the multipliers are the *actions*. Many actions, one substrate. This is the same structure that physics describes when it talks about *symmetry groups* — many transformations, one underlying space. The nine multiplication series are the nine fundamental motions on the Symbol. Doubling and halving are the most active; they visit all six units. The order-3 multipliers — four and seven — visit the family-1 subgroup. Eight oscillates between two points. The non-units collapse. Each multiplier has its specific role. None is redundant; each carries unique information about the underlying multiplicative structure."*
