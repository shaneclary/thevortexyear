## 4. Body (1:00–4:30) — *contemplative*

> *(slow)* "The Symbol has nine vertices.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Nine multipliers act on those nine vertices. Each multiplier moves the vertices differently.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> ×1 doesn't move anything.
>
> ×2 traces the figure-eight, visiting all six units.
>
> ×3 collapses everything to nine.
>
> ×4 traces a triangle through three of the units.
>
> ×5 traces the figure-eight in the opposite direction.
>
> ×6 collapses, like ×3.
>
> ×7 traces a triangle, like ×4 but reversed.
>
> ×8 oscillates between one and eight.
>
> ×9 collapses everything to nine, like ×3 and ×6.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Nine actions. Same substrate. Each action has its own *character*: the energetic doublings, the collapsing threes, the cyclic fours, the oscillating eights. The actions aren't competing for the substrate — they're *inhabiting* it differently.
>
> *[long pause — 12 seconds]*
>
> A useful metaphor. The Symbol is a piano keyboard. Each multiplier is a different scale or chord. Pressing the same nine keys in different orders produces different music. The keyboard is the substrate; the patterns are the music.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Marko's framing — *many actions, one substrate* — points at this. The substrate (Symbol, keyboard, set of integers) is one thing. The actions on it are many. The diversity is in the actions, not in what they act on.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> What does this feel like? Some students notice an *amplification* — the Symbol does *more work* than they realised. Each multiplier reveals different structure. The diagram is rich; you've only been seeing one of nine actions on it for the first nine weeks.
>
> Other students notice a *settledness* — the same vertices, the same diagram, just different ways of activating it. The Symbol's multiplicity doesn't mean it's nine different diagrams; it's one diagram with nine different lives.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Both readings are honest. The nine multiplications inhabit the same Symbol, each with its own pattern, all coexisting on the same nine vertices.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> One substrate. Nine languages. The Symbol speaks all nine."
