## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Each of the multiplication-series shapes appears in real-world patterns. Three examples.
>
> *Triangle (×4 / ×7 series).* Three-fold rotational symmetry.
>
> Real-world appearances: the structure of three-phase electrical power (three currents at 120° offset); the propeller of a three-bladed wind turbine; the fluorine atom's three lone electron pairs; trefoil (three-leaf) plants; the structure of triplet codons in genetic transcription.
>
> The triangle shape captures *triadic stability* — systems that need three points to balance.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Figure-eight (×2 / ×5 series).* Lemniscate, infinity sign.
>
> Real-world appearances: figure-skating tracks; trajectories of orbiting bodies under specific perturbations; the *Bernoulli lemniscate* in calculus; toroidal field lines in plasma physics; some pendulum motions; the path of a Möbius-strip walk; the *infinity-shaped* logos of perpetual-motion or eternity symbols.
>
> The figure-eight shape captures *cyclic interplay* — systems with two interlocking phases.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Line (×8 series).* Diameter, simple back-and-forth.
>
> Real-world appearances: pendulum swings; oscillating springs; AC current in a single wire (peak and trough); sine-wave generators driving simple oscillators; binary digital signals (on/off).
>
> The line shape captures *binary alternation* — the simplest oscillator.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical use. When you encounter a real-world system showing one of these shapes, the Symbol's multiplication series gives you a *mathematical handle* on it. The system isn't necessarily following VBM — but its symmetry signature matches one of the nine series.
>
> Pattern recognition: see the shape, name the series, locate the system on the Symbol's structural map.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One observation. *Two of the nine series produce no shape* — the collapses (×3, ×6, ×9). Their counterpart in real-world systems is *equilibrium without dynamics* — systems that settle to a fixed state without oscillating. Most physical systems with damping eventually reach this state."
