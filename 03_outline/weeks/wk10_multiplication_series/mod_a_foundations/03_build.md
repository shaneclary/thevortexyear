## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

*[Camera close on hand drawing.]*

> "Take a fresh printed Symbol. Pick five different colours.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Polygon 1 — *the Doubling Circuit (×2)*. Trace 1 → 2 → 4 → 8 → 7 → 5 → 1. The figure-eight. Use one colour.
>
> Polygon 2 — *the Halving Circuit (×5)*. Trace 1 → 5 → 7 → 8 → 4 → 2 → 1. Same figure-eight, opposite direction. Use the same colour as ×2 (since they trace the same shape) or a different colour to mark direction.
>
> Polygon 3 — *the ×4 / ×7 triangle*. Trace 1 → 4 → 7 → 1. An equilateral triangle through the family-1 vertices. Use a third colour.
>
> Polygon 4 — *the ×8 line*. Trace 1 → 8 → 1. A diameter, two endpoints. Use a fourth colour.
>
> Polygon 5 — *the ×3 / ×6 / ×9 family-three collapse*. The triangle 3-6-9 from Wk 7. Already on your Symbol from earlier. Use a fifth colour or reuse the family-three colour.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What you've drawn: the *figure-eight* (×2 / ×5), the *family-1 triangle* (×4 / ×7 — same path, different direction), the *diameter* (×8), and the *family-three triangle* (×3 / ×6 / ×9 collapse). Plus the trivial point (×1).
>
> Five distinct geometric shapes for the nine multipliers.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Notice the *symmetries*. The figure-eight has 2-fold rotational symmetry (visible at 180° rotation). The triangles have 3-fold rotational symmetry. The diameter has 2-fold rotational symmetry. The full Symbol with all polygons has 9-fold rotational symmetry (since the underlying Symbol has it).
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Date the page. *Nine multiplication-series polygons drawn.* Build log."
