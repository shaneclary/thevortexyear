## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Counting distinct winding configurations from the nine multiplications.
>
> *Step 1 — count distinct polygon shapes.*
>
> Five shapes: trivial (×1), figure-eight (×2/×5), triangle (×4/×7), line (×8), family-three (×3/×6/×9).
>
> The trivial isn't a winding (no traversal). So *four useful shapes*.
>
> *Step 2 — count direction variants.*
>
> Most shapes can be wound in two directions:
>
> *Figure-eight*: doubling-direction (×2) or halving-direction (×5). Two variants.
>
> *Triangle (1-4-7)*: ×4 traverses 1→4→7→1; ×7 traverses 1→7→4→1. Two variants.
>
> *Line (1-8)*: ×8 just swaps. One direction (the swap is its own reverse).
>
> *Family-three*: ×3 traverses 3→9; ×6 traverses 3→9 also (since 3·6 = 18 → 9). Wait, let me recheck. The non-unit elements collapse rather than cycle, so the *family-three* polygon is degenerate — a triangle drawn but not traversed cyclically.
>
> So practical direction-distinct configurations:
>
> Figure-eight: 2.
>
> Triangle: 2.
>
> Line: 1.
>
> Family-three: 1 (degenerate, no cycle direction).
>
> Total: 6 configurations.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Step 3 — count multi-strand combinations.*
>
> Each configuration can be wound with multiple strands. Adding strands gives:
>
> Figure-eight: single, three-pair (Wk 35), nine-strand family-phase (Wk 35 advanced).
>
> Triangle: single, three-strand (one per family-1 vertex).
>
> Line: single, two-strand (paired antiphase).
>
> Family-three: single, three-strand (3, 6, 9 separate).
>
> Each shape has at least 3-4 multi-strand variants.
>
> *Step 4 — count drive configurations.*
>
> Each multi-strand variant can be driven at different timings (same-phase, antiphase, 120° offset, etc.).
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Total configurations explode quickly. The canonical Wk-33 build is *one specific configuration* — figure-eight, single-strand, single-drive. Many other configurations exist.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> The Wk-35 advanced section covers two of the most useful: figure-eight multi-strand (polar pairs) and family-phase multi-strand (three-phase). Plus this week's preview of triangle and line configurations.
>
> The combinatorial space of Rodin Coil configurations is large. The course covers the most-studied configurations; experienced builders explore the rest."
