## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)
> "Lens 5 of 9. Six minutes."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30) — *contemplative*

> *(slow)* "The Symbol is one diagram. From it, the math derives many possible coils.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Single-strand figure-eight. Multi-strand polar-pair. Three-phase family. Triangle. Line. Family-three. Combinations of these. Variants of each. Different sizes. Different drive configurations.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> The configuration space is *large*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Most builders explore a small region of this space. The canonical Wk-33 single-strand build is *one point* in the space. The Wk-35 polar-pair build is *another point*. The advanced family-phase build is a *third*. Most builders never explore beyond these.
>
> *[long pause — 12 seconds]*
>
> Yet the space exists. The math holds the whole landscape, not just the points builders visit. Every multiplication series gives a possible coil; every winding direction gives a variant; every multi-strand combination gives more variants; every drive configuration gives more.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What does this *feel* like, contemplatively? Some students experience *expansion* — the breadth of possibility excites them, points to experiments not yet done. Others experience *focus* — the breadth is overwhelming; they prefer to commit to the canonical build and study it deeply.
>
> Both responses are honest. The breadth doesn't demand that you explore it; it just exists.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> Marko's framing — *the family of nine polygons is the complete catalogue* — is mathematically true and *humanly partial*. No single builder builds the whole catalogue. The catalogue's existence is what the math gives us; the choice of *which builds to make* is up to the practitioner.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Sit with the breadth without needing to fill it. The configuration space contains many possibilities; the year contains time for a few of them. The math is patient. Builders are finite.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> What you'll build in Wk 33 is *one* coil. The space contains many more. Both facts are real. The choice of where in the space to work is yours."
