## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The objection. *We've shown the figure-eight Rodin Coil isn't the only viable winding. Triangle, line, and family-three coils are equally valid mathematically. So the canonical Rodin Coil isn't actually special — it's one option among several.*
>
> The objection is technically correct: the figure-eight is one configuration among several. But three reasons it's *practically* central.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *First — most-studied.* The canonical Rodin Coil has fifty years of accumulated data, build experience, and refinement. Its winding patterns, terminations, drive configurations, and measurement protocols are well-documented. Alternative configurations have less accumulated knowledge; building one is more experimental.
>
> *Second — richest structure.* The figure-eight visits all six units of `(ℤ/9ℤ)*` in the maximal cycle. Other configurations visit subgroups or non-units. The figure-eight contains *more structural information* per turn than alternatives.
>
> *Third — best entry point for builders.* The figure-eight's complexity is challenging but tractable for first-time builders. The triangle is simpler; the line is even simpler — but their simplicity means they don't expose builders to the full structure of the system. Starting with the figure-eight gives builders the *complete* skill set; alternative configurations can be built as needed.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> So the calibrated answer to *if alternatives exist, why is the figure-eight central?*:
>
> *The figure-eight is the most-studied configuration, the richest in structural information (visiting all six units), and the best entry point for builders. Alternatives are valid for specific experimental purposes but require comparison to the figure-eight baseline. Centring on the figure-eight is practical, not arbitrary.*
>
> Forty-three words.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One nuance. Alternative-configuration researchers do exist — practitioners who specialise in line coils or triangle coils for specific applications. They're rare but real. The course supports them by teaching the alternatives in this module; it doesn't require them by leading with the figure-eight.
>
> Practical takeaway. When describing your build to others, lead with *I built the canonical figure-eight Rodin Coil* if that's what you built. If you built an alternative, lead with the configuration name explicitly: *I built a triangle (×4) Rodin Coil for three-fold-symmetry testing*. The clear naming prevents confusion."
