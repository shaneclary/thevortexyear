## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Compare the canonical figure-eight Rodin Coil to three alternatives across measurable properties.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Inductance.* Roughly proportional to (number of turns)² × form factor. The figure-eight has 96 turns (24 per traversal × 4); triangle and line variants have fewer turns per traversal, so their inductances are smaller.
>
> Figure-eight (96 turns): baseline inductance L₀.
>
> Triangle (typical 48 turns: 12 per traversal × 4): roughly L₀/4.
>
> Line (typical 24 turns: 6 per traversal × 4): roughly L₀/16.
>
> Family-three (similar to triangle): roughly L₀/4.
>
> *DC resistance.* Proportional to wire length. Shorter cycles use less wire.
>
> Figure-eight: baseline R₀.
>
> Triangle: roughly R₀/2.
>
> Line: roughly R₀/3.
>
> Family-three: roughly R₀/2.
>
> *Resonant frequency.* Inversely related to √(LC). Shorter cycles have lower L, so higher resonance.
>
> Figure-eight: baseline f₀.
>
> Triangle: roughly 2 × f₀.
>
> Line: roughly 4 × f₀.
>
> *Field-pattern complexity.* The cycle's coverage of the units determines field structure.
>
> Figure-eight: visits all 6 units; complex 6-fold field structure.
>
> Triangle: visits 3 units; 3-fold field structure.
>
> Line: visits 2 units; 2-fold (antipodal) field structure.
>
> Family-three: visits 3 non-units; different region.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One observation. The four configurations span a *complexity hierarchy*. The figure-eight is the most complex; the line is the simplest. Each level offers different experimental opportunities.
>
> *Pick your complexity level.* Beginners and most engineering applications benefit from the canonical figure-eight. Researchers seeking specific isolated effects can use lower-complexity variants. The full hierarchy is available; choose based on your research question.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Useful framing for cross-comparing builds. *Wind any subset of the four configurations on identical form factors with identical wire. The differences in measurements isolate the effects of the winding pattern alone.* This is a clean experimental design that the multiplication series enables."
