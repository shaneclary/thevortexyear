## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Three use cases for non-doubling windings.
>
> *Use case one — Triangle (×4 / ×7) for three-fold field testing.* When you want to study three-fold symmetry in EM fields, the triangle coil is purpose-built. It produces a field with explicit 120° rotational symmetry — easier to characterise than the more complex figure-eight field.
>
> Application: comparison studies. Build a triangle coil and a doubling coil; drive both at the same frequency; compare radiation patterns. The triangle's three-fold field is simpler; differences from the doubling coil's field structure attribute to the cycle complexity.
>
> *Use case two — Line (×8) for simplest-possible-Rodin baseline.* The line coil is the simplest non-trivial Rodin geometry — just two-position oscillation. Useful as a *baseline* against which more complex builds are compared.
>
> Application: control experiments. The line coil's behaviour is close to a standard bifilar coil's; comparing the line coil to the figure-eight coil isolates *cycle-following effects* from *general toroidal-coil effects*.
>
> *Use case three — Family-three (×3 / ×6 / ×9) for axis-aligned experiments.* The family-three coil winds on the 3-6-9 vertices — the *trigger axis* positions from Wk 8 Mod-C. Useful for experiments specifically targeting axis-aligned field components.
>
> Application: trigger-axis isolation. Build a family-three coil; drive it; measure axial field components without the doubling-cycle interference. Allows direct testing of trigger-axis effects.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical guidance.
>
> *Don't build alternatives until you've completed the canonical build.* The Wk-33 figure-eight is the comparison baseline. Without it, alternative-coil measurements have nothing to compare against.
>
> *Build alternatives when you have a specific question.* Generic exploration is unfocused; targeted comparison studies are productive.
>
> *Document scrupulously.* Alternative-coil measurements are less standardised than figure-eight measurements; clear documentation lets others reproduce or critique your work.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Most students never build alternatives. The canonical build is enough for most purposes. Researchers who want to systematically explore the configuration space build several. The course supports both paths."
