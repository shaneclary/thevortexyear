## 4. Body (1:00–4:30) — *contemplative*

> *(slow)* "*Group action* is a mathematical term. It means: a way that a group (set of operations) acts on a set (something the operations do things to).
>
> *[pause]*
>
> The nine vertices of the Symbol form a set. The integers 1 through 9 form a group under multiplication mod 9 (well, their units do — six of nine). The group acts on the set: each element of the group is a *function* on the set.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> Nine multipliers. Nine functions. Each function is a *different way of permuting* the nine vertices.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Some functions are identity (×1).
>
> Some are full permutations (×2, ×4, ×5, ×7, ×8 — moving the units around).
>
> Some are projections (×3, ×6, ×9 — collapsing onto family three).
>
> Each function has its character. Each character is a distinct *experience* of the same set.
>
> *[long pause — 12 seconds]*
>
> What does this *feel* like, contemplatively? The Symbol holds these nine experiences simultaneously. Whether you're thinking about doubling, halving, tripling, quadrupling, quintupling — the Symbol is *already prepared* to display the corresponding action. The diagram is a *passive resource* that nine distinct multiplicative perspectives can activate.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> A useful image. The Symbol is a stage. The multiplications are dancers. Each dancer has their own choreography on the same stage. The stage doesn't change; the dance does.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> Marko's framing — *the Symbol is a theatre; the multiplications are plays* — captures this. The same nine vertices host nine different geometric stories.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Hold the multiplicity for a moment. Same Symbol. Different actions. Each action is real, full, complete in itself. Each action is also part of a *family of nine* with structural relationships among them.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> What you're sitting with is the experience of *one substrate, multiple lives*. The Symbol is more than nine vertices and a fixed circular order. It's a stage that hosts a family of related but distinct geometric actions, each illuminating a different aspect of multiplication mod 9.
>
> The richness is in the multiplicity, not in any one action. The Symbol's depth comes from carrying all nine simultaneously."
