## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The objection. *VBM treats the doubling circuit (×2) as the centre of the system. But we now see ×4, ×7, ×8, etc., all produce real orbits. Why is ×2's orbit privileged over the others as the structural centre?*
>
> Three reasons.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *First — ×2's orbit is maximal in the subgroup lattice.* The orbit of ×2 visits all six units: {1, 2, 4, 5, 7, 8}. The orbits of ×4 and ×7 visit only three units: {1, 4, 7}. The orbit of ×8 visits two: {1, 8}. The order-3 and order-2 orbits are *subgroups* of the order-6 orbit. The maximal orbit *contains* all the others.
>
> *Second — ×2's cycle traces the figure-eight, the most visually distinctive shape.* The order-3 orbits trace triangles. The order-2 orbit traces a line. The figure-eight has more *visual content* than the smaller shapes; it's the easier shape to recognise and reference.
>
> *Third — ×2 corresponds to the simplest physical operation.* Doubling is *the* fundamental operation across many fields: doubling time in biology, doubling distance in physics, doubling in computer-science recursion. ×3, ×4, etc., have engineering uses but aren't as ubiquitous.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> So the calibrated answer to *why centre on ×2's orbit?*:
>
> *×2's orbit is the maximal cycle in `(ℤ/9ℤ)*` — it visits all six units, while smaller-order orbits visit subgroups. The smaller orbits are subgroups of the maximal one. ×2 is also visually distinctive (figure-eight) and physically ubiquitous (the doubling operation). Centring on ×2's orbit is mathematically natural.*
>
> Forty-seven words.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One nuance worth noting. *Any maximal-order generator could play the role of centre*. ×5 also generates the full units. The course centres on ×2 by *convention* (Marko encountered it first; the doubling cycle is the canonical entry point); but ×5 (Halving Circuit) is structurally equivalent and gets equal billing in Wk 5. The privileging of ×2 over ×5 is *historical*, not mathematical.
>
> Practical takeaway. When you describe VBM's central structure, lead with *the doubling cycle, with its inverse the halving cycle, generates the multiplicative units of mod 9*. That sentence places ×2 and ×5 as a *pair*, the maximal generators, which is structurally accurate."
