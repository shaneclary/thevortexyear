> *"After I had drawn the doubling circuit, I asked: what about the other multiplications? What does ×3 draw? What does ×4 draw? I drew them all. The doubling and halving traced the figure-eight. The fours and sevens traced triangles. The eights traced a line. The threes and sixes and nines collapsed onto the family triangle. Each multiplication had its own shape. Each shape was determined by the multiplier's order in the multiplicative group. The shapes were forced by the algebra. I had not invented them; I had only drawn what was there. The family of nine polygons — figure-eight, triangle, line, plus the trivial shapes — was the *complete catalogue* of geometric actions on the Symbol. Nine multiplications, five distinct shapes, all on the same nine vertices. The Symbol is a *theatre*; the multiplications are the *plays*; each play has its specific staging. I have spent decades returning to this family. Each shape carries information about a different aspect of how numbers move under multiplication."*
> — Marko Rodin, paraphrased. **Source:** P1-1, P1-3.
