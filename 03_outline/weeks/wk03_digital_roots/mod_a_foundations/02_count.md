## 0. Authoring notes
Reduce four specific numbers — 47, 144, 999, 2026 — slowly, by hand, with the student following along. Lens 2 numerical-precise. Each example is one paragraph; the host narrates the digit-sum out loud at a transcribable pace.

## 1. Outcome
By the end of these 6 minutes, the student has reduced four numbers by hand and can do the same for any number on demand.

## 2. Hook (0:00–0:30)
*[On screen: four numbers stacked — 47, 144, 999, 2026. Each one will reduce in turn during the lesson.]*

> "Four numbers. Four digital roots. Pencil ready. Six minutes."

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)
> "Lens 2 of 9. Yesterday we watched the cascade. Today we do four ourselves, slowly. By the end the operation is automatic."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

*[Camera close on a hand on lined paper. The host writes each number and reduces it.]*

> "**Number one — 47.** Two digits. Sum them. Four plus seven. Eleven. That's still two digits. Sum again. One plus one. Two. Digital root of 47 is two. Place a finger on the 2 vertex of your Symbol. That's where 47 lives.
>
> *[pause for the student to write and reduce]*
>
> **Number two — 144.** Three digits. One plus four plus four. Nine. Single digit, done. Digital root of 144 is nine. Place a finger on the 9 vertex. The number 144, which happens to be twelve squared, reduces to the top of the Symbol. Notice — every multiple of nine reduces to nine. We'll prove this next week.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> **Number three — 999.** Three digits. Nine plus nine plus nine. Twenty-seven. Two digits, reduce again. Two plus seven. Nine. Digital root of 999 is nine. Same vertex as 144. Three nines and two ones-and-fours reduce to the same place. The Symbol doesn't care about the route — it cares about the destination.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> **Number four — 2026.** This year. Four digits. Two plus zero plus two plus six. Ten. Two digits, reduce. One plus zero. One. Digital root of 2026 is one. Place a finger on the 1 vertex. This year sits at the one-o'clock position on your Symbol. Marko, when he speaks of years, occasionally points to the year's vertex. 2025 reduced to nine — same as 144 and 999. 2024 reduced to eight. 2023 to seven. Every year traces its own path around the Symbol over time.
>
> *[pause — show the four numbers, four arrows, four highlighted vertices]*
>
> Four reductions. Four single digits — 2, 9, 9, 1. Four positions on the Symbol. Notice the pattern: two of our four numbers — 144 and 999 — collapsed to the same vertex. That's not unusual. Most pairs of large numbers, picked at random, will share a digital root *some* of the time. But the *distribution* across the nine vertices is not random — it's determined by the multiplicative and additive structure of the original numbers. We'll spend the rest of Q1 mapping that distribution."

## 5. Try it (4:30–5:30)
> "Pause. Pick four numbers from your life. Your house number. Your year of birth. Your phone number's last four digits. Today's date. Reduce each. Place a small mark — a coloured dot — at each digital root on your printed Symbol. Sixty seconds."

*[Pause card on screen for 60 seconds.]*

> "Welcome back. Four marks on your Symbol. Numbers from your life now live there."
