## 0. Authoring notes
Place digit-summing next to its mainstream-math cousin: modular arithmetic. Same operation, different vocabulary. The student leaves able to translate between Marko's vocabulary and a textbook's. ## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Mainstream mathematics calls the operation *reduction modulo 9* — written *n mod 9* or *n ≡ k (mod 9)*. The operation takes any integer *n* and returns the remainder when *n* is divided by 9. Standard high-school and undergraduate math.
>
> Take 47 again. Forty-seven divided by nine is five, remainder two. So 47 mod 9 equals 2. Same answer as the digital-root cascade.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Why the same? Because *summing digits is equivalent to taking modulo 9*, due to a neat property of base ten. The number 47 written out is 4 × 10 + 7 × 1. Reduce mod 9: 4 × (10 mod 9) + 7 × (1 mod 9). Ten mod nine is one. One mod nine is one. So 47 mod 9 = 4 × 1 + 7 × 1 = 11 mod 9 = 2. The digit-sum captures the modulo-9 residue exactly.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three vocabulary translations:
>
> *Digit-summing* in VBM equals *reduction modulo 9* in mainstream math.
>
> *Digital root* equals *the residue of n modulo 9, with 0 represented as 9 on the Symbol*.
>
> *Multiples of 9 reducing to 9* equals *the residue class of 0 in ℤ/9ℤ*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> The historical name for the operation in classical arithmetic is *casting out nines* — a verification trick used by accountants for centuries. The technique: when adding a column of numbers, sum the digits of each, take the digit-roots, and check the digit-root of the total against the digit-root of the sum-of-digit-roots. If they don't match, there's an arithmetic error somewhere.
>
> Casting out nines was taught in elementary schools through the early twentieth century. The technique is exactly the digit-summing operation we're using. Marko didn't invent it; he *names* it differently and *uses* it differently.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> So the calibrated translation. When a mathematician says *47 mod 9 equals 2*, you say *the digital root of 47 is 2*. When a textbook says *the residue class of 0 in ℤ/9ℤ*, you say *the position labelled 9 on the Symbol*. When an old accountant says *cast out nines*, you say *reduce by digit-summing*. Three vocabularies. One operation."
