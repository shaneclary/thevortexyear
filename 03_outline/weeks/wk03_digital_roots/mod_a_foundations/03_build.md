## 0. Authoring notes
A 50-number reduction worksheet, by hand. Imperative voice. The lesson is the doing. Camera holds on the host's hand reducing numbers in real time; the student works alongside. By the end, the cascade is automatic.

## 1. Outcome
By the end of these 6 minutes, the student has reduced fifty numbers and can perform the digital-root cascade automatically.

## 2. Hook (0:00–0:30)
*[On screen: a notebook page with the numbers 1–50 listed down the left margin. Each row has space for the digital root.]*

> "Fifty numbers. Fifty digital roots. The hand learns by doing. Sixty seconds for the page setup, then we begin."

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)
> "Lens 3 of 9. Today is a worksheet. The lesson is the operation, fifty times in a row. By the end the digital-root cascade runs without thinking."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

*[Camera follows the host's hand through reductions in real time.]*

> "Open your notebook to a fresh page. Down the left margin, write the numbers 1, 2, 3, 4 ... up to 50. Skip every other line so you have room. That's the worksheet.
>
> *[long pause for setup]*
>
> Now we reduce. I'll go fast. Pause if you need to. Don't worry about keeping pace with me — the worksheet is yours to finish.
>
> One — digital root one. Two — two. Three — three. Four — four. Five — five. Six — six. Seven — seven. Eight — eight. Nine — nine. Ten — sum is one. Eleven — sum is two. Twelve — sum is three. Thirteen — four. Fourteen — five. Fifteen — six. Sixteen — seven. Seventeen — eight. Eighteen — nine. Nineteen — ten — one. Twenty — two. Twenty-one — three.
>
> *[pause for the student to catch up]*
>
> Notice the pattern emerging. The digital roots cycle 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 1, 2, 3 ... The cycle has length nine. Every block of nine consecutive integers traces all nine vertices of the Symbol once.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Continue to fifty on your own. Twenty-two through fifty. Don't just memorise the cycle — write each digit-sum. The point is to build the *operation* into your hand, not the *result*.
>
> *[long pause — let the student work for ~90 seconds]*
>
> Done? Look at your worksheet. Fifty numbers, fifty digital roots. The right column reads 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, repeating five times plus a partial cycle through 5. Numbers 46 to 50 reduce to 1, 2, 3, 4, 5. The pattern is clean and predictable.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One observation. The cycle of digital roots through consecutive integers is the *additive* structure on ℤ/9ℤ — adding one moves you one step around the Symbol. We'll meet the *multiplicative* structure starting next week with the Doubling Circuit. Same Symbol, different operation. Today you've trained your hand on the additive cycle."

## 5. Try it (4:30–5:30)
> "Pause. If you didn't finish the worksheet — finish it now. The cascade has to be in your hand by Week 4. Sixty seconds, or as long as it takes."

*[Pause card on screen for 60 seconds, with extra time built in for the actual work.]*

> "Welcome back. Fifty digital roots in your notebook. The operation is yours."

## 6. Bridge (5:30–6:00)
