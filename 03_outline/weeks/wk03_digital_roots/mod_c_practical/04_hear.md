**[ORG-CLIP]** *— canonical clip selection. Candidate text:*

> *"When I reduce a number, I am not calculating. The arithmetic is fast — I do it in my head before I have finished reading the number. What is happening, when I reduce, is that I am *seeing* the number differently. The number was a measurement, a count, a quantity. After the reduction, it is also a *position*. A vertex on the Symbol. A place. The number used to live in the abstract of mathematics. Now it lives in a specific place on a specific drawing. I have given it a home. When I look at the number, I now see where it lives, what its neighbours are, what other numbers share its place. This is not a computation. It is an act of perception — the digit-root operation as a way of looking, not a way of calculating. After thirty years of doing it, I cannot read a number without simultaneously seeing its position. The two have collapsed into one act of attention."*
> — Marko Rodin, paraphrased from RodinAerodynamics.org and lecture material. **Source:** P1-1, P1-3. **Permission status:** to be confirmed by org before publication.
