> *"Modulo-nine arithmetic isn't just a Symbol thing. It's everywhere. ISBN numbers — every book has one — use a checksum that's effectively mod 11 with a digit-summing variant. Banking routing numbers use mod-10 checksums. Old accountants used to verify long calculations with mod-9 — they called it casting out nines. The DNA codon table has structure that, when laid out properly, shows mod-9 patterns. The Rodin Coil winding follows mod-9 structure. The reason this isn't a Marko-thing or a VBM-thing is that mod-9 — and modular arithmetic generally — captures a deep regularity in how integers behave under repeated operations. We didn't invent it. We're using it the way every error-correcting code in your phone uses it. The novelty Marko brings isn't the math; it's the claim that the math means something physical and metaphysical, that it isn't just useful for checksums but reflects real structure in the world. That claim is contested. The math underneath isn't."*
> — Randy Powell, paraphrased from Red Ice Radio Hour 1 and TEDxCharlotte 2010 lecture material. **Source:** P1-7, P1-13. **Permission status:** to be confirmed by org before publication.
