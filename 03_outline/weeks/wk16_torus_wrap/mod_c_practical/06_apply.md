## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The torus former you'll acquire by Q3 will be used in nine consecutive weeks. Today's apply maps out what each week does with it.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Wk 27 — ★ Doubling Circuit Revisited.* The first ★ revisit week. The doubling cycle is now traced *on the former* rather than just on a flat strip. Hand-trace the cycle on the unwound former with a marker.
>
> *Wk 28 — ★ Polar Number Pairs Revisited.* Mark the three antipodal pairs on the former. Verify they're physically opposite.
>
> *Wk 29 — ★ The 3-6-9 Pulse Revisited.* Mark the 3-6-9 axis as a physical orthogonal direction through the former's centre.
>
> *Wk 30 — Aetheron / Aetheric Polarity.* Theory week. The former waits.
>
> *Wk 31 — Aetheron Flux Monopole Emanations.* Theory week. The former still waits but is now context.
>
> *Wk 32 — Rodin Coil — Topology.* The former gets *prepared* for winding. Surface treatment if needed (sanding, sealing). Strand-attachment points planned. No copper yet.
>
> *Wk 33 — Rodin Coil — Single-Strand Build.* *First copper.* Wind a single-strand single-traversal coil following the strip from Wk 14. The former is now a wound coil.
>
> *Wk 34 — Rodin Coil — Measurement.* The wound coil gets measured. Resistance, inductance, magnetic field. The former is now an instrumented object.
>
> *Wk 35 — Rodin Coil — Multi-Strand & Phased.* If you want to attempt multi-strand: rewind on a fresh former (or unwind and rewind). The canonical six-strand four-traversal build happens here for advanced students.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Across the nine weeks.* The former goes from:
>
> *Naked plastic* (weeks 27-31) → *prepared for wind* (week 32) → *single-strand wound* (week 33) → *instrumented* (week 34) → *multi-strand wound* (week 35).
>
> The former is *the same physical object* across all nine weeks; what changes is what's *on it* and *measured from it*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Practical implication for now.*
>
> *Implication one.* You need the former by Wk 27, ten weeks from now. The acquisition timeline (Lens 3) is real. Don't postpone.
>
> *Implication two.* Plan to *preserve* the unwound former through Wks 27-29. The revisit weeks need it bare. Don't pre-wind.
>
> *Implication three.* If you can afford it, *get two formers*. One for the single-strand build (Wk 33), one to keep for the multi-strand build (Wk 35). Otherwise, plan to unwind and rewind.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three observations.*
>
> *Observation one.* The former is a *re-usable platform*. Unlike the paper torus from Mod A, it can be wound and unwound multiple times. The reuse is part of why 3D-printed plastic is preferable to other materials.
>
> *Observation two.* The former is *the bench*, not the build. The build is the *coil* — the wound assembly. The former is the substrate the coil is built on.
>
> *Observation three.* The single former you acquire could last *years*. Many practitioners use the same former across multiple builds and many measurement experiments.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Plan for one former, possibly two.* Acquire by Wk 27. Preserve unwound through Wk 31. Wind, measure, possibly rewind across Wks 32-35.
>
> The former is the *single most important physical artefact* of Q3. Its acquisition deserves careful planning."
