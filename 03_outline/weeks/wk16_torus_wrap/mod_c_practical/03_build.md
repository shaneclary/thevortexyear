## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Today's build introduces the *3D-printed torus former* — a plastic torus that will serve as the substrate for Q3's actual coil winding. This isn't a teaching artefact; it's the engineering substrate.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Why a 3D-printed former.*
>
> The paper torus is for *learning*. The 3D-printed torus is for *winding*. Different purposes:
>
> *Paper.* Foldable, cheap, demonstration-quality. Good for understanding topology and positioning.
>
> *Plastic.* Rigid, durable, dimensionally precise. Holds wire under tension during winding. Survives years of handling.
>
> The Rodin Foundation publishes a canonical STL file for the torus former. Major diameter, minor diameter, surface markings — all standard. **`[ORG-INPUT]`** — file location and ratified dimensions to be confirmed by the foundation.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Specifications (per the canonical foundation file).*
>
> *Major diameter.* Approximately 10 cm. Large enough to handle comfortably; small enough to print on a hobbyist printer.
>
> *Minor diameter.* Approximately 2.5 cm. Provides enough space for six strands without crowding.
>
> *Surface markings.* The 6×24 grid is *embossed* into the surface — slight ridges that the wire follows, ensuring the wind path stays correct.
>
> *Material.* PLA or PETG plastic. Both are common 3D-printer materials. Avoid ABS for outdoor use.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Three printing options.*
>
> *Option 1 — print yourself.* If you have a 3D printer or access to one (maker space, library), download the STL and print. Print time: 4-8 hours depending on resolution. Cost: $1-3 in plastic.
>
> *Option 2 — service print.* Send the STL to an online service (Shapeways, Sculpteo, local makerspace's print queue). Cost: $15-50 typically. Turnaround: 1-2 weeks.
>
> *Option 3 — alternative former.* If 3D printing isn't accessible, *use a wooden former*. Lathe-turned wooden tori or bought wooden torus blanks (search 'turned wooden torus' or 'wedding-ring forms') work well. Cost: $5-30. Mark the grid on by hand.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *When to acquire the former.*
>
> Q3 begins at Wk 27, eleven weeks from now. The former needs to be in hand by Wk 33 (the canonical-build week) at the latest.
>
> *Suggested timeline.*
>
> Now (Wk 16): identify how you'll get the former (print, service, or alternative).
>
> Wk 20-22: place the order if using a service or buying a wooden former.
>
