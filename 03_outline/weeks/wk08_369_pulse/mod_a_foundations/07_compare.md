## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The Yin/Yang symbol — used in Taoism and East Asian philosophy for over two millennia — depicts two complementary forces in dynamic balance. Yin (dark) and Yang (light) flow into each other; each contains a seed of the other.
>
> Standard reading: *binary*. Yin and Yang are the two opposing forces. Two states.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Marko's reading: *trinary*. Yin and Yang are two of three components; the *Tao* — the underlying principle — is the third. The Tao is *not depicted* in the standard Yin/Yang symbol but is *implied* as the field within which the dance occurs.
>
> Mapped to the 3-6-9 Pulse:
>
> - Yin → 3 (or 6).
> - Yang → 6 (or 3).
> - Tao → 9 (the still centre).
>
> The 3↔6 oscillation is Yin/Yang dancing. The fixed 9 is the Tao watching.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Marko's reading isn't mainstream Taoism. Most Taoist scholars treat the binary Yin/Yang as primary; the Tao is conceptually distinct from the dance and isn't usually depicted as a *third element* on the same diagram. Marko's reframing — Yin/Yang as a *visible binary* in a *trinary structure* — is his own.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Whether the reframing is *historically faithful* to Taoism is debatable. *Whether the structural correspondence is real* is mathematically clear: any cyclic group of order 2 (binary oscillation) embedded in a larger system with a fixed point produces a structurally trinary system. The 3-6-9 family has this exact form: 3↔6 oscillation + 9 fixed point. The trinary structure is forced by the algebra.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Useful framing. *The Yin/Yang trinary reading is Marko's contribution, not standard Taoism. The mathematical structure — binary oscillation around a fixed centre — is real and matches the 3-6-9 family.* That sentence respects both the philosophical tradition and the mathematical fact.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. When you draw the Yin/Yang and overlay the 3-6-9 Pulse from your Symbol, the two figures *suggest* each other structurally — even if they're not historically the same diagram. The visual correspondence is part of why students find Marko's reframing compelling. The structural correspondence is part of why mathematicians find it interesting."
