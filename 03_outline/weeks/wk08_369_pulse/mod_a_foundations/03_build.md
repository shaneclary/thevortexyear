## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

*[Camera close on hand.]*

> "Take your printed Symbol — by now annotated with Doubling Circuit (Wk 4), Halving Circuit (Wk 5), Polar Pair lines (Wk 6), Family triangles (Wk 7). Today we add the 3-6-9 Pulse markings.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> First marking — the 3↔6 oscillation. Draw a *two-headed arrow* between vertices 3 and 6. Make it visually distinct from the polar-pair line that runs across the Symbol — the 3-6 arrow is *not* a diameter; 3 and 6 sit at four-o'clock and eight-o'clock positions on the Symbol's nine-vertex layout, which means the 3-6 line passes *near* but *not through* the Symbol's centre.
>
> Use a different colour from the polar pairs and family triangles. The 3-6 arrow needs to stand out as its own structural element.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Second marking — the 9 fixed point. Draw a small *circular self-loop* at vertex 9 — a tight circle adjacent to the 9 marker, indicating the doubling action sends 9 to itself.
>
> Use the same colour as the 3-6 arrow, since both are part of the 3-6-9 Pulse structure.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Third marking — the *family triangle* connecting 3, 6, 9. Draw the equilateral triangle on top of the existing markings. This is the family-three triangle from Wk 7, now revisited as the carrier of the Pulse.
>
> *[pause — show the fully annotated Symbol]*
>
> Look at your Symbol now. It carries:
>
> - The Doubling Circuit's figure-eight (Wk 4).
> - The Halving Circuit traced backwards (Wk 5).
> - Three Polar Pair diameters (Wk 6).
> - Three Family triangles (Wk 7).
> - The 3-6-9 Pulse: two-headed arrow + self-loop + family-three triangle (today).
>
> Five layers of annotation on one Symbol. The Symbol is becoming dense with information.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Date the page. *3-6-9 Pulse marked.* Goes in build log.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One observation. The 3-6 line on your Symbol is *not* a diameter — yet it's structurally important. The two-cycle is real and short, the line just happens to pass near (but not through) the centre. This is one place where the visual placement convention of the Symbol — 9 at top, 1 through 8 clockwise — produces non-obvious geometric facts. The pulse is real; the line's position is what the placement gives you."
