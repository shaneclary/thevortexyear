## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Three vertices on the Symbol — 3, 6, 9 — make up the third Family Number Group from last week. They sit *outside* the Doubling Circuit. Today we look at how they move when doubling acts on them.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Apply doubling to 3. *3 × 2 = 6.* Reduce: single digit, no reduction. Land on 6.
>
> Apply doubling to 6. *6 × 2 = 12.* Reduce: *1 + 2 = 3.* Land on 3.
>
> So 3 and 6 *trade places* under doubling. The 3↔6 oscillation. A *two-cycle*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Apply doubling to 9. *9 × 2 = 18.* Reduce: *1 + 8 = 9.* Stay at 9.
>
> 9 is a *fixed point* — doubling doesn't move it.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> So the 3-6-9 family has *two components* under doubling: a two-cycle on {3, 6}, and a fixed point at {9}. Different from the six-cycle that the units {1, 2, 4, 5, 7, 8} form.
>
> *[pause — show the three components on the Symbol]*
>
> The full doubling map on the Symbol now has three components:
> - The big six-cycle through {1, 2, 4, 5, 7, 8} — Doubling Circuit.
> - The two-cycle on {3, 6} — the *3-6 oscillation*.
> - The fixed point at {9} — *Spirit*.
>
> Three components, no leaks between them. A number's family determines which component it lives in.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Marko names the 3-6-9 family the *Pulse* because of its temporal structure. The 3↔6 two-cycle is a *binary oscillation* — fastest possible cycle, two beats. The 9 fixed point is a *constant* — no motion, no change. Together they form a trinary structure: oscillation + constancy.
>
> The Pulse is *outside the doubling cycle* but *part of the Symbol*. It's where the action of doubling has its quietest behaviour."
