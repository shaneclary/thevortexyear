## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The objection. *The 3-6-9 Pulse exists because 9 = 3² is composite. The non-units {3, 6, 9} are the multiples of 3 that share factor with 9. If we used a prime modulus — say 7 or 11 — there would be no non-units (every nonzero element is a unit in ℤ/p for prime p). So no 3-6-9 family. The trinary structure is an artefact of choosing 9.*
>
> The objection is correct about the algebra. *9 = 3²* is the reason {3, 6, 9} forms a non-trivial ideal. In a prime modulus, the ideal {0} would be the only proper ideal, and there'd be no analogous trinary structure.
>
> But this is exactly the point.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Marko's claim isn't that the 3-6-9 structure is *universal*. His claim is that *9 is the natural modulus for base 10*, and that 9's specific factorisation as *3²* gives base 10 a structurally rich pattern that prime moduli wouldn't.
>
> Three things this *richness* enables:
>
> *First — non-trivial ideal.* The {3, 6, 9} ideal exists. In prime moduli, no such ideal does. The structure is content base-10 carries.
>
> *Second — visual triadic structure.* The three families (cosets of 3) tile the Symbol cleanly. In prime moduli, only the trivial coset structure exists. The Symbol's three-fold symmetry is forced by 9 = 3².
>
> *Third — the doubling cycle's specific length.* The order of 2 in `(ℤ/9ℤ)*` is 6, leaving exactly 3 non-units. This 6-and-3 partition is base-10-specific. Prime moduli give different orbit structures, often less aesthetically clean.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> So the honest answer to *the trinary structure is just an artefact of composite 9?*:
>
> *Yes — the trinary structure depends on 9 being composite (specifically 9 = 3²). That's the point. Marko argues base 10 is privileged precisely because its modulus 9 has this composite structure, while prime-modulus alternatives don't have the analogous trinary. Whether base 10's privilege is *real* or *coincidence* is what Wk 9 (scale invariance) and the broader course are about. The trinary is real given base 10; whether base 10 is privileged is contested.*
>
> Forty-eight words.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> One more nuance. Even an opponent of Marko's *base-10-is-privileged* claim should grant that the 3-6-9 trinary structure is *real* given base 10. The math doesn't depend on whether base 10 is universally special; it depends only on choosing 10 (which most of mathematics does, for human-anatomical reasons). The structure is robust within the choice."
