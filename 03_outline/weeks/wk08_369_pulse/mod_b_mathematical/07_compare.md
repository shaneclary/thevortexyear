## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The set {3, 6, 9} (equivalently {0, 3, 6} in standard notation) has two roles in mod 9 algebra. It's an *ideal* of the ring `ℤ/9ℤ` *and* it's *not* a multiplicative subgroup.
>
> Let me unpack both.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Ideal.* In ring theory, an ideal *I* of a ring *R* is a subset closed under addition (a subgroup of the additive group) and absorbent under multiplication (multiplying any element of *I* by any element of *R* gives back something in *I*).
>
> {0, 3, 6} satisfies both:
>
> Closed under addition: *0 + 3 = 3, 0 + 6 = 6, 3 + 3 = 6, 3 + 6 = 0, 6 + 6 = 3*. All sums stay in the set.
>
> Absorbent: multiply any element by any *r* in `ℤ/9ℤ*. *3 × r* always gives a multiple of 3 mod 9, hence stays in {0, 3, 6}. Same for 6.
>
> So {0, 3, 6} is an ideal — specifically, the *principal ideal generated by 3*, written *(3) = 3·ℤ/9ℤ*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Not a multiplicative subgroup.* For {0, 3, 6} to be a multiplicative subgroup of `(ℤ/9ℤ)*`, it would need to (a) contain the multiplicative identity 1 (it doesn't — 1 isn't in the set) and (b) be closed under multiplication.
>
> It fails both. 1 isn't there. *3 × 6 = 18 ≡ 0*, but 0 isn't in `(ℤ/9ℤ)*` either (it's the additive identity, not a unit). So the set isn't even a sub*set* of `(ℤ/9ℤ)*`.
>
> The set lives in the *complement* of the multiplicative group within ℤ/9ℤ. It's where the *non-units* live.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Why this distinction matters.
>
> *As an ideal*: {3, 6, 9} is the natural carrier of the *multiplicative absorption* — multiplying anything by 3, 6, or 9 keeps the result in {3, 6, 9}.
>
> *As non-multiplicative-subgroup*: {3, 6, 9} doesn't have a *clean multiplicative structure* on itself — multiplication isn't well-defined as a closed binary operation (because 3 × 6 = 0 = 9, which is fine in our representation, but it means the set isn't a group, just an ideal).
>
> The 3-6-9 Pulse — the doubling action on this set — is well-defined because doubling is multiplication by 2, which maps the ideal to itself. The Pulse exists as the *image of the doubling map restricted to the ideal*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Useful framing for an algebraist. *The 3-6-9 Pulse is the action of multiplication-by-2 on the principal ideal generated by 3 in ℤ/9ℤ.* Standard ring theory; no novelty in the math; the visualisation as the *Pulse* is VBM's contribution."
