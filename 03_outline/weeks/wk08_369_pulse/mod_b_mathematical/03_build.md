## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Open notebook. Draw a 9-row, 3-column table. Header: *n*, *n × 3*, *n × 6*, *n × 9*. Fill rows for *n = 1 through 9*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Compute each entry mod 9.
>
> *n = 1*: *1 × 3 = 3, 1 × 6 = 6, 1 × 9 = 9.* Identity. Each non-unit fixed.
>
> *n = 2*: *2 × 3 = 6, 2 × 6 = 12 → 3, 2 × 9 = 18 → 9.* Swap 3↔6, fix 9. (Today's main case.)
>
> *n = 3*: *3 × 3 = 9, 3 × 6 = 18 → 9, 3 × 9 = 27 → 9.* All collapse to 9.
>
> *n = 4*: *4 × 3 = 12 → 3, 4 × 6 = 24 → 6, 4 × 9 = 36 → 9.* Identity on non-units. (×4 acts trivially on the ideal.)
>
> *n = 5*: *5 × 3 = 15 → 6, 5 × 6 = 30 → 3, 5 × 9 = 45 → 9.* Same as ×2 — swap 3↔6, fix 9.
>
> *n = 6*: *6 × 3 = 18 → 9, 6 × 6 = 36 → 9, 6 × 9 = 54 → 9.* All collapse.
>
> *n = 7*: *7 × 3 = 21 → 3, 7 × 6 = 42 → 6, 7 × 9 = 63 → 9.* Identity on non-units. (Same as ×4.)
>
> *n = 8*: *8 × 3 = 24 → 6, 8 × 6 = 48 → 3, 8 × 9 = 72 → 9.* Same as ×2 and ×5 — swap 3↔6, fix 9.
>
> *n = 9*: *9 × 3 = 27 → 9, 9 × 6 = 54 → 9, 9 × 9 = 81 → 9.* All collapse.
>
> *[pause — show the full table]*
>
> Patterns. The actions fall into three behavioural classes:
>
> *Identity action* (×1, ×4, ×7): each non-unit maps to itself.
>
> *Swap action* (×2, ×5, ×8): 3 and 6 swap; 9 fixed.
>
> *Collapse action* (×3, ×6, ×9): all non-units collapse to 9.
>
> Three classes, three multipliers each. The classes correspond to the *family* the multiplier belongs to:
>
> - Family 1 (1, 4, 7) → identity action.
> - Family 2 (2, 5, 8) → swap action.
> - Family 3 (3, 6, 9) → collapse action.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *The multiplier's family determines the action's class.* This is a beautiful property — the family structure on the multipliers maps onto the action structure on the non-units. Multiply by anything in family 1, you get identity. Multiply by family 2, you get swap. Multiply by family 3, you get collapse.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> The 3-6-9 Pulse is *one specific case* of this pattern — namely the swap action, which corresponds to multiplying by family-2 elements (including ×2, our doubling map)."
