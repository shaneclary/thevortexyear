## 4. Body (1:00–4:30) — *contemplative*

> *(slow)* "A *fixed point* of a function is an element the function maps to itself.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *f(x) = x.* The function does not move *x*.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> Most functions move most elements. The doubling map on the Symbol moves 1 to 2, moves 2 to 4, moves 4 to 8. The motion is general.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> The 9 is the exception. Doubling sends 9 to 9. The function does not move it.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> Fixed points have a specific role in mathematical dynamics. They are *attractors* — points around which trajectories settle. They are *invariants* — properties preserved by the system. They are *symmetries* — points where the dynamics has nothing to act on.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> In a system with *one fixed point*, the dynamics has *one centre*. The other elements move; the centre stays. The motion is *around* the centre; the centre is *not part of* the motion.
>
> *[long pause — 12 seconds]*
>
> The Symbol's doubling map has *two fixed points* technically — the additive identity (which we call 9) and the multiplicative identity (which we call 1). The 1 is fixed by doubling because *2 × 1 = 2*, not 1, so actually 1 is *not* fixed by doubling. Wait — we need to be more careful.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Let me restate. The doubling map on `(ℤ/9ℤ)*` (the units) has *no fixed points among the units* — because the multiplicative identity 1 is not fixed by ×2. The doubling map on the *whole* ℤ/9ℤ has the *additive* identity 0 (= 9) as its unique fixed point.
>
> The 9 is *the* fixed point. There is no other.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> A unique fixed point in a dynamical system is *the still centre*. It is what the system is not moving. Marko's framing of the 9 as Spirit aligns with this *mathematical* fact: Spirit, in traditional theology, is what *cannot be moved*. The math gives one element with this property. The frame and the math align.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> Sit with the still centre. The doubling moves everything else. The 9 stays.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> The Symbol contains the still centre. So does most of mathematics. Recognising the fixed points is part of what dynamicists do.
>
> *[long pause]*
>
> Stillness as a mathematical fact. Stillness as a contemplative experience. The two are not separate."
