## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "When you trace the Doubling Circuit's helix on a torus, the wire follows the path 1 → 2 → 4 → 8 → 7 → 5 → back to 1. Six segments per cycle.
>
> The 3-6-9 vertices sit *between* certain of these segments — geometrically, where the helix passes near the family-three triangle but doesn't visit those exact vertices.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *How often does the helix pass *near* the 3-6-9 triangle in one cycle?* Three times — once per family-three vertex. The helix's six segments alternate between *passing-near-3*, *passing-near-6*, *passing-near-9*, with three more passes interleaved.
>
> Specifically: the segment from 1 to 2 passes near vertex 9 (between them on the 9-1-2 arc). The segment from 2 to 4 passes near vertex 3. The segment from 4 to 8 passes near vertex 6. Each subsequent segment also passes near one of {3, 6, 9}, completing two full passes around the triangle per cycle.
>
> Wait — let me re-check. Six segments, three triangle vertices, so each segment passes near *one* triangle vertex on average, with each vertex visited *twice* per six-segment cycle.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Cleaner statement. *The Doubling Circuit's six-segment cycle passes near each of {3, 6, 9} exactly twice. Total triggers per cycle: six.*
>
> If the trigger axis has a sensor at each of the three vertices, you get *six trigger events* per Doubling Circuit cycle — two per vertex.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical use. When the main coil is driven at a frequency *f*, the helix completes *f* cycles per second. The trigger axis sees *6f* trigger events per second — six times the main drive frequency.
>
> This is *not* a sixth-harmonic effect in the usual electromagnetic sense. It's a *geometric coincidence*: the trigger axis happens to be hit six times per cycle because of the helix's specific geometry. But it provides a clean *6f signal* if you want one.
>
> Useful for: phase-locked measurement (the 6f signal serves as a reference), harmonic analysis (the 6f component is large), modulation experiments (drive both at related frequencies for resonance).
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Build log addition. When recording a build session, note the trigger-axis configuration: *enabled / disabled, sensor / driver, expected trigger frequency 6f when main coil drive is f*."
