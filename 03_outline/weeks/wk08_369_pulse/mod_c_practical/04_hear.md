## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

**[ORG-CLIP]**

> *"The trigger axis is what you wire through the 3-6-9 positions on the form. It is separate from the main coil's helical winding. Where the helix follows the doubling cycle, the trigger axis sits *outside* the cycle. That outsideness is what makes it useful. When you drive the main coil at a frequency f, the trigger axis sees a clean 6f signal because the helix passes the trigger positions six times per cycle. Drive the trigger axis at 6f independently and you have a phase-locked reference. Drive it at f and you have a modulator. Drive it at 3f or 9f and you set up specific harmonic resonances. The trigger axis turns the 3-6-9 family from a mathematical observation into an engineering tool. Build it once and you have a control input the standard Rodin Coil doesn't expose."*
> — Randy Powell, paraphrased. **Source:** P1-7, P1-13.
