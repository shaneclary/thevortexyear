## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Two three-fold systems often confused.
>
> *Standard 3-phase electrical engineering.* Three windings spaced 120° apart on a stator (motor's fixed core). Driven with three currents at 120° offset in time. Produces a rotating field. Used in motors, generators, power transmission.
>
> *Rodin 3-6-9 trigger axis.* Three points on a torus former at the family-three positions {3, 6, 9}. Wired as a single trigger or three separate points. Driven independently of the main coil. Produces 6f triggers when the main coil runs at f.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three differences.
>
> *Difference one — what's three-fold.* Standard 3-phase: three independent *drive currents* with 120° timing offsets. Rodin 3-6-9: three *physical positions* on a single circuit, possibly all driven at one timing.
>
> *Difference two — what produces what.* Standard 3-phase: three currents → rotating field. Rodin 3-6-9: main coil drive at f → trigger axis sees 6f signal at three positions.
>
> *Difference three — what the structure represents.* Standard 3-phase: angular spacing of windings (120° around the stator's axis). Rodin 3-6-9: family-three vertices on the Symbol, mapping to specific positions on the torus surface.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Despite these differences, the two systems can be combined. The Wk-35 advanced family-phased build (covered last week) uses standard 3-phase drive principles (120° offset currents) on the Rodin family structure (three families becoming three phases). That build is *both* three-phase electrical *and* Rodin 3-6-9 family-phased — the two structures align rather than competing.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Useful framing. *Standard 3-phase is about angular spacing in time. Rodin 3-6-9 is about position spacing on the torus surface. The advanced family-phased build combines both.*
>
> Practical takeaway. When an electrical engineer reads about a Rodin Coil's 3-6-9 structure, they often assume *standard 3-phase* — and are surprised to find the structure is geometric, not temporal. Clarifying *which three-fold system you mean* prevents miscommunication.
>
> When you describe your build, specify: *the trigger axis at 3-6-9 positions, optionally driven independently of the main coil*. This is unambiguous to an engineer."
