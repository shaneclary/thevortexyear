## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Today's build is a derivation. You will write the strip's six rows from the axioms of the multiplicative group of integers modulo 9.
>
> Open your notebook. Title the page *The 6-row strip — derivation*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Axiom 1.* The integers modulo 9, denoted ℤ/9ℤ, form a commutative ring with identity. Write that down.
>
> *Axiom 2.* The units of ℤ/9ℤ — denoted (ℤ/9ℤ)* — are the elements coprime to 9. Compute them: gcd(0, 9) = 9; gcd(1, 9) = 1; gcd(2, 9) = 1; gcd(3, 9) = 3; gcd(4, 9) = 1; gcd(5, 9) = 1; gcd(6, 9) = 3; gcd(7, 9) = 1; gcd(8, 9) = 1.
>
> Units: {1, 2, 4, 5, 7, 8}. Six elements.
>
> *Axiom 3.* (ℤ/9ℤ)* is a finite abelian group under multiplication, of order 6.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Step one — assert.* In a finite group of order 6, by Lagrange's theorem, every element's order divides 6. Possible orders: 1, 2, 3, 6.
>
> *Step two — find the order of 2 in (ℤ/9ℤ)*.* Compute the orbit of 2:
>
> 2¹ = 2.
>
> 2² = 4.
>
> 2³ = 8.
>
> 2⁴ = 16 ≡ 7 mod 9.
>
> 2⁵ = 32 ≡ 5 mod 9. (Or 2 × 7 = 14 ≡ 5.)
>
> 2⁶ = 64 ≡ 1 mod 9. (Or 2 × 5 = 10 ≡ 1.)
>
> The order of 2 is 6. Therefore *2 generates the entire group*. (ℤ/9ℤ)* is cyclic, generated by 2.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Step three — define the rows.* The 6-row strip's row r contains the value 2ʳ mod 9. So:
>
> Row 0: 2⁰ = 1.
>
> Row 1: 2¹ = 2.
>
> Row 2: 2² = 4.
>
> Row 3: 2³ = 8.
>
> Row 4: 2⁴ ≡ 7.
>
> Row 5: 2⁵ ≡ 5.
>
> *Six rows.* Row 6 would be 2⁶ ≡ 1, returning to row 0. The cycle closes.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Step four — verify completeness.* The six rows hold {1, 2, 4, 8, 7, 5} = (ℤ/9ℤ)*. *Every unit appears exactly once.* No unit is missing; no unit is repeated. The strip's vertical axis is a *complete bijection* with the multiplicative group of units.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Conclude.* Write the conclusion. Something like: *The 6-row strip's vertical axis is in canonical bijection with (ℤ/9ℤ)*, with row r corresponding to the unit 2ʳ. The strip is the cyclic-group generator's orbit, displayed linearly rather than circularly.*
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What just happened. *You derived the strip's structure from the axioms of (ℤ/9ℤ)*.* About a hundred and fifty words. Eight or so lines of arithmetic. A statement, an axiom set, a derivation, a verification, a conclusion.
>
> The proof is short because the underlying mathematics is small. (ℤ/9ℤ)* is a finite group of order 6 with a known cyclic structure. The strip is a *visual presentation* of this structure, and the visual is justified by the algebra.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three observations.
>
> *Observation one.* The proof generalises. For any modulus n, the units (ℤ/nℤ)* are computable; if cyclic, a generator gives a 1D 'strip'. For mod 7, the strip would have 6 rows but a different ordering.
>
> *Observation two.* The proof is *self-contained*. Once you have the axioms, the strip's structure follows. No additional input from VBM, geometry, or physical interpretation is needed.
>
> *Observation three.* The horizontal axis is *not* derived in this proof. The horizontal axis comes from the Fibonacci/Pisano structure (Wk 16). Today's derivation only justifies the *vertical*.
>
> Pin the proof in your notebook. It's the first formal derivation of Q2."
