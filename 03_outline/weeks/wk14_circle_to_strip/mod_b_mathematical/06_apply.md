## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Today's application is preparation. We scaffold the empty 6×24 grid that Wk 15 will fill in. The grid is the first 2D substrate of Q2; getting it cleanly drawn now saves time and frustration next week.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Materials.*
>
> A blank A4 or US-letter sheet of paper, oriented landscape (longer side horizontal).
>
> A ruler, twelve inches or thirty centimetres.
>
> A pencil and a sharpener.
>
> Optional: a fine-point pen for tracing once the pencil grid is correct.
>
> Your build log, with a fresh page reserved.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Step 1 — measure the grid area.* On the landscape sheet, measure a rectangle approximately 24 cm wide and 6 cm tall. That gives 1 cm × 1 cm cells if the grid is exactly 24 columns × 6 rows. Mark the four corners lightly in pencil.
>
> If your paper is too small, reduce the cell size to 0.5 cm × 0.5 cm. The grid will be 12 cm × 3 cm. Either size works.
>
> *Step 2 — draw the horizontal lines.* Seven horizontal lines, evenly spaced, define six rows. Use the ruler. Mark each line lightly. Lines should be straight; cell heights should be uniform.
>
> *Step 3 — draw the vertical lines.* Twenty-five vertical lines define twenty-four columns. Same approach: ruler, light pencil, even spacing.
>
> *Step 4 — label the row axis.* In the left margin, label the rows top-to-bottom with the row index r and the doubling value 2ʳ mod 9:
>
> Row 0: r = 0, 2⁰ = 1.
>
> Row 1: r = 1, 2¹ = 2.
>
> Row 2: r = 2, 2² = 4.
>
> Row 3: r = 3, 2³ = 8.
>
> Row 4: r = 4, 2⁴ ≡ 7.
>
> Row 5: r = 5, 2⁵ ≡ 5.
>
> *Step 5 — label the column axis.* Above the grid, label the columns left-to-right 0, 1, 2, … 23. Optionally, also label them with the scale value 10ᶜ mod 9 (which is always 1, but writing it explicitly reinforces the scale-invariance fact).
>
> *Step 6 — leave the cells empty.* Do not fill values today. The grid is *prepared but blank*. Wk 15 fills it.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> *Step 7 — paste or attach the grid into your build log.* Glue or tape the grid sheet onto a Wk 14 build-log page. Title the page *Wk 14-15 grid substrate — empty*.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What you've built. *A 6×24 cell grid with axes labelled, ready to receive values.* The drawing should take ten to fifteen minutes if you go carefully.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three observations on the act of drawing.
>
> *Observation one.* Drawing the grid by hand — slowly, carefully, with a ruler — is *itself part of the lesson*. The act of measuring teaches the structure in a way that printing a pre-made grid wouldn't. Don't print; draw.
>
> *Observation two.* Imperfect grids are fine. The cells don't need to be exactly equal. The mathematical content doesn't depend on metric precision; it depends on the count being right.
>
> *Observation three.* The empty grid will *feel* anticipatory. The cells are waiting for values. The waiting is part of the pedagogy. Wk 15's first lesson will fill cells one by one and the slow filling is part of how the pattern reveals itself.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Practical takeaway. *Doing the prep work in advance smooths the next week.* Most students who draw the empty grid this week have a markedly easier Wk 15 than students who arrive next Monday and discover they need to draw the substrate from scratch."
