## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "Module A of Week 14. Eight lessons reviewed.
>
> Mod A opened with the unfolding move. The Symbol of Enlightenment cut between 9 and 1, straightened into a strip. The math preserved; the visual closure traded for visual room.
>
> Then the cell counts. 1×6, 2×6, 6×24. Six and twenty-four are the dimensions; 144 is their product. The numbers come from Q1's group structure (the doubling order is 6; the Pisano period is 24).
>
> Then the build — physically cutting a printed Symbol and pasting the strip into your build log. The unfolding made tangible.
>
> Marko spoke on needing a wider canvas. The circle ran out of room when the second axis tried to appear. Hand-drawing was part of the recognition.
>
> A contemplative beat on the trade of closure for room. Closed forms feel like home; open forms feel like threshold. Q2 voluntarily steps from home to threshold.
>
> Then we applied. The strip as a working bookmark — annotated on the back, placed in a daily-use book, supporting passive exposure and active recall.
>
> Comparison: the circle and the strip as cross-sections of the same math. Each makes different features easy. Both belong in a working practitioner's toolkit.
>
> Then the calibrated defence. *Isn't this just a fancy table?* Eighty words: spatial orientation, prepared incompleteness, eventual topological closure. The strip carries structure a flat table doesn't.
>
