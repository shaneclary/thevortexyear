## 0. Authoring notes
First lesson of Q2. The Symbol unfolded into a strip — the conceptual move from 1D loop to 2D extension. Sets the visual ground for the geometric quarter.

## 1. Outcome
Student can describe what unfolding the circle into a strip *changes* and what it *preserves*.

## 2. Hook (0:00–0:30)
*[A circle of 1-9 on the left. An arrow. A strip of nine numbered cells on the right. The 9 from the top of the circle and the 9 at one end of the strip glow.]*

> "Unfold the circle. The math stays. The room expands."

## 3. Setup (0:30–1:00)
*[Carry-over: yesterday's bridge closed Q1 with the spiral fully lit through Wk 13 and the Q2 arc pulsing.]*

> "Lens 1 of 9, Module A, Week 14. Q2 opens. The first geometric move."

## 4. Body (1:00–4:30)

> "The Symbol of Enlightenment is a circle. Nine numbers on a closed loop. For twelve weeks you have drawn it and traced doubling on it.
>
> Today we *cut* the circle. Cut it once, between 9 and 1. The closed loop becomes an open arc. Then straighten the arc into a *strip*.
>
> The result: a row of nine cells, numbered 1 through 9 along its length. The last cell (9) and the first cell (1) used to be neighbours; they're now at opposite ends.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> Three things to notice about this unfolding.
>
> *Notice one — what's preserved.* The nine numbers are the same. The doubling rule is the same. *1 doubles to 2; 2 doubles to 4; 4 doubles to 8; 8 doubles to 16 ≡ 7; 7 doubles to 14 ≡ 5; 5 doubles to 10 ≡ 1.* The arithmetic doesn't notice that you cut the circle. Modular reduction works the same on the strip as on the loop.
>
> *Notice two — what's lost.* The *visual immediacy of the closure*. On the circle, position 9 and position 1 are adjacent — you see the wraparound. On the strip, they are at opposite ends. To follow the doubling from 5 back to 1, your eye has to jump across the strip. The closure is now *implicit* rather than visible.
>
> *Notice three — what's gained.* *Room for a second axis.* The circle is a one-dimensional object. It has no perpendicular direction. The strip has *two* directions: along its length and across its width. The width is empty — *for now*. Wk 15 fills it.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> The unfolding move, stated abstractly. *We exchanged visual closure for visual room.* On the circle, closure was free; room was unavailable. On the strip, room is free; closure is implicit. Each visualisation has its strengths.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> What the strip will become.
>
> *Wk 15.* The strip's empty width fills with a second arithmetic axis: powers of 10. The strip becomes a *grid*: nine rows × twenty-four columns. The Rodin Number Map.
>
> *Wk 16.* The grid wraps. Both axes close into loops, the strip becomes the surface of a torus. The closure that was lost when we cut the circle returns — in two dimensions instead of one.
>
> *Wk 17 onward.* The doubling circuit, drawn on the toroidal surface, traces a *helix*. The figure-eight you've drawn on circles for twelve weeks lifts off the page and into 3D as a coil winding.
>
> *[pause]*
>
> So today's move — circle to strip — is the *first* move of a sequence that takes us from 2D to 3D. Each move is small; the cumulative effect is a complete dimensional lift.
>
> Hold the strip flat in your build log. Open at one end, closed at the other (implicitly). The width waits for next week."
