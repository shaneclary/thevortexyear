# Vortex-Based Mathematics — Tiered Course Outline (Draft v0.1)

> **Status:** First-pass outline based on the public material catalogued in `01_sources/SOURCE_LIBRARY.md` and the concept glossary in `01_sources/KEY_CONCEPTS.md`. Designed for the organisation to review, mark up, and have us iterate before any lesson scripts are written.

## Course philosophy

- **A → Z, but in three tracks.** A single linear path tries to serve everyone and serves no one. Rodin's material spans contemplative/spiritual framing → number-theoretic patterns → physical engineering. Different students arrive with different goals.
- **Each track is independently completable** but the modules are aligned, so students can step up a tier when ready.
- **Common spine:** every track works through the *same nine canonical artefacts* (Symbol of Enlightenment, Doubling Circuit, 3-6-9 oscillation, Polar Number Pairs, Family Number Groups, ABHA Torus, Decoqubit, Rodin Coil, Aetheron framework). The depth and tooling differ; the artefacts do not.
- **Authorial voice is preserved.** Where a concept is named or framed by Rodin or Powell, the course uses *their* terminology and quotes them directly. The course's job is to teach — not to rewrite.
- **Anticipates objections.** Rather than ignoring critical reception, each module ends with a "what skeptics ask" sidebar with the org's preferred response. This prepares students who'll meet pushback in the wild.

**Audience:** No prior math beyond grade-school arithmetic. Open-minded learners drawn to the spiritual/sacred-geometry framing.
**Length target:** 12 modules, ~30–45 minutes each.
**Prereqs:** None.
**Outcome:** Student can draw the Symbol of Enlightenment, explain the Doubling Circuit, articulate the role of 9, and identify the ABHA Torus structure on sight.

**Length target:** 10 modules, project-based, weeks not hours.
**Prereqs:** Track 2 or equivalent. Comfort with: basic electronics, soldering, reading wiring diagrams. Ideally some 3D-printing or fabrication access.

## Cross-track elements

### Per-module structure (proposed standard)
Each module across all tracks follows the same shape, so students moving up tiers don't get pedagogical whiplash:

1. **Setup** (1–2 min): What you'll be able to do at the end.
2. **Concept** (8–15 min): The teaching, with diagrams and primary-source quotes.
3. **Walkthrough** (10–20 min): Worked example or hands-on exercise.
4. **Practice** (variable): Exercises with answers.
5. **Skeptics' corner** (3–5 min): Common objections and the org's response.
6. **Further reading**: Direct links into `01_sources/SOURCE_LIBRARY.md`.

### Glossary
Every named term links to `01_sources/KEY_CONCEPTS.md`. Student-facing version is auto-generated from that file.

### Assessment
- **Track 1:** Quiz at end of each module + a "draw and explain the Symbol of Enlightenment" capstone.
- **Track 2:** Problem sets (digital-root proofs, wrapping exercises) + a written explanation of the Decoqubit-Torus relationship.
- **Track 3:** Build portfolio + bench-test report.

### Materials inventory we'd need to commission
- Diagrams: ~40 original or licensed VBM diagrams (Symbol, doubling traces, polar pairs, family groups, torus wrap, coil winding, Aetheron flow, enneagram overlay).
- Animations: torus wrap, doubling-circuit pulse, 3-6-9 oscillation.
- Worked-example videos for Track 2.
- Build videos for Track 3.
